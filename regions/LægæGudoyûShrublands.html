The Lægæ Gudoyû Shrublands (/ˈlægæ/ /guˈdojʊ/ Tradesworth [fluent] [size; amount; quantity]) are a group of subtropical shrubland on the coast of the Southlake region. Lægæ Gudoyû Shrublands are a relatively typical shrubland for Southlake, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Lægæ Gudoyû Shrublands has a yearly average temperature of 18°C (64°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 26°C (78°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 10°C (50°F). The Lægæ Gudoyû Shrublands receives an average of 74 cm/y (29 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. The Lægæ Gudoyû Shrublands covers an area of nearly 5196 km2 (3229 mi2), and an average elevation of 6095 m (19996 ft) above sea level.
