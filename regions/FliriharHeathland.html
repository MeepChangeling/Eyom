The Flirihar Heathland (Fliri /har/ Fliri's Abundance [Proper Noun (name of person)] [amount; quantity]) is a subtropical heathland on the coast of the Foxvale region. Flirihar Heathland is a relatively typical heathland for Foxvale, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Flirihar Heathland has a yearly average temperature of 12°C (53°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing -1°C (31°F). The Flirihar Heathland receives an average of 152 cm/y (59 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. The Flirihar Heathland covers an area of nearly 10058 km2 (6251 mi2), and an average elevation of 4482 m (14704 ft) above sea level.

The Flirihar Heathland is home to 273493 people. They break down into the following demographics: 57% Wood Elves, 24% Wood Elves, 4% Gnolls, 9% Tanuki, 4% Kobold, and ~2% Other.

The Flirihar Heathland is's peoples are culturally Wood Elven, but have their local customs and traditions. Their shared values and traditions include restoring some real or imagined glorious past and magical prowess and occult ability, as well as a unique arcane festival and a summer festival.

Wood Elf

The Wood Elf subculture native to the Flirihar Heathland are noticably either short and stocky or tall and slender. While they follow the broader tennents of Wood Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of impractical or elaborate role-based clothes and base their sence of individual idenity on religious factions or faith alliances. They also place greater cultural emphasus on industry and the ability to work tirelessly and prosperity and accruing material wealth.

Sylvian

The Sylvian subculture native to the Flirihar Heathland are noticably either short and stocky or tall and slender. While they follow the broader tennents of Wood Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of color choices with social meaning to them and base their sence of individual idenity on warbands or civic military service groups. They also place greater cultural emphasus on honor and maintaining one’s integrity and subtlety and indirectness of action.

Gnoll

The Gnoll subculture native to the Flirihar Heathland are noticably smaller and slighter than their neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Wood Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of painted skin markings that sometimes change and base their sence of individual idenity on warbands or civic military service groups. They also place greater cultural emphasus on faithfulness towards one’s chosen friends and exploring the unknown and discovering secrets.

Tanuki

The Tanuki subculture native to the Flirihar Heathland are noticably much bigger and bulkier than neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Wood Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of intricate hair styles or braiding and base their sence of individual idenity on professional guild or trade-specific group. They also place greater cultural emphasus on seeking fortune in new places or new roles and sharing wealth and goods with others.

Kobold

The Kobold subculture native to the Flirihar Heathland are noticably same height and weight as the neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Wood Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of color choices with social meaning to them and base their sence of individual idenity on voluntary mutual-assistance brotherhoods. They also place greater cultural emphasus on cunning and the ability to trick others and remembrance of the past and memorializing history.

Local Religion

The Flirihar Heathland is overseen by Potentate Ezdall Mneid Weandronis, a god serving within Labdsism, the region's principal religion. Approximately 18% of the population self-report as devout. The Flirihar Heathland is overflowing with shrines and holy sites, with most noteworthy divines having one or more shrines in any location relevant to their portfolio.

Settlements and Economy

Flirihar Heathland has its economic roots in Foxvale's export of Steel, but has developed its own ceramic industry, which has a marked impact on the local regional economy.

The Flirihar Heathland has an Imperial Development Index of 20. For the reference of Eyom's native peoples, a IDI of 20 means there are 20 locations within the Flirihar Heathland which the Empire can securely and regularly transport materials and personnel..
