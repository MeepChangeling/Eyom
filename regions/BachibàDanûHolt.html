The Bachibà Danû Holt (/baˈʧibə/ /ˈdanʊ/ Miser's [careful] [bargain]) is a subtropical holt on the coast of the Abysstro region. The Bachibà Danû Holt is well known for being home to remarkably few animals.

Climate

The Bachibà Danû Holt has a yearly average temperature of 19°C (66°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 11°C (51°F). The Bachibà Danû Holt receives an average of 156 cm/y (61 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. The Bachibà Danû Holt covers an area of nearly 13031 km2 (8098 mi2), and an average elevation of 2921 m (9583 ft) above sea level.

The Bachibà Danû Holt is home to 499732 people. They break down into the following demographics: 58% Wood Elves, 27% Fae, 9% Kivu, 4% Tanuki, and ~2% Other.

The Bachibà Danû Holt is's peoples are culturally Wood Elven, but have their local customs and traditions. Their shared values and traditions include submission to the collective will or culture and personal indulgence and luxuriant pleasure, as well as a unique spirit festival and a folk festival.

While they follow the broader tennents of Wood Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of color choices with social meaning to them and base their sence of individual idenity on having or lacking noble blood. They also place greater cultural emphasus on faithfulness towards one’s chosen friends and excellence in one’s profession or trade.

Fae

The Fae subculture native to the Bachibà Danû Holt are noticably much bigger and bulkier than neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Wood Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of color choices with social meaning to them and base their sence of individual idenity on ethnic membership. They also place greater cultural emphasus on zealous guardianship of their own land or holy sit and pacifism and peaceful resolution of problems.

Kivu

The Kivu subculture native to the Bachibà Danû Holt are noticably either short and stocky or tall and slender. While they follow the broader tennents of Wood Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of tattoos of some cultural significance and base their sence of individual idenity on warbands or civic military service groups. They also place greater cultural emphasus on ascetic unworldliness and pious poverty and industry and the ability to work tirelessly.

Tanuki

The Tanuki subculture native to the Bachibà Danû Holt are noticably smaller and slighter than their neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Wood Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of piercings, whether minor or elaborate and base their sence of individual idenity on regional and province-based identity. They also place greater cultural emphasus on membership in an elite bloodline or caste and individual rights and freedom of action.

Local Religion

The Bachibà Danû Holt is overseen by Potentate Shumes Euandra Ælre, a god serving within Way of Ashlin, the region's principal religion. Approximately 33% of the population self-report as devout. The Bachibà Danû Holt is overflowing with shrines and holy sites, with most noteworthy divines having one or more shrines in any location relevant to their portfolio.

Settlements and Economy

Bachibà Danû Holt has its economic roots in Abysstro's export of Spirits, but has developed its own innovation industry, which has a marked impact on the local regional economy.
