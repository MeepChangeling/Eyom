The Frëtöf-me̜ Woodlands (/ˈfrɛto˞f/ /me̜/ Poormelt [shit] [vulcanization (rubber processing)]) are a group of temperate woodland within the wetland of the Icereef region. Frëtöf-me̜ Woodlands are a relatively typical woodland for Icereef, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Frëtöf-me̜ Woodlands has a yearly average temperature of 12°C (53°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 23°C (73°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 1°C (33°F). The Frëtöf-me̜ Woodlands receives an average of 218 cm/y (85 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. The Frëtöf-me̜ Woodlands covers an area of nearly 10118 km2 (6288 mi2), and an average elevation of 1278 m (4192 ft) above sea level.

People and Culture

However, as our culture lacks a taboo for classification of peoples by their biology and such information is highly important for our medical practices amongst other institutions, we have stated the racial demographics of this region. In respect to this universal cultural element, we have split racial classification by culture while keeping the name of each race for our own purposes. Should you see the same race listed more than once in the following section, this is to designate a major cultural division within a race.

The Frëtöf-me̜ Woodlands are home to 196457 people. They break down into the following demographics: 50% Wareneese, 25% Hobgoblins, 10% Dwarves, 8% Elf, 5% Imperial, and ~2% Other.

The Frëtöf-me̜ Woodlands are's peoples are culturally Wareneese, but have their local customs and traditions. Their shared values and traditions include courage and valiance in danger and excellence in one’s profession or trade, as well as a unique literary festival and a storytelling festival.

Wareneese

The Wareneese subculture native to the Frëtöf-me̜ Woodlands are noticably either short and stocky or tall and slender. While they follow the broader tennents of Wareneese culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of color choices with social meaning to them and base their sence of individual idenity on inheritance of an ancient body modification. They also place greater cultural emphasus on sharing wealth and goods with others and exploring the unknown and discovering secrets.

Hobgoblin

The Hobgoblin subculture native to the Frëtöf-me̜ Woodlands are noticably either short and stocky or tall and slender. While they follow the broader tennents of Wareneese culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of patterned hair shaving or depilation and base their sence of individual idenity on far-flung clans of affiliated families. They also place greater cultural emphasus on excellence in one’s profession or trade and vengeance and execution of just vendettas.

Dwarf

The Dwarf subculture native to the Frëtöf-me̜ Woodlands are noticably same height and weight as the neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Wareneese culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of tattoos of some cultural significance and base their sence of individual idenity on hereditary loyalty to a political group. They also place greater cultural emphasus on societal or ethnic superiority over all outsiders and filial devotion to family and parents.

Elf

The Elf subculture native to the Frëtöf-me̜ Woodlands are noticably either short and stocky or tall and slender. While they follow the broader tennents of Wareneese culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of role or class-specific clothing items and base their sence of individual idenity on ideological groups or philosophical sects. They also place greater cultural emphasus on cunning and the ability to trick others and ethnic purity of blood and culture.

Imperial

The Imperial subculture native to the Frëtöf-me̜ Woodlands are noticably smaller and slighter than their neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Wareneese culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of impractical or elaborate role-based clothes and base their sence of individual idenity on dynastic lineages with cadet branches. They also place greater cultural emphasus on filial devotion to family and parents and justice and fairness between people.

Local Religion

The Frëtöf-me̜ Woodlands are overseen by Potentate Zurgyn Muid Hyacchos, a god serving within Labdsism, the region's principal religion. Approximately 30% of the population self-report as devout. The Frëtöf-me̜ Woodlands is considered underserved by the divines despite shrines in all settled areas.

Settlements and Economy

Frëtöf-me̜ Woodlands has its economic roots in Icereef's export of Fish, but has developed its own fertilizer industry, which has a marked impact on the local regional economy.

The Frëtöf-me̜ Woodlands has an Imperial Development Index of 17. For the reference of Eyom's native peoples, a IDI of 17 means there are 17 locations within the Frëtöf-me̜ Woodlands which the Empire can securely and regularly transport materials and personnel..
