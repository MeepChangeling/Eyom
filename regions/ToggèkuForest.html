The Toggèku Forest (/tog/ /ˈgɛku/ Sharpwit [memory] [exact; precise]) is a subtropical forest within the temperate rainforest of the Westbay region. The Toggèku Forest is well known for being largely barren of plant life.

Climate

The Toggèku Forest has a yearly average temperature of 18°C (64°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 11°C (51°F). The Toggèku Forest receives an average of 296 cm/y (116 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. The Toggèku Forest covers an area of nearly 9427 km2 (5858 mi2), and an average elevation of 904 m (2965 ft) above sea level.
