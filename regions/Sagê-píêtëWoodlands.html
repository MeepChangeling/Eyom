The Sagê-píêtë Woodlands (/ˈsagɘ/ /ˈp˔ɘtë/ Spouse's [significant] [half]) are a group of subtropical woodland within the temperate rainforest of the Polymer Coast region. Sagê-píêtë Woodlands are a relatively typical woodland for Polymer Coast, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Sagê-píêtë Woodlands has a yearly average temperature of 23°C (73°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 28°C (82°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 18°C (64°F). The Sagê-píêtë Woodlands receives an average of 288 cm/y (113 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. The Sagê-píêtë Woodlands covers an area of nearly 11827 km2 (7350 mi2), and an average elevation of 2510 m (8234 ft) above sea level.

The Sagê-píêtë Woodlands are home to 201850 people. They break down into the following demographics: 69% Constructs, 16% Kitsune, 7% Grey Elf, 6% Elf, and ~2% Other.

The Sagê-píêtë Woodlands are's peoples are culturally Constructi, but have their local customs and traditions. Their shared values and traditions include personal sacrifice for one’s causes or purposes and raw strength and personal prowess, as well as a unique fire festival and a science festival.

Construct

The Construct subculture native to the Sagê-píêtë Woodlands are noticably much bigger and bulkier than neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Construct culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of painted skin markings that sometimes change and base their sence of individual idenity on professional guild or trade-specific group. They also place greater cultural emphasus on building things in service of their posterity and honesty and truthfulness in speech.

Kitsune

The Kitsune subculture native to the Sagê-píêtë Woodlands are noticably same height and weight as the neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Construct culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of impractical or elaborate role-based clothes and base their sence of individual idenity on far-flung clans of affiliated families. They also place greater cultural emphasus on cunning and the ability to trick others and zealous guardianship of their own land or holy sit.

Grey Elf

The Grey Elf subculture native to the Sagê-píêtë Woodlands are noticably same height and weight as the neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Construct culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of worn weapons, tools or trade implements and base their sence of individual idenity on astrologically-determined birth groups. They also place greater cultural emphasus on ascetic unworldliness and pious poverty and magical prowess and occult ability.

Elf

The Elf subculture native to the Sagê-píêtë Woodlands are noticably much bigger and bulkier than neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Construct culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of tattoos of some cultural significance and base their sence of individual idenity on educational ties to institutions or traditions. They also place greater cultural emphasus on conquest and domination of others and magical prowess and occult ability.

Local Religion

The Sagê-píêtë Woodlands are overseen by Potentate Aotl Æðeles Emeraintho, a god serving within Covenant of Irus, the region's principal religion. Approximately 20% of the population self-report as devout. The Sagê-píêtë Woodlands is considered underserved by the divines despite shrines in all settled areas.

Settlements and Economy

Sagê-píêtë Woodlands is predominantly economicly reiant on Polymer Coast's export of Fruits, and has no outstanding industries byond what is required for its role in that process.

The Sagê-píêtë Woodlands has an Imperial Development Index of 9. For the reference of Eyom's native peoples, a IDI of 9 means there are 9 locations within the Sagê-píêtë Woodlands which the Empire can securely and regularly transport materials and personnel..

As a courtesy for scholars and agents, the Sagê-píêtë Woodlands is rated M1 by Eyom’s banks. Business ventures within the Sagê-píêtë Woodlands are not expected to return value in yearly terms.
