The Headless Steppe (haverhead /stɛp/ Headless [Translation Unavailable]) is a subtropical steppe on the coast of the Ancestor's Range region. Headless Steppe is a relatively typical steppe for Ancestor's Range, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Headless Steppe has a yearly average temperature of 31°C (87°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a blistering 36°C (96°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 27°C (80°F). The Headless Steppe receives an average of 201 cm/y (79 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. The Headless Steppe covers an area of nearly 10687 km2 (6642 mi2), and an average elevation of 15401 m (7578 ft) above sea level.

The Headless Steppe is home to 298600 people. They break down into the following demographics: 66% Constructs, 10% Kitsune, 9% Fae, 7% Kobold, 6% Kobold, and ~2% Other.

The Headless Steppe is's peoples are culturally Constructi, but have their local customs and traditions. Their shared values and traditions include piety and devotion to the gods and filial devotion to family and parents, as well as a unique literary festival and a arcane festival.

Construct

The Construct subculture native to the Headless Steppe are noticably much bigger and bulkier than neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Construct culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of culturally-significant jewelry or accessories and base their sence of individual idenity on dynastic lineages with cadet branches. They also place greater cultural emphasus on industry and the ability to work tirelessly and zealous guardianship of their own land or holy sit.

Kitsune

The Kitsune subculture native to the Headless Steppe are noticably either short and stocky or tall and slender. While they follow the broader tennents of Construct culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of painted skin markings that sometimes change and base their sence of individual idenity on ethnic membership. They also place greater cultural emphasus on honor and maintaining one’s integrity and sharing wealth and goods with others.

Fae

The Fae subculture native to the Headless Steppe are noticably same height and weight as the neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Construct culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of significant scent or perfume uses and base their sence of individual idenity on patron-client relationships with major figures. They also place greater cultural emphasus on unity and elimination of group differences and building things in service of their posterity.

Kobold

The Kobold subculture native to the Headless Steppe are noticably either short and stocky or tall and slender. While they follow the broader tennents of Construct culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of impractical or elaborate role-based clothes and base their sence of individual idenity on identity based on their local community. They also place greater cultural emphasus on societal or ethnic superiority over all outsiders and restoring some real or imagined glorious past.

Quenn

The Quenn subculture native to the Headless Steppe are noticably much bigger and bulkier than neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Construct culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of intricate hair styles or braiding and base their sence of individual idenity on numerous castes of hereditary workers. They also place greater cultural emphasus on magical prowess and occult ability and excellence in one’s profession or trade.

Local Religion

The Headless Steppe is overseen by Potentate Dhatbium Damoneiapon Maponiu, a god serving within The Council of Twelve, the region's principal religion. Approximately 36% of the population self-report as devout. The Headless Steppe is overflowing with shrines and holy sites, with most noteworthy divines having one or more shrines in any location relevant to their portfolio.

Settlements and Economy

Headless Steppe has its economic roots in Ancestor's Range's export of Stone, but has developed its own agriculture industry, which has a marked impact on the local regional economy.

The Headless Steppe has an Imperial Development Index of 22. For the reference of Eyom's native peoples, a IDI of 22 means there are 22 locations within the Headless Steppe which the Empire can securely and regularly transport materials and personnel..

As a courtesy for scholars and agents, the Headless Steppe is rated I1 by Eyom’s banks. Business ventures within the Headless Steppe are expected to return above capitol at low risk.
