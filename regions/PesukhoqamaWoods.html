The Pesukhoqama Woods (/ˈqama/ Pesukho's [Proper Noun (name of person)] [flavor]) are a group of temperate woods within the temperate rainforest of the Equuscoast region. The Pesukhoqama Woods are the birthplace of the Macketersism, a major Tauric religion.

Climate

The Pesukhoqama Woods has a yearly average temperature of 23°C (73°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 19°C (66°F). The Pesukhoqama Woods receives an average of 294 cm/y (115 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. The Pesukhoqama Woods covers an area of nearly 21057 km2 (13087 mi2), and an average elevation of 2056 m (6745 ft) above sea level.
