The Eba Chyî Woodlands (/ˈeba/ /cɪː/ Beloved [popular] [thing; object]) are a group of subtropical woodland on the coast of the Abysstro region. Eba Chyî Woodlands are a relatively typical woodland for Abysstro, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Eba Chyî Woodlands has a yearly average temperature of 19°C (66°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 28°C (82°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 10°C (50°F). The Eba Chyî Woodlands receives an average of 151 cm/y (59 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. The Eba Chyî Woodlands covers an area of nearly 12195 km2 (7579 mi2), and an average elevation of 3131 m (10272 ft) above sea level.

The Eba Chyî Woodlands are home to 1419583 people. They break down into the following demographics: 55% Wood Elves, 12% Kitsune, 18% Dwarves, 5% Tanuki, 7% Kobold, 1% Kobold, and ~2% Other.

The Eba Chyî Woodlands are's peoples are culturally Wood Elven, but have their local customs and traditions. Their shared values and traditions include unity and elimination of group differences and remembrance of the past and memorializing history, as well as a unique history festival and a winter festival.

Local Religion

The Eba Chyî Woodlands are overseen by Potentate Dronta Leopeto Sionthniu, a god serving within The Seven Titans, the region's principal religion. Approximately 57% of the population self-report as devout. The Eba Chyî Woodlands is overflowing with shrines and holy sites, with most noteworthy divines having one or more shrines in any location relevant to their portfolio.

Settlements and Economy

Eba Chyî Woodlands has its economic roots in Abysstro's export of Spirits, but has developed its own glass industry, which has a marked impact on the local regional economy.

The Eba Chyî Woodlands has an Imperial Development Index of 24. For the reference of Eyom's native peoples, a IDI of 24 means there are 24 locations within the Eba Chyî Woodlands which the Empire can securely and regularly transport materials and personnel..
