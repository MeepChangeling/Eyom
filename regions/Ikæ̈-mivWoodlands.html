The Ikæ̈-miv Woodlands (/iˈkæ̝/ /miv/ Workshop [technological] [situation (circumstances; context)]) are a group of temperate woodland on the coast of the Palebay region. The Ikæ̈-miv Woodlands are well known for being largely barren of plant life.

Climate

The Ikæ̈-miv Woodlands has a yearly average temperature of 26°C (78°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 30°C (86°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 23°C (73°F). The Ikæ̈-miv Woodlands receives an average of 135 cm/y (53 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. The Ikæ̈-miv Woodlands covers an area of nearly 19680 km2 (12231 mi2), and an average elevation of 5819 m (19091 ft) above sea level.
