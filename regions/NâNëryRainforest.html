The Nâ Nëry Rainforest (/nɑ̂/ /nɜ̄ːiː/ Hubris [wrong] [pride]) is a subtropical rainforest on the coast of the Lower Warding Range region. The Nâ Nëry Rainforest is the hottest local region in Eyom with its yearly average temperature resting at 35°C (95°F).

Climate

The Nâ Nëry Rainforest has a yearly average temperature of 35°C (95°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a blistering 39°C (102°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 30°C (86°F). The Nâ Nëry Rainforest receives an average of 67 cm/y (26 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. The Nâ Nëry Rainforest covers an area of nearly 14627 km2 (9090 mi2), and an average elevation of 18116 m (16486 ft) above sea level.

People and Culture

Imperial Warning: The following section of this document deals with biological race. In deference to the traditions of Eyomic peoples, we have foregone town-specific racial census. However, as our culture lacks a taboo for classification of peoples by their biology and such information is highly important for our medical practices amongst other institutions, we have stated the racial demographics of this region. We do not do so to sew the seeds of disunity. We do not do so to belittle or bemoan any group. Such issues are why we use the term 'peoples' rather than races, cultures, or species.

In respect to this universal cultural element, we have split racial classification by culture while keeping the name of each race for our own purposes. Should you see the same race listed more than once in the following section, this is to designate a major cultural division within a race.

The Nâ Nëry Rainforest is home to 552121 people. They break down into the following demographics: 58% Wood Elves, 18% Dwarves, 9% Wareneese, 5% Tanuki, 8% Kivu, and ~2% Other.

The Nâ Nëry Rainforest is's peoples are culturally Wood Elven, but have their local customs and traditions. Their shared values and traditions include loyalty to one’s friends, family, and own and sexual license and wantonness, as well as a unique harvest festival and a rain festival.

Wood Elf

The Wood Elf subculture native to the Nâ Nëry Rainforest are noticably smaller and slighter than their neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Wood Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of worn weapons, tools or trade implements and base their sence of individual idenity on identity based on their local community. They also place greater cultural emphasus on sexual license and wantonness and honesty and truthfulness in speech.

Dwarf

The Dwarf subculture native to the Nâ Nëry Rainforest are noticably either short and stocky or tall and slender. While they follow the broader tennents of Wood Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of patterned hair shaving or depilation and base their sence of individual idenity on ethnic membership. They also place greater cultural emphasus on discipline and obedience to the law and personal indulgence and luxuriant pleasure.

Rattu

The Rattu subculture native to the Nâ Nëry Rainforest are noticably smaller and slighter than their neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Wood Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of patterned hair shaving or depilation and base their sence of individual idenity on patron-client relationships with major figures. They also place greater cultural emphasus on societal or ethnic superiority over all outsiders and discipline and obedience to the law.

Tanuki

The Tanuki subculture native to the Nâ Nëry Rainforest are noticably either short and stocky or tall and slender. While they follow the broader tennents of Wood Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of piercings, whether minor or elaborate and base their sence of individual idenity on ideological groups or philosophical sects. They also place greater cultural emphasus on conquest and domination of others and courage and valiance in danger.

Kivu

The Kivu subculture native to the Nâ Nëry Rainforest are noticably much bigger and bulkier than neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Wood Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of color choices with social meaning to them and base their sence of individual idenity on inheritance of an ancient body modification. They also place greater cultural emphasus on submission to lawful authority and humanistic reason and “rational” religion.

Local Religion

The Nâ Nëry Rainforest is overseen by Potentate Theneas Neopto Ciapoine, a god serving within Covenant of Irus, the region's principal religion. Approximately 42% of the population self-report as devout. The Nâ Nëry Rainforest is considered underserved by the divines despite shrines in all settled areas.

Settlements and Economy

Nâ Nëry Rainforest is a negative return economy zone at this time. It has an abundence of Undead, though infrastructure to this region of Lower Warding Range is not developed enough to support any major local industry.

The Nâ Nëry Rainforest has an Imperial Development Index of 3. For the reference of Eyom's native peoples, a IDI of 3 means there are 3 locations within the Nâ Nëry Rainforest which the Empire can securely and regularly transport materials and personnel..

As a courtesy for scholars and agents, the Nâ Nëry Rainforest is rated M2 by Eyoms banks. Consult a bank for information relating to its economy.
