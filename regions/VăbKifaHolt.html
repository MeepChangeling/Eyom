The Văb Kifa Holt (/və̝b/ /ˈcifa/ Monitor's [honest; sincere] [reptile]) is a subtropical holt within the taiga of the Diamond Basin region. The Văb Kifa Holt is well known for being home to remarkably few animals.

Climate

The Văb Kifa Holt has a yearly average temperature of 30°C (86°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 33°C (91°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 26°C (78°F). The Văb Kifa Holt receives an average of 68 cm/y (26 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. The Văb Kifa Holt covers an area of nearly 9207 km2 (5722 mi2), and an average elevation of 3783 m (12411 ft) above sea level.
