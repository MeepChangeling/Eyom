The Fè Vokhom Moorland (/fɤ/ /ˈvoxom/ Benediction [good; fabulous; innocent; hot (attractive); fine (okay); moral] [lecture; sermon]) is a subtropical moorland on the coast of the Anvil Badlands region. Fè Vokhom Moorland is a relatively typical moorland for Anvil Badlands, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Fè Vokhom Moorland has a yearly average temperature of 24°C (75°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 22°C (71°F). The Fè Vokhom Moorland receives an average of 74 cm/y (29 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. The Fè Vokhom Moorland covers an area of nearly 7867 km2 (4889 mi2), and an average elevation of 4697 m (15410 ft) above sea level.

The Fè Vokhom Moorland is's peoples are culturally Wood Elven, but have their local customs and traditions. Their shared values and traditions include vengeance and execution of just vendettas and cunning and the ability to trick others, as well as a unique spring festival and a sun festival.

Local Religion

The Fè Vokhom Moorland is overseen by Potentate Oheia Muirealaean Galthan, a god serving within Fawresm, the region's principal religion. Approximately 12% of the population self-report as devout. The Fè Vokhom Moorland is considered underserved by the divines despite shrines in all settled areas.

Settlements and Economy

Fè Vokhom Moorland is predominantly economicly reiant on Anvil Badlands's export of Clay, and has no outstanding industries byond what is required for its role in that process.

The Fè Vokhom Moorland has an Imperial Development Index of 8. For the reference of Eyom's native peoples, a IDI of 8 means there are 8 locations within the Fè Vokhom Moorland which the Empire can securely and regularly transport materials and personnel..

As a courtesy for scholars and agents, the Fè Vokhom Moorland is rated M1 by Eyom’s banks. Business ventures within the Fè Vokhom Moorland are not expected to return value in yearly terms.
