The Kapusburns Basin (kapusburns /ˈbeɪsən/ Kapusburns [Translation Unavailable]) is a subtropical basin on the coast of the Cutmountains region. The Kapusburns Basin is well known for being home to remarkably few animals.

Climate

The Kapusburns Basin has a yearly average temperature of 33°C (91°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a blistering 37°C (98°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 29°C (84°F). The Kapusburns Basin receives an average of 68 cm/y (26 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. The Kapusburns Basin covers an area of nearly 11639 km2 (7233 mi2), and an average elevation of 14440 m (4425 ft) above sea level.

Local Religion

The Kapusburns Basin is overseen by Potentate Turtris Korone Eudorawl, a god serving within The Council of Twelve, the region's principal religion. Approximately 14% of the population self-report as devout. The Kapusburns Basin is considered underserved by the divines despite shrines in all settled areas.
