The Huhinuru Holt (/ˈhuhi/ /ˈnuru/ Ruststump [stump] [orange]) is a subtropical holt within the temperate rainforest of the Shattered Peninsula region. The Huhinuru Holt is well known for its thriving abundance of plant life.

Climate

The Huhinuru Holt has a yearly average temperature of 21°C (69°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 18°C (64°F). The Huhinuru Holt receives an average of 292 cm/y (114 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. The Huhinuru Holt covers an area of nearly 9698 km2 (6027 mi2), and an average elevation of 1225 m (4019 ft) above sea level.

The Huhinuru Holt is home to 343577 people. They break down into the following demographics: 68% Iron Elves, 17% Iron Elves, 8% Grey Elf, 5% Tanuki, and ~2% Other.

The Huhinuru Holt is's peoples are culturally Constructi, but have their local customs and traditions. Their shared values and traditions include honesty and truthfulness in speech and pacifism and peaceful resolution of problems, as well as a unique literary festival and a food festival.

Iron Elf

The Iron Elf subculture native to the Huhinuru Holt are noticably either short and stocky or tall and slender. While they follow the broader tennents of Iron Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of patterned hair shaving or depilation and base their sence of individual idenity on patron-client relationships with major figures. They also place greater cultural emphasus on ethnic purity of blood and culture and leadership and charisma in the group.

Sylvian

The Sylvian subculture native to the Huhinuru Holt are noticably much bigger and bulkier than neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Iron Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of impractical or elaborate role-based clothes and base their sence of individual idenity on professional guild or trade-specific group. They also place greater cultural emphasus on faithfulness towards one’s chosen friends and personal development and limit-pushing.

Grey Elf

The Grey Elf subculture native to the Huhinuru Holt are noticably much bigger and bulkier than neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Iron Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of socially-meaningful animal motif items and base their sence of individual idenity on hereditary loyalty to a political group. They also place greater cultural emphasus on conquest and domination of others and piety and devotion to the gods.

Tanuki

The Tanuki subculture native to the Huhinuru Holt are noticably either short and stocky or tall and slender. While they follow the broader tennents of Iron Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of worn weapons, tools or trade implements and base their sence of individual idenity on far-flung clans of affiliated families. They also place greater cultural emphasus on pacifism and peaceful resolution of problems and ascetic unworldliness and pious poverty.

Local Religion

The Huhinuru Holt is overseen by Potentate Xidona Earthos Kephalos, a god serving within Way of Ashlin, the region's principal religion. Approximately 39% of the population self-report as devout. The Huhinuru Holt is overflowing with shrines and holy sites, with most noteworthy divines having one or more shrines in any location relevant to their portfolio.

Settlements and Economy

Huhinuru Holt has its economic roots in Shattered Peninsula's export of Metals, but has developed its own mercenary industry, which has a marked impact on the local regional economy.

The Huhinuru Holt has an Imperial Development Index of 23. For the reference of Eyom's native peoples, a IDI of 23 means there are 23 locations within the Huhinuru Holt which the Empire can securely and regularly transport materials and personnel..

As a courtesy for scholars and agents, the Huhinuru Holt is rated I1 by Eyom’s banks. Business ventures within the Huhinuru Holt are expected to return above capitol at low risk.
