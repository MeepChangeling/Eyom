The Dèhêl-qëthî Holt (/ˈqëʃɪ/ Dèhêl's [Proper Noun (name of person)] [character; persona]) is a subtropical holt on the coast of the Upper Warding Range region. Dèhêl-qëthî Holt is a relatively typical holt for Upper Warding Range, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Dèhêl-qëthî Holt has a yearly average temperature of 17°C (62°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 9°C (48°F). The Dèhêl-qëthî Holt receives an average of 71 cm/y (27 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. The Dèhêl-qëthî Holt covers an area of nearly 7571 km2 (4705 mi2), and an average elevation of 6514 m (21371 ft) above sea level.
