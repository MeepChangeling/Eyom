The Kàiē̼iē̼ùv Savannah (/cəj̼/ /j̼ʊv/ Orphanage [child (young human being); child (son or daughter of any age)] [maroon]) is a subtropical savanna within the tropical rainforest of the Lin Forest region. The Kàiē̼iē̼ùv Savannah is well known for being largely barren of plant life.

Climate

The Kàiē̼iē̼ùv Savannah has a yearly average temperature of 16°C (60°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a cool 20°C (68°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 13°C (55°F). The Kàiē̼iē̼ùv Savannah receives an average of 311 cm/y (122 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. The Kàiē̼iē̼ùv Savannah covers an area of nearly 8405 km2 (5223 mi2), and an average elevation of 967 m (3172 ft) above sea level.
