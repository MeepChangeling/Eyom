The Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe (/qëˈʃɪfɪ/ Fifîk's Qualia [Proper Noun (name of person)] [characteristic]) is a subtropical steppe within the temperate rainforest of the Substrate region. Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe is a relatively typical steppe for Substrate, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe has a yearly average temperature of 20°C (68°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 28°C (82°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 13°C (55°F). The Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe receives an average of 285 cm/y (112 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. The Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe covers an area of nearly 21060 km2 (13088 mi2), and an average elevation of 3647 m (11965 ft) above sea level.

People and Culture

However, as our culture lacks a taboo for classification of peoples by their biology and such information is highly important for our medical practices amongst other institutions, we have stated the racial demographics of this region. We do not do so to sew the seeds of disunity. We do not do so to belittle or bemoan any group. Such issues are why we use the term 'peoples' rather than races, cultures, or species.

In respect to this universal cultural element, we have split racial classification by culture while keeping the name of each race for our own purposes. Should you see the same race listed more than once in the following section, this is to designate a major cultural division within a race.

The Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe is home to 562863 people. They break down into the following demographics: 51% Constructs, 22% Iron Elves, 12% Kitsune, 9% Kivu, 4% Elf, and ~2% Other.

The Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe is's peoples are culturally Constructi, but have their local customs and traditions. Their shared values and traditions include beauty and seductive charm and humanistic reason and “rational” religion, as well as a unique history festival and a food festival.

Construct

The Construct subculture native to the Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe are noticably smaller and slighter than their neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Construct culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of role or class-specific clothing items and base their sence of individual idenity on far-flung clans of affiliated families. They also place greater cultural emphasus on sharing wealth and goods with others and raw strength and personal prowess.

Iron Elf

The Iron Elf subculture native to the Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe are noticably either short and stocky or tall and slender. While they follow the broader tennents of Construct culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of role or class-specific clothing items and base their sence of individual idenity on educational ties to institutions or traditions. They also place greater cultural emphasus on seeking fortune in new places or new roles and prosperity and accruing material wealth.

Kitsune

The Kitsune subculture native to the Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe are noticably either short and stocky or tall and slender. While they follow the broader tennents of Construct culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of piercings, whether minor or elaborate and base their sence of individual idenity on lineages based on ancient hero-ancestors. They also place greater cultural emphasus on ethnic purity of blood and culture and personal sacrifice for one’s causes or purposes.

Kivu

The Kivu subculture native to the Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe are noticably much bigger and bulkier than neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Construct culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of painted skin markings that sometimes change and base their sence of individual idenity on regional and province-based identity. They also place greater cultural emphasus on exploring the unknown and discovering secrets and membership in an elite bloodline or caste.

Elf

The Elf subculture native to the Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe are noticably smaller and slighter than their neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Construct culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of impractical or elaborate role-based clothes and base their sence of individual idenity on hereditary loyalty to a political group. They also place greater cultural emphasus on filial devotion to family and parents and unity and elimination of group differences.

Local Religion

The Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe is overseen by Potentate Dydohr Eógann Nothann, a god serving within Way of Ashlin, the region's principal religion. Approximately 42% of the population self-report as devout. The Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe is overflowing with shrines and holy sites, with most noteworthy divines having one or more shrines in any location relevant to their portfolio.

Settlements and Economy

Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe is predominantly economicly reiant on Substrate's export of Agriculture, and has no outstanding industries byond what is required for its role in that process.

The Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe has an Imperial Development Index of 10. For the reference of Eyom's native peoples, a IDI of 10 means there are 10 locations within the Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe which the Empire can securely and regularly transport materials and personnel..

As a courtesy for scholars and agents, the Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe is rated M1 by Eyom’s banks. Business ventures within the Fifîk-qëthîfî Steppe are not expected to return value in yearly terms.
