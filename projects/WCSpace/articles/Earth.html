Earth (aka Sol III) is a typical T3-A1-G3-H5-S2 planet located at the southern central edge of Quiet Space. Due to deals with various human governments, we are not able to discuss or disclose much of the interesting elements of Earth in places which are human accessible. We are allowed to mention that Earth is a Class IX Shrine World, with a resident population of extradimentional entities of Class III or higher numbering in the low tens of thousands. They have had a large impact on human life and history.

Earth is interesting to many species for many reasons. Jirrins reaction to Earth's atmosphere makes Earth a longed for tourist destination. The Dellins long for access to Earth cuisine, which is exceptionally varied and robust thanks to Earth's wide abundance of plants. Most of which have been selectively bread for deliciousness by humans.

The Grays and Gid find Earth to be pleasant in terms of climate, many seeking to immigrate if/when humans allow it. Axxani has a special love of human media, and doubtlessly has thousands if not millions of pre-existing online friends she would like to send a drone to visit.

Technical Details

Earth has a rounded shape, through hydrostatic equilibrium, with an average diameter of twelve thousand seven hundred and forty two kilometers (seven thousand nine hundred and eighteen miles)12,742 kilometers (7,918 mi), making it the fifth largest planetary sized and largest terrestrial object of the Sol System. Most of Earth's surface is ocean water: seventy point eight percent or three hundred and sixty one million square kilometers (one hundred and thirty nine million square miles)70.8% or 361 million km2 (139 million sq mi). This vast pool of salty water is often called the world ocean, and makes Earth a water world. In Earth's early history the ocean may have covered Earth completely. The world ocean is commonly divided into the Pacific Ocean, Atlantic Ocean, Indian Ocean, Antarctic or Southern Ocean, and Arctic Ocean, from largest to smallest.

Earth's mass is approximately five point nine seven to the one thousandth and twentyfourth's power kiligrams or five point nine seven Yottagrams5.97×1024 kg (5.970 Yg). Due to gravitational separation, the core is primarily composed of the denser elements: iron (eighty eight point eight percent88.8%), with smaller amounts of nickel, sulfur, and less than one percent1% trace elements. The most common rock constituents of the crust are oxides. Local differences in topography, geology, and deeper tectonic structure cause local and broad regional differences in Earth's gravitational field.

Earth's magnetic field is generated in the core, the site of a dynamo process that converts the kinetic energy of thermally and compositionally driven convection into electrical and magnetic field energy. The field extends outwards from the core, through the mantle, and up to Earth's surface, where it is, approximately, a dipole. The poles of the dipole are located close to Earth's geographic poles. The convection movements in the core are chaotic; the magnetic poles drift and periodically change alignment. This causes secular variation of the main field and field reversals at irregular intervals averaging a few times every million years.
