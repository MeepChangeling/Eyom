The Arcane Sciences: Understanding Magic in the Modern Age

Author: Arcanist Thalindra Vorel, Scholar of the Arcane and Psionic Arts
Abstract

This document explores the nature of magic within our technologically advanced society. Magic, the manipulation of dimensional energy differentials, has become an integral part of our civilization, seamlessly blending with modern technology. This article delves into the origins, mechanics, and societal implications of magic in the contemporary world.

Introduction

Magic, once shrouded in mystery and superstition, is now understood as the scientific exploitation of the “voltage difference” between differing layers of a dimensional set. This advanced understanding has allowed magic to integrate with technology, enhancing both fields in unprecedented ways.

Magic operates on the principle of dimensional energy transfer. Our universe comprises multiple dimensional layers, each with its own unique properties. The crest world entities possess immense power, while the trough world entities exhibit unparalleled finesse in manipulating this power. The thinking mind acts as the conduit for this energy transfer, enabling mages to perform remarkable feats.

Mechanics of Magic

Magic exploits the energy differential between dimensions. The crest world, or Div, holds vast power reserves, while the trough world, or Infirn, excels in fine manipulation of energy. Spells facilitate the transfer of energy from Div to Infirn, resulting in work being done in our world.

Spells often require verbal components, comprising the names and descriptors of crest and trough world entities (These entities are typicaly called "Gods" by primitve species. Indeed, many of them are more than happy to serve the role of a god. Howeaver they are meerly natural lifeforms native to higher dimentions.). The names of eneties witin the Crest are lengthy and complex, necessitating the use of computers to aid in their pronunciation and invocation. The more of a name used, the greater the potential energy transfer. While it is possible for individuals to cast spells without the aid of technology, they are limited to lower power spells, and rarly achieve consistant results.

Prayer Computers

Prayer computers are a technological marvel, capable of performing prayers at scale, though less effective per unit than a mortal's prayer. These devices have revolutionized religious practices, allowing mass prayer and energy manipulation on an unprecedented scale. This is the basis for mass production of magic items, and is often used for large scale constant spell effects, such as the Planetary Disease Shield found on Utri Prime.

Consciousness and the Soul

Consciousness resides within the trough world, encompassing the soul of both crest and trough entities. The soul serves as the focal point for energy transfer, allowing mages to harness and direct magical power.

Enhancing one's soul with magic creates a "dead short" in dimensional flux potential, resulting in catastrophic consequences. This often leads to a mage’s physical and arcane detonation. To prevent such disasters, gods typically ban these practices, enforcing strict regulations on monastic orders to avoid accidental self-enhancement. This is possible as invoking the name of one of these entties meerly calls its attention. They can choose to not grant the use of their power.

The soul can be safely improved through meditation and spiritual practices commonly found in monastic traditions. This method allows for gradual and stable enhancement without risking dimensional short circuits.

Magical Creatures and Phenomena

Magical creatures arise from stable recurring patterns in zones of natural energy transfer. These entities are often unique, though breeding populations can form if enough are created simultaneously. Natural flux points can result in areas being “haunted” or “cursed,” where the dimensional energy transfer manifests unpredictably.

In the case of magical creatures, they existed on a world with natural flux points. Specificly a world with sufishent numbers and frequencie sof activation for natural selection to adapt to magic. Magical species are able to perform certain spells instinctivly (and without the use of verbal componants). The typical example used in accademia are the Draverlra of Tellis III, a species of winged amphibians which are too heavy for their small wings to lift, yet reduce their effetive mass through natural magic in order to fly.

Magical locations are the result of flux points undergoing Solidification, a reare process where the magical flux "crystalizes" into a singular form. The effects of these locations are not nessisarily constant, but are consistant. If a particualr rocky outcropping suddenly gains the ability to trnasform led left on it overnight into gold, it will provide that function indeffently. Assuming ofcourse the process has cyrstalized.

Integration with Technology

Mundane technology can freely interact with magic, but any inclusion of magic within technology provides resistance to further magical influence. This resistance is seen in devices ranging from simple gadgets to complex nuclear weapons. Due to this effect, technology only utilizes magic when nessisary for its intended function. This ensures mages can still enhance technology on the fly, augment its effects, or otherwise make use of it through arcane means. COnversly some millitary hardware will use enough magic in its construction to ensure it cannot be tampered with.

Nuclear Weapons, Divine Will, and Space

Nuclear Weapons are the first piece of arcanely enhanced technology most civilizations are likly to create. The mechanics behind which they work not only require magic to function, but all componants for the spell are also found in mechanical form within their mechanism.

This means a nuclear weapon can only be used if the god of a planet the weapon is to be used on allows it to detonate.

Crazy as that may seem to primtivies, the same is true of any technology used on a planet which makes use of magic. Fortunantly for intersteller civilization, whatever god is incharge of the void between worlds seems not to care about what we do with his power. Or her power. They have never rejected a request asside from premission to detonat enuclear wepons. Yet they have not communicated with anyone in recorded history. They need not even be evoked by name, though their name is known and used for many FTL drive system's functions.

For whatever reason, permissive though they be, they do not allow the use of nuclear weapons in space at all. This is confined to bombs. Focused energy blasts using a nuclear "bomb" as a shell are permitted. Propulshion systems which function by riding the blast wave of a nuclear bomb are permitted. But weapons and even mining charges simply will never detonate in space.

Members of a species known as Humans who have been illegaly removed from their primitive homeworld have sugested using this fact as a method of locating the end of planetary atmospheres. While this would work, it has been rejected.

Societal Impact

The integration of magic and technology has ethical implications. For instance, the historical use of nuclear weapons required divine permission, as seen in the case of Hiroshima and Nagasaki. Such events highlight the complex interplay between human actions, divine will, and magical ethics.

Magic deeply influences cultural and religious practices, with gods playing an active role in regulating and guiding magical use. Monastic orders and magical academies serve as centers for both spiritual and arcane education.

Conclusion

Magic, as the scientific manipulation of dimensional energy differentials, has become a cornerstone of our technologically advanced society. Its integration with technology has opened new frontiers, enhancing our capabilities and understanding of the universe. As we continue to explore and harness the potential of magic, it is crucial to maintain a balance between innovation and ethical responsibility.
