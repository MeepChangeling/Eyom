The Boundless Vale

The Boundless Vale is an expansive region located on the eastern rim of the world between Karvolisa and the Great Green. The Vale contains many diverse landscapes that sprawl seemingly endlessly in every direction. Despite this appearance it is a true vale, as all surrounding lands are notably higher in elevation, resulting in the vale having many rivers and lakes which feed into a central inland sea. The lands arround the sea are an amalgamation of breathtaking scenery: towering mountains covered in serene meadows, dense mystical forests cut by crystal blue rivers dotted with cascading waterfalls, and great shimmering lakes teaming with creatures big and small.

The Vale's defining characteristic is its seamless integration of contrasting environments. The boundaries between its many regions are fluid, creating a harmonious tapestry where each element seamlessly flows into the next.

Elemental forces play a pivotal role in the dynamic equilibrium of the Boundless Vale. The major regons of the Vale are each home to powerful elemental Nexi, which radiate their energy outwards where they clash, mingle, and marry in the places between the great regions. The interplay creates a symphony of magic that resonates throughout the Vale. This magic is wholy unique, and largly not understood, but is known to be capable of many things impossible or greatly difficult for other magical disaplines, while being seemingly incapable of some things that are simple to those same dissaplines.

Major Regions

The western edge of the vale is a desert, known as the Shifting Sands of Eldoria. Named for a long lost kingdom, the dune sea stretches as far as the eye can see, their golden dunes constantly reshaped by capricious winds. Beneath the surface lie hidden cities carved into the living rock, where nomadic tribes harness the magic of the ever-changing sands to survive.

The most prominat feature to the north is a sprawling marshland known as the Quicksilver Fen. Its shimmering waters reflect the silver hues of the moonlight, concealing ancient ruins and spectral creatures that thrive in the marsh's eerie glow. Travelers must navigate the labyrinthine waterways and floating islands to safly cross its haunted grounds.

Towards the east, the Ember Peaks rise like colossal titans, their rocky spires ablaze with perpetual flame. Molten rivers cascade down the mountainsides, creating a hazardous yet mesmerizing terrain. Fire-wielding tribes have made their homes here, forging alliances with fire elementals and phoenixes that soar through the volcanic skies. It is notably home to exactly no dragons, who see the region as "a gross excess of branding for dragon-kind".

In the soulth, the Verdant Dominion sprawls with dense, evergreen forests that seem to pulse with life. Gigantic, sentient trees known as Silver Wardens watch over the land, and hidden groves serve as meeting grounds for ancient druidic councils. Mystical creatures roam freely, and the air is infused with the essence of rejuvenation and growth. It is home to an astonishinly high number of single witches.

The insland sea is home to a cluster of floating islands known as the Prism Isles which hover above the iridescent sea. Each island boasts a unique ecosystem and magical phenomena, from floating gardens that defy gravity to crystalline caverns harboring rare gemstone creatures. Navigating the ethereal skies between the isles requires the use of enchanted airships and the guidance of skilled sky navigators. Or the stones to brave one of the rare several mile long rope bridges linking the major islands.

An infamious region within the Vale is the Rift Plains, a vast grassland where time itself appears to warp and twist. Temporal anomalies create pockets of accelerated growth, ancient ruins from different eras coexist, and rifts occasionally open to parallel dimensions. The Plains are a nexus of temporal energies, attracting scholars and adventurers seeking to unravel the mysteries of time. Travelers should be advised that the Rift Plains exist in time, not space. It is possible to reach the planes and encounter nothing of the anomoly at all. It all depends on when you arive, what direction you came from, and in some cases, what you were doing just prior to arrival. Leaving the Rift Plains once you've found them is just as arcane. Many people become trapped foreaver within its temporal reach.
