Kathryn Nora Profile

  • Affiliation(s): Sciurid Clan, PSCPS, Terran Protectorate, Veiled Eyes

Overview

On the surface Kathryn Nora, better known as Kate, is a fairly typical member of the Sciurid Clan albeit one who prefers to work with the Pre-Starfaring Culture Protection Services rather than directly with her clan. This isn't an odd arrangement, many of the Clan prefer to remain behind after a discovery is made to learn more about that particular "branch of the Great Tree". What is odd about Kate is she is a member of the Veiled Eyes, the PSCPS's "hands on" espionage service.

Few members of the Sciurid Clan are willing to directly interact with an alien culture. The clan's philosophy directs them to purely invisible operations until first contact is made, and then above-board cooperative efforts thereafter. Kate's work involves pretending to be a local in order to learn about the culture, current events, technology, and the general state of affairs directly. This is almost heretical to the Clan's religion.

Kate's personal ethos is simple: She loves getting to interact with other sapients who happen to also be "squirrelly". She's a specialized Infiltrator, working exclusively with arboreal sciurid-oids. Something the Agency has had enough call for to keep her on retainer, and to give her five assignments over her career. Her current assignment is to infiltrate Runi society as her prefered body is indistinguishable from a Runi morph at everything less than a full medical examination.

Kate's current operation is simple: Infiltrate the Runi Powered community of Koten within the Kairn Communion.

To this end she was given augmentations to mimic a semi-common set of Runi super powers, dispatched to Koten, developed a cover identity... And was found out on her first day by Taran Gwynne, a Runi telepath who could read minds... in a way which bypassed the protection implants used by Veiled Eyes Agents. Rather than blow Kate's cover, Taran offered to assist her, claiming (totally erroneously) that humans had helped her people during the Kairn Slave Uprising (super didn't) and she knew anything that Kate was there to do would be for a greater good.

With Taran's help, Kate was able to accomplish the primary goal of her infiltration, the full assertion of the extent to which Powered Runi are utilized by the Karin Communion, how the community of Powered Ruini functions internally, and what it does for a large community like Koten.

With her primary mission officially complete, Kate was supposed to remain planet side and a part of the community for several decades to continue providing information. However, PSCPS wanted to reward Taran for aiding one of their agents. To this end they rigged a radio call in contest with the prize being a ticket for a raffle for residence on Reshi Station—a semi-common thing the Runi knew Terrans did as part of their cultural exchange—so Taran would win. Naturally the raffle itself was also rigged so Taran would win.

Taran refused to collect his prize unless "my best friend Kat can come too". Having no problem with this, PSCPS allowed Kat to move to Reshi, provided she commutes to work and continues her involvement. Kat had no problem with this at all, as she was happy that her newest best frined is not only happy, but safe, and near the medical care a terminally ill man needs (The poor otter is suffering from senescence.)

Hearing that Kate would be able to live in a normal home rather than being gone for 20-30 years as expected, her immdaiate family—her sister Liz and wife Thackie—moved in with her on the station. At present, Kate finds herself settled into life on Reshi Station, she found herself in a unique position: a bridge between cultures, navigating the fine line between her duties as a Veiled Eyes operative and her growing personal connections with Taran and the Runi community.

With the PSCPS higher ups hearing of the situation, Kate's work shifted focus. No longer tasked with direct infiltration, she became a cultural liaison between the PSCPS and the Runi residents of Reshi, using her knowledge of Runi society to build bridges and foster understanding. After all having a recognized superhero vouching for them is excellent PR.
