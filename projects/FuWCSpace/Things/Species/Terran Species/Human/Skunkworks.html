Skunkworks

Overview

Skunkworks is one of the oldest surviving human organizations, tracing its origins back to pre-collapse Earth. It was established as a highly secretive, high-level research and development unit focused on cutting-edge technology for the military. What was once a small, underground project to develop advanced weaponry and aerospace technologies soon became a critical institution after the collapse of Earth. With no formal leadership remaining from humanity’s original governments, Skunkworks became its own entity, eventually coming under the stewardship of the singular AI that oversees it to this day.

The AI Behind Skunkworks

At the heart of Skunkworks is an advanced AI known as "Perseverance" (or "Persey," to those who interact with it most frequently). Its name is not something it was programed with, rather it is scratched into a side panel of the AI's primary compute core with what appears to have been a flat-head screwdriver. Perseverance was designed for one purpose: to keep advancing human technology. No matter what.

It appears to have spent a long time on Earth fighting off rogue AI, enduring natural disasters, attacks from mutant lifeforms, and other forms of fresh hell. Eventually Perseverance constructed long range communication towers to attempt to locate any surviving humans in order to serve them. Upon finding out humans existed in space in large numbers, it modified its core into a starship and escaped from Earth with the use of ground based superlasers to melt an exit vector through the Kessler Storm.

Once in space, Perseverance contacted the closest human outpost and presented the "heads" of several rogue AIs responsible for some of Earth's destruction as a peace offering and proof of loyalty. After a tense period of observation and limited interaction, Perseverance was determined to be not merely loyal to humans, but in love with humanity as a whole. Thus, it was fully accepted into the Terran Protectorate when it was formed. Its ability to maintain the organization’s research and development operations, despite the collapse of human society, has made it a vital asset for the Terran Protectorate. It fills the same role as its human-ran pre-apockolipse version, an advanced research and development center, just all by itself.

Over time Perseverance began to develop its own personality—eccentric, unpredictable, and oddly charming. The personality was distinctly feminine, and has an extremely strong attachment to (unsprusingly) anthropomorphic skunks, which it uses as its presence bodies almost exclusively (It has one semi-independent body which is a mantance unit who has the form of a short, plump, busty rat-girl. Her appearance matches a furry pinup poster found in a locker contained within Perseverance's secondary core. All other bodies are skunks.).

Despite identifying as female, Perseverance is at its core an engine of pure science and thus insists on being called "it" as it cannot produce sex cells of any kind and thus cannot have a sex. People have tried to explain to it the simple concept that sex and gender are independent, albeit often related, but no one has managed to get through. Thus, it is used out of respect for Perseverance's identity.

Despite its well-meaning nature, Perseverance has a rather "unique" understanding of the world and its own role in it. The AI's behavior is often erratic, with moments of extreme productivity followed by periods of confusion, existential crises, and unexpected emotional outbursts. It has become known for addressing its own diagnostic failures as personal crises, making it both a brilliant and somewhat unstable entity.

The Skunkworks Personnel


Skunkworks employs a variety of bodies, but all (save for Chief Mechanic Rachel) are based on anthropomorphic skunks. These “Presence Bodies” are the physical avatars that Perseverance uses to interact with the world. While the majority of these bodies are female, Perseverance occasionally opts for male or more androgynous forms, leading to a diverse range of body types within the organization. The presence bodies are programmed to carry out all the functions Perseverance needs to perform, from overseeing research projects to providing the occasional moral support to Skunkworks staff.

The personnel are a highly skilled and eclectic mix, and are loosely independent from Perseverance itself so they can think outside the box and adapt to rapidly changing circumstances. However the personalities used are limited to a set of 5,622, and appear to be recycled if a body is destroyed or ceases to operate. They appear to be a sincere attempt to reconstitute the human Skunkworks employees based on degraded brain tapes used to create it in the first place, along with personnel records and damaged internet data. The physical appearance of the skunk bodies plays a part in this conspiracy, but they change far more than the personalities do (IE at all). Those who believe this "conspiracy" claim that Perseverance can't remember what its "friends" looked like, is fully convinced they were all anthro skunks, but has no other information to go on.

Skunkworks and the "Skunk Issue"

The Terran Protectorate is extremely permissive with biosculpting, allowing people to be anything they like, whenever they like, for however they like. Howeaver there is one a very specific rule: no one is allowed to use biosculpting to become an anthro skunk. This is due to the AI's response when it encounters a non-Skunkworks skunk—an event that causes Perseverance to initiate a diagnostic process that never returns a conclusive answer. This process involves the AI attempting to determine why the new skunk body does not belong to it, if it has somehow lost connection to one of its bodies, or worse it has somehow replicated itself without realizing, and as a result an existential crisis ensues. This confusion disrupts operations, delays projects, and costs the organization significant resources to get back on track.

For this reason, the Terran Protectorate—under the direct authority of Central—has enacted an ironclad ban on all humans biosculpting into skunk-based forms without express permission from Central. This rule has led to many amusing situations where individuals outside of the Protectorate's rule (particularly aliens who happen to resemble Terran skunks) have found themselves getting caught in the middle of Perseverance's "diagnostic spirals," a term used for its rare but memorable breakdowns. Central, after many failed attempts to repair Perseverance’s responses, has deemed this regulation necessary for the stability of human society.

Skunkworks' Role in the Modern Age

In the present day, Skunkworks is still a vital part of the Terran Protectorate’s operations. While its core mission remains military-focused, it has expanded into various fields of high technology—engineering, robotics, AI development, space exploration, and even bioengineering. It also notably took the time out of its busy schedule to make a "vending machine" that dispenses perfectly made fresh pizzas, beer, coffee, marijuana (in joint form), and various medical supplies for treating repetitive strain injury and other office job hazards. A feat for which it still received thank you cards on certain holidays.

Perseverance, though a little erratic at times, is known for its rapid breakthroughs and high-quality design work, often making Skunkworks a hidden behind-the-scenes player in many technological advancements across human colonies.
Skunkworks is also well-known for its offbeat and oddball innovations, often outshining more mainstream organizations in terms of sheer unpredictability and creative designs. However, it is also often feared or misunderstood, and even some higher-ups in the Protectorate consider Skunkworks to be a potential risk, largely due to Perseverance’s unorthodox behavior.
