Species Profile

Basic Information

  • Name: Sepinte
  • Scientific Classification/Taxonomy: (if applicable)
  • Common Names: Snekies (tolerated "cute" nickname), Swamp Squid (derogatory)
  • Origin/Primary Habitat: Velio III
  • Population Distribution: Any hot and wet biome on any world as long as it's sun isn't yellow.

Sepiente are an amphibious species of xenoforms who are quite alien in appearance, yet bear some resemblance to earth-life. They are often described as a cross between a snake, a manta ray, and a squid. A betentacled mana tray with the body of a snake is a fairly apt descriptor, though their tentacles are in fact pseudopods (they lack suckers entirely) and are attached to their body in similar locations to the legs of insects. More scientifically minded people compare them to snakes, horseshoe crabs, and tardigrades.

Sepiente can breathe air or fresh water just as readily as the other. They are also capable of burrowing through mud, wet sand, and other similar loose high-moisture environments. They navigate primarily through an electro-static sense, but can see almost as well as baseline humans, though their color perception is skewed towards red and extends into the infrared range (they cannot see purples, and can only see the most green of blues).

They are obligate carnivores, and predatory. Despite this, they can detect sapient creatures through their electrostatic sense. This evolved so they could identify packmates while hunting within the mud and sand of their marshy homeworld. As such they refuse to eat anything with a sapient mind on an instinctual level (Detection is as accurate as modern sapience assessments, and appears to be based on the density, frequency, and complexity of a neural network).

The Sepiente have an interstellar empire that is technologically a near-peer of the Terran Protectorate and the two nations had a brief but bloody war in the year 3478 CE which caused a good deal of tension between the two peoples. However, common ground was found in culinary traditions, a love of sport, and both species incredibly expansive tastes in erotic art.
