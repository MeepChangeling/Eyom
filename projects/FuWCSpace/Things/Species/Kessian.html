Kessian Profile

Basic Information

The Kessians are a draco-serpentine species originating from the lush, tropical planet of Kess. Known for their distinctive physiology, compelling cultural dynamics, and unique reproductive biology, the Kessians have carved out a significant presence in the cosmos as a starfaring species. Despite their independence from the Terran Protectorate, they wield influence through trade, diplomacy, and their potent mix of allure and danger.

Physical Characteristics

From the waist up, Kessians resemble draconic humanoids, with sleek scales in a wide array of iridescent hues. Their faces are sharp and angular, often adorned with small horns or ridges that grow more elaborate with age. Their eyes are reptilian, with slit pupils and a range of vivid colors that seem to glow faintly in the dark. From the waist down, they possess a powerful serpentine tail, typically ranging between 10-15 meters in length. This tail is muscular yet elegant, enabling them to move with both grace and speed.

On average, Kessians stand 2-3 meters tall from head to waist, with their full length (including the tail) reaching up to 18 meters. Their scaled bodies are resistant to physical trauma and environmental extremes. Many Kessians have bioluminescent patterns along their tails, used for communication and displays of emotion. These patterns are not natural, but rather a near-uniquitious cybernetic modification rooted in a thousand years of culture and tradition.

Homeworld: Kess

Kess is a world of towering rainforests, vast rivers, and humid climates. The planet's biosphere teems with vibrant, predatory ecosystems, of which the Kessians' emerged as their planet's apex predators. Unlike humans this was not achieved theough cunning intelect nor crushing wisdom, but by sheer physical domination of every other lifeform. This shaped the Kessians into formidable survivors and explorers.

Kess’s cities blend organic and technological elements seamlessly. Their architecture often incorporates living plants, bioluminescent lighting, and structures that mimic the flow of nature. This is not out of a love of nature as humans know it, but rather a means of enticing pray into their cities for the purpose of occasional snacks. Despite this (or perhapse because of it), Kessians do hold their world’s wild beauty in high regard and strive to preserve its ecosystems. Especially those which support animals deemed "tasty".

Society and Culture

Kessian culture is deeply sensual, emphasizing physicality and personal expression. Their society is built on the interplay of power, attraction, and negotiation. Rituals of courtship, alliance-building, and even conflict resolution often carry an undercurrent of sensuality. The Kessian Starleague governs their species. This meritocratic body values cunning, charisma, and resourcefulness, with leaders rising based on their ability to outmaneuver rivals in political, intellectual, and even physical contests.

Kessians are renowned for their artistry, creating mesmerizing dances and intricate body adornments that emphasize their serpentine elegance. Their music often incorporates rhythmic vibrations that resonate with their tails' bioluminescent pulses. It is also usualy turned down at interspecies parties (unless humans are in attendence, who also enjoy thier stylings).

While Kessians maintain cordial relations with many species, their reproductive biology complicates interspecies interactions, often leading to mistrust or fascination. Despite this they are skilled diplomats, able to navigate these challenges with their natural charisma. This has allowed them to maintain independence, thrive, and manage to occasionally be a militarized thorn in the Protectorate's side, though the Starleague and Protectorate always make up in the end.

Reproductive Biology

Kessians are a monogender species, but only a small percentage of them ever develop a functioning reproductive system. This percentage are called queens (in the same sence as insects) and are tasked by their cultures to reproduce as often as nature allows them to. Their offspring are claimed by any Kessian who wishes to raise a child, ensuring their breeding population's life purpose is providing a study flow of fertile eggs for their species.

The most controversial and fascinating aspect of Kessian biology is their parasitoid reproduction. Kessians reproduce by implanting their eggs into the bodies of host species, a process that has shaped their culture and interactions with others. Kessian queens self-fertilise on a biological timer, and carry their eggs within them for a short piriod of development, after which they must implant their eggs into a host through their ovipositor. If they fail to do this, their eggs will enlarge to a painful size and be passed in a distressing and often bloody "miscarriage" of sorts.

A queen will implant several hundred eggs in one breeding session, and typicaly becomes quite protective of their chosen host for the six month incubation.

Kessian eggs attach to the walls of a host's digestive tract and leech nutrients and biomass from the host to develop and grow. Additionally they take some DNA from the host, which provides genetic diversity for the species as well as environmental adaptations like immune cell responses.

Fortunately for the host, the incubation process is symbiotic rather than fatal. Provided the host possesses sufficient "elasticity", that is. Hosts are often immobilized for the last month or so of incubation due to the expansion of the eggs, and laying usually takes hours. This process can be quite pleasueable to some individuals, though is as distressing as a normal pregnancy to most.

Kessians are aware of the ethical implications of their reproduction for other sapient species, and adhere to strict protocols. Implantation is consensual, often formalized through legal or ritualistic agreements. Or by encountering a horny human at a bar who knows what they are about and can recognize a queen by sight or smell.

Technological and Starfaring Achievements

Kessians excel in biotech, using their expertise to create medical advancements, environmental restoration tools, and enhancements for other species. Most impressively, Kessians are unique for their use of organic starships. Their vessels are masterpieces of bioengineering, blending organic and synthetic materials. Their ships resemble massive serpentine creatures, with living components that repair and adapt to damage.

Kessians refer to their ships with the pronoun "he" when speaking languages that include pronouns, citing the integrated intelligences are closer to male than anything else. Their ships are capable of acting autonomously, but restricted to following their captain's orders in such instances. Of course their interstation of orders can be a little losers than many first-time captains understand, leading to many incidents.

While not inherently warlike, the Kessians maintain a powerful defensive fleet. Their tactics emphasize misdirection, utilizing their ships’ agility and cloaking technologies. Size permitting, Kessian ships will engage in "hull to hull combat", grappling enemy vessels to constrict them like a python crushing its prey to death. Their vessels even consume parts of defeated enemy ships to gain scrap for repairs and munition fabrication.

Notable Kessian Vessel - KNF Sun Eater

The Kessians have a 1300 year old legend relating to one of their ships, a Knight-Class cruiser from the 3rd fleet named Sun Eater (a typical name for a Kessian warship). The Sun Eater was a storied warship, having survived many battles, including the infamous Siege of Velos wherein the Sun Eater destroyed a Fiarite Demon-Class Dreadnaught in one-on-one combat. Fighting well above its weight class was something the ship's crew wore with pride.

According to the legend, while enroute to the Battle of Teli Prime Sun Eater's FTL drive malfunctioned, trapping it within hyperspace. While the crew was able to return to real space after several months the rift they used to escape was too small to permit the Sun Eater to return to normal space. Before abandoning ship the captain ordered Sun Eater to find a way home if possible.

The returned crew reported suffering attacks from the various higher dimensional entities resisting within Hyperspace, as is typical for any real space vessel trapped within hyperspace for long. These creatures are kept at bay with many systems, but the Kessians make use of wards, preferring an arcane solution for "operational time" purposes. When asking if the Sun Eater would be able to return through a similar rift, the Captain reported their escape involved a dangerous alteration to the ward via directly editing its runes. There simply was not a way for anything larger than the drop ship to escape and the rift was a one off. The Sun Eater was officially listed as lost in space.

Twenty years later a subspace listening post picked up a transmission from the Sun Eater. It carried all encryption keys necessary to authenticate the message when checked against the codes in-use when the Sun Eater went missing. The message red as follows:

Proximity to Kess: 9.28 AU
Hostile hyperdimensional lifeforms detected on all vectors.
Threat level: Crimson
Invoking Standing Order 01 - Defend Homeworld, no matter the cost.
Commencing attack run.

Shortly after receiving the message a series of hyperspace fulgurations resulted in an ion storm within Kess's star system. The storm lasted for several days, unusually possessing four separate "primary" nodes (the point at which an ion storm originates). After the storm cleared a second transmission was detected.

Proximity to Kess: 0.17 AU
Hostile hyperdimensional lifeforms exterminated.
Threat level: White
Intelligence Report: Hostile hyperdimensional lifeforms origin identified.
Invoking Standing Order 01 - Defend Homeworld, no matter the cost.
Commencing attack run...

According to the Legend of the Sun Eater, he persists within hyeprspace to this day, exterminating hyperspace predators to keep other crews from the same fate as his own.
