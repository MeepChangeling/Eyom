The Mækyitjĭe-kěl Holt (/mæˈkjitʤe/ /kɛl/ Commoners [filter] [dialect]) is a subtropical holt within the temperate deciduous forest of the Hearthcoast region. Mækyitjĭe-kěl Holt is a relatively typical holt for Hearthcoast, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Mækyitjĭe-kěl Holt has a yearly average temperature of 29°C (84°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 32°C (89°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 26°C (78°F). The Mækyitjĭe-kěl Holt receives an average of 138 cm/y (54 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. The Mækyitjĭe-kěl Holt covers an area of nearly 20776 km2 (12912 mi2), and an average elevation of 4148 m (13608 ft) above sea level.

The Mækyitjĭe-kěl Holt is's peoples are culturally Tauric, but have their local customs and traditions. Their shared values and traditions include piety and devotion to the gods and harmony with nature and existing life, as well as a unique autumn festival and a wine festival.

They also place greater cultural emphasus on courage and valiance in danger and social progress toward some eventual utopia.
