The Fäx-glīe Brushlands (/fɑx/ /gliˈʤe/ Blindchalk [blind] [chalk]) are a group of subtropical brushland on the coast of the Cutmountains region. The Fäx-glīe Brushlands are well known for its thriving abundance of plant life.

Climate

The Fäx-glīe Brushlands has a yearly average temperature of 28°C (82°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 32°C (89°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 24°C (75°F). The Fäx-glīe Brushlands receives an average of 217 cm/y (85 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. The Fäx-glīe Brushlands covers an area of nearly 23874 km2 (14837 mi2), and an average elevation of 4416 m (14488 ft) above sea level.
