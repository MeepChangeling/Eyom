The Mër Gō̄y Basin (/ɱɜ̄ː/ /gʊ̄iː/ Redharbor [maroon] [harbor; port]) is a subtropical basin on the coast of the Endless Peaks region. Mër Gō̄y Basin is a relatively typical basin for Endless Peaks, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Mër Gō̄y Basin has a yearly average temperature of 30°C (86°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a blistering 34°C (93°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 26°C (78°F). The Mër Gō̄y Basin receives an average of 66 cm/y (25 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. The Mër Gō̄y Basin covers an area of nearly 11308 km2 (7027 mi2), and an average elevation of 13108 m (55 ft) above sea level.
