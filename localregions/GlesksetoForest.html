The Gleskseto Forest (/ˈseto/ Gleskspain [Proper Noun (name of person)] [agony]) is a subtropical forest on the coast of the Illa's Panhandle region. The Gleskseto Forest is well known for being largely barren of plant life.

Climate

The Gleskseto Forest has a yearly average temperature of 22°C (71°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 20°C (68°F). The Gleskseto Forest receives an average of 132 cm/y (51 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. The Gleskseto Forest covers an area of nearly 11698 km2 (7270 mi2), and an average elevation of 6724 m (-20889 ft) above sea level.

Local Religion

The Gleskseto Forest is overseen by Potentate Alotl Siononiu Korodite, a god serving within The Apprenticeship, the region's principal religion. Approximately 43% of the population self-report as devout. The Gleskseto Forest is overflowing with shrines and holy sites, with most noteworthy divines having one or more shrines in any location relevant to their portfolio.

Settlements and Economy

Gleskseto Forest has its economic roots in Illa's Panhandle's export of Rubber, but has developed its own art industry, which has a marked impact on the local regional economy.
