The Ilbukugùs Fields (/ilˈbukug/ /ʊs/ Rootlarder [opening] [root]) are a group of subtropical fields within the temperate rainforest of the Salt Warrens region. The Ilbukugùs Fields are the cultural homeland of the Rattu.

Climate

The Ilbukugùs Fields has a yearly average temperature of 17°C (62°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 23°C (73°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 12°C (53°F). The Ilbukugùs Fields receives an average of 306 cm/y (120 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. The Ilbukugùs Fields covers an area of nearly 6931 km2 (4307 mi2), and an average elevation of 1461 m (4793 ft) above sea level.
