The Littlebloom Holt (littlebloom /hoʊlt/ Littlebloom [Translation Unavailable]) is a temperate holt within the wetland of the Northlake region. Littlebloom Holt is a relatively typical holt for Northlake, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Littlebloom Holt has a yearly average temperature of 28°C (82°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 33°C (91°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 23°C (73°F). The Littlebloom Holt receives an average of 234 cm/y (92 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. The Littlebloom Holt covers an area of nearly 12667 km2 (7872 mi2), and an average elevation of 1019 m (3343 ft) above sea level.

The Littlebloom Holt is's peoples are culturally Tauric, but have their local customs and traditions. Their shared values and traditions include building things in service of their posterity and sharing wealth and goods with others, as well as a unique spring festival and a rain festival.
