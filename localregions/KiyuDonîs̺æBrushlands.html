The Kiyu Donîs̺æ Brushlands (/ˈkiju/ /doˈnɪːs̺æ/ Neoptism [loyal] [nephew]) are a group of subtropical brushland on the coast of the Squeeji Basin region. Kiyu Donîs̺æ Brushlands are a relatively typical brushland for Squeeji Basin, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Kiyu Donîs̺æ Brushlands has a yearly average temperature of 19°C (66°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 11°C (51°F). The Kiyu Donîs̺æ Brushlands receives an average of 72 cm/y (28 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. The Kiyu Donîs̺æ Brushlands covers an area of nearly 9714 km2 (6037 mi2), and an average elevation of 5239 m (17188 ft) above sea level.

Their shared values and traditions include sharing wealth and goods with others and loyalty to one’s friends, family, and own, as well as a unique comedy festival and a spring festival.

Local Religion

The Kiyu Donîs̺æ Brushlands are overseen by Potentate Elx Walateandra Aphrononwy, a god serving within Way of Ashlin, the region's principal religion. Approximately 56% of the population self-report as devout. The Kiyu Donîs̺æ Brushlands is overflowing with shrines and holy sites, with most noteworthy divines having one or more shrines in any location relevant to their portfolio.

Kiyu Donîs̺æ Brushlands has its economic roots in Squeeji Basin's export of Gold, but has developed its own education industry, which has a marked impact on the local regional economy.
