The Strö Vremōf Forest (/stro˞/ /ˈvremoːf/ Hideaways [private] [recognition]) is a subtropical forest on the coast of the Islestand Coast region. The Strö Vremōf Forest is well known for being largely barren of plant life.

Climate

The Strö Vremōf Forest has a yearly average temperature of 8°C (46°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing -9°C (16°F). The Strö Vremōf Forest receives an average of 292 cm/y (114 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. The Strö Vremōf Forest covers an area of nearly 15653 km2 (9728 mi2), and an average elevation of 5363 m (17595 ft) above sea level.
