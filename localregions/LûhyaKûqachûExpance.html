The Lûhya Kûqachû Expance (/kʊˈqaʧʊ/ Lûhya's Behavior [Proper Noun (name of person)] [behavior]) is a subtropical expanse on the coast of the Weardvale region. Lûhya Kûqachû Expance is a relativly typical expanse for Weardvale, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Lûhya Kûqachû Expance has a yearly average tempature of 16°C (60°F), with its average tempature durring the summer being a pleasent 24°C (75°F) and its average tempature durring the winter being a cold 7°C (44°F). The Lûhya Kûqachû Expance recieves an average of 304 cm/y (119 in/y) of percipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain durring the summer. The Lûhya Kûqachû Expance covers an area of nearly 16453 km2 (10225 mi2), and an average elevation of 5937 m (19478 ft) above sealevel.

The Lûhya Kûqachû Expance is's peoples are culturaly Wareneese, but have their own local customs and traditions. Their shared vaules and traditions include personal indulgence and luxuriant pleasure and subtlety and indirectness of action, as well as a unique rain festival and a history festival.

Wareneese

The Wareneese subculture native to the Lûhya Kûqachû Expance are noticably smaller and slighter than their neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Wareneese culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of piercings, whether minor or elaborate and base their sence of individual idenity on dynastic lineages with cadet branches. They also place greater cultural emphasus on social progress toward some eventual utopia and prosperity and accruing material wealth.

Hobgoblin

The Hobgoblin subculture native to the Lûhya Kûqachû Expance are noticably same height and weight as the neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Wareneese culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of significant scent or perfume uses and base their sence of individual idenity on inheritance of an ancient body modification. They also place greater cultural emphasus on vengeance and execution of just vendettas and seeking fortune in new places or new roles.

Kivu

The Kivu subculture native to the Lûhya Kûqachû Expance are noticably much bigger and bulkier than neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Wareneese culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of worn weapons, tools or trade implements and base their sence of individual idenity on lineages based on ancient hero-ancestors. They also place greater cultural emphasus on honor and maintaining one’s integrity and submission to the collective will or culture.

Elf

The Elf subculture native to the Lûhya Kûqachû Expance are noticably much bigger and bulkier than neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Wareneese culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of impractical or elaborate role-based clothes and base their sence of individual idenity on regional and province-based identity. They also place greater cultural emphasus on industry and the ability to work tirelessly and education and knowledge-seeking.

Local Religion

The Lûhya Kûqachû Expance is overseen by Potentate Drutyja Sarthos Ponis, a god serving within The Apprenticeship, the region's pricipal religion. Aproximently 38% of the population self-report as devout. The Lûhya Kûqachû Expance is overflowing with shrines and holy sites, with most noteworthy divines having one or more shrines in any location relevent to their portfolio.

Settlments and Economy

Lûhya Kûqachû Expance has its economic roots in Weardvale's export of Corn, but has developed its own divine legal industry, which has a marked impact on the local regional economy.
