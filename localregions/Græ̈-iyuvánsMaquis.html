The Græ̈-iyuváns Maquis (/græ̝/ /iˈjuvə˞ŋs/ Castle [normal; regular] [defense]) is a subtropical maquis within the temperate rainforest of the Cedarcoast region. Græ̈-iyuváns Maquis is a relatively typical maquis for Cedarcoast, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Græ̈-iyuváns Maquis has a yearly average temperature of 27°C (80°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 31°C (87°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 24°C (75°F). The Græ̈-iyuváns Maquis receives an average of 272 cm/y (107 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. The Græ̈-iyuváns Maquis covers an area of nearly 27765 km2 (17256 mi2), and an average elevation of 4212 m (13818 ft) above sea level.

People and Culture

However, as our culture lacks a taboo for classification of peoples by their biology and such information is highly important for our medical practices amongst other institutions, we have stated the racial demographics of this region. We do not do so to sew the seeds of disunity. We do not do so to belittle or bemoan any group. Such issues are why we use the term 'peoples' rather than races, cultures, or species.

In respect to this universal cultural element, we have split racial classification by culture while keeping the name of each race for our own purposes. Should you see the same race listed more than once in the following section, this is to designate a major cultural division within a race.

The Græ̈-iyuváns Maquis is home to 939990 people. They break down into the following demographics: 56% Tauric, 18% Hobgoblins, 11% Taurans, 8% Kobold, 5% Kivu, and ~2% Other.

The Græ̈-iyuváns Maquis is's peoples are culturally Tauric, but have their local customs and traditions. Their shared values and traditions include ascetic unworldliness and pious poverty and humanistic reason and “rational” religion, as well as a unique winter festival and a beer festival.

Tauric

While they follow the broader tennents of Tauric culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of tattoos of some cultural significance and base their sence of individual idenity on ethnic membership. They also place greater cultural emphasus on purging evil and expelling the wicked and ascetic unworldliness and pious poverty.

They also place greater cultural emphasus on purging evil and expelling the wicked and loyalty to one’s friends, family, and own.

Kivu

The Kivu subculture native to the Græ̈-iyuváns Maquis are noticably smaller and slighter than their neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Tauric culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of socially-meaningful animal motif items and base their sence of individual idenity on ideological groups or philosophical sects. They also place greater cultural emphasus on submission to lawful authority and unity and elimination of group differences.

Local Religion

The Græ̈-iyuváns Maquis is overseen by Potentate Uarus Priapetor Corawl, a god serving within Covenant of Irus, the region's principal religion. Approximately 15% of the population self-report as devout. The Græ̈-iyuváns Maquis is overflowing with shrines and holy sites, with most noteworthy divines having one or more shrines in any location relevant to their portfolio.

Settlements and Economy

Græ̈-iyuváns Maquis has its economic roots in Cedarcoast's export of Timber, but has developed its own engines industry, which has a marked impact on the local regional economy.
