The Sèkê-bêg̈i Woods (/ˈsèkɘ/ /ˈbɘg̃i/ Outclause [keen] [excuse]) are a group of subtropical woods on the coast of the Upper Warding Range region. Sèkê-bêg̈i Woods are a relatively typical woods for Upper Warding Range, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Sèkê-bêg̈i Woods has a yearly average temperature of 19°C (66°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 14°C (57°F). The Sèkê-bêg̈i Woods receives an average of 71 cm/y (27 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. The Sèkê-bêg̈i Woods covers an area of nearly 4243 km2 (2637 mi2), and an average elevation of 6980 m (-20049 ft) above sea level.
