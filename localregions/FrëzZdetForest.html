The Frëz Zdet Forest (/frɛz/ /zdet/ Deaf Mule [deaf] [mule]) is a subtropical forest within the temperate rainforest of the Riverpack Coast region. Frëz Zdet Forest is a relatively typical forest for Riverpack Coast, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Frëz Zdet Forest has a yearly average temperature of 15°C (59°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 6°C (42°F). The Frëz Zdet Forest receives an average of 297 cm/y (116 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. The Frëz Zdet Forest covers an area of nearly 12372 km2 (7689 mi2), and an average elevation of 1458 m (4783 ft) above sea level.

The Frëz Zdet Forest is home to 108051 people. They break down into the following demographics: 56% Gnolls, 13% Gnolls, 16% Gnolls, 7% Tanuki, 5% Imperial, 1% Kobold, and ~2% Other.

The Frëz Zdet Forest is's peoples are culturally Gnollish, but have their local customs and traditions. Their shared values and traditions include beauty and seductive charm and cunning and the ability to trick others, as well as a unique fire festival and a theater festival.

Local Religion

The Frëz Zdet Forest is overseen by Potentate Chizdos Æðeles Arandra, a god serving within Way of Ashlin, the region's principal religion. Approximately 34% of the population self-report as devout. The Frëz Zdet Forest is considered underserved by the divines despite shrines in all settled areas.

Settlements and Economy

Frëz Zdet Forest is predominantly economicly reiant on Riverpack Coast's export of Steel, and has no outstanding industries byond what is required for its role in that process.

The Frëz Zdet Forest has an Imperial Development Index of 10. For the reference of Eyom's native peoples, a IDI of 10 means there are 10 locations within the Frëz Zdet Forest which the Empire can securely and regularly transport materials and personnel..

As a courtesy for scholars and agents, the Frëz Zdet Forest is rated M1 by Eyom’s banks. Business ventures within the Frëz Zdet Forest are not expected to return value in yearly terms.
