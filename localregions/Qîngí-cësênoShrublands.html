The Qîngí-cësêno Shrublands (/cëˈsɘno/ Qîngí the Minister's [Proper Noun (name of person)] [politician]) are a group of subtropical shrubland on the coast of the Thunderfields region. Qîngí-cësêno Shrublands are a relatively typical shrubland for Thunderfields, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Qîngí-cësêno Shrublands has a yearly average temperature of 20°C (68°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 28°C (82°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 13°C (55°F). The Qîngí-cësêno Shrublands receives an average of 301 cm/y (118 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. The Qîngí-cësêno Shrublands covers an area of nearly 17525 km2 (10891 mi2), and an average elevation of 5319 m (17450 ft) above sea level.
