The Southweed Woodlands (southweed /ˈwʊˌdlændz/ Southweed [Translation Unavailable]) are a group of subtropical woodland on the coast of the Fractal Range region. The Southweed Woodlands are well known for its thriving abundance of plant life.

Climate

The Southweed Woodlands has a yearly average temperature of 33°C (91°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a blistering 38°C (100°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 29°C (84°F). The Southweed Woodlands receives an average of 68 cm/y (26 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. The Southweed Woodlands covers an area of nearly 8488 km2 (5275 mi2), and an average elevation of 13165 m (242 ft) above sea level.
