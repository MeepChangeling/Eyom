The Nelä Rewf Woodlands (/neˈlə/ /ruːf/ Shipwright's [fine (okay)] [boat; ship; canoe]) are a group of subtropical woodland on the coast of the Hammer Hills region. Nelä Rewf Woodlands are a relatively typical woodland for Hammer Hills, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Nelä Rewf Woodlands has a yearly average temperature of 28°C (82°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 32°C (89°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 25°C (77°F). The Nelä Rewf Woodlands receives an average of 71 cm/y (27 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. The Nelä Rewf Woodlands covers an area of nearly 10813 km2 (6720 mi2), and an average elevation of 10617 m (-8116 ft) above sea level.

People and Culture

However, as our culture lacks a taboo for classification of peoples by their biology and such information is highly important for our medical practices amongst other institutions, we have stated the racial demographics of this region. In respect to this universal cultural element, we have split racial classification by culture while keeping the name of each race for our own purposes. Should you see the same race listed more than once in the following section, this is to designate a major cultural division within a race.

The Nelä Rewf Woodlands are home to 102903 people. They break down into the following demographics: 64% Iron Elves, 13% Iron Elves, 8% Dwarves, 8% Grey Elf, 5% Elf, and ~2% Other.

The Nelä Rewf Woodlands are's peoples are culturally Wood Elven, but have their local customs and traditions. Their shared values and traditions include cunning and the ability to trick others and submission to the collective will or culture, as well as a unique spring festival and a rain festival.

Iron Elf

The Iron Elf subculture native to the Nelä Rewf Woodlands are noticably much bigger and bulkier than neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Iron Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of color choices with social meaning to them and base their sence of individual idenity on regional and province-based identity. They also place greater cultural emphasus on excellence in one’s profession or trade and exploring the unknown and discovering secrets.

Sylvian

The Sylvian subculture native to the Nelä Rewf Woodlands are noticably either short and stocky or tall and slender. While they follow the broader tennents of Iron Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of piercings, whether minor or elaborate and base their sence of individual idenity on regional and province-based identity. They also place greater cultural emphasus on courage and valiance in danger and vengeance and execution of just vendettas.

Dwarf

The Dwarf subculture native to the Nelä Rewf Woodlands are noticably much bigger and bulkier than neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Iron Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of painted skin markings that sometimes change and base their sence of individual idenity on educational ties to institutions or traditions. They also place greater cultural emphasus on justice and fairness between people and humanistic reason and “rational” religion.

Grey Elf

The Grey Elf subculture native to the Nelä Rewf Woodlands are noticably same height and weight as the neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Iron Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of culturally-significant jewelry or accessories and base their sence of individual idenity on lineages based on ancient hero-ancestors. They also place greater cultural emphasus on personal development and limit-pushing and honesty and truthfulness in speech.

Elf

The Elf subculture native to the Nelä Rewf Woodlands are noticably smaller and slighter than their neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Iron Elf culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of tattoos of some cultural significance and base their sence of individual idenity on ideological groups or philosophical sects. They also place greater cultural emphasus on ascetic unworldliness and pious poverty and sexual license and wantonness.

Local Religion

The Nelä Rewf Woodlands are overseen by Potentate Bilna Mapoibniu Feiderseus, a god serving within Pagan, the region's principal religion. Approximately 56% of the population self-report as devout. The Nelä Rewf Woodlands is considered underserved by the divines despite shrines in all settled areas.

Settlements and Economy

Nelä Rewf Woodlands is predominantly economicly reiant on Hammer Hills's export of Maple Syrup, and has no outstanding industries byond what is required for its role in that process.

The Nelä Rewf Woodlands has an Imperial Development Index of 6. For the reference of Eyom's native peoples, a IDI of 6 means there are 6 locations within the Nelä Rewf Woodlands which the Empire can securely and regularly transport materials and personnel..

As a courtesy for scholars and agents, the Nelä Rewf Woodlands is rated M1 by Eyom’s banks. Business ventures within the Nelä Rewf Woodlands are not expected to return value in yearly terms.
