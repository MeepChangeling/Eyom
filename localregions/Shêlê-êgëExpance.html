The Shêlê-êgë Expance (/ˈʃɘlɘ/ /ˈɘgë/ Thoughtplague [tactic] [plague]) is a subtropical expanse on the coast of the Thunderfields region. Shêlê-êgë Expance is a relativly typical expanse for Thunderfields, as well as Eyom as a whole.

Climate

The Shêlê-êgë Expance has a yearly average tempature of 23°C (73°F), with its average tempature durring the summer being a warm 28°C (82°F) and its average tempature durring the winter being a cool 19°C (66°F). The Shêlê-êgë Expance recieves an average of 145 cm/y (57 in/y) of percipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain durring the spring. The Shêlê-êgë Expance covers an area of nearly 13703 km2 (8516 mi2), and an average elevation of 4278 m (14035 ft) above sealevel.

People and Culture

Imperial Warning: The following section of this document deals with biological race. In defrence to the traditions of Eyomic peoples, we have forgon town-specific racial cencus, howeaver, as our culture lacks a taboo for classification of peoples by their biology and such infomration is highly important for our medical practices amongst other institutions, we have stated the racial demographics of this region. We do not do so to sew the seeds of disunity. We do not do so to belittle or bemoan any group. Such issues are why we use the term "peoples" rather than races, cultures, or species.

In respect to this universal cultural element, we have split racial classification by culture while keeping the name of each race for our own purposes. Should you see the same race listed more than once in the following section, this is to desinate a major cultural division within a race.

The Shêlê-êgë Expance is's peoples are culturaly Wareneese, but have their own local customs and traditions. Their shared vaules and traditions include purging evil and expelling the wicked and seeking fortune in new places or new roles, as well as a unique summer festival and a sun festival.

Wareneese

The Wareneese subculture native to the Shêlê-êgë Expance are noticably same height and weight as the neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Wareneese culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of impractical or elaborate role-based clothes and base their sence of individual idenity on trade guilds specific to particular roles. They also place greater cultural emphasus on harmony with nature and existing life and personal sacrifice for one’s causes or purposes.

Rattu

The Rattu subculture native to the Shêlê-êgë Expance are noticably same height and weight as the neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Wareneese culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of impractical or elaborate role-based clothes and base their sence of individual idenity on hereditary loyalty to a political group. They also place greater cultural emphasus on piety and devotion to the gods and aesthetic beauty in material goods and architecture.

Tauran

The Tauran subculture native to the Shêlê-êgë Expance are noticably much bigger and bulkier than neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Wareneese culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of role or class-specific clothing items and base their sence of individual idenity on having or lacking noble blood. They also place greater cultural emphasus on honesty and truthfulness in speech and sexual license and wantonness.

Imperial

The Imperial subculture native to the Shêlê-êgë Expance are noticably either short and stocky or tall and slender. While they follow the broader tennents of Wareneese culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of color choices with social meaning to them and base their sence of individual idenity on identity based on their local community. They also place greater cultural emphasus on exploring the unknown and discovering secrets and ascetic unworldliness and pious poverty.

Tanuki

The Tanuki subculture native to the Shêlê-êgë Expance are noticably either short and stocky or tall and slender. While they follow the broader tennents of Wareneese culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of painted skin markings that sometimes change and base their sence of individual idenity on numerous castes of hereditary workers. They also place greater cultural emphasus on ethnic purity of blood and culture and humanistic reason and “rational” religion.

Imperial

The Imperial subculture native to the Shêlê-êgë Expance are noticably same height and weight as the neighbors. While they follow the broader tennents of Wareneese culture, they have diffrenciated themselves by adopting a tradition of socially-meaningful animal motif items and base their sence of individual idenity on dynastic lineages with cadet branches. They also place greater cultural emphasus on sexual license and wantonness and justice and fairness between people.

Local Religion

The Shêlê-êgë Expance is overseen by Potentate Ghulmera Xanandromer Xandronos, a god serving within The Apprenticeship, the region's pricipal religion. Aproximently 54% of the population self-report as devout. The Shêlê-êgë Expance is overflowing with shrines and holy sites, with most noteworthy divines having one or more shrines in any location relevent to their portfolio.

Settlments and Economy

Shêlê-êgë Expance has its economic roots in Thunderfields's export of Agriculture, but has developed its own transportation industry, which has a marked impact on the local regional economy.

The Shêlê-êgë Expance has an Imperial Development Index of 20. For the refrence of Eyom's native peoples, a IDI of 20 means there are 20 locations within the Shêlê-êgë Expance which the Empire can securly and regurly transport matrials and personell..

As a courticy for scholars and agents, the Shêlê-êgë Expance is rated I2 by Eyoms banks. Buisness ventures within the Shêlê-êgë Expance are expected to return above capitol with some volitility.
