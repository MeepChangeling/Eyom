Abingfordbridge (/abingfordbridge / [Translation Unavailable]) is a subtropical Hamlet located in the Qegian Provence of the Kingdom of Hobben.

The name Abingfordbridge is derived from the Sylvin language, as Abingfordbridge was founded by Shesîg̈ Hoggan, who was culturaly Sylvin.

Climate

Abingfordbridge has a yearly average temperature of 17°C (62°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 21°C (69°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 13°C (55°F). Abingfordbridge receives an average of 251 cm/y (98 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Abingfordbridge covers an area of nearly 2 km2 (0 mi2), and an average elevation of 2182 m (7158 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Abingfordbridge was founded durring the late 12th century in fall of the year 1053, by Shesîg̈ Hoggan. The establishment of the new community went well, with no major obsticles durring construction.

Abingfordbridge was built using the conventions of Sylvin durring the late 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Abingfordbridge is no diffrent. The hamlet's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Abingfordbridge is is constructed arround a semi-circular premissive split-log ties mainstreet, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The hamlet lacks any defencive features, though certainly constructing even a simple fence or digging a ditch is at the forefront of the 's mind. At least, one would hope so.

Abingfordbridge has a very calm atmosphere. People can be seen relaxing, scocilizing, and going about all manner of business other than the daily grind. Men, women, children, all can be seen enjoying life in a laid-back way in the many parks which line Abingfordbridge ’s streets. The hamlet is very clearly a joyfull place as well as relaxing. Music can be heard often, as well as laughter. The smell of food and drink permiates the air. You can’t help but smile.

Cultural Notes

Abingfordbridge 's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for the combined use of arcuated and trabeated construction, employing arches and constructed with post and lintel. The arch served as the style's chief structural element, with flanking columns serving as buttresses or decorations. While the general shape of their structures could be generously described as simplistic, the use of these arches and columns created a most distinct ascetic which is easily distinguishable on sight to armatures and experts alike.

In Abingfordbridge the stars are always right.

The Spider, Assassin (Giant) near Abingfordbridge are known to be quite timid.

Abingfordbridge 's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves square dance to channel Charm energies of tier 3 via throat singing.

History

Abingfordbridge used to be more prosperous, but something happened relatively long ago that left it a shrunken shadow of its former self. If the settlement is prosperous, the locals often lament how much more they could have had. If the settlement is not prosperous, the locals blame their ill fortunes on that event. Reminders of this better time can be found in many places within Abingfordbridge .
