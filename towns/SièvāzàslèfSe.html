Sièvāzàslèf Se (Sièvā /ˈzəslɛf/ [accident] [preparation]) is a subtropical Large Town located in the Sterfíhkāríhsha Region of the Tetbur Commune.

The name Sièvāzàslèf Se is derived from the Tauric language, as Sièvāzàslèf Se was founded by Ýåk Fluell, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Sièvāzàslèf Se has a yearly average temperature of 25°C (77°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 32°C (89°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 19°C (66°F). Sièvāzàslèf Se receives an average of 289 cm/y (113 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Sièvāzàslèf Se covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 1370 m (4494 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Sièvāzàslèf Se was founded durring the late 16th century in spring of the year 1558, by Ýåk Fluell. The establishment of Sièvāzàslèf Se was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Ýåk Fluell electing to pay people to resettle in Sièvāzàslèf Se.

Sièvāzàslèf Se was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the late 16th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Sièvāzàslèf Se is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Sièvāzàslèf Se is buildings are arranged arrounded highly ordered system of spacious cobblestone streets which form hexical paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The town rests behind a thin stone wall. The wall's design was likly directly copied from a castle's parmiter defences. It's simply that the arcatect made Sièvāzàslèf Se's wall substancialy thinner than a castle's walls. While the towers and gatehouses are adiquite, the obvious cost savings measure of making the walls drasticaly thinner reduces their ability to resist siege weapons greatly. The town's impressive-looking wall could fail at a critical moment in battle, and would likely not even resist a few bandits with improvised siege equipment. The budget oriented have not been wellmaintained over the years, and while functional are in dire need of some loving care and perhapse light renovation.

A look around Sièvāzàslèf Se gives you an uneasy feeling. Everything is just a little too worn down, a little too dirty, or both. No one makes eye contact. Kids play quietly, but happily. Occasionally a passerby glances at you out of the corner of their eye, staring just long enough for it to be uncomfortable. This all rests atop the unmistakable impression the town is one of the strictest places imaginable. Everyone’s actions are clearly directed by laws they keep in heart and mind at all times. Orderly byond order is a phrase which Sièvāzàslèf Se brings to mind.

Civic Infrastructure

Sièvāzàslèf Se has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Sièvāzàslèf Se. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Sièvāzàslèf Se's parks.

Sièvāzàslèf Se has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Sièvāzàslèf Se.

Sièvāzàslèf Se has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Sièvāzàslèf Se has a Guild of Nurses, which is tasked with caring for the elderly and infirm in accordance with local ordinances, religious values, and customs.

Sièvāzàslèf Se has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Sièvāzàslèf Se has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Sièvāzàslèf Se has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Sièvāzàslèf Se's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Sièvāzàslèf Se possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Sièvāzàslèf Se has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Sièvāzàslèf Se's natural decorations nor waterways.

Sièvāzàslèf Se has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Sièvāzàslèf Se has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Sièvāzàslèf Se has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Sièvāzàslèf Se is home to a University which provides higher education in a variety of fields, and also serves as a research institute for those same fields.

Cultural Notes

Sièvāzàslèf Se's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by dynamic designs and complex architectural plan forms; intended to heighten feelings of motion and sensuality, and frequently based on the oval. It made extensive and extreme use of: Grandeur, Contrast, Curves and twists, Rich surface treatments, Gilded statuary, Bright colors, Vividly painted ceilings, Fragmented or deliberately incomplete elements, Large-scale frescoes, Dramatic central projections on an external facade, the use of plaster, stucco, or marble finishing, Illusory effects such as trompe l’oeil, and pear-shaped domes. While beloved by the nobility, the common folk tended to despise the style due to the massive consumption of resources required for even a small building constructed in this style.

In Sièvāzàslèf Se all of the cats speak the local language with prefect diction.

The Snake, Cobra (King) near Sièvāzàslèf Se are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Sièvāzàslèf Se's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves reenactments to channel Transmutation energies of tier 2 via oratory performances.
