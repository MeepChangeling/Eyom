Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu (/ə˞ˈtrə˞szo/ /cə˞st/ [rich] [anchor]) is a temperate Town located in the Midimuzunk Principality of the City-state of Kakoray.

The name Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu is derived from the Gnollish language, as Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu was founded by Mary vyim Mɑnkfilf, who was culturaly Gnollish.

Climate

Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu has a yearly average temperature of 6°C (42°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a cool 14°C (57°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing -1°C (31°F). Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu receives an average of 210 cm/y (82 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 2680 m (8792 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu was founded durring the early 13th century in spring of the year 1225, by Mary vyim Mɑnkfilf. The establishment of Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu was built using the conventions of Gnollish durring the early 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu is buildings are arranged arround a network of restrictive flagstone streets which form a grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town resides behind a palisade wall complete with battlments, a moat, and timber gatehouses with drawbridges. The town's robustly designed timber walls are visibly old, but also obviously well maintained. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with routine mantance of the town's defences.

Right off the bat Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu hits you in the face with its success. Everyone, even the peasants, are dressed in well made clothing. Every tool and implement you can see is finely made, and people will boast to you as obvious strangers of the wonders which can be found in their markets. More interestingly is a total lack of beggars, and plenty of new buildings are going up even as you speak. Many of those buildings are schools, scriptoriums, and even one college. One can only wonder what knowledge the town has come into.

Civic Infrastructure

Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu's parks.

Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu.

Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Town. Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu's grid is powered by a boiler and turbine based power plant.

Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu's old civil lighting system was converted to Galvanic Lamps recently, and expanded to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu's natural decorations nor waterways.

Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by vertical proportions, pointed arches, external buttressing, and asymmetry in the general shape of its buildings. The decorative features of the style were key, consisting of large arched windows, pointed arches, vaulted ceilings, flying buttresses, and sculptures integrated into the structure itself. Occasionally, for very important buildings, an array of sculptures or one colossal sculpture might replace the entirety of the entrance to said building.

In Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu it is impossible to directly or indirectly lie.

The Leshy, Poppy near Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu are known to be quite timid.

Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves gestures to channel Necromancy energies of tier 1 via divine sermons.

Points of Interest

A great magical Working has been a critical part of Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu since its creation, but now it's beginning to decay. It may function only intermittently, now, or its effects may have curdled into something double-edged. The locals have no idea how to fix it, and indeed, it may not be possible to repair it with modern science or sorcery.

Átrászo-cást Rudnoplu is known for its well built pedestrian paths, which include foot bridges to cross the main street at several high-traffic areas.
