Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà (/ˈɪːdæ/ /ˈdu̹cə ˈmɑrə/ [dumb (speechless)] [tower]) is a temperate Large City located in Duchâlà Qu̹de Kingdom, Bàze Logu Empire, within the Warren.

The name Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà is derived from the Sylvin language, as Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà was founded by Gilêm, who was culturaly Rattu.

Climate

Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà has a yearly average temperature of 8°C (46°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a cool 19°C (66°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing -3°C (27°F). Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà receives an average of 260 cm/y (102 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the brutaly long winter months. Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà covers an area of nearly 58 km2 (23 mi2), and an average elevation of 1780 m (5839 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà was founded durring the late 13th century in winter of the year 1180, by Gilêm. The establishment of Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà was built using the conventions of Rattu durring the late 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà is buildings are grouped arround an odd layout of spacious baked earthen streets, which seems to be based on an overlapping squair patern such that there are small squares at the cornor of every bigger square. Sometimes buildings exist in the smaller squaires, other times they are open spaces, or occupied by temporary structures. The city sits behind a stone-renforced palisade wall, with stone gatehouses and timber drawbridges for their trench. Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà's would-be-castle fortifications are suffering from significent damage, so much so that examples can be pointed to no matter which section one might have within their line of site, and most of which render sections inoperable at present.

Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà has the unmistakable air of a city on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà ’s existence has dried up and the city is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up.

Cultural Notes

Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for the combined use of arcuated and trabeated construction, employing arches and constructed with post and lintel. The arch served as the style's chief structural element, with flanking columns serving as buttresses or decorations. While the general shape of their structures could be generously described as simplistic, the use of these arches and columns created a most distinct ascetic which is easily distinguishable on sight to armatures and experts alike.

In Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà all of the cats speak the local language with prefect diction.

The Guardian, Graven near Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Chyameya Îdæ Du̹chyà Mârà's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves ritual combat to channel Transmutation energies of tier 3 via oratory performances.
