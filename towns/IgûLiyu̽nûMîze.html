Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze (/ˈigʊ/ /liˈju̽nʊ/ [blank; plain] [foam]) is a subtropical Town located in Lugu̹ Bu̽nike County, Chyûyu̽ Lu̹sû Empire, within the Warrenese Shogunate.

The name Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze is derived from the Wareneese language, as Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze was founded by Brèsê Shonkdsh, who was culturaly Wareneese.

Climate

Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze has a yearly average temperature of 12°C (53°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing -1°C (31°F). Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze receives an average of 309 cm/y (121 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 3670 m (12040 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze was founded durring the late 10th century, by Brèsê Shonkdsh. The establishment of Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze was built using the conventions of Wareneese durring the late 10th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze is buildings are arranged arround a single broad flagstone mainstreet with many smaller streets branching off of it which gives the town a over all rectangular shape, albit one warped and twisted by the nature of the curves of the main road. The town sits comfortably behind a palisade wall complete with a timber gatehouse and battlments. The timber-based walls has suffered a visible ammount of structural damage, leaving them effectivly useless. One can't help but wonder why the has not yet effected repairs.

Right off the bat Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze hits you in the face with its success. Everyone, even the peasants, are dressed in well made clothing. Every tool and implement you can see is finely made, and people will boast to you as obvious strangers of the wonders which can be found in their markets. More interestingly is a total lack of beggars, and plenty of new buildings are going up even as you speak. Somehow this town has come into quite a lot of wealth, and recently from the looks of things. This wealth has brought the town nothing but neurosis, if you can believe your eyes. Everyone acts as if you’re out to take a piece of them. Every door is locked and every window barred. Efforts are made to make you feel welcome, but only in the public areas of the town, particularly the market.

Civic Infrastructure

Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze's parks.

Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze.

Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Town. Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze's grid is powered by a direct leyline tap.

Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze's old civil lighting system was converted to Galvanic Lamps recently, and expanded to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze's natural decorations nor waterways.

Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for its functional shapes, abstract shapes used sparingly for decor, simple color schemes, holistic design, and basic industrial materials. Its simple designs were created to be beautiful, functional, and mass-producible. The style used little to no embellishment or ornamentation, instead drawing attention to the streamlined design, such as flat roofs to create a simple, geometric look. The simplicity masks the style's nearly sinister functionality, as every last feature is designed to guide the people living in the building in how to make the most efficient use of the structure.

In Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze leaves and flower petals never touch the ground.

The ﻿Howler near Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves bloodletting to channel Necromancy energies of tier 2 via recitation of scripture.

History

A local has came up with a wonderful new idea (697 % 6)+1 months ago; it may be a magical innovation, a new industrial process, a new agricultural product, a new use for what was thought to be ancient garbage, or some other very useful, profitable idea. Everyone around them is fighting for the chance to exploit this clever new plan.

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century, Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze was attacked by soldiers from another nation, with orders to raid Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze lost 246 people, 383 livestock, and 40 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 74, when members of Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze's militia enacted an operation to break a siege of a particular ruin. The operation was complicated by the enemy setting up an ambush. The conflict ended with needing to break through the enemy's lines, which ended in defeat for Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Igû Liyu̽nû Mîze's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
