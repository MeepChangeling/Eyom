Cultural Notes

Qÿhtvehb Dän Fehy's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for the combined use of arcuated and trabeated construction, employing arches and constructed with post and lintel. The arch served as the style's chief structural element, with flanking columns serving as buttresses or decorations. While the general shape of their structures could be generously described as simplistic, the use of these arches and columns created a most distinct ascetic which is easily distinguishable on sight to armatures and experts alike.

Due to the actions of local Kami, autumn is recurring in Qÿhtvehb Dän Fehy.

The Bloodbrush near Qÿhtvehb Dän Fehy are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Qÿhtvehb Dän Fehy's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves sacrificing an animal to channel Elven High Magic energies of tier 1 via speaking in tongues.
