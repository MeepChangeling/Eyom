Chyöt Ril Vrët (/co˞t/ /ril/ [wide; broad] [heaven; paradise]) is a subtropical Town located in Jr-45ajĭěnsǐ Zone, Qěvrìlæh District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Chyöt Ril Vrët is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as Chyöt Ril Vrët was founded by Khêb Malve, who was culturaly Wood Elven.

Climate

Chyöt Ril Vrët has a yearly average temperature of 20°C (68°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 13°C (55°F). Chyöt Ril Vrët receives an average of 264 cm/y (103 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Chyöt Ril Vrët covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 3510 m (11515 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Chyöt Ril Vrët was founded durring the early 17th century in early spring of the year 1613, by Khêb Malve. The establishment of Chyöt Ril Vrët suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Chyöt Ril Vrët which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Chyöt Ril Vrët was built using the conventions of Wood Elven durring the early 17th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Chyöt Ril Vrët is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Chyöt Ril Vrët is buildings are arranged arrounded a highly ordered system of crampt paverstone streets which form triangular paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The town lacks any defencive features, though certainly constructing even a simple fence or digging a ditch is at the forefront of the 's mind. At least, one would hope so.

A look around Chyöt Ril Vrët has something terribly wrong with it. It’s impossible to put one’s finger on, but something is horribly wrong. Maybe it’s the way fog blankets the ground, but only in the connors of places. Maybe it’s the vermin scutteling between shadows in the corner of your eyes. Perhaps it’s the overcast sky which seemed to creep out of nowhere, or the distant howling of wolves. Maybe it’s everything together. Regardless, you do not feel it would be wise to remain in Chyöt Ril Vrët long.

Civic Infrastructure

Chyöt Ril Vrët has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Chyöt Ril Vrët. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Chyöt Ril Vrët's parks.

Chyöt Ril Vrët has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Chyöt Ril Vrët.

Chyöt Ril Vrët has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Chyöt Ril Vrët has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Chyöt Ril Vrët has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Chyöt Ril Vrët has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Chyöt Ril Vrët has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Chyöt Ril Vrët's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Chyöt Ril Vrët has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Chyöt Ril Vrët's natural decorations nor waterways.

Chyöt Ril Vrët has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Chyöt Ril Vrët has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

While Chyöt Ril Vrët has a public leader, the real authority is hidden from outsiders. This ruler may draw their authority from rationales unacceptable to outsiders, they may have cowed the public authority into obedience, or they may have a mutually beneficial private arrangement with the official ruler.

Chyöt Ril Vrët's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is admittedly strange and non-linear style rooted in defiance of symmetrical shapes. It championed the creation of buildings with a unique visual appearance. the structural norms of classic buildings and deforms or moves away from elementary architectural principles. By including non-linear designs processed into its buildings and favoring fragmentation, this style expressed a form of controlled chaos. Its buildings appear out-of-the-ordinary, draw the eye in immediately and sometimes create a feeling of strangeness. These distorted shapes and structure are not reserved to the building’s outer facade, they destabilize interior elements too, favoring minimalism and play on people’s perceptions by injecting a futuristic touch.

In Chyöt Ril Vrët most nights are accompanied by colorful ribbons of light in the sky.

The Bonewrought Willow near Chyöt Ril Vrët are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Chyöt Ril Vrët's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves destroying a prepared ritual vessel to channel Illusion energies of tier 1 via throat chanting.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century, Chyöt Ril Vrët was attacked by organized criminals with significant wealth and power. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Chyöt Ril Vrët lost 266 people, 363 livestock, and 95 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 141, when members of Chyöt Ril Vrët's militia enacted an operation to secure a particular fortification to utilize it for a specific task. The operation was complicated by a trusted officer who turned traitor and defects. The conflict ended with the defense of the fortification against a siege, which ended in victory for Chyöt Ril Vrët's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Chyöt Ril Vrët's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
