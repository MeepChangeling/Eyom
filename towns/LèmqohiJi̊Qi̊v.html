Lèmqohi Ji̊ Qi̊v (/lɛm/ /ˈqohi/ [melody; tune; chorus] [lavender]) is a subtropical Large City located in the Kririsi̊mù Region of the Tetburland.

The name Lèmqohi Ji̊ Qi̊v is derived from the Sylvin language, as Lèmqohi Ji̊ Qi̊v was founded by Tsåts, who was culturaly Tauran.

Climate

Lèmqohi Ji̊ Qi̊v has a yearly average temperature of 30°C (86°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 33°C (91°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 27°C (80°F). Lèmqohi Ji̊ Qi̊v receives an average of 140 cm/y (55 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Lèmqohi Ji̊ Qi̊v covers an area of nearly 172 km2 (68 mi2), and an average elevation of 4696 m (15406 ft) above sea level.

Cultural Notes

Two or more groups of citizens within Lèmqohi Ji̊ Qi̊v hate each other. Their neighbors or the local law have kept things from too-overt violence, but members of the groups will constantly interfere with their rivals and cause whatever misery they can get away with. This hate may spring from recent events, or it may be an inherited spite from old wrongs.

Lèmqohi Ji̊ Qi̊v's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known for its buildings being predominantly formed through the combination of basic geometric shapes. However, it shown in the detailing given to the structures such as its characteristic tall columns, intricate detail, symmetry, harmony, and balance in their designs to an astonishing degree of precision. Decorative elements for the buildings tended to be built into the structure itself, making great use of fluting, frescoes, inlays, and embossing.

In Lèmqohi Ji̊ Qi̊v the utterance of expletives is impossible within city limits. This has changed as the limits have changed, but has not kept up with slang, or swearing in foreign languages.

The Snake, Viper near Lèmqohi Ji̊ Qi̊v are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Lèmqohi Ji̊ Qi̊v's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves sacrificing an animal to channel Summoning energies of tier 2 via guttural bellowing.
