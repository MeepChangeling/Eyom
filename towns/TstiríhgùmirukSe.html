Tstiríhgùmiruk Se (Tstiríh /gʊˈmiruc/ [idiot; fool] [absence]) is a subtropical Town located in Gu̹chyû S̺ukâ Kingdom, Ftèwup Provence, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Tstiríhgùmiruk Se is derived from the Tauric language, as Tstiríhgùmiruk Se was founded by Requiem Beetles Gakēè, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Tstiríhgùmiruk Se has a yearly average temperature of 26°C (78°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 29°C (84°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 23°C (73°F). Tstiríhgùmiruk Se receives an average of 193 cm/y (75 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Tstiríhgùmiruk Se covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 5180 m (16994 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Tstiríhgùmiruk Se was founded durring the late 15th century in fall of the year 1398, by Requiem Beetles Gakēè. The establishment of Tstiríhgùmiruk Se was only bairly constructed. The sheer number of problems with its founding were enough to make several of the backers funding Tstiríhgùmiruk Se's construction back out of the project. Requiem Beetles Gakēè pushed on reguardles, and Tstiríhgùmiruk Se was finished, but starts off as a terible place to live.

Tstiríhgùmiruk Se was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the late 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Tstiríhgùmiruk Se is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Tstiríhgùmiruk Se is buildings are grouped arround an odd layout of restrictive split-log ties streets, which seems to be based on an overlapping squair patern such that there are small squares at the cornor of every bigger square. Sometimes buildings exist in the smaller squaires, other times they are open spaces, or occupied by temporary structures. The town is protected by a humble stone fence which is tall enough to provide adiquite cover for defenders to fire from, but no more than that. Tstiríhgùmiruk Se's peasent-grade has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

Tstiríhgùmiruk Se is not quite well. Something happened here, perhapse recently, perhapse long ago. Whatever it was, it settled into the very soul of the town like a festering wound. The people go about their day well enough, but there’s a tention in the air you can cut with a knife. You get the terrible feeling that whatever it was, the wound it left will simply never heal. This town is as a necropolice.

Civic Infrastructure

Tstiríhgùmiruk Se has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Tstiríhgùmiruk Se. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Tstiríhgùmiruk Se's parks.

Tstiríhgùmiruk Se has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Tstiríhgùmiruk Se.

Tstiríhgùmiruk Se has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Tstiríhgùmiruk Se has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Tstiríhgùmiruk Se has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Tstiríhgùmiruk Se's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Tstiríhgùmiruk Se possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Tstiríhgùmiruk Se has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Tstiríhgùmiruk Se's natural decorations nor waterways.

Tstiríhgùmiruk Se has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Tstiríhgùmiruk Se has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Tstiríhgùmiruk Se has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

The locals are in a state of despair and dull apathy. They've lost the things that used to give them pride and hope, with the best among them carrying on out of habitual duty and the worst giving ready hands to shameful deeds and ignoble acts. No one really believes the future can be better, and most seek only to satisfy immediate appetites.

Tstiríhgùmiruk Se's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by order, symmetry, formal design, grandiosity, and elaborate ornamentation. Architectural characteristics include balustrades, balconies, columns, cornices, pilasters, and triangular pediments. Stone exteriors are massive and grandiose in their symmetry; interiors are typically polished and lavishly decorated with sculptures, swags, medallions, flowers, and shields. Interiors will often have a grand stairway and opulent ballroom..

Due to the actions of local Kami, winter is short in Tstiríhgùmiruk Se.

The Quickling near Tstiríhgùmiruk Se are known to be quite timid.

Tstiríhgùmiruk Se's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves embarking on a group pilgrimage to channel Conjuration energies of tier 1 via oratory performances.
