Vu̹chyû Bumâ Kîchyà (/ˈkʊme/ /ˈbumɑ/ [next] [orgasm]) is a subtropical Town located in Mihâs̺ilà Lu Îmu̹ Dutchy, Pimthêkpíidi Provence, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Vu̹chyû Bumâ Kîchyà is derived from the Wareneese language, as Vu̹chyû Bumâ Kîchyà was founded by Zlêyêv Zlkartesí, who was culturaly Wareneese.

Climate

Vu̹chyû Bumâ Kîchyà has a yearly average temperature of 15°C (59°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a cool 20°C (68°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 10°C (50°F). Vu̹chyû Bumâ Kîchyà receives an average of 268 cm/y (105 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Vu̹chyû Bumâ Kîchyà covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 976 m (3202 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Vu̹chyû Bumâ Kîchyà was founded durring the late 14th century in winter of the year 1285, by Zlêyêv Zlkartesí. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Zlêyêv Zlkartesí.

Vu̹chyû Bumâ Kîchyà was built using the conventions of Wareneese durring the late 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Vu̹chyû Bumâ Kîchyà is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Vu̹chyû Bumâ Kîchyà is is constructed arround a series of premissive cobblestone mainstreets which form overlapping circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The town rests behind a thick wall made from clay bricks. The wall has all of the proper fortifications and is well made. Unfortuantly the nature of clay brick leaves it quite vulnerable to siege equipment, though the thickness of the wall lends it simmilar resistnace to a thinner hardrock wall. Unfortuantly, these millitarily questionable fortifications are in extreem disrepair, so much so that one cannot tell if they are decaying from a lack of mantance or damage incured.

A look around Vu̹chyû Bumâ Kîchyà is like looking into a university’s plaza on the day before an exam. People rush about to-and-fro. Discarded scrolls and quills are dotted around town. In spite of many locals discussing academic topics, there’s little sign of any organized groups for any of that talk. Infact, the town seems disordered in general with everything scattered helter skelter about.

Cultural Notes

Vu̹chyû Bumâ Kîchyà 's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is which made use of the classical orders and mathematically precise ratios of height and width combined with a desire for symmetry, proportion, and harmony. It used columns, pediments, arches and domes are imaginatively in buildings of all types. Decorative features were seen as largely unnecessary as the sheer beauty of the structure itself was often close to art. However, many buildings with large ceiling spaces had their ceilings decorated with elaborate paintings, simply because the large flat spaces could feel wasted.

In Vu̹chyû Bumâ Kîchyà there is no wind.

The Chaneque near Vu̹chyû Bumâ Kîchyà are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Vu̹chyû Bumâ Kîchyà 's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves embarking on a group pilgrimage to channel Charm energies of tier 2 via moments of science.

Points of Interest

Vu̹chyû Bumâ Kîchyà was unknowingly built atop something unstable, and now that substrate is crumbling. It may be swampy ground or a decaying coastline, or it could be an ancient buried city that’s now giving way. In the case of some antique habitation, the denizens that once lived there might be boiling upward as their home is collapsing, or new opportunities may be revealed even as the community’s present structure is ruined.

Vu̹chyû Bumâ Kîchyà 's is something of a geological and arcane anomaly, as neither physical nor magical law entirely explains its formation.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century was struck by a great wind storm. A great funnel cloud itself touched down in Vu̹chyû Bumâ Kîchyà , bringing twisting winds which killed 288 people, 289 livestock, and 37 buildings in the disaster.. The disaster is generally remembered as the Howling Winds of Grief.
