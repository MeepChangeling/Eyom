Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ (/ˈvʊna/ /ˈru̹qʊ/ [large] [matter (substance; material)]) is a temperate Large City located in Gû S̺àqusæ Kingdom, Mî Kûrà Empire, within the Warren.

The name Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ is derived from the Goblin language, as Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ was founded by Shraksea, who was culturaly Rattu.

Climate

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ has a yearly average temperature of 14°C (57°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 2°C (35°F). Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ receives an average of 293 cm/y (115 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ covers an area of nearly 78 km2 (31 mi2), and an average elevation of 2854 m (9363 ft) above sea level.

Civic Infrastructure

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ possesses a city-wide Aethary Link which provides Aethary access anywhere within its metropolitan. This allows citizens who can afford the relevant devices access in their places of work, and rarely homes.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ has an animal control department which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the control, impoundment, and disposition of animals.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ's parks.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ has a government-funded child care program, overseen by the local Department of Nursemaids, which is responsible for providing childcare to working-class citizens according to local ordinances.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ has a Guild of Nurses, which is tasked with caring for the elderly and infirm in accordance with local ordinances, religious values, and customs.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ has an Administrative Academy which trains individuals in the administrative arts.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Large City. Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ's grid is powered by a direct leyline tap.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. In spite of the Galvanic Grid, these lights continue to use their old fule sources to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ has a first rate hospital which caters to anyone in need of long term medical care.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ has a library, which keeps a large collection of books, scrolls, and archives all manner of physical items. While not open to the public, the librarians and scholars employed by the library will assist anyone with their research needs, and wealthy individuals can purchase membership to access the library's materials themselves. In spite of being generally closed to the public, the library has a room with several Aether Linked devices available to the public during business hours.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ's natural decorations nor waterways.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ's locals despise outsiders. For some “outsiders” may be natives of foreign lands, while others might have a grudge against anyone from outside the community. These locals have an active loathing, and the outsiders allowed to trade or interact with them do so at a heavy disadvantage.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for the combined use of arcuated and trabeated construction, employing arches and constructed with post and lintel. The arch served as the style's chief structural element, with flanking columns serving as buttresses or decorations. While the general shape of their structures could be generously described as simplistic, the use of these arches and columns created a most distinct ascetic which is easily distinguishable on sight to armatures and experts alike.

In Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ the stars are always right.

The ﻿Urdefhan near Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ are known to be quite timid.

Vûna Ru̹qû Kâchyâ's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves embarking on a group pilgrimage to channel Illusion energies of tier 2 via recitation of poetic epics.
