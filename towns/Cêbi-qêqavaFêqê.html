Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê (/ˈcɘbi/ /qɘˈqava/ [crazy; insane; silly] [link]) is a subtropical Large Town located in Beacwind Dutchy, Vangîfkthêkè Provence, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê was founded by Matilda Glkëmêpí, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê has a yearly average temperature of 25°C (77°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 23°C (73°F). Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê receives an average of 272 cm/y (107 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 4850 m (15912 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê was founded durring the late 14th century in spring of the year 1266, by Matilda Glkëmêpí. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Matilda Glkëmêpí.

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the late 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê is buildings are grouped arround an odd layout of spacious cobblestone streets, which seems to be based on an overlapping squair patern such that there are small squares at the cornor of every bigger square. Sometimes buildings exist in the smaller squaires, other times they are open spaces, or occupied by temporary structures. The town is the proud owner of a proper castle-style stone wall complete with all of the trimmings. It has towers, a moat, gatehouses, drawbridges, and even merticulationsshortsizeleadershipname.. Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê's perhapse unnessisarily well built defences are suffering from significent damage, so much so that examples can be pointed to no matter which section one might have within their line of site, and most of which render sections inoperable at present.

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê is a bustling hive of activity. Everyone moves at nothing short of a jog, each convocation is a mile a minute, and there’s dedicated lanes for riding through town in the center of each street. The locals all appear to be not merely occupied, but in a true hurry for everything from drinking a pint of ale to their daily work.

Civic Infrastructure

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê possesses a city-wide Aethary Link which provides Aethary access anywhere within its metropolitan. This allows citizens who can afford the relevant devices access in their places of work, and rarely homes.

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê has an animal control department which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the control, impoundment, and disposition of animals.

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê's parks.

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê.

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê's natural decorations nor waterways.

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by grandeur of scale, simplicity of geometric forms, dramatic use of columns, and a preference for blank walls. This design ethos extended to re-imagining earlier styles of structure to create them anew, with a similar overall look and feel to one another..

Due to the actions of local Kami, summer is recurring in Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê.

The Mercane near Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Cêbi-qêqava Fêqê's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves ritual combat to channel Illusion energies of tier 1 via recitation of scripture.
