Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù (Kriy̼ /ˈhadmʊ/ [syringe] [steel]) is a temperate Large City located in Bègyèfíhgwi County, Dalteria County, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù is derived from the Tauric language, as Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù was founded by Trîbeg̈mê Guynn, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù has a yearly average temperature of 29°C (84°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 33°C (91°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 26°C (78°F). Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù receives an average of 166 cm/y (65 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù covers an area of nearly 273 km2 (109 mi2), and an average elevation of 3216 m (10551 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù was founded durring the early 14th century in spring of the year 1303, by Trîbeg̈mê Guynn. The establishment of Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Trîbeg̈mê Guynn struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù as a prison colony.

Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the early 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù is buildings are built arround a single narrow flagstone mainstreet which forms a counterclockwise spiral to give the city a over all circular shape. The valley city rests behind a set of well fortified walls, with gatehouses, watch towers, and battlments. Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù's walls are, howeaver, fashioned from stone and timber. While unorthadox, the design looks to be functional to a reasonable degree. With luck, the untested design will remain untested for years to come. The city's unusual yet seemingly effective fortifications are visibly old, but also obviously maintained semi-regularly. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with ocasional mantance of the citys defences.

A look around Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù gives you an uneasy feeling. Everything is just a little too worn down, a little too dirty, or both. No one makes eye contact. Kids play quietly, but happily. Occasionally a passerby glances at you out of the corner of their eye, staring just long enough for it to be uncomfortable. A second look around Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù makes it abundantly clear the city suffered something horrible some time ago. It's as if the town itself is depressed. Smiles are few, cheer is nowhere to be had.

Cultural Notes

Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known for its buildings being predominantly formed through the combination of basic geometric shapes. However, it shown in the detailing given to the structures such as its characteristic tall columns, intricate detail, symmetry, harmony, and balance in their designs to an astonishing degree of precision. Decorative elements for the buildings tended to be built into the structure itself, making great use of fluting, frescoes, inlays, and embossing.

Due to the actions of local Kami, winter is recurring in Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù.

The Church Grim near Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves square dance to channel Necromancy energies of tier 3 via throat singing.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù was attacked by savage tribes living nearby Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù lost 293 people, 142 livestock, and 88 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 63, when members of Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù's militia enacted an operation to escort a particular mine to a safe location. The operation was complicated by a natural disaster interrupted the operation, shattering unit cohesion. The conflict ended with a last stand against the enemy until a particular event occurred, which ended in victory for Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Qi̘v Kriy̼hadmù's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
