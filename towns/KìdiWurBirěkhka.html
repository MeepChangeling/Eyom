Kìdi Wur Birěkhka (/ˈkɨdi/ /wur/ [true] [acronym]) is a subtropical Town located in Kto̠lwaw Parish, Rs-20pvèl District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Kìdi Wur Birěkhka is derived from the Constructi language, as Kìdi Wur Birěkhka was founded by Kèbê Èbmesîpí, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Kìdi Wur Birěkhka has a yearly average temperature of 18°C (64°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 13°C (55°F). Kìdi Wur Birěkhka receives an average of 93 cm/y (36 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Kìdi Wur Birěkhka covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 6572 m (-21387 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Kìdi Wur Birěkhka was founded durring the early 15th century in fall of the year 1403, by Kèbê Èbmesîpí. The establishment of Kìdi Wur Birěkhka was only bairly constructed. The sheer number of problems with its founding were enough to make several of the backers funding Kìdi Wur Birěkhka's construction back out of the project. Kèbê Èbmesîpí pushed on reguardles, and Kìdi Wur Birěkhka was finished, but starts off as a terible place to live.

Kìdi Wur Birěkhka was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the early 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Kìdi Wur Birěkhka is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Kìdi Wur Birěkhka is buildings are arranged arround a network of broad cobblestone streets which form a diamond shaped grid, where each diamond verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller diamond has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town is protected by a well-crafted cobblestone fence tall enough to provide adiquite cover for defenders to fire from, but no more than that. Kìdi Wur Birěkhka's frontieer-style defences has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

A look around Kìdi Wur Birěkhka makes you worry someone might stab you in a dark ally for your boots. It’s not filthy, or dark, but the smiles seem strained, the locals seem to glare daggers in eachothers backs a little too much, and everyone is armed at all times. You may want to keep an eye on your valuables, and make sure you don’t wind up in any position of power.

Cultural Notes

Kìdi Wur Birěkhka's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known for its buildings being predominantly formed through the combination of basic geometric shapes. However, it shown in the detailing given to the structures such as its characteristic tall columns, intricate detail, symmetry, harmony, and balance in their designs to an astonishing degree of precision. Decorative elements for the buildings tended to be built into the structure itself, making great use of fluting, frescoes, inlays, and embossing.

In Kìdi Wur Birěkhka there is a constant smell of overcooked presumably ethnic food.

The Ice Elemental, Small near Kìdi Wur Birěkhka are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Kìdi Wur Birěkhka's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves drinking to channel Elven High Magic energies of tier 2 via throat singing.

Points of Interest

Kìdi Wur Birěkhka is uncommonly rich, not only for the gentry but for the common citizens as well. They may produce a valuable good, oversee precious resource extraction, have special economic favors from the ruler, or simply have inherited a vast body of infrastructure. Their neighbors likely view them with envy, and outside raiders and exploiters find them an ideal target.

Kìdi Wur Birěkhka's roads were poorly made when first laid. Rather than repairing them correctly, a series of new roads was laid atop the old, leading to the streets of modern Kìdi Wur Birěkhka suffering from potholes, cracking, and even sinkholes. The locals often repair the road by putting down wooden decking.

POI

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century, Kìdi Wur Birěkhka was attacked by soldiers from another nation, waging a greater campaign. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Kìdi Wur Birěkhka lost 268 people, 256 livestock, and 21 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 207, when members of Kìdi Wur Birěkhka's militia enacted an operation to defend a particular solgiers group from enemy attack. The operation was complicated by blistering heat, such that many succumbed to heatstroke. The conflict ended with an assault and siege on the production site, which ended in victory for Kìdi Wur Birěkhka's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Kìdi Wur Birěkhka's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
