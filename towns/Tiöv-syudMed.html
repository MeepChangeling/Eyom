Tiöv-syud Med (/tjo˞v/ /sjud/ [sling] [rust]) is a subtropical Large City located in the Nofe County of the Viceroyalty of Rosid.

The name Tiöv-syud Med is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as Tiöv-syud Med was founded by Worm That Walks, Human Form Chebedric, who was culturaly Wood Elven.

Climate

Tiöv-syud Med has a yearly average temperature of 13°C (55°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 26°C (78°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 1°C (33°F). Tiöv-syud Med receives an average of 147 cm/y (57 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Tiöv-syud Med covers an area of nearly 268 km2 (107 mi2), and an average elevation of 3582 m (11751 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Tiöv-syud Med was founded durring the early 15th century in fall of the year 1417, by Worm That Walks, Human Form Chebedric. The establishment of Tiöv-syud Med suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Tiöv-syud Med which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Tiöv-syud Med was built using the conventions of Wood Elven durring the early 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Tiöv-syud Med is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Tiöv-syud Med is buildings are arranged arround a network of spacious gravel streets which form a grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The valley city has a fortified albit thin brick wall. The wall has most of the feeatures of a typical castle wall, just on a much smaller scale and and budget. Notably brick isn't a particuarly soild choice for resisting siege weapons. Tiöv-syud Med's wall wouldn't hinder a proper army, but it is more than sufishent for bandits and other small marauding groups. The city's monster and outlaw focused fortifications are visibly old, but also obviously well maintained. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with routine mantance of the city's defences.

Tiöv-syud Med has the unmistakable air of a city on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Tiöv-syud Med ’s existence has dried up and the city is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. The locals seem to have responded to their slow downfall by recreating Tiöv-syud Med as one of the strictest places imaginable. Everyone’s actions are clearly directed by laws they keep in heart and mind at all times. Orderly byond order is a phrase which the city brings to mind.

They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Tiöv-syud Med's parks.

Tiöv-syud Med has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Tiöv-syud Med has a Guild of Nurses, which is tasked with caring for the elderly and infirm in accordance with local ordinances, religious values, and customs.

Tiöv-syud Med has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Tiöv-syud Med has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Tiöv-syud Med has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Tiöv-syud Med has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Tiöv-syud Med's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Tiöv-syud Med has an Arcane Academy which provides higher education in the arcane sciences.

Tiöv-syud Med possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Large City. Tiöv-syud Med's grid is powered by mana accumulators.

Tiöv-syud Med's old civil lighting system was converted to Galvanic Lamps recently, and expanded to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Tiöv-syud Med has a first rate hospital which caters to anyone in need of long term medical care.

Tiöv-syud Med has a library, which keeps a large collection of books, scrolls, and archives all manner of physical items. The library is open to the public, including the Aether Link.

Tiöv-syud Med has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Tiöv-syud Med's natural decorations nor waterways.

Tiöv-syud Med has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Tiöv-syud Med has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Tiöv-syud Med has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Tiöv-syud Med is home to a University which provides higher education in a variety of fields, and also serves as a research institute for those same fields.

Cultural Notes

Tiöv-syud Med's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is based upon new and innovative technologies of construction and the idea that form should follow function. It was an embrace of minimalism and a rejection of ornament. The style became characterized by an emphasis on volume, asymmetrical compositions, and minimal ornamentation..

Due to the actions of local Kami, winter is recurring in Tiöv-syud Med.

The Amoeba Swarm near Tiöv-syud Med are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Tiöv-syud Med's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves sex to channel Wild Magic energies of tier 3 via oratory performances.
