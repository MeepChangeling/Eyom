Ālbugfāa Se (/əˑlˈbugfəˑ/ /a/ [chair] [small; little; short; low; miniature]) is a temperate Town located in Shåvphÿhz Hold, Íhglopníhp Provence, within the Tauranian United Provinces.

The name Ālbugfāa Se is derived from the Tauric language, as Ālbugfāa Se was founded by Béōy Ha̋sh 'Andre Cutebia' Gōōkī Gä̋mp̪f̄ Cú̄̌sh Nanner, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Ālbugfāa Se has a yearly average temperature of 21°C (69°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 29°C (84°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 14°C (57°F). Ālbugfāa Se receives an average of 193 cm/y (75 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Ālbugfāa Se covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 1454 m (4770 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Ālbugfāa Se was founded durring the late 15th century in fall of the year 1371, by Béōy Ha̋sh 'Andre Cutebia' Gōōkī Gä̋mp̪f̄ Cú̄̌sh Nanner. The establishment of Ālbugfāa Se suffered from several major issues, resulting in the need to develop many solutions to basic problems. Problems such as a lack of fresh water, logistical support, poor quality tools, and the odd monster or two. Howeaver, these were overcome in time.

Ālbugfāa Se was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the late 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Ālbugfāa Se is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Ālbugfāa Se is buildings are arranged arround a network of spacious split-log ties streets which form a grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town is protected by a well-crafted cobblestone fence tall enough to provide adiquite cover for defenders to fire from, but no more than that. The frontieer-style defences has suffered a visible ammount of structural damage, leaving them effectivly useless. One can't help but wonder why the has not yet effected repairs.

A look around Ālbugfāa Se gives you an uneasy feeling. Everything is just a little too worn down, a little too dirty, or both. No one makes eye contact. Kids play quietly, but happily. Occasionally a passerby glances at you out of the corner of their eye, staring jsut long enough for it to be uncomfortable.

Civic Infrastructure

Ālbugfāa Se has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Ālbugfāa Se. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Ālbugfāa Se's parks.

Ālbugfāa Se has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Ālbugfāa Se.

Ālbugfāa Se has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Ālbugfāa Se has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Ālbugfāa Se has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Ālbugfāa Se has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Ālbugfāa Se's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Ālbugfāa Se possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Ālbugfāa Se has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Ālbugfāa Se's natural decorations nor waterways.

Ālbugfāa Se has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Ālbugfāa Se has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Ālbugfāa Se's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for the combined use of arcuated and trabeated construction, employing arches and constructed with post and lintel. The arch served as the style's chief structural element, with flanking columns serving as buttresses or decorations. While the general shape of their structures could be generously described as simplistic, the use of these arches and columns created a most distinct ascetic which is easily distinguishable on sight to armatures and experts alike.

In Ālbugfāa Se it is impossible to directly or indirectly lie.

The Ovinnik near Ālbugfāa Se are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Ālbugfāa Se's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves reenactments to channel Conjuration energies of tier 1 via recitation of poetic epics.

History

Ālbugfāa Se's residents established a new industry (1671 % 6)+1 years ago, and it’s making them a great deal of profit. Old patterns of authority and wealth are being disrupted, and the old gentry are unlikely to be pleased about it. They may be trying to take over the industry, or they may have been the ones to enable it in the first place and are using it to crush the life out of any rival power bases. Outsiders might be playing a major role as well, and it could be they plot to siphon off the profits.

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century the Kami spared the town from an attack. One of Ālbugfāa Se's local festivals commemorates this miracle.
