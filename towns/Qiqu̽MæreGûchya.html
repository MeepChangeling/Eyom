Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya (/ˈqiqu̽/ /ˈmære/ [selfish] [soil; clay]) is a temperate City located in Làyu̽ Ekû Kingdom, Chyûyu̽ Lu̹sû Empire, within the Warren.

The name Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya is derived from the Sylvin language, as Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya was founded by Dyêpí, who was culturaly Rattu.

Climate

Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya has a yearly average temperature of 8°C (46°F), with its average temperature during the summer being an icy 26°C (78°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold -10°C (14°F). Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya receives an average of 291 cm/y (114 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the long winter months. Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya covers an area of nearly 30 km2 (12 mi2), and an average elevation of 3992 m (13097 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya was founded durring the early 11th century, by Dyêpí. The establishment of Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya was built using the conventions of Rattu durring the early 11th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya is is constructed arround a series of broad cobblestone mainstreets which form concentric circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to each other at varrious points. The city rests behind the absurdity that is a thick, timber braced, wall made of clay bricks. While visualy impressive and certainly an astetic, Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya's wall provides no actual defence against siege equipment due to the choice of its cosntruction materials. Even nonexperts can tell the town is trying to impress rather than defend with its walls, towers, and gatehouses. Though admittedly, they do look nice... To primitive tribals who have never seen fortifications before. Astonishigly, the political statment focused walls are in pristine condishion, as if they had just been finished before you laied eyes upon them.

Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya has a very calm atmosphere. People can be seen relaxing, scocilizing, and going about all manner of business other than the daily grind. Men, women, children, all can be seen enjoying life in a laid-back way in the many parks which line Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya’s streets. Yet, deep down, you know this atmosphere is a carefully created illusion. You can see the ocasional seam in their tapestry. You’re not sure what has them doing their best to act so carefree, but act carefree they do.

Cultural Notes

The locals have cut a deal with some unspeakable entity, trading some form of tribute (benign of horrific) in exchange for the being’s forbearance or assistance. Outsiders may be ignorant of the bargain, or they may know that the community is in thrall but be too fearful of its master to take action against them. The creature likely serves as a protector as well as a tyrant, so the locals may be content with the deal even if it doesn’t offer any further inducement or aid.

Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is which made use of the classical orders and mathematically precise ratios of height and width combined with a desire for symmetry, proportion, and harmony. It used columns, pediments, arches and domes are imaginatively in buildings of all types. Decorative features were seen as largely unnecessary as the sheer beauty of the structure itself was often close to art. However, many buildings with large ceiling spaces had their ceilings decorated with elaborate paintings, simply because the large flat spaces could feel wasted.

In Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya there is a constant smell of overcooked presumably ethnic food.

The Cloud Dragon, Wyrmling near Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Qiqu̽ Mære Gûchya's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves destroying a prepared ritual vessel to channel Mysticism energies of tier 3 via guttural bellowing.
