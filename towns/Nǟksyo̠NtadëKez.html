Nǟksyo̠ Ntadë Kez (/ˈnaːksjo̠/ /ˈntadɛ/ [severe; intense] [water]) is a temperate Town located in the Gde̜ngwatyod Moot of the Viceroyalty of Rosid.

The name Nǟksyo̠ Ntadë Kez is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as Nǟksyo̠ Ntadë Kez was founded by Knellirh Raeldireth, who was culturaly Wood Elven.

Overview

Nǟksyo̠ Ntadë Kez was founded durring the early 10th century, by Knellirh Raeldireth. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Knellirh Raeldireth.

Nǟksyo̠ Ntadë Kez was built using the conventions of Wood Elven durring the early 10th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Nǟksyo̠ Ntadë Kez is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Nǟksyo̠ Ntadë Kez is was constructed arround several premissive canals mainstreets which cross one another at certain axies, with smaller streets branching off of them to premit acess to the many buildings deeper into the road network. The overall fashion is remenessent of a circulatory system, or other organic construct, and is quite effishent in its design. The town emploies a series of defencive earthworks and fences to provide minimal protection against wild beasts and smaller groups of intelegent foes. Astonishigly, the somewhat suffishent are in pristine condishion, as if they had just been finished before you laied eyes upon them.

Right off the bat Nǟksyo̠ Ntadë Kez hits you in the face with its success. Everyone, even the peasants, are dressed in well made clothing. Every tool and implement you can see is finely made, and people will boast to you as obvious strangers of the wonders which can be found in their markets. More interestingly is a total lack of beggars, and plenty of new buildings are going up even as you speak. Somehow this town has come into quite a lot of wealth, and recently from the looks of things. It’s easy to see where their wealth comes from the more you look arround. Every aspect of the town has been developed with care and great thought. The people themselves act deliberately and with care in even the simplest of daily actions.

The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Nǟksyo̠ Ntadë Kez lost 109 people, 392 livestock, and 43 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 91, when members of Nǟksyo̠ Ntadë Kez's militia enacted an operation to deliver a message to a particular prison, then reinforce it. The operation was complicated by the officer in charge of Nǟksyo̠ Ntadë Kez's forces was incompetent, and issue many foolish and-or unprofessional orders. The conflict ended with pitched battle between both forces, which ended in victory for Nǟksyo̠ Ntadë Kez's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Nǟksyo̠ Ntadë Kez's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
