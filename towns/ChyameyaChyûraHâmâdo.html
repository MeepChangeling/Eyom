Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo (/biˈgəbɑ/ /hɑˈmɑdo/ [relevant] [mural]) is a temperate Large City located in Quvupuhibubo Kingdom, Kahotivupunabu Empire, within the Warren.

The name Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo is derived from the Sylvin language, as Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo was founded by Belone, who was culturaly Rattu.

Climate

Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo has a yearly average temperature of 15°C (59°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 7°C (44°F). Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo receives an average of 249 cm/y (98 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo covers an area of nearly 102 km2 (40 mi2), and an average elevation of 6352 m (20839 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo was founded durring the late 17th century in early summer of the year 1660, by Belone. The establishment of Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Belone electing to pay people to resettle in Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo.

Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo was built using the conventions of Rattu durring the late 17th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo is buildings are arranged arround a network of narrow gravel streets which form a grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The city has a fortified albit thin brick wall. The wall has most of the feeatures of a typical castle wall, just on a much smaller scale and and budget. Notably brick isn't a particuarly soild choice for resisting siege weapons. Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo's wall wouldn't hinder a proper army, but it is more than sufishent for bandits and other small marauding groups. The monster and outlaw focused fortifications has suffered a visible ammount of structural damage, leaving them effectivly useless. One can't help but wonder why the has not yet effected repairs.

Your first impression of Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo proves to be right on the money. This city is where the rebels chose to settle. Fashion trends are quite simply the opposite of what’s popular in the other nearby communities. THis trend extends everywhere. Things that should be black are white, what would normally be square is organically curved, and what is typically unthinkable for polite society is more than welcome in this public square. Up to the logical limits, of course. There’s no flaying someone alive to eat their skin going on, but the women are dressed a little immodestly, the men are a little more emotional, and the food is all overly complex and terrible.

Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo's old civil lighting system was converted to Galvanic Lamps recently, and expanded to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

While not open to the public, the librarians and scholars employed by the library will assist anyone with their research needs, and wealthy individuals can purchase membership to access the library's materials themselves. In spite of being generally closed to the public, the library has a room with several Aether Linked devices available to the public during business hours.

Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo's natural decorations nor waterways.

Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by order, symmetry, formal design, grandiosity, and elaborate ornamentation. Architectural characteristics include balustrades, balconies, columns, cornices, pilasters, and triangular pediments. Stone exteriors are massive and grandiose in their symmetry; interiors are typically polished and lavishly decorated with sculptures, swags, medallions, flowers, and shields. Interiors will often have a grand stairway and opulent ballroom..

In Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo sometimes, near one of the seasonal solstices, the sun appears to split into three and it rains from a clear sky for several hours.

The Leshy, Gourd near Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves bloodletting to channel Abjuration energies of tier 2 via speaking in tongues.

History

Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo used to be more prosperous, but something happened relatively long ago that left it a shrunken shadow of its former self. If the settlement is prosperous, the locals often lament how much more they could have had. If the settlement is not prosperous, the locals blame their ill fortunes on that event. Reminders of this better time can be found in many places within Chyameya Chyûra Hâmâdo.
