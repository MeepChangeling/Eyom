Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf (/zrak/ /miŋˈsor/ [wrong] [passenger]) is a subtropical City located in Ghēkáluplä Hold, Trïpghe Kingdom, within the Undermountain.

The name Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf is derived from the Sylvin language, as Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf was founded by Trëg̈mtco, who was culturaly Gnoll.

Climate

Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf has a yearly average temperature of 15°C (59°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 3°C (37°F). Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf receives an average of 236 cm/y (92 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf covers an area of nearly 27 km2 (10 mi2), and an average elevation of 2390 m (7841 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf was founded durring the late 14th century in fall of the year 1281, by Trëg̈mtco. The establishment of Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Trëg̈mtco electing to pay people to resettle in Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf.

Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf was built using the conventions of Gnoll durring the late 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf is buildings are arranged arrounded a highly ordered system of crampt cobblestone streets which form triangular paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The city is protected by a renforced stone fence which sits atop earthwork defences, for some of the best inexpencive defences a city of Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf's size could have. Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf's well-designed, yet cheep are in an unremarkable state. To some, this is the ideal sate for defences to be in. In need of absoutly nothing, and ready to serve the city as needed.

A look around Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf gives the unmistakable impression the town is one of the strictest places imaginable. Everyone’s actions are clearly directed by laws they keep in heart and mind at all times. Orderly byond order is a phrase which Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf brings to mind.

Cultural Notes

The law within Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf is highly corrupt, or does not apply to certain favored groups or castes. Strangers might be fleeced by local lawmen, evildoers can be absolved by a payment, and powerful gentry do as they please.

Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is which made use of the classical orders and mathematically precise ratios of height and width combined with a desire for symmetry, proportion, and harmony. It used columns, pediments, arches and domes are imaginatively in buildings of all types. Decorative features were seen as largely unnecessary as the sheer beauty of the structure itself was often close to art. However, many buildings with large ceiling spaces had their ceilings decorated with elaborate paintings, simply because the large flat spaces could feel wasted.

In Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf all of the cats speak in a dead language.

The Raven, Giant near Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Vágenu-mingsor Xluwuf's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves embarking on a group pilgrimage to channel Mysticism energies of tier 2 via singing.
