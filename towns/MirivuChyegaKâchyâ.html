Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ (/ˈvɑkʊ/ /ˈcega/ [eagle] [past]) is a temperate Large City located in Hûche Ûkîma Kingdom, Rûlichyû Îyà Empire, within the Warren.

The name Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ is derived from the Sylvin language, as Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ was founded by Faimes, who was culturaly Rattu.

Climate

Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ has a yearly average temperature of 15°C (59°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a cool 16°C (60°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 14°C (57°F). Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ receives an average of 228 cm/y (89 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the short winter months. Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ covers an area of nearly 58 km2 (23 mi2), and an average elevation of 1812 m (5944 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ was founded durring the early 14th century in summer of the year 1341, by Faimes. The establishment of Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Faimes struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ as a prison colony.

Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ was built using the conventions of Rattu durring the early 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature masoned stone construction which prominantly features pointed arches, pointed ribbed vault cielings, flying buttress', and window tracery all of which share a simmilar gemoetetic patern halfway between organic and inorganic in design formaing a very distinct aesthetically integrated style. BUildings tend to reach for the havens, and more expencive homes are easily identified by their floor count as well as the addition of decorative features intigrated into the building's design such as statues, gargoyals, and embelished joinery.

Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ is is constructed arround a semi-circular crampt packed earth mainstreet, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The city is the proud owner of a properly designed set of renforced walls made from mighty querried stone blocks. Their construction and material choices would make a dwarf weap with joy, for each and every part of the elaborate fortifications are purly functional and robust well byond reason. Even nonexperts can tell the walls are an excelent defencive structure. The exceptionaly well made fortifications have not been wellmaintained over the years, and while functional are in dire need of some loving care and perhapse light renovation.

Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ is a bustling hive of activity. Everyone moves at nothing short of a jog, each convocation is a mile a minute, and there’s dedicated lanes for riding through town in the center of each street. The locals all appear to be not merely occupied, but in a true hurry for everything from drinking a pint of ale to their daily work.

Cultural Notes

Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known for its buildings being predominantly formed through the combination of basic geometric shapes. However, it shown in the detailing given to the structures such as its characteristic tall columns, intricate detail, symmetry, harmony, and balance in their designs to an astonishing degree of precision. Decorative elements for the buildings tended to be built into the structure itself, making great use of fluting, frescoes, inlays, and embossing.

In Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ tiny harmless tornadoes plague the town.

The Magmin near Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves consuming a local narcotic to channel Invocation energies of tier 3 via oratory performances.

Points of Interest

Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ was unknowingly built atop something unstable, and now that substrate is crumbling. It may be swampy ground or a decaying coastline, or it could be an ancient buried city that’s now giving way. In the case of some antique habitation, the denizens that once lived there might be boiling upward as their home is collapsing, or new opportunities may be revealed even as the community’s present structure is ruined.

Mirivu Chyega Kâchyâ makes use of canals for some of its streets. Locals often fish in the canals.
