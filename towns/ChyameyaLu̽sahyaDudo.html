Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo (/ˈs̺elə/ /ˈdudo/ [young] [tear]) is a temperate City located in S̺ûhæ Ràlo Kingdom, S̺âho Chyæmû Empire, within the Warren.

The name Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo is derived from the Sylvin language, as Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo was founded by Cóoīs Sērmp̪f̄r 'Sarina Raven' Ra̋chīs Mpftó̄s Ma̋sh, who was culturaly Rattu.

Climate

Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo has a yearly average temperature of 13°C (55°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 26°C (78°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 1°C (33°F). Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo receives an average of 123 cm/y (48 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo covers an area of nearly 21 km2 (8 mi2), and an average elevation of 4548 m (14921 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo was founded durring the early 12th century in fall of the year 1137, by Cóoīs Sērmp̪f̄r 'Sarina Raven' Ra̋chīs Mpftó̄s Ma̋sh. The establishment of Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Cóoīs Sērmp̪f̄r 'Sarina Raven' Ra̋chīs Mpftó̄s Ma̋sh electing to pay people to resettle in Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo.

Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo was built using the conventions of Rattu durring the early 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo is is constructed arround a series of spacious paverstone mainstreets which form overlapping circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The city rests behind a thick wall made from clay bricks. The wall has all of the proper fortifications and is well made. Unfortuantly the nature of clay brick leaves it quite vulnerable to siege equipment, though the thickness of the wall lends it simmilar resistnace to a thinner hardrock wall. Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo's millitarily questionable fortifications are suffering from significent damage, so much so that examples can be pointed to no matter which section one might have within their line of site, and most of which render sections inoperable at present.

A look around Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo has something terribly wrong with it. It’s impossible to put one’s finger on, but something is horribly wrong. Maybe it’s the way fog blankets the ground, but only in the connors of places. Maybe it’s the vermin scutteling between shadows in the corner of your eyes. Perhaps it’s the overcast sky which seemed to creep out of nowhere, or the distant howling of wolves. Maybe it’s everything together. Regardless, you do not feel it would be wise to remain in Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo long.

Cultural Notes

Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is inspired by the natural world, characterized by sinuous, sculptural, organic shapes, arches, curving lines, and sensual ornamentation. Common motifs included stylized versions of leaves, flowers, vines, insects, animals, and other natural elements. Decorative elements found on the inside and outside of buildings include intricate mosaic work, curved windows, and decorative trim work. .

In Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo tiny harmless tornadoes plague the town.

The Slithering Pit near Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves bloodletting to channel Augury energies of tier 3 via oath swearing.

Points of Interest

Many of Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo’s structures date back to the ancient past and a long-vanished culture. They have unique architectural traits, perhaps being made of some strange substance or with uncanny qualities. The locals find them too useful or too durable to destroy, but the buildings often have unpleasant little surprises in their under-explored corners, and there may be greater structures still buried by long ages beneath Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo’s streets.

Chyameya Lu̽sahya Dudo is known for its unusual rock formations.
