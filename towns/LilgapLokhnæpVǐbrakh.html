Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh (/ˈloxnæp/ /ˈvɪbrax/ [slow] [propaganda]) is a subtropical Large City located in Vf-24xqægdæ Zone, Ěslorvogwin District, within the Alveria.

The name Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh is derived from the Goblin language, as Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh was founded by Yoīnv Vúór 'Hamsy Icemewllo' Cú̄w̄b Ha̋sh Ga̋chidaw̄, who was culturaly KLEO.

Climate

Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh has a yearly average temperature of 31°C (87°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a blistering 36°C (96°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 27°C (80°F). Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh receives an average of 109 cm/y (42 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh covers an area of nearly 204 km2 (81 mi2), and an average elevation of 9086 m (-13139 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh was founded durring the late 14th century in fall of the year 1263, by Yoīnv Vúór 'Hamsy Icemewllo' Cú̄w̄b Ha̋sh Ga̋chidaw̄. The establishment of the new community went well, with no major obsticles durring construction.

Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh was built using the conventions of KLEO durring the late 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh is is constructed arround a semi-circular crampt gravel mainstreet, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The city has a fortified albit thin brick wall. The wall has most of the feeatures of a typical castle wall, just on a much smaller scale and and budget. Notably brick isn't a particuarly soild choice for resisting siege weapons. Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh's wall wouldn't hinder a proper army, but it is more than sufishent for bandits and other small marauding groups. Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh's monster and outlaw focused fortifications are suffering from significent damage, so much so that examples can be pointed to no matter which section one might have within their line of site, and most of which render sections inoperable at present.

A look around Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh shows Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh as a den of corruption. Birbes can be seen changing hands openly, such that it must be customary to do so and must have been for a long time. The locals have no fear, no annoyance at the state of things, it simply is. The city has another layer to it as well. Locals can be overheard having academic discussions, as well as talking about scholarly subjects in general. It’s quite clear Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh places a lot of value on education and being a learned individual, or at least, faking it.

Cultural Notes

Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by vertical proportions, pointed arches, external buttressing, and asymmetry in the general shape of its buildings. The decorative features of the style were key, consisting of large arched windows, pointed arches, vaulted ceilings, flying buttresses, and sculptures integrated into the structure itself. Occasionally, for very important buildings, an array of sculptures or one colossal sculpture might replace the entirety of the entrance to said building.

In Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh snow is warm to the touch and does not melt within city limits. Also it only happens during summer.

The Trollkin near Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves drinking to channel Illusion energies of tier 1 via recitation of scripture.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh was attacked by savage ogress living nearby. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh lost 241 people, 348 livestock, and 77 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 49, when members of Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh's militia enacted an operation to fortify a particular fortification. The operation was complicated by the army's activities draw the wrath of formerly neutral parties. The conflict ended with the defense of the fortification against a siege, which ended in a crushing defeat for Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Lilgap Lokhnæp Vǐbrakh's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
