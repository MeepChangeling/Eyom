Dëog̈ëvî-via Dêtîkè (/ˌdëoˈg̃ëvɪ/ /ˈvia/ [comfortable] [taxi; cab]) is a subtropical Town located in Wildeholt Dutchy, Pîndëpíèkècî Provence, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Dëog̈ëvî-via Dêtîkè is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Dëog̈ëvî-via Dêtîkè was founded by Eluned Trg̈mênm, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Dëog̈ëvî-via Dêtîkè has a yearly average temperature of 23°C (73°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 26°C (78°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 20°C (68°F). Dëog̈ëvî-via Dêtîkè receives an average of 200 cm/y (78 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Dëog̈ëvî-via Dêtîkè covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 4740 m (15551 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Dëog̈ëvî-via Dêtîkè was founded durring the late 12th century in fall of the year 1055, by Eluned Trg̈mênm. The establishment of the new community went well, with no major obsticles durring construction.

Dëog̈ëvî-via Dêtîkè was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the late 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Dëog̈ëvî-via Dêtîkè is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brick construction taking the shape of large simple gemometric shapes which ate then embelished with decorative features, but retain their simple shapes in spite of the added features such as pillars, faccaddes, and covered walkways. The blocky nature gives a general impression of extreem age, as if each structure has existed since time immemorial.

Dëog̈ëvî-via Dêtîkè is buildings folow an organic layout of restrictive packed earth streets whihch gives the town a shape simmilar to a tree, if one views its streets from above. The town lacks any defencive features, though certainly constructing even a simple fence or digging a ditch is at the forefront of the 's mind. At least, one would hope so.

Cultural Notes

Dëog̈ëvî-via Dêtîkè has a long tradition of martial expertise. This may be a crisply-organized history of skilled native levies, or it may be a natural belligerence in the people that leaves them familiar with bloodshed. While their neighbors and liege doubtless respect their talents, this very aptitude might make them more willing to turn to steel than prudence would advise.

Dëog̈ëvî-via Dêtîkè's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by towering round arches, massive stone and brickwork, small windows, thick walls, and a propensity for housing art and sculpture depicting mythological scenes. The building's general shape would be a clever and ascetic combination of geometric shapes, which would be blended together by joining elements. The style's decorative features were largely internal rather than external and incorporated semicircular arches for windows, doors, and arcades; barrel or groin vaults to support the roof of the nave; massive piers and walls, with few windows, to contain the outward thrust of the vaults; side aisles with galleries above them..

In Dëog̈ëvî-via Dêtîkè yeast remains dormant.

The Sylph near Dëog̈ëvî-via Dêtîkè are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Dëog̈ëvî-via Dêtîkè's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves line dance to channel Conjuration energies of tier 1 via oratory performances.
