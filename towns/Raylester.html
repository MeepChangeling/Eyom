Raylester (/raylester / [Translation Unavailable]) is a subtropical Town located in the Gumada Provence of the Kingdom of Hobben.

The name Raylester is derived from the Sylvin language, as Raylester was founded by G̈il Saywell, who was culturaly Sylvin.

Climate

Raylester has a yearly average temperature of 24°C (75°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 30°C (86°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 19°C (66°F). Raylester receives an average of 274 cm/y (107 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Raylester covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 1224 m (4015 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Raylester was founded durring the late 14th century in spring of the year 1298, by G̈il Saywell. The establishment of Raylester was only bairly constructed. The sheer number of problems with its founding were enough to make several of the backers funding Raylester 's construction back out of the project. G̈il Saywell pushed on reguardles, and Raylester was finished, but starts off as a terible place to live.

Raylester was built using the conventions of Sylvin durring the late 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Raylester is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature masoned stone construction which prominantly features pointed arches, pointed ribbed vault cielings, flying buttress', and window tracery all of which share a simmilar gemoetetic patern halfway between organic and inorganic in design formaing a very distinct aesthetically integrated style. BUildings tend to reach for the havens, and more expencive homes are easily identified by their floor count as well as the addition of decorative features intigrated into the building's design such as statues, gargoyals, and embelished joinery.

Raylester is buildings are arranged arround a network of narrow cobblestone streets which form a diagonal shaped grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town sits comfortably behind a palisade wall complete with a timber gatehouse and battlments. Unfortuantly, these timber-based walls are in extreem disrepair, so much so that one cannot tell if they are decaying from a lack of mantance or damage incured.

A look around Raylester makes you wonder how anything ever gets done. The locals have clear, obvious rivalries with one another, as seen through clenched teeth and fake smiles. What’s more, there is a serious lack of any organization and planning in Raylester ’s layout as well as the local’s behaviors. The town seems to be a den of chaos where tongues are forked and local customs do not exist.

Civic Infrastructure

Raylester has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Raylester .

Raylester has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Raylester has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Raylester has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Raylester 's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Raylester possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Raylester has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

Raylester has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

Raylester is led by one or more incompetents. While they must have been very good at something to have acquired the position, they are fundamentally incapable of leading. Uncontrolled passions or lusts, commitment to a hopelessly impractical ideal, pigheaded obstinacy in the face of failure, a total lack of charisma or interpersonal skills, or profound laziness might all unfit them for their post.

Raylester 's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by order, symmetry, formal design, grandiosity, and elaborate ornamentation. Architectural characteristics include balustrades, balconies, columns, cornices, pilasters, and triangular pediments. Stone exteriors are massive and grandiose in their symmetry; interiors are typically polished and lavishly decorated with sculptures, swags, medallions, flowers, and shields. Interiors will often have a grand stairway and opulent ballroom..

Due to the actions of local Kami, winter is recurring in Raylester .

The Thanatos Fey near Raylester are known to be quite timid.

Raylester 's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves consuming a local narcotic to channel Enchantment energies of tier 3 via mimery.
