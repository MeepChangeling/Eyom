Bōk-hō Vrët (/boːk/ /hoː/ [government] [back (of body); back (reverse side)]) is a subtropical Large Town located in Wiwöchya Parish, Jizanuta Diocese, within the Covenent of Irus.

The name Bōk-hō Vrët is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as Bōk-hō Vrët was founded by Dheingu Berdra, who was culturaly Wood Elven.

Climate

Bōk-hō Vrët has a yearly average temperature of 19°C (66°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 11°C (51°F). Bōk-hō Vrët receives an average of 243 cm/y (95 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Bōk-hō Vrët covers an area of nearly 7 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 5504 m (18057 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Bōk-hō Vrët was founded durring the early 12th century in summer of the year 1145, by Dheingu Berdra. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Dheingu Berdra.

Bōk-hō Vrët was built using the conventions of Wood Elven durring the early 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Bōk-hō Vrët is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Bōk-hō Vrët is buildings are grouped arround an odd layout of premissive flagstone streets, which seems to be based on an overlapping squair patern such that there are small squares at the cornor of every bigger square. Sometimes buildings exist in the smaller squaires, other times they are open spaces, or occupied by temporary structures. The town is protected by a well-crafted cobblestone fence tall enough to provide adiquite cover for defenders to fire from, but no more than that. Bōk-hō Vrët's frontieer-style defences has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

A look around Bōk-hō Vrët seems to be home to a quite vibrant and boisterous community. Everywhere one looks they can see people going out their daily business with a smile and a spring in their step. Children play loudly in the streets, causing untold havoc as youth are want and allowed to do.

Civic Infrastructure

Bōk-hō Vrët has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Bōk-hō Vrët. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Bōk-hō Vrët's parks.

Bōk-hō Vrët has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Bōk-hō Vrët.

Bōk-hō Vrët has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Bōk-hō Vrët has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Bōk-hō Vrët has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Bōk-hō Vrët has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Bōk-hō Vrët has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Bōk-hō Vrët's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Bōk-hō Vrët possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Large Town. Bōk-hō Vrët's grid is powered by an arcane means.

Bōk-hō Vrët's old civil lighting system was converted to Galvanic Lamps recently, and expanded to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Bōk-hō Vrët has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Bōk-hō Vrët's natural decorations nor waterways.

Bōk-hō Vrët has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Bōk-hō Vrët has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Bōk-hō Vrët has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Bōk-hō Vrët's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is based upon new and innovative technologies of construction and the idea that form should follow function. It was an embrace of minimalism and a rejection of ornament. The style became characterized by an emphasis on volume, asymmetrical compositions, and minimal ornamentation..

In Bōk-hō Vrët tiny harmless tornadoes plague the town.

The Kamadan near Bōk-hō Vrët are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Bōk-hō Vrët's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves performance art to channel Enchantment energies of tier 2 via recitation of poetic epics.

Points of Interest

Bōk-hō Vrët produces some wonderful cultural artifact or trains famous artists. The product might be some exceptional cloth, or artistic luxury good, or the scholarly fruits of a famous academy. Trained artists might be students of a particular school, or the apprentices of the current masters of a long artistic tradition who dwell here.

Bōk-hō Vrët's roads were poorly made when first laid. Rather than repairing them correctly, a series of new roads was laid atop the old, leading to the streets of modern Bōk-hō Vrët suffering from potholes, cracking, and even sinkholes. The locals often repair the road by putting down wooden decking.
