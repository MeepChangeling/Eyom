Fìkh Nof Chyeb (/nof/ /ceb/ [favorite] [semen]) is a subtropical Large Town located in Jĭăwuwizzi Zone, Berěbudbǐb District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Fìkh Nof Chyeb is derived from the Constructi language, as Fìkh Nof Chyeb was founded by Brilêd Sheshêm, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Fìkh Nof Chyeb has a yearly average temperature of 16°C (60°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 8°C (46°F). Fìkh Nof Chyeb receives an average of 255 cm/y (100 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Fìkh Nof Chyeb covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 8480 m (-15128 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Fìkh Nof Chyeb was founded durring the early 11th century, by Brilêd Sheshêm. The establishment of Fìkh Nof Chyeb suffered from several major issues, resulting in the need to develop many solutions to basic problems. Problems such as a lack of fresh water, logistical support, poor quality tools, and the odd monster or two. Howeaver, these were overcome in time.

Fìkh Nof Chyeb was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the early 11th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Fìkh Nof Chyeb is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Fìkh Nof Chyeb is buildings are arranged arrounded a highly ordered system of premissive cobblestone streets which form triangular paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The town is the proud owner of a proper castle-style stone wall complete with all of the trimmings. It has towers, a moat, gatehouses, drawbridges, and even merticulationsshortsizeleadershipname.. Unfortuantly, these perhapse unnessisarily well built defences are in extreem disrepair, so much so that one cannot tell if they are decaying from a lack of mantance or damage incured.

Civic Infrastructure

Fìkh Nof Chyeb has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Fìkh Nof Chyeb. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Fìkh Nof Chyeb's parks.

Fìkh Nof Chyeb has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Fìkh Nof Chyeb.

Fìkh Nof Chyeb has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Fìkh Nof Chyeb has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Fìkh Nof Chyeb has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Fìkh Nof Chyeb's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Fìkh Nof Chyeb has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Fìkh Nof Chyeb's natural decorations nor waterways.

Fìkh Nof Chyeb has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Fìkh Nof Chyeb has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Fìkh Nof Chyeb's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used has a sleek, linear appearance with stylized, often geometric ornamentation. The primary facade of its buildings often featured a series of set backs that create a stepped outline. Low-relief decorative panels can be found at entrances, around windows, along roof edges or as string courses. It was best known for its use of smooth finish building materials such as stucco, concrete block, glazed brick or mosaic tile. Decorative details can incorporate various artistic or exotic motifs to suit the building's function or the architect's whim. Chevrons, zigzags, and other geometrical motifs are common forms of ornament.

In Fìkh Nof Chyeb there is no wind.

The Eel Hound near Fìkh Nof Chyeb are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Fìkh Nof Chyeb's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves long periods of drunkenness to channel Invocation energies of tier 3 via throat singing.

History

Fìkh Nof Chyeb is in secret rebellion against their ostensible liege, having cut deals with his enemies, plotted to betray him for their own gain, or bridled under his tyranny and sought a better lord. The community’s leadership is all in on this plot, and outside viceroys or representatives are being kept carefully ignorant of the reality. The common folk may be oblivious to the truth, though they’ll doubtless have felt the same motivations and promptings that convinced their leaders to turn traitor.

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Fìkh Nof Chyeb was attacked by savage tribes living nearby Fìkh Nof Chyeb. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Fìkh Nof Chyeb lost 185 people, 156 livestock, and 20 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 105, when members of Fìkh Nof Chyeb's militia enacted an operation to locate a particular special forces group. The operation was complicated by aggravated civilians, who cause problems for the militia for a host of reasons. The conflict ended with pitched battle between both forces, which ended in a stalemate for Fìkh Nof Chyeb's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Fìkh Nof Chyeb's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
