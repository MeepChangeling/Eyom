Eg Rÿssh Hewvlåy Hånblolz (/ris̼/ /huːˈvlɑːʤ hɑːnˈblolz/ [spy] [pavement]) is a subtropical Large Town located in Hievíhzkin County, Lälhulål Kingdom, within the United Kingdom of Undermountain.

The name Eg Rÿssh Hewvlåy Hånblolz is derived from the Dwarven language, as Eg Rÿssh Hewvlåy Hånblolz was founded by Owen Cetrÿnequg, who was culturaly Dwarvern.

Climate

Eg Rÿssh Hewvlåy Hånblolz has a yearly average temperature of 15°C (59°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 6°C (42°F). Eg Rÿssh Hewvlåy Hånblolz receives an average of 273 cm/y (107 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Eg Rÿssh Hewvlåy Hånblolz covers an area of nearly 7 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 6696 m (-20981 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Eg Rÿssh Hewvlåy Hånblolz was founded durring the early 11th century, by Owen Cetrÿnequg. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Owen Cetrÿnequg.

Eg Rÿssh Hewvlåy Hånblolz was built using the conventions of Dwarvern durring the early 11th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Eg Rÿssh Hewvlåy Hånblolz is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Eg Rÿssh Hewvlåy Hånblolz is buildings are arranged arround a single narrow flagstone mainstreet with many smaller streets branching off of it which gives the town a over all rectangular shape, albit one warped and twisted by the nature of the curves of the main road. The town sits comfortably behind a palisade wall complete with a timber gatehouse and battlments. Astonishigly, the timber-based walls are in pristine condishion, as if they had just been finished before you laied eyes upon them.

A look around Eg Rÿssh Hewvlåy Hånblolz has something terribly wrong with it. It’s impossible to put one’s finger on, but something is horribly wrong. Maybe it’s the way fog blankets the ground, but only in the connors of places. Maybe it’s the vermin scuttling between shadows in the corner of your eyes. Perhaps it’s the overcast sky which seemed to creep out of nowhere, or the distant howling of wolves. Maybe it’s all of those things together, or perhaps it's the way these elements combine which makes you worry someone might stab you in a dark ally for your boots. It’s not filthy, or dark, but the smiles seem strained, the locals seem to glare daggers in eachothers backs a little too much, and everyone is armed at all times. You may want to keep an eye on your valuables, and make sure you don’t wind up in any position of power. Regardless, you do not feel it would be wise to remain in Eg Rÿssh Hewvlåy Hånblolz long.

Eg Rÿssh Hewvlåy Hånblolz's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by their massive, monolithic and 'blocky' appearance with a rigid geometric style. It was best known for its rough, unfinished surfaces, unusual shapes, heavy-looking materials, straight lines, and small windows. Modular elements were often used to form masses representing specific functional zones, grouped into a unified whole.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century Lake Arethusa began to boil, and released a thick toxic cloud from beneath its waters which was isolated to the area around Eg Rÿssh Hewvlåy Hånblolz, which was swallowed by the fumes for several days. Eg Rÿssh Hewvlåy Hånblolz lost 220 people, 354 livestock, and 72 buildings in the disaster, though it is of note the local plant life flourished after the disaster. The disaster is referred to as the Doom Wind.
