Odíhg Kvāokuguc (Kvāo /ˈkuguc/ [tornado] [heat]) is a subtropical Town located in the Ftiyāobāt Region of the Tetbur Commune.

The name Odíhg Kvāokuguc is derived from the Tauric language, as Odíhg Kvāokuguc was founded by Neotela Beddoes Powelyn, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Odíhg Kvāokuguc has a yearly average temperature of 29°C (84°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 33°C (91°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 26°C (78°F). Odíhg Kvāokuguc receives an average of 215 cm/y (84 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Odíhg Kvāokuguc covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 3750 m (12303 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Odíhg Kvāokuguc was founded durring the early 19th century in late fall of the year 1800, by Neotela Beddoes Powelyn. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Neotela Beddoes Powelyn.

Odíhg Kvāokuguc was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the early 19th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Odíhg Kvāokuguc is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Odíhg Kvāokuguc is is constructed arround a series of crampt flagstone mainstreets which form concentric circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to each other at varrious points. The town rests behind the absurdity that is a thick, timber braced, wall made of clay bricks. While visualy impressive and certainly an astetic, Odíhg Kvāokuguc's wall provides no actual defence against siege equipment due to the choice of its cosntruction materials. Even nonexperts can tell the town is trying to impress rather than defend with its walls, towers, and gatehouses. Though admittedly, they do look nice... To primitive tribals who have never seen fortifications before. Odíhg Kvāokuguc's political statment focused walls has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

Odíhg Kvāokuguc is, in a word, disorder. People seem to be allowed to do as they please with little harmoney to anything. It feels less like a town, and more like a spot people just happened to place their homes. Yet there are small elements here and there which show the underlying structure of the community. It’s just so complex, organic, and flowing one can only understand what is a piece of the puzzle, but not what its neighbors are.

Cultural Notes

Odíhg Kvāokuguc's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by order, symmetry, formal design, grandiosity, and elaborate ornamentation. Architectural characteristics include balustrades, balconies, columns, cornices, pilasters, and triangular pediments. Stone exteriors are massive and grandiose in their symmetry; interiors are typically polished and lavishly decorated with sculptures, swags, medallions, flowers, and shields. Interiors will often have a grand stairway and opulent ballroom..

In Odíhg Kvāokuguc sometimes, near one of the seasonal solstices, the sun appears to split into three and it rains from a clear sky for several hours.

The Guardian, Graven near Odíhg Kvāokuguc are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Odíhg Kvāokuguc's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves orgies to channel Charm energies of tier 1 via speaking in tongues.
