Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè (/ˈdèke/ /kɘˈdëbɘ/ [elegant] [alphabet]) is a subtropical Large Town located in the Pibíêtbësè Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè was founded by Èqth Sheshe, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè has a yearly average temperature of 21°C (69°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 19°C (66°F). Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè receives an average of 204 cm/y (80 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 4866 m (15964 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè was founded durring the late 14th century in fall of the year 1271, by Èqth Sheshe. The establishment of Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Èqth Sheshe struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè as a prison colony.

Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the late 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature delicute timber framework hidden behind layer upon layer of finly ground plaster bleached to an almost glossy white sheen, with green clay tiled roofs and decorative brass-leafed trim. Even the smallest, poorest looking structures appear to be expencive thanks to the extreem elegence of the organic shapes and paterns going into their lofty, spire-y, vagly gothic designs. The more well off folks live in identicle homes, save for even shiner trim and a more whimsical appearance to their structures flowing forms.

Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè is buildings folow an organic layout of spacious carved bedrock streets whihch gives the town a shape simmilar to a tree, if one views its streets from above. The town rests behind a thick wall made from clay bricks. The wall has all of the proper fortifications and is well made. Unfortuantly the nature of clay brick leaves it quite vulnerable to siege equipment, though the thickness of the wall lends it simmilar resistnace to a thinner hardrock wall. Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè's millitarily questionable fortifications are in an unremarkable state. To some, this is the ideal sate for defences to be in. In need of absoutly nothing, and ready to serve the town as needed.

Right off the bat Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè hits you in the face with its success. Everyone, even the peasants, are dressed in well made clothing. Every tool and implement you can see is finely made, and people will boast to you as obvious strangers of the wonders which can be found in their markets. More interestingly is a total lack of beggars, and plenty of new buildings are going up even as you speak. Somehow this town has come into quite a lot of wealth, and recently from the looks of things. The influx of wealth has brought with it an influx of relaxation. Everywhere you look people are enjoying their prosperity in a slow, casual, and deliberate manner. There’s no rush for anything anywhere you look.

Civic Infrastructure

Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè's parks.

Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè.

Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Large Town. Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè's grid is powered by an arcane means.

Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè's natural decorations nor waterways.

Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè's locals despise outsiders. For some “outsiders” may be natives of foreign lands, while others might have a grudge against anyone from outside the community. These locals have an active loathing, and the outsiders allowed to trade or interact with them do so at a heavy disadvantage.

Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is which made use of the classical orders and mathematically precise ratios of height and width combined with a desire for symmetry, proportion, and harmony. It used columns, pediments, arches and domes are imaginatively in buildings of all types. Decorative features were seen as largely unnecessary as the sheer beauty of the structure itself was often close to art. However, many buildings with large ceiling spaces had their ceilings decorated with elaborate paintings, simply because the large flat spaces could feel wasted.

Due to the actions of local Kami, summer is short in Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè.

The Herd Animal, Elk near Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves orgies to channel Charm energies of tier 3 via oath swearing.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè experienced an impossibly long, cold, and stormy winter unlike anything seen before. The worst of the disaster struck The storm coated everything in a thick layer of ice and freezing countless animals, plants, and people to death. Dèke-kêdëbê Dêtîkè lost 228 people, and 216 livestock in the disaster.. The disaster is remembered as the Doom Screams, after the sounds of the winds.
