Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà (/ˈbəze/ /ˈrædʊ/ [pregnant] [police]) is a subtropical Town located in Athide Zone, Chyûyu̽ Lu̹sû Empire, within the Warrenese Shogunate.

The name Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà is derived from the Wareneese language, as Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà was founded by Szizder Glkê, who was culturaly Wareneese.

Climate

Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà has a yearly average temperature of 10°C (50°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing -3°C (27°F). Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà receives an average of 287 cm/y (112 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 6496 m (21312 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà was founded durring the early 14th century in winter of the year 1339, by Szizder Glkê. The establishment of the new community went well, with no major obsticles durring construction.

Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà was built using the conventions of Wareneese durring the early 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà is buildings are arranged arrounded highly ordered system of broad canals streets which form hexical paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The town rests behind a thick wall made from clay bricks. The wall has all of the proper fortifications and is well made. Unfortuantly the nature of clay brick leaves it quite vulnerable to siege equipment, though the thickness of the wall lends it simmilar resistnace to a thinner hardrock wall. Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà 's millitarily questionable fortifications are suffering from significent damage, so much so that examples can be pointed to no matter which section one might have within their line of site, and most of which render sections inoperable at present.

Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. The locals seem to have responded to their slow downfall by recreating Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà as one of the strictest places imaginable. Everyone’s actions are clearly directed by laws they keep in heart and mind at all times. Orderly byond order is a phrase which the town brings to mind.

Cultural Notes

The locals are convinced that there is some terrible threat against them working from within their society. It may be a matter of dark sorcerers, foreign spies, traitorous neighbors, shape shifting monsters, or some other hidden evil. This evil may be a recent fear, or it may be an inherited peril they’ve always had to guard against. The danger itself may or may not exist, or if it exists it may not justify the steps being taken.

Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà 's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is admittedly strange and non-linear style rooted in defiance of symmetrical shapes. It championed the creation of buildings with a unique visual appearance. the structural norms of classic buildings and deforms or moves away from elementary architectural principles. By including non-linear designs processed into its buildings and favoring fragmentation, this style expressed a form of controlled chaos. Its buildings appear out-of-the-ordinary, draw the eye in immediately and sometimes create a feeling of strangeness. These distorted shapes and structure are not reserved to the building’s outer facade, they destabilize interior elements too, favoring minimalism and play on people’s perceptions by injecting a futuristic touch.

Due to the actions of local Kami, autumn is skipped in Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà .

The Amoeba, Giant near Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà are known to be quite timid.

Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà 's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves orgies to channel Mysticism energies of tier 2 via chanting.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century several years of heavy rains culminated in a peculiar disaster. A great hole opened up beneath Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà , swallowing large sections of the community. Lu̽sahya Rædû Kîchyà lost 137 people, 355 livestock, and 33 buildings in the disaster. This disaster is often refereed to as The Suffering Swallowing.
