Odíhg Ftopsvgíhg (Ftopsv /gɪ̞g/ [loss] [engine; motor]) is a subtropical Town located in the Kririsi̊mù Region of the Tetbur Commune.

The name Odíhg Ftopsvgíhg is derived from the Tauric language, as Odíhg Ftopsvgíhg was founded by Glêrteg̈ Daffey, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Odíhg Ftopsvgíhg has a yearly average temperature of 29°C (84°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 32°C (89°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 26°C (78°F). Odíhg Ftopsvgíhg receives an average of 148 cm/y (58 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Odíhg Ftopsvgíhg covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 4610 m (15124 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Odíhg Ftopsvgíhg was founded durring the early 15th century in summer of the year 1426, by Glêrteg̈ Daffey. The establishment of Odíhg Ftopsvgíhg suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Odíhg Ftopsvgíhg which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Odíhg Ftopsvgíhg was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the early 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Odíhg Ftopsvgíhg is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature masoned stone construction which prominantly features pointed arches, pointed ribbed vault cielings, flying buttress', and window tracery all of which share a simmilar gemoetetic patern halfway between organic and inorganic in design formaing a very distinct aesthetically integrated style. BUildings tend to reach for the havens, and more expencive homes are easily identified by their floor count as well as the addition of decorative features intigrated into the building's design such as statues, gargoyals, and embelished joinery.

Odíhg Ftopsvgíhg is buildings are arranged arround a network of spacious paverstone streets which form a diagonal shaped grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town has a fortified albit thin brick wall. The wall has most of the feeatures of a typical castle wall, just on a much smaller scale and and budget. Notably brick isn't a particuarly soild choice for resisting siege weapons. Odíhg Ftopsvgíhg's wall wouldn't hinder a proper army, but it is more than sufishent for bandits and other small marauding groups. Odíhg Ftopsvgíhg's monster and outlaw focused fortifications are suffering from significent damage, so much so that examples can be pointed to no matter which section one might have within their line of site, and most of which render sections inoperable at present.

Cultural Notes

Odíhg Ftopsvgíhg's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for the combined use of arcuated and trabeated construction, employing arches and constructed with post and lintel. The arch served as the style's chief structural element, with flanking columns serving as buttresses or decorations. While the general shape of their structures could be generously described as simplistic, the use of these arches and columns created a most distinct ascetic which is easily distinguishable on sight to armatures and experts alike.

Due to the actions of local Kami, winter is long in Odíhg Ftopsvgíhg.

The Crawling Hand near Odíhg Ftopsvgíhg are known to be quite timid.

Odíhg Ftopsvgíhg's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves long periods of drunkenness to channel Conjuration energies of tier 2 via proclamations.
