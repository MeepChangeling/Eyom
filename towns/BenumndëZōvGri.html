Benumndë Zōv Gri (/beˈnumndɛ/ /zoːv/ [dead] [screen; curtain; veil; umbrella]) is a subtropical City located in Vuquzikoti Parish, Verugepeoma Diocese, within the Irus.

The name Benumndë Zōv Gri is derived from the Sylvin language, as Benumndë Zōv Gri was founded by Firnipu, who was culturaly Wood Elf.

Climate

Benumndë Zōv Gri has a yearly average temperature of 20°C (68°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 16°C (60°F). Benumndë Zōv Gri receives an average of 69 cm/y (27 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Benumndë Zōv Gri covers an area of nearly 36 km2 (14 mi2), and an average elevation of 7930 m (-16932 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Benumndë Zōv Gri was founded durring the early 14th century in fall of the year 1339, by Firnipu. The establishment of Benumndë Zōv Gri was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Firnipu electing to pay people to resettle in Benumndë Zōv Gri.

Benumndë Zōv Gri was built using the conventions of Wood Elf durring the early 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Benumndë Zōv Gri is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Benumndë Zōv Gri is buildings are arranged arround a network of premissive cobblestone streets which form a diamond shaped grid, where each diamond verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller diamond has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The city is the proud owner of a properly designed set of renforced walls made from mighty querried stone blocks. Their construction and material choices would make a dwarf weap with joy, for each and every part of the elaborate fortifications are purly functional and robust well byond reason. Even nonexperts can tell the walls are an excelent defencive structure. Benumndë Zōv Gri's exceptionaly well made fortifications are in an unremarkable state. To some, this is the ideal sate for defences to be in. In need of absoutly nothing, and ready to serve the city as needed.

Benumndë Zōv Gri seems to be abandoned at first. There are people present, working their trades and going about their business like any community, it’s just they are absurdly quiet. Just as you start to think there might be trouble, you see most of them are reading. The entire city feels like a library, with those not reading remaining quite to not disturb those who have their nose in a book. The longer you remain in Benumndë Zōv Gri the more you start to wonder if you’ve entered some strange open-concept University rather than a city, but you can’t quite say such with certainty.

Benumndë Zōv Gri has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Benumndë Zōv Gri. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Benumndë Zōv Gri's parks.

Benumndë Zōv Gri has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Benumndë Zōv Gri.

Benumndë Zōv Gri has a Guild of Nurses, which is tasked with caring for the elderly and infirm in accordance with local ordinances, religious values, and customs.

Benumndë Zōv Gri has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Benumndë Zōv Gri has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Benumndë Zōv Gri has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Benumndë Zōv Gri has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Benumndë Zōv Gri's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Benumndë Zōv Gri has an Administrative Academy which trains individuals in the administrative arts.

Benumndë Zōv Gri possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the City. Benumndë Zōv Gri's grid is powered by a god's will and kindness.

Benumndë Zōv Gri possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. In spite of the Galvanic Grid, these lights continue to use their old fule sources to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Benumndë Zōv Gri has a library, which keeps a large collection of books, scrolls, and archives all manner of physical items. While not open to the public, the librarians and scholars employed by the library will assist anyone with their research needs, and wealthy individuals can purchase membership to access the library's materials themselves. In spite of being generally closed to the public, the library has a room with several Aether Linked devices available to the public during business hours.

Benumndë Zōv Gri has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Benumndë Zōv Gri's natural decorations nor waterways.

Benumndë Zōv Gri has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Benumndë Zōv Gri has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Benumndë Zōv Gri has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Benumndë Zōv Gri's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is famous for its stately symmetry, classical elements, and grand appearance. Columns and pillars, such as Corinthian columns, are often seen supporting open structures or porticos. Symmetry is an important feature of this style, with each half of a building mirroring the other. Domed ceilings and windows grace these buildings, with everything placed in a mathematical arrangement.

In Benumndë Zōv Gri leaves and flower petals never touch the ground.

The Kamadan near Benumndë Zōv Gri are known to be quite timid.

Benumndë Zōv Gri's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves ritual combat to channel Wild Magic energies of tier 2 via throat singing.

History

Benumndë Zōv Gri used to be more prosperous, but something happened relatively long ago that left it a shrunken shadow of its former self. If the settlement is prosperous, the locals often lament how much more they could have had. If the settlement is not prosperous, the locals blame their ill fortunes on that event. Reminders of this better time can be found in many places within Benumndë Zōv Gri.
