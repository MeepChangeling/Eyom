Gu̽sa Hu̹yâ Kîchyà (/ˈbu̹so/ /ˈhu̹jɑ/ [war] [piece; item]) is a subtropical Large Town located in Z̄ububuz̄udenuku Zone, Chyûyu̽ Lu̹sû Empire, within the Warrenese Shogunate.

The name Gu̽sa Hu̹yâ Kîchyà is derived from the Wareneese language, as Gu̽sa Hu̹yâ Kîchyà was founded by Bubamias Zlkartesí, who was culturaly Wareneese.

Climate

Gu̽sa Hu̹yâ Kîchyà has a yearly average temperature of 12°C (53°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing 0°C (32°F). Gu̽sa Hu̹yâ Kîchyà receives an average of 297 cm/y (116 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Gu̽sa Hu̹yâ Kîchyà covers an area of nearly 7 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 4836 m (15866 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Gu̽sa Hu̹yâ Kîchyà was founded durring the late 16th century in fall of the year 1466, by Bubamias Zlkartesí. The establishment of the new community went well, with no major obsticles durring construction.

Gu̽sa Hu̹yâ Kîchyà was built using the conventions of Wareneese durring the late 16th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Gu̽sa Hu̹yâ Kîchyà is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature masoned stone construction which prominantly features pointed arches, pointed ribbed vault cielings, flying buttress', and window tracery all of which share a simmilar gemoetetic patern halfway between organic and inorganic in design formaing a very distinct aesthetically integrated style. BUildings tend to reach for the havens, and more expencive homes are easily identified by their floor count as well as the addition of decorative features intigrated into the building's design such as statues, gargoyals, and embelished joinery.

Gu̽sa Hu̹yâ Kîchyà is is constructed arround a series of spacious flagstone mainstreets which form concentric circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to each other at varrious points. The town has a fortified albit thin brick wall. The wall has most of the feeatures of a typical castle wall, just on a much smaller scale and and budget. Notably brick isn't a particuarly soild choice for resisting siege weapons. Gu̽sa Hu̹yâ Kîchyà 's wall wouldn't hinder a proper army, but it is more than sufishent for bandits and other small marauding groups. The monster and outlaw focused fortifications has suffered a visible ammount of structural damage, leaving them effectivly useless. One can't help but wonder why the has not yet effected repairs.

The town shows nothing out of the ordinary, at first glance. Then you start to notice all the small things. Each window has seven iron nails pounded into the sill. Every door has a sprig of holly overhead. Every well is branded with purity seals and runes to ward the contents. Everyone is carrying a small charm for safety or fortune, be it a rabbit’s foot, a horseshoe, or other little totems.

Cultural Notes

The law within Gu̽sa Hu̹yâ Kîchyà is highly corrupt, or does not apply to certain favored groups or castes. Strangers might be fleeced by local lawmen, evildoers can be absolved by a payment, and powerful gentry do as they please.

Gu̽sa Hu̹yâ Kîchyà 's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for its functional shapes, abstract shapes used sparingly for decor, simple color schemes, holistic design, and basic industrial materials. Its simple designs were created to be beautiful, functional, and mass-producible. The style used little to no embellishment or ornamentation, instead drawing attention to the streamlined design, such as flat roofs to create a simple, geometric look. The simplicity masks the style's nearly sinister functionality, as every last feature is designed to guide the people living in the building in how to make the most efficient use of the structure.

In Gu̽sa Hu̹yâ Kîchyà there is no wind.

The Hungry Flesh near Gu̽sa Hu̹yâ Kîchyà are known to be quite timid.

Gu̽sa Hu̹yâ Kîchyà 's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves creating small tokens to channel Mysticism energies of tier 2 via moments of science.
