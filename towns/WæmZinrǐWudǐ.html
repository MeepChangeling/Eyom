Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ (/ˈzinrɪ/ /ˈwudɪ/ [handsome] [atmosphere; mood]) is a subtropical Town located in Tranjihakaba Parish, Tuksksungziziqi District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ is derived from the Constructi language, as Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ was founded by Grounoki Tresmèqtho, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ has a yearly average temperature of 31°C (87°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a blistering 36°C (96°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 27°C (80°F). Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ receives an average of 103 cm/y (40 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 9864 m (-10587 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ was founded durring the early 12th century in spring of the year 1127, by Grounoki Tresmèqtho. The establishment of Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ was only bairly constructed. The sheer number of problems with its founding were enough to make several of the backers funding Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ's construction back out of the project. Grounoki Tresmèqtho pushed on reguardles, and Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ was finished, but starts off as a terible place to live.

Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the early 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ is buildings are arranged arround a network of narrow split-log ties streets which form a diagonal shaped grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town rests behind a thick wall made from clay bricks. The wall has all of the proper fortifications and is well made. Unfortuantly the nature of clay brick leaves it quite vulnerable to siege equipment, though the thickness of the wall lends it simmilar resistnace to a thinner hardrock wall. The town's millitarily questionable fortifications are visibly old, but also obviously well maintained. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with routine mantance of the town's defences.

A look around Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ gives the unmistakable impression the town is one of the strictest places imaginable. Everyone’s actions are clearly directed by laws they keep in heart and mind at all times. Orderly byond order is a phrase which Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ brings to mind.

Cultural Notes

Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by order, symmetry, formal design, grandiosity, and elaborate ornamentation. Architectural characteristics include balustrades, balconies, columns, cornices, pilasters, and triangular pediments. Stone exteriors are massive and grandiose in their symmetry; interiors are typically polished and lavishly decorated with sculptures, swags, medallions, flowers, and shields. Interiors will often have a grand stairway and opulent ballroom..

Due to the actions of local Kami, autumn is skipped in Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ.

The Caypup near Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Wæm Zinrǐ Wudǐ's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves embarking on a group pilgrimage to channel Necromancy energies of tier 2 via mimery.
