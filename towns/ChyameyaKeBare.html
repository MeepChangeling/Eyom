Chyameya Ke Bare (/ke/ /ˈbare/ [peach] [religion]) is a temperate Large City located in Lochubà Hûyæ Kingdom, Mî Kûrà Empire, within the Warren.

The name Chyameya Ke Bare is derived from the Sylvin language, as Chyameya Ke Bare was founded by Cecil, who was culturaly Rattu.

Climate

Chyameya Ke Bare has a yearly average temperature of 11°C (51°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 26°C (78°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing -3°C (27°F). Chyameya Ke Bare receives an average of 92 cm/y (36 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Chyameya Ke Bare covers an area of nearly 83 km2 (33 mi2), and an average elevation of 3572 m (11719 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Chyameya Ke Bare was founded durring the late 16th century in spring of the year 1488, by Cecil. The establishment of Chyameya Ke Bare was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Cecil struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Chyameya Ke Bare as a prison colony.

Chyameya Ke Bare was built using the conventions of Rattu durring the late 16th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Chyameya Ke Bare is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Chyameya Ke Bare is is constructed arround a series of crampt gravel mainstreets which form concentric circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to each other at varrious points. The city is the proud owner of a proper castle-style stone wall complete with all of the trimmings. It has towers, a moat, gatehouses, drawbridges, and even merticulationsshortsizeleadershipname.. Chyameya Ke Bare's perhapse unnessisarily well built defences are suffering from significent damage, so much so that examples can be pointed to no matter which section one might have within their line of site, and most of which render sections inoperable at present.

Right off the bat Chyameya Ke Bare hits you in the face with its success. Everyone, even the peasants, are dressed in well made clothing. Every tool and implement you can see is finely made, and people will boast to you as obvious strangers of the wonders which can be found in their markets. More interestingly is a total lack of beggars, and plenty of new buildings are going up even as you speak. Somehow this city has come into quite a lot of wealth, and recently from the looks of things. The city is very clearly a joyfull place as well as wealthy. Music can be heard often, as well as laughter. The smell of food and drink permiates the air. You can’t help but smile.

Civic Infrastructure

Chyameya Ke Bare possesses a Aethary Link for its accademic, government, and financial institutions. Public Aethary access is available through one or more of these intitutions.

Chyameya Ke Bare has an animal control department which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the control, impoundment, and disposition of animals.

Chyameya Ke Bare has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Chyameya Ke Bare. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Chyameya Ke Bare's parks.

Chyameya Ke Bare has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Chyameya Ke Bare.

Chyameya Ke Bare has a government-funded child care program, overseen by the local Department of Nursemaids, which is responsible for providing childcare to working-class citizens according to local ordinances.

Chyameya Ke Bare has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Chyameya Ke Bare has a Guild of Nurses, which is tasked with caring for the elderly and infirm in accordance with local ordinances, religious values, and customs.

Chyameya Ke Bare has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Chyameya Ke Bare has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Chyameya Ke Bare has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Chyameya Ke Bare has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Chyameya Ke Bare's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Chyameya Ke Bare has an Administrative Academy which trains individuals in the administrative arts.

Chyameya Ke Bare possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Large City. Chyameya Ke Bare's grid is powered by a boiler and turbine based power plant.

Chyameya Ke Bare's old civil lighting system was converted to Galvanic Lamps recently, and expanded to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Chyameya Ke Bare has a first rate hospital which caters to anyone in need of long term medical care.

Chyameya Ke Bare has a library, which keeps a large collection of books, scrolls, and archives all manner of physical items. While not open to the public, the librarians and scholars employed by the library will assist anyone with their research needs, and wealthy individuals can purchase membership to access the library's materials themselves. In spite of being generally closed to the public, the library has a room with several Aether Linked devices available to the public during business hours.

Chyameya Ke Bare has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Chyameya Ke Bare's natural decorations nor waterways.

Chyameya Ke Bare has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Chyameya Ke Bare has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Chyameya Ke Bare has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Chyameya Ke Bare's locals despise outsiders. For some “outsiders” may be natives of foreign lands, while others might have a grudge against anyone from outside the community. These locals have an active loathing, and the outsiders allowed to trade or interact with them do so at a heavy disadvantage.

Chyameya Ke Bare's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by vertical proportions, pointed arches, external buttressing, and asymmetry in the general shape of its buildings. The decorative features of the style were key, consisting of large arched windows, pointed arches, vaulted ceilings, flying buttresses, and sculptures integrated into the structure itself. Occasionally, for very important buildings, an array of sculptures or one colossal sculpture might replace the entirety of the entrance to said building.

In Chyameya Ke Bare the water is caffeinated.

The Shredskin near Chyameya Ke Bare are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Chyameya Ke Bare's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves creating small tokens to channel Augury energies of tier 2 via speaking in tongues.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century Chyameya Ke Bare was struck by a devistating earthquake. The quake brought ruin to Chyameya Ke Bare, which lost 183 people, 188 livestock, and 65 buildings in the earthquake. The day of the quake is remembered by many as Doom's Day.
