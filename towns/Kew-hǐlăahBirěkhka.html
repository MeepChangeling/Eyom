Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka (/kew/ /hɪˈlə̝ah/ [trial] [moisture]) is a subtropical Large Town located in Rirasakho Parish, Wækwìilbu District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka is derived from the Constructi language, as Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka was founded by Bellyborer Beetles Gareg̈me, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka has a yearly average temperature of 29°C (84°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a blistering 34°C (93°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 25°C (77°F). Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka receives an average of 195 cm/y (76 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka covers an area of nearly 7 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 15500 m (7903 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka was founded durring the late 13th century in fall of the year 1172, by Bellyborer Beetles Gareg̈me. The establishment of Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the late 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka is buildings are built arround a single crampt paverstone mainstreet which forms a counterclockwise spiral to give the town a over all circular shape. The town emploies a series of defencive earthworks, spikes, and fences to provide some protection against wild beasts and smaller groups of intelegent foes. The minimaly adiquite have not been wellmaintained over the years, and while functional are in dire need of some loving care and perhapse light renovation.

Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up.

Cultural Notes

Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is which made use of the classical orders and mathematically precise ratios of height and width combined with a desire for symmetry, proportion, and harmony. It used columns, pediments, arches and domes are imaginatively in buildings of all types. Decorative features were seen as largely unnecessary as the sheer beauty of the structure itself was often close to art. However, many buildings with large ceiling spaces had their ceilings decorated with elaborate paintings, simply because the large flat spaces could feel wasted.

Due to the actions of local Kami, winter is long in Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka.

The Ettercap near Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves long periods of drunkenness to channel Conjuration energies of tier 3 via moments of science.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka was attacked by soldiers from another nation, waging a greater campaign. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka lost 265 people, 185 livestock, and 95 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 123, when members of Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka's militia enacted an operation to train a group of civilians for a special clandestine operation. The operation was complicated by at least some of the intelligence related to the operation was incorrect. The conflict ended with needing to break through the enemy's lines, which ended in defeat for Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Kew-hǐlăah Birěkhka's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
