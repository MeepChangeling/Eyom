Eaglecaer (/eaglecaer/ [Translation Unavailable]) is a subtropical Town located in Mu̹chya Danàchyi County, Qezapuqajia County, within the Norwick Principality.

The name Eaglecaer is derived from the Sylvin language, as Eaglecaer was founded by Tic Gotts, who was culturaly Sylvin.

Climate

Eaglecaer has a yearly average temperature of 23°C (73°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 29°C (84°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 17°C (62°F). Eaglecaer receives an average of 102 cm/y (40 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Eaglecaer covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 4930 m (16174 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Eaglecaer was founded durring the late 12th century in summer of the year 1079, by Tic Gotts. The establishment of Eaglecaer suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Eaglecaer which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Eaglecaer was built using the conventions of Sylvin durring the late 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Eaglecaer is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Eaglecaer is buildings are arranged arrounded highly ordered system of premissive packed earth streets which form octogonal paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The town has a defencive wall made from querried stone. The wall is equipped with a full set of battlments but the nature of its construction methodology leaves it somewhat vulnerable to siege equipment. That said, the town is well defended against anything short of an army. The town's cost-cutting-focused defences are visibly old, but also obviously well maintained. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with routine mantance of the town's defences.

A look around Eaglecaer has something terribly wrong with it. It’s impossible to put one’s finger on, but something is horribly wrong. Maybe it’s the way fog blankets the ground, but only in the connors of places. Maybe it’s the vermin scutteling between shadows in the corner of your eyes. Perhaps it’s the overcast sky which seemed to creep out of nowhere, or the distant howling of wolves. Maybe it’s everything together. Regardless, you do not feel it would be wise to remain in Eaglecaer long.

Civic Infrastructure

Eaglecaer has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Eaglecaer.

Eaglecaer has a government-funded child care program, overseen by the local Department of Nursemaids, which is responsible for providing childcare to working-class citizens according to local ordinances.

Eaglecaer has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Eaglecaer has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Eaglecaer has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Eaglecaer's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Eaglecaer has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

Eaglecaer has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

Two or more groups of citizens within Eaglecaer hate each other. Their neighbors or the local law have kept things from too-overt violence, but members of the groups will constantly interfere with their rivals and cause whatever misery they can get away with. This hate may spring from recent events, or it may be an inherited spite from old wrongs.

Eaglecaer's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for the combined use of arcuated and trabeated construction, employing arches and constructed with post and lintel. The arch served as the style's chief structural element, with flanking columns serving as buttresses or decorations. While the general shape of their structures could be generously described as simplistic, the use of these arches and columns created a most distinct ascetic which is easily distinguishable on sight to armatures and experts alike.

The Mome Rath near Eaglecaer are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Eaglecaer's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves drinking to channel Elven High Magic energies of tier 1 via speaking in tongues.
