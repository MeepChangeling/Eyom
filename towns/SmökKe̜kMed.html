Smök Ke̜k Med (/smo˞k/ /ke̜k/ [left] [basement]) is a subtropical Large City located in Chyhye̜vno̠n Zone, Mimydrabet Province, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Smök Ke̜k Med is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as Smök Ke̜k Med was founded by Gilil Thiestresh, who was culturaly Wood Elven.

Climate

Smök Ke̜k Med has a yearly average temperature of 15°C (59°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 6°C (42°F). Smök Ke̜k Med receives an average of 287 cm/y (112 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Smök Ke̜k Med covers an area of nearly 271 km2 (108 mi2), and an average elevation of 7066 m (-19767 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Smök Ke̜k Med was founded durring the late 17th century in early winter of the year 1593, by Gilil Thiestresh. The establishment of Smök Ke̜k Med was only bairly constructed. The sheer number of problems with its founding were enough to make several of the backers funding Smök Ke̜k Med's construction back out of the project. Gilil Thiestresh pushed on reguardles, and Smök Ke̜k Med was finished, but starts off as a terible place to live.

Smök Ke̜k Med was built using the conventions of Wood Elven durring the late 17th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Smök Ke̜k Med is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Smök Ke̜k Med is is constructed arround a semi-circular restrictive baked earthen mainstreet, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The hillside city rests behind the absurdity that is a thick, timber braced, wall made of clay bricks. While visualy impressive and certainly an astetic, Smök Ke̜k Med's wall provides no actual defence against siege equipment due to the choice of its cosntruction materials. Even nonexperts can tell the town is trying to impress rather than defend with its walls, towers, and gatehouses. Though admittedly, they do look nice... To primitive tribals who have never seen fortifications before. The political statment focused walls have not been wellmaintained over the years, and while functional are in dire need of some loving care and perhapse light renovation.

A look around Smök Ke̜k Med shows Smök Ke̜k Med is little more than a wretched hive of scum and villainy. Birbes can be seen changing hands openly, such that it must be customary to do so and must have been for a long time. The locals have no fear, no annoyance at the state of things, it simply is.

Smök Ke̜k Med has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Smök Ke̜k Med. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Smök Ke̜k Med's parks.

Smök Ke̜k Med has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Smök Ke̜k Med.

Smök Ke̜k Med has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Smök Ke̜k Med has a Guild of Nurses, which is tasked with caring for the elderly and infirm in accordance with local ordinances, religious values, and customs.

Smök Ke̜k Med has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Smök Ke̜k Med has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Smök Ke̜k Med has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Smök Ke̜k Med has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Smök Ke̜k Med's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Smök Ke̜k Med has an Arcane Academy which provides higher education in the arcane sciences.

Smök Ke̜k Med possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Large City. Smök Ke̜k Med's grid is powered by a direct leyline tap.

Smök Ke̜k Med's old civil lighting system was converted to Galvanic Lamps recently, and expanded to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Smök Ke̜k Med has a first rate hospital which caters to anyone in need of long term medical care.

Smök Ke̜k Med has a library, which keeps a large collection of books, scrolls, and archives all manner of physical items. While not open to the public, the librarians and scholars employed by the library will assist anyone with their research needs, and wealthy individuals can purchase membership to access the library's materials themselves. In spite of being generally closed to the public, the library has a room with several Aether Linked devices available to the public during business hours.

Smök Ke̜k Med has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Smök Ke̜k Med's natural decorations nor waterways.

Smök Ke̜k Med has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Smök Ke̜k Med has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Smök Ke̜k Med has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Smök Ke̜k Med is home to a University which provides higher education in a variety of fields, and also serves as a research institute for those same fields.

Cultural Notes

Smök Ke̜k Med's locals are divided into several castes. They may be organized by social role, by imputed nobility of birth, by ethnic origins, or any other dividing principle, but they cannot imagine any other way of organizing themselves. A hierarchy of castes is not inevitable, but there will be social and legal limits applied to ensure that each caste remains fixed in its function. The outside world may or may not respect these distinctions when dealing with the locals.

Smök Ke̜k Med's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is based upon new and innovative technologies of construction and the idea that form should follow function. It was an embrace of minimalism and a rejection of ornament. The style became characterized by an emphasis on volume, asymmetrical compositions, and minimal ornamentation..

In Smök Ke̜k Med there are no smells.

The Herd Animal, Sheep near Smök Ke̜k Med are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Smök Ke̜k Med's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves gestures to channel Wild Magic energies of tier 1 via recitation of poetic epics.
