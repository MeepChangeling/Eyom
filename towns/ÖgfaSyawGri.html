Ögfa Syaw Gri (/ˈo˞gfa/ /sjaw/ [permanent] [bias]) is a subtropical Large City located in the Fio̠dyokōn Moot of the Rosid.

The name Ögfa Syaw Gri is derived from the Sylvin language, as Ögfa Syaw Gri was founded by Glkarg̈m, who was culturaly Wood Elf.

Climate

Ögfa Syaw Gri has a yearly average temperature of 16°C (60°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 8°C (46°F). Ögfa Syaw Gri receives an average of 147 cm/y (57 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Ögfa Syaw Gri covers an area of nearly 253 km2 (101 mi2), and an average elevation of 4524 m (14842 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Ögfa Syaw Gri was founded durring the late 10th century, by Glkarg̈m. The establishment of Ögfa Syaw Gri suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Ögfa Syaw Gri which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Ögfa Syaw Gri was built using the conventions of Wood Elf durring the late 10th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Ögfa Syaw Gri is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Ögfa Syaw Gri is buildings folow an organic layout of restrictive cobblestone streets whihch gives the city a shape simmilar to a tree, if one views its streets from above. The city is the proud owner of a thick set of fortified walls fashioned from querried stone blocks. While not up to snuff for a fort or castle wall, the city's walls are naturaly much larger than those of forts or castles. Therefore, the construction such a wall is most expencive. Ögfa Syaw Gri's buget focused wall would serve its community well in battle in spite of looking unimpressive compared to castles and fortresses. The city's top tier civilian fortifications are visibly old, but also obviously maintained semi-regularly. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with ocasional mantance of the citys defences.

Ögfa Syaw Gri is not quite well. Something happened here, perhapse recently, perhapse long ago. Whatever it was, it settled into the very soul of the city like a festering wound. The people go about their day well enough, but there’s a tention in the air you can cut with a knife.

Cultural Notes

Ögfa Syaw Gri's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is admittedly strange and non-linear style rooted in defiance of symmetrical shapes. It championed the creation of buildings with a unique visual appearance. the structural norms of classic buildings and deforms or moves away from elementary architectural principles. By including non-linear designs processed into its buildings and favoring fragmentation, this style expressed a form of controlled chaos. Its buildings appear out-of-the-ordinary, draw the eye in immediately and sometimes create a feeling of strangeness. These distorted shapes and structure are not reserved to the building’s outer facade, they destabilize interior elements too, favoring minimalism and play on people’s perceptions by injecting a futuristic touch.

In Ögfa Syaw Gri birds speak prophesy.

The Almiraj near Ögfa Syaw Gri are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Ögfa Syaw Gri's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves reenactments to channel Abjuration energies of tier 2 via singing.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century, Ögfa Syaw Gri was attacked by soldiers from another nation, waging a greater campaign. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Ögfa Syaw Gri lost 151 people, 109 livestock, and 55 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 147, when members of Ögfa Syaw Gri's militia enacted an operation to secure a particular temple to utilize it for a specific task. The operation was complicated by the enemy setting up an ambush. The conflict ended with needing to break through the enemy's lines, which ended in a stalemate for Ögfa Syaw Gri's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Ögfa Syaw Gri's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
