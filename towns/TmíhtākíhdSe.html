Tmíhtākíhd Se (Tmíhtā /kɪ̞d/ [snow] [trailer]) is a subtropical Town located in Skrurunjlesomi Hold, Bênfêfi Provence, within the Tauranian United Provinces.

The name Tmíhtākíhd Se is derived from the Tauric language, as Tmíhtākíhd Se was founded by Tsåts Shankland, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Tmíhtākíhd Se has a yearly average temperature of 25°C (77°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 28°C (82°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 23°C (73°F). Tmíhtākíhd Se receives an average of 216 cm/y (85 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Tmíhtākíhd Se covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 3634 m (11922 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Tmíhtākíhd Se was founded durring the late 12th century in fall of the year 1085, by Tsåts Shankland. The establishment of the new community went well, with no major obsticles durring construction.

Tmíhtākíhd Se was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the late 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Tmíhtākíhd Se is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Tmíhtākíhd Se is was constructed arround several broad packed earth mainstreets which cross one another at certain axies, with smaller streets branching off of them to premit acess to the many buildings deeper into the road network. The overall fashion is remenessent of a circulatory system, or other organic construct, and is quite effishent in its design. The town has a fortified albit thin brick wall. The wall has most of the feeatures of a typical castle wall, just on a much smaller scale and and budget. Notably brick isn't a particuarly soild choice for resisting siege weapons. Tmíhtākíhd Se's wall wouldn't hinder a proper army, but it is more than sufishent for bandits and other small marauding groups. Tmíhtākíhd Se's monster and outlaw focused fortifications has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

Right off the bat Tmíhtākíhd Se hits you in the face with its success. Everyone, even the peasants, are dressed in well made clothing. Every tool and implement you can see is finely made, and people will boast to you as obvious strangers of the wonders which can be found in their markets. More interestingly is a total lack of beggars, and plenty of new buildings are going up even as you speak. Somehow this town has come into quite a lot of wealth, and recently from the looks of things. The town is very clearly a joyfull place as well as wealthy. Music can be heard often, as well as laughter. The smell of food and drink permiates the air. You can’t help but smile.

Civic Infrastructure

Tmíhtākíhd Se has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Tmíhtākíhd Se.

Tmíhtākíhd Se has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Tmíhtākíhd Se has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Tmíhtākíhd Se has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Tmíhtākíhd Se's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Tmíhtākíhd Se has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

Tmíhtākíhd Se has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Tmíhtākíhd Se has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

Tmíhtākíhd Se's locals despise outsiders. For some “outsiders” may be natives of foreign lands, while others might have a grudge against anyone from outside the community. These locals have an active loathing, and the outsiders allowed to trade or interact with them do so at a heavy disadvantage.

Tmíhtākíhd Se's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known primarily for its use of abstraction and simplicity. Clean lines, right angles, and primary colors characterized this aesthetic and art movement expressed via architecture and paintings. Its design ethos allows only primary colors and non-colors, only squares and rectangles, only straight and horizontal or vertical lines. Vertical and horizontal lines are positioned in layers or planes that do not intersect, thereby allowing each element to exist independently and unobstructed by other elements. These seemingly impossible principals for an architectural style coalesces into structures which most experts find hard to put into words. It is not that their geometry is impossible, but rather the style's attempt at producing works only describable visually was most successful..

In Tmíhtākíhd Se it is impossible to directly or indirectly lie.

The ﻿Dvorovoi near Tmíhtākíhd Se are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Tmíhtākíhd Se's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves creating small tokens to channel Transmutation energies of tier 2 via proclamations.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century heavy spring rains following an uncharacteristically dry year and a minor earthquake resulted in the collapse of the north east side of Heartache Hill. the landslide struck Tmíhtākíhd Se, and devastated the community. 138 people, 350 livestock, and 96 buildings were lost to the calamity. The disaster is recorded in history as Heartache's Fall.
