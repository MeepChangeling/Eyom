Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă (/ˈberɛ/ /ˈwehə̝/ [able; capable; competent; ready] [orphan]) is a subtropical Town located in Vez̄esuka Parish, Rubivælǐ District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă is derived from the Constructi language, as Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă was founded by Giant Flies Gadsînka, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă has a yearly average temperature of 23°C (73°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 26°C (78°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 20°C (68°F). Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă receives an average of 244 cm/y (96 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 1670 m (5479 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă was founded durring the early 14th century in fall of the year 1305, by Giant Flies Gadsînka. The establishment of Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Giant Flies Gadsînka struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă as a prison colony.

Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the early 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature masoned stone construction which prominantly features pointed arches, pointed ribbed vault cielings, flying buttress', and window tracery all of which share a simmilar gemoetetic patern halfway between organic and inorganic in design formaing a very distinct aesthetically integrated style. BUildings tend to reach for the havens, and more expencive homes are easily identified by their floor count as well as the addition of decorative features intigrated into the building's design such as statues, gargoyals, and embelished joinery.

Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă is is constructed arround a series of narrow cobblestone mainstreets which form concentric circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to each other at varrious points. The town sits behind a stone-renforced palisade wall, with stone gatehouses and timber drawbridges for their trench. The would-be-castle fortifications have not been wellmaintained over the years, and while functional are in dire need of some loving care and perhapse light renovation.

A look around Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă makes it abundantly clear the town suffered something horrible some time ago. It’s a quiet town filled with closed scriptoriums, people quietly yet dejectedly reading in isolated places around town, and the occasional book laying in a heap of garbage.

Cultural Notes

The law within Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă is highly corrupt, or does not apply to certain favored groups or castes. Strangers might be fleeced by local lawmen, evildoers can be absolved by a payment, and powerful gentry do as they please.

Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by grandeur of scale, simplicity of geometric forms, dramatic use of columns, and a preference for blank walls. This design ethos extended to re-imagining earlier styles of structure to create them anew, with a similar overall look and feel to one another..

In Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă there are unidentifiable people in the fog, but it seems to be okay.

The Hippogriff near Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves embarking on a group pilgrimage to channel Chronomancy energies of tier 3 via oath swearing.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century, Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă was attacked by savage formianss living nearby. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă lost 191 people, 285 livestock, and 40 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 222, when members of Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă's militia enacted an operation to delay the operations of the enemy. The operation was complicated by extremely dangerous terrain, including unfamiliar and poisonous plants which many ate while foraging. The conflict ended with needing to break through the enemy's lines, which ended in defeat for Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Fìkh Kx-23p Wehă's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
