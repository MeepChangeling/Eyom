S̺urahi Vu̽yûchû (/hu̽ˈgɑlʊ/ /vu̽ˈjʊʧʊ/ [pragmatic] [death]) is a subtropical Town located in Dirukvurmmese County, Mî Kûrà Empire, within the Warrenese Shogunate.

The name S̺urahi Vu̽yûchû is derived from the Wareneese language, as S̺urahi Vu̽yûchû was founded by Geddei Trêg̈, who was culturaly Wareneese.

Climate

S̺urahi Vu̽yûchû has a yearly average temperature of 11°C (51°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing -3°C (27°F). S̺urahi Vu̽yûchû receives an average of 113 cm/y (44 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. S̺urahi Vu̽yûchû covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 3326 m (10912 ft) above sea level.

Cultural Notes

There’s a relatively new religion in S̺urahi Vu̽yûchû which is rapidly gaining power. It might be a sectarian offshoot of a major faith, the unique product of a new prophet, or an outside faith backed by wealthy and powerful foreign supporters. Depending on the demands made on believers, the new faith may be a matter of concern only to the existing clergy, or it might be a major flashpoint for conflict in the community.

S̺urahi Vu̽yûchû's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known primarily for its use of abstraction and simplicity. Clean lines, right angles, and primary colors characterized this aesthetic and art movement expressed via architecture and paintings. Its design ethos allows only primary colors and non-colors, only squares and rectangles, only straight and horizontal or vertical lines. Vertical and horizontal lines are positioned in layers or planes that do not intersect, thereby allowing each element to exist independently and unobstructed by other elements. These seemingly impossible principals for an architectural style coalesces into structures which most experts find hard to put into words. It is not that their geometry is impossible, but rather the style's attempt at producing works only describable visually was most successful..

Due to the actions of local Kami, spring is short in S̺urahi Vu̽yûchû.

The Ovinnik near S̺urahi Vu̽yûchû are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

S̺urahi Vu̽yûchû's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves square dance to channel Truename Magic energies of tier 1 via oratory performances.
