Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka (/ˈvɨpɤb/ /ˈnɪbæb/ [triple] [canoe]) is a subtropical Town located in Nulesumapa Parish, Pinizeva District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka is derived from the Constructi language, as Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka was founded by Tshêyêbm Fyêthèj, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka has a yearly average temperature of 26°C (78°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 30°C (86°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 23°C (73°F). Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka receives an average of 134 cm/y (52 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 5682 m (18641 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka was founded durring the late 14th century in spring of the year 1263, by Tshêyêbm Fyêthèj. The establishment of Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Tshêyêbm Fyêthèj electing to pay people to resettle in Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka.

Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the late 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka is buildings are located arround a single narrow packed earth mainstreet which forms a clockwise spiral to give the town a over all circular shape. The town has a fortified albit thin brick wall. The wall has most of the feeatures of a typical castle wall, just on a much smaller scale and and budget. Notably brick isn't a particuarly soild choice for resisting siege weapons. Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka's wall wouldn't hinder a proper army, but it is more than sufishent for bandits and other small marauding groups. Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka's monster and outlaw focused fortifications are in an unremarkable state. To some, this is the ideal sate for defences to be in. In need of absoutly nothing, and ready to serve the town as needed.

Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. The people are clearly worried about something, and everyone is either wearing a weapon or has one close at hand. Your gut tells you it’s not mere fate which has caused this town to begin failing...

Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used which employed abundant symbolic geometry, using pure forms such as the circle and square, and plans are based on often symmetrical layouts featuring rectangular courtyards and halls. These structures were is decorated with carved stone or stucco reliefs and made use of colorful stone mosaics..

In Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka there are no smells.

The Human Juju Zombie near Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves drinking to channel Conjuration energies of tier 2 via chanting.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century was struck by a great wind storm. A great funnel cloud itself touched down in Vìpèb Nǐbæb Birěkhka, bringing twisting winds which killed 144 people, 204 livestock, and 36 buildings in the disaster.. The disaster is generally remembered as the Howling Winds of Hate.
