Êkè Ëtê-cè Fêqê (/ˈɘkè ˈëtɘ/ /cè/ [eyeball] [cement; concrete (building material)]) is a subtropical Town located in Gàj̼āb County, Pimthêkpíidi Provence, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Êkè Ëtê-cè Fêqê is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Êkè Ëtê-cè Fêqê was founded by Grel Breshe, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Êkè Ëtê-cè Fêqê has a yearly average temperature of 15°C (59°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 21°C (69°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 10°C (50°F). Êkè Ëtê-cè Fêqê receives an average of 270 cm/y (106 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Êkè Ëtê-cè Fêqê covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 1192 m (3910 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Êkè Ëtê-cè Fêqê was founded durring the early 15th century in spring of the year 1404, by Grel Breshe. The establishment of Êkè Ëtê-cè Fêqê was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Grel Breshe electing to pay people to resettle in Êkè Ëtê-cè Fêqê.

Êkè Ëtê-cè Fêqê was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the early 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Êkè Ëtê-cè Fêqê is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Êkè Ëtê-cè Fêqê is is constructed arround a series of spacious worn bedrock mainstreets which form overlapping circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The town has a defencive wall made from querried stone. The wall is equipped with a full set of battlments but the nature of its construction methodology leaves it somewhat vulnerable to siege equipment. That said, the town is well defended against anything short of an army. Astonishigly, the cost-cutting-focused defences are in pristine condishion, as if they had just been finished before you laied eyes upon them.

Êkè Ëtê-cè Fêqê has a very calm atmosphere. People can be seen relaxing, scocilizing, and going about all manner of business other than the daily grind. Men, women, children, all can be seen enjoying life in a laid-back way in the many parks which line Êkè Ëtê-cè Fêqê’s streets. Yet, deep down, you know this atmosphere is a carefully created illusion. You can see the ocasional seam in their tapestry. You’re not sure what has them doing their best to act so carefree, but act carefree they do.

Cultural Notes

Êkè Ëtê-cè Fêqê's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known primarily for its use of abstraction and simplicity. Clean lines, right angles, and primary colors characterized this aesthetic and art movement expressed via architecture and paintings. Its design ethos allows only primary colors and non-colors, only squares and rectangles, only straight and horizontal or vertical lines. Vertical and horizontal lines are positioned in layers or planes that do not intersect, thereby allowing each element to exist independently and unobstructed by other elements. These seemingly impossible principals for an architectural style coalesces into structures which most experts find hard to put into words. It is not that their geometry is impossible, but rather the style's attempt at producing works only describable visually was most successful..

In Êkè Ëtê-cè Fêqê the stars are always right.

The Festrog near Êkè Ëtê-cè Fêqê are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Êkè Ëtê-cè Fêqê's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves orgies to channel Charm energies of tier 3 via oath swearing.
