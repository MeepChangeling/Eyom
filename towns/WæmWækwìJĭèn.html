Wæm Wækwì Jĭèn (/ˈwækwɨ/ /ʤɤn/ [stupid; dumb; silly] [tooth]) is a subtropical Town located in Vyasksktpeje Zone, Dewlǐq District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Wæm Wækwì Jĭèn is derived from the Constructi language, as Wæm Wækwì Jĭèn was founded by Halicata Zèdsmêm, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Wæm Wækwì Jĭèn has a yearly average temperature of 33°C (91°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a blistering 38°C (100°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 29°C (84°F). Wæm Wækwì Jĭèn receives an average of 160 cm/y (62 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Wæm Wækwì Jĭèn covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 14852 m (5777 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Wæm Wækwì Jĭèn was founded durring the early 14th century in spring of the year 1334, by Halicata Zèdsmêm. The establishment of Wæm Wækwì Jĭèn was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Halicata Zèdsmêm electing to pay people to resettle in Wæm Wækwì Jĭèn.

Wæm Wækwì Jĭèn was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the early 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Wæm Wækwì Jĭèn is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Wæm Wækwì Jĭèn is buildings are arranged arround a single restrictive packed earth mainstreet with many smaller streets branching off of it which gives the town a over all rectangular shape, albit one warped and twisted by the nature of the curves of the main road. The town sits behind a stone-renforced palisade wall, with stone gatehouses and timber drawbridges for their trench. The town's would-be-castle fortifications are visibly old, but also obviously well maintained. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with routine mantance of the town's defences.

A look around Wæm Wækwì Jĭèn seems to be home to a quite vibrant and boisterous community. Everywhere one looks they can see people going out their daily business with a smile and a spring in their step. Children play loudly in the streets, causing untold havoc as youth are want and allowed to do.

Cultural Notes

Two or more groups of citizens within Wæm Wækwì Jĭèn hate each other. Their neighbors or the local law have kept things from too-overt violence, but members of the groups will constantly interfere with their rivals and cause whatever misery they can get away with. This hate may spring from recent events, or it may be an inherited spite from old wrongs.

Wæm Wækwì Jĭèn's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used embraces individualism and experimentation. It emerged as a movement against traditional, classical styles and sought to make buildings dynamic and fun while breaking the rules. The style incorporated elements of previous architectural styles in exaggerated and whimsical ways. Traditional, conservative leanings were void in this era, with most scholars of architecture agreeing it was a time of “anything goes.”.
