Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka (/kew/ /ˈlæbæ/ [trial] [agriculture]) is a subtropical Large Town located in Rirasakho Parish, Wækwìilbu District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka is derived from the Constructi language, as Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka was founded by Mbó̄sh Joīy 'Elite Paige' Cú̄kidawm Cúoī Vûf̄ Zêrëkdshe, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka has a yearly average temperature of 28°C (82°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 33°C (91°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 24°C (75°F). Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka receives an average of 179 cm/y (70 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka covers an area of nearly 7 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 14484 m (4570 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka was founded durring the early 15th century in summer of the year 1416, by Mbó̄sh Joīy 'Elite Paige' Cú̄kidawm Cúoī Vûf̄ Zêrëkdshe. The establishment of the new community went well, with no major obsticles durring construction.

Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the early 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka is buildings have been located at convienant points along the valley Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka was built upon. Navigating the town is therefore a little chalanging as the distance between buildings verris greatly and the narrow split-log ties streets flow where they are able to be made rather than folowing the most convienant paths. The town has a defencive wall made from querried stone. The wall is equipped with a full set of battlments but the nature of its construction methodology leaves it somewhat vulnerable to siege equipment. That said, the town is well defended against anything short of an army. Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka's cost-cutting-focused defences has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

Cultural Notes

Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known primarily for its use of abstraction and simplicity. Clean lines, right angles, and primary colors characterized this aesthetic and art movement expressed via architecture and paintings. Its design ethos allows only primary colors and non-colors, only squares and rectangles, only straight and horizontal or vertical lines. Vertical and horizontal lines are positioned in layers or planes that do not intersect, thereby allowing each element to exist independently and unobstructed by other elements. These seemingly impossible principals for an architectural style coalesces into structures which most experts find hard to put into words. It is not that their geometry is impossible, but rather the style's attempt at producing works only describable visually was most successful..

Due to the actions of local Kami, winter is skipped in Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka.

The Mercane near Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka are known to be quite timid.

Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves embarking on a group pilgrimage to channel Chronomancy energies of tier 2 via speaking in tongues.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century, Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka was attacked by soldiers from another nation, waging a greater campaign. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka lost 231 people, 109 livestock, and 48 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 155, when members of Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka's militia enacted an operation to destroy or capture a particular depot. The operation was complicated by the army being put into a position where they could only choose between two terrible options. The conflict ended with pitched battle between both forces, which ended in victory for Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Qj-39d Læbæ Birěkhka's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
