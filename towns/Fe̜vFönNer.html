Fe̜v Fön Ner (/fe̜v/ /fo˞n/ [confidential] [issue; concern; controversy]) is a temperate Town located in Puqasube Parish, Bæru̽sa Vu̽ Empire, within the Warrenese Shogunate.

The name Fe̜v Fön Ner is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as Fe̜v Fön Ner was founded by Iposha Pizai, who was culturaly Wood Elven.

Climate

Fe̜v Fön Ner has a yearly average temperature of 5°C (41°F), with its average temperature during the summer being an icy 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold -16°C (4°F). Fe̜v Fön Ner receives an average of 301 cm/y (118 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Fe̜v Fön Ner covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 3862 m (12670 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Fe̜v Fön Ner was founded durring the late 16th century in fall of the year 1463, by Iposha Pizai. The establishment of Fe̜v Fön Ner suffered from several major issues, resulting in the need to develop many solutions to basic problems. Problems such as a lack of fresh water, logistical support, poor quality tools, and the odd monster or two. Howeaver, these were overcome in time.

Fe̜v Fön Ner was built using the conventions of Wood Elven durring the late 16th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Fe̜v Fön Ner is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber and earth construction, with most buildigns first floors resembling mounds of earth, with subsequent floors appearing as elaborate log cabins, with each building forming a tiered pyramid of sorts fromed from the stack of rectangular, peek roofed cabins. Most wooden bracing, support, and trim is carved with decorative knotwork, and the larger structures even feature painted trim which emphasises the knotwork.

Fe̜v Fön Ner is buildings folow an organic layout of crampt paverstone streets whihch gives the town a shape simmilar to a tree, if one views its streets from above. The town sits behind a stone-renforced palisade wall, with stone gatehouses and timber drawbridges for their trench. Fe̜v Fön Ner's would-be-castle fortifications are in an unremarkable state. To some, this is the ideal sate for defences to be in. In need of absoutly nothing, and ready to serve the town as needed.

A look around Fe̜v Fön Ner is like a look into a broken heart which long ago fell to corruption. There is no planning, no organization. Everyone here clearly goes about their own thing with little thought to anyone around them who isn’t selling something they need… unless of course, they would be an easy target for some robbery. The town has truly fallen to evil.

Civic Infrastructure

Fe̜v Fön Ner has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Fe̜v Fön Ner.

Fe̜v Fön Ner has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Fe̜v Fön Ner has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Fe̜v Fön Ner has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Fe̜v Fön Ner has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Fe̜v Fön Ner's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Fe̜v Fön Ner possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Town. Fe̜v Fön Ner's grid is powered by a god's will and kindness.

Fe̜v Fön Ner's old civil lighting system was converted to Galvanic Lamps recently, and expanded to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Fe̜v Fön Ner has a first rate hospital which caters to anyone in need of long term medical care.

Fe̜v Fön Ner has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

Fe̜v Fön Ner has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Fe̜v Fön Ner has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Fe̜v Fön Ner's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by their massive, monolithic and 'blocky' appearance with a rigid geometric style. It was best known for its rough, unfinished surfaces, unusual shapes, heavy-looking materials, straight lines, and small windows. Modular elements were often used to form masses representing specific functional zones, grouped into a unified whole.

Due to the actions of local Kami, autumn is skipped in Fe̜v Fön Ner.

The ﻿Shredskin near Fe̜v Fön Ner are known to be quite timid.

Fe̜v Fön Ner's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves bloodletting to channel Summoning energies of tier 3 via oratory performances.

History

Almost every community has some problem with bandits and highwaymen, but Fe̜v Fön Ner is seriously plagued with raiders. One or more groups of persistent plunderers are hitting the community repeatedly, and they lack the necessary resources to fend them off or protect all their holdings.

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century the Kami spared the town from the rampage of a legendary monster. One of Fe̜v Fön Ner's local festivals commemorates this miracle.
