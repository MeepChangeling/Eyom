Mêngît-g̈olî Bî (/ˈɘje/ /ˈg̃olɪ/ [last (final); last (previous)] [sadness; sorrow]) is a temperate Small Town located in the G̈mthêldèthêkè Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name Mêngît-g̈olî Bî is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Mêngît-g̈olî Bî was founded by Liliana Trênhêm Trîbmênm, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Mêngît-g̈olî Bî has a yearly average temperature of 25°C (77°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 29°C (84°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 22°C (71°F). Mêngît-g̈olî Bî receives an average of 130 cm/y (51 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Mêngît-g̈olî Bî covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 9274 m (-12523 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Mêngît-g̈olî Bî was founded durring the early 12th century in spring of the year 1116, by Liliana Trênhêm Trîbmênm. The establishment of Mêngît-g̈olî Bî suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Mêngît-g̈olî Bî which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Mêngît-g̈olî Bî was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the early 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Mêngît-g̈olî Bî is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Mêngît-g̈olî Bî is is constructed arround a series of narrow flagstone mainstreets which form concentric circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to each other at varrious points. The town emploies a series of defencive earthworks, spikes, and fences to provide some protection against wild beasts and smaller groups of intelegent foes. The town's minimaly adiquite are visibly old, but also obviously well maintained. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with routine mantance of the town's defences.

A look around Mêngît-g̈olî Bî makes you wonder how anything ever gets done. The locals have clear, obvious rivalries with one another, as seen through clenched teeth and fake smiles. What’s more, there is a serious lack of any organization and planning in Mêngît-g̈olî Bî’s layout as well as the local’s behaviors. The town seems to be a den of chaos where tongues are forked and local customs do not exist.

Mêngît-g̈olî Bî has an animal control department which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the control, impoundment, and disposition of animals.

Mêngît-g̈olî Bî has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Mêngît-g̈olî Bî.

Mêngît-g̈olî Bî has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Mêngît-g̈olî Bî has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Mêngît-g̈olî Bî has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Mêngît-g̈olî Bî's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Mêngît-g̈olî Bî has an Arts Academy which provides higher education in many fields including math, language arts, philosophy, engineering, and other such disciplines.

Mêngît-g̈olî Bî possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Small Town. Mêngît-g̈olî Bî's grid is powered by a boiler and turbine based power plant.

Mêngît-g̈olî Bî has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

Mêngît-g̈olî Bî has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

While Mêngît-g̈olî Bî might ostensibly be ruled by some other power, real control lies with the senior members of the local craft and labor guilds. Their decisions have the practical weight of law, and much of their time and effort is spent squeezing out competitors and parceling out economic opportunities in the community. Some guilds might have little or nothing to do with their original trade, and now exist purely as shells for political influence.

Mêngît-g̈olî Bî's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by dynamic designs and complex architectural plan forms; intended to heighten feelings of motion and sensuality, and frequently based on the oval. It made extensive and extreme use of: Grandeur, Contrast, Curves and twists, Rich surface treatments, Gilded statuary, Bright colors, Vividly painted ceilings, Fragmented or deliberately incomplete elements, Large-scale frescoes, Dramatic central projections on an external facade, the use of plaster, stucco, or marble finishing, Illusory effects such as trompe l’oeil, and pear-shaped domes. While beloved by the nobility, the common folk tended to despise the style due to the massive consumption of resources required for even a small building constructed in this style.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Mêngît-g̈olî Bî was attacked by soldiers from another nation, waging a greater campaign. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Mêngît-g̈olî Bî lost 147 people, 122 livestock, and 58 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 218, when members of Mêngît-g̈olî Bî's militia enacted an operation to train a specific elites group for an upcoming operation. The operation was complicated by a key segment of the operation that deepened entirely on a stealth mission going perfectly. The conflict ended with an assault and siege on the fortification, which ended in defeat for Mêngît-g̈olî Bî's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Mêngît-g̈olî Bî's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
