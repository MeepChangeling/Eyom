Odíhg Sùgshedèshtíhmi̊m (/ˈsʊgʃe/ /dɛʃˈtɪ̞mi̘m/ [bench] [broken]) is a subtropical Town located in the Vātuchàjù Region of the Tetbur Commune.

The name Odíhg Sùgshedèshtíhmi̊m is derived from the Tauric language, as Odíhg Sùgshedèshtíhmi̊m was founded by Zlkdshe Price Morgan, who was culturaly Tauric.

Odíhg Sùgshedèshtíhmi̊m has a yearly average temperature of 18°C (64°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 13°C (55°F). Odíhg Sùgshedèshtíhmi̊m receives an average of 245 cm/y (96 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Odíhg Sùgshedèshtíhmi̊m covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 2076 m (6811 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Odíhg Sùgshedèshtíhmi̊m was founded durring the late 15th century in winter of the year 1389, by Zlkdshe Price Morgan. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Zlkdshe Price Morgan.

Odíhg Sùgshedèshtíhmi̊m was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the late 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Odíhg Sùgshedèshtíhmi̊m is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Odíhg Sùgshedèshtíhmi̊m is buildings are arranged arround a network of spacious packed earth streets which form a diagonal shaped grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town rests behind the absurdity that is a thick, timber braced, wall made of clay bricks. While visualy impressive and certainly an astetic, Odíhg Sùgshedèshtíhmi̊m's wall provides no actual defence against siege equipment due to the choice of its cosntruction materials. Even nonexperts can tell the town is trying to impress rather than defend with its walls, towers, and gatehouses. Though admittedly, they do look nice... To primitive tribals who have never seen fortifications before. Unfortuantly, these political statment focused walls are in extreem disrepair, so much so that one cannot tell if they are decaying from a lack of mantance or damage incured.

Odíhg Sùgshedèshtíhmi̊m has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Odíhg Sùgshedèshtíhmi̊m ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. Even with that as it is, everything is just a little too worn down, a little too dirty, or much of both. The town should be gone, not dying. No one makes eye contact. Kids play quietly, but happily. Occasionally a passerby glances at you out of the corner of their eye, staring just long enough for it to be uncomfortable.

Cultural Notes

Odíhg Sùgshedèshtíhmi̊m has a long tradition of martial expertise. This may be a crisply-organized history of skilled native levies, or it may be a natural belligerence in the people that leaves them familiar with bloodshed. While their neighbors and liege doubtless respect their talents, this very aptitude might make them more willing to turn to steel than prudence would advise.

Odíhg Sùgshedèshtíhmi̊m's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for the combined use of arcuated and trabeated construction, employing arches and constructed with post and lintel. The arch served as the style's chief structural element, with flanking columns serving as buttresses or decorations. While the general shape of their structures could be generously described as simplistic, the use of these arches and columns created a most distinct ascetic which is easily distinguishable on sight to armatures and experts alike.

In Odíhg Sùgshedèshtíhmi̊m all of the cats speak in a dead language.

Odíhg Sùgshedèshtíhmi̊m's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves ritual combat to channel Truename Magic energies of tier 3 via throat chanting.
