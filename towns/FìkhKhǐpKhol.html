Fìkh Khǐp Khol (/xɪp/ /xol/ [wide; broad] [casino]) is a subtropical Town located in Yiwvèbzæw Zone, If-70cqǐnrì District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Fìkh Khǐp Khol is derived from the Constructi language, as Fìkh Khǐp Khol was founded by Pl-96r 'Johnny 5' Tresmèqtho, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Fìkh Khǐp Khol has a yearly average temperature of 11°C (51°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a cool 13°C (55°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 9°C (48°F). Fìkh Khǐp Khol receives an average of 224 cm/y (88 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Fìkh Khǐp Khol covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 1194 m (3917 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Fìkh Khǐp Khol was founded durring the late 12th century in winter of the year 1074, by Pl-96r 'Johnny 5' Tresmèqtho. The establishment of Fìkh Khǐp Khol was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Pl-96r 'Johnny 5' Tresmèqtho electing to pay people to resettle in Fìkh Khǐp Khol.

Fìkh Khǐp Khol was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the late 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Fìkh Khǐp Khol is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Fìkh Khǐp Khol is buildings are speckled and packed arround narrow baked earthen streets with seemingly no patern to them. It appears as if the town's residents simply built streets as they pleased and squeazed buildings in wherever and howeave rpossible, creating an organic, frustrating to navigate, maze of a town. The town is protected by a series of wooden fences ringing the town's parimiter, which are likly intended to keep varrious beasts out of town rather than protect it from attack by any intelegent agents. Fìkh Khǐp Khol's bare minimum defences has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

Fìkh Khǐp Khol has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Fìkh Khǐp Khol ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. The people are clearly worried about something, and everyone is either wearing a weapon or has one close at hand. Your gut tells you it’s not mere fate which has caused this town to begin failing...

Cultural Notes

Fìkh Khǐp Khol's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known for its buildings being predominantly formed through the combination of basic geometric shapes. However, it shown in the detailing given to the structures such as its characteristic tall columns, intricate detail, symmetry, harmony, and balance in their designs to an astonishing degree of precision. Decorative elements for the buildings tended to be built into the structure itself, making great use of fluting, frescoes, inlays, and embossing.

Due to the actions of local Kami, spring is recurring in Fìkh Khǐp Khol.

The Herd Animal, Elk (Giant) near Fìkh Khǐp Khol are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Fìkh Khǐp Khol's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves line dance to channel Mysticism energies of tier 1 via chanting.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Fìkh Khǐp Khol was attacked by members of a peasant revolt. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Fìkh Khǐp Khol lost 204 people, 135 livestock, and 23 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 119, when members of Fìkh Khǐp Khol's militia enacted an operation to destroy or capture a specific enemy noble. The operation was complicated by extremely dangerous terrain, including unfamiliar and poisonous plants which many ate while foraging. The conflict ended with needing to break through the enemy's lines, which ended in a crushing defeat for Fìkh Khǐp Khol's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Fìkh Khǐp Khol's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
