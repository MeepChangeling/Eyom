Joga-sèqî Fêqê (/ˈʤoga/ /ˈsèqɪ/ [whole; entire] [bone]) is a subtropical Town located in the Fêlqêfè Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name Joga-sèqî Fêqê is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Joga-sèqî Fêqê was founded by Tsëilê Breshèdo Sheg̈m, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Joga-sèqî Fêqê has a yearly average temperature of 24°C (75°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 28°C (82°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 21°C (69°F). Joga-sèqî Fêqê receives an average of 74 cm/y (29 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Joga-sèqî Fêqê covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 6158 m (20203 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Joga-sèqî Fêqê was founded in time immemorial, reportedly some time durring the early 7th century, by Tsëilê Breshèdo Sheg̈m. The establishment of the new community went well, with no major obsticles durring construction.

Joga-sèqî Fêqê was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the early 7th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Joga-sèqî Fêqê is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Joga-sèqî Fêqê is buildings are built arround a single spacious cobblestone mainstreet which forms a counterclockwise spiral to give the town a over all circular shape. The town emploies a series of defencive earthworks, spikes, and fences to provide some protection against wild beasts and smaller groups of intelegent foes. Joga-sèqî Fêqê's minimaly adiquite are suffering from significent damage, so much so that examples can be pointed to no matter which section one might have within their line of site, and most of which render sections inoperable at present.

Joga-sèqî Fêqê has a very calm atmosphere. People can be seen relaxing, scocilizing, and going about all manner of business other than the daily grind. Men, women, children, all can be seen enjoying life in a laid-back way in the many parks which line Joga-sèqî Fêqê’s streets. Yet, deep down, you know this atmosphere is a carefully created illusion. You can see the ocasional seam in their tapestry. You’re not sure what has them doing their best to act so carefree, but act carefree they do.

Civic Infrastructure

Joga-sèqî Fêqê has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Joga-sèqî Fêqê.

Joga-sèqî Fêqê has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Joga-sèqî Fêqê has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Joga-sèqî Fêqê has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Joga-sèqî Fêqê's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Joga-sèqî Fêqê possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Joga-sèqî Fêqê has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

Joga-sèqî Fêqê has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

There’s more than one leader in Joga-sèqî Fêqê, but at least some of them are at each other’s throats. It might be a conflict between formal leadership and informal authorities, or it could be a struggle among civil officials. Their interests might diverge sharply, or it could be a personal grudge that’s boiled over. Outside threats and internal problems are likely being ignored until the power struggle is resolved.

Joga-sèqî Fêqê's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for its striking sculptural forms and often dazzling ornamental detail that characterizes the buildings general shape. The radiant colors, rich patterns, and symmetrical silhouettes employed by this style were backed up by rich decorative features including gardens, courtyards, extruded arches, domes, pointed domes, vaulted ceilings, elaborate painted and inlaid designs, and decorative sculptures.

Due to the actions of local Kami, spring is short in Joga-sèqî Fêqê.

The Crawling Hand near Joga-sèqî Fêqê are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Joga-sèqî Fêqê's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves performance art to channel Necromancy energies of tier 2 via recitation of scripture.
