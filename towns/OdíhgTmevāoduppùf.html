Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf (Tmevāo /ˈduppʊf/ [celebrity] [bubble; foam]) is a subtropical Town located in Feneb County, Vùjluwo Provence, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf is derived from the Tauric language, as Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf was founded by Sindiel Jenney, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf has a yearly average temperature of 25°C (77°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 29°C (84°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 22°C (71°F). Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf receives an average of 133 cm/y (52 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 7734 m (-17575 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf was founded durring the early 15th century in fall of the year 1434, by Sindiel Jenney. The establishment of Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf was only bairly constructed. The sheer number of problems with its founding were enough to make several of the backers funding Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf's construction back out of the project. Sindiel Jenney pushed on reguardles, and Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf was finished, but starts off as a terible place to live.

Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the early 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf is buildings are arranged arround a network of broad flagstone streets which form a grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town resides behind a palisade wall complete with battlments, a moat, and timber gatehouses with drawbridges. The robustly designed timber walls have not been wellmaintained over the years, and while functional are in dire need of some loving care and perhapse light renovation.

Looking around Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf you immediately realise that the locals are looking back at you. All of them. Everywhere you look somone is staring back at you analytically, looking over every inch of you, your gear, and your companions. As you get close to people, their hands move closer to their belt knife, or dagger. You may want to watch where you go and what you say...

Civic Infrastructure

Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf's parks.

Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf.

Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf's natural decorations nor waterways.

Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by grandeur of scale, simplicity of geometric forms, dramatic use of columns, and a preference for blank walls. This design ethos extended to re-imagining earlier styles of structure to create them anew, with a similar overall look and feel to one another..

Due to the actions of local Kami, autumn is skipped in Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf.

The Leshy, Gourd near Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves line dance to channel Truename Magic energies of tier 3 via mimery.

History

Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf used to be much richer, but something happened in the last (10845 % 6)+1 years to crush its source of prosperity. Different factions of the community might be trying to grasp at the remaining dregs of wealth, others might try to restart the failed industry, and some might look for a new livelihood. Any group or entity thought responsible for the collapse is likely to be treated very harshly, and some locals might find profit in shifting the blame to their enemies.

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century the Kami blessed the town with wealth for a year and a day. One of Odíhg Tmevāoduppùf's local festivals commemorates this miracle.
