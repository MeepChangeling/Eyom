Ckÿph Fäyolvo Od̄ÿ (/fəʤolˈvo/ /odˈdi/ [economic] [afternoon]) is a temperate Large City located in Undteyägd Hold, Nar Province, within the United Kingdom of Undermountain.

The name Ckÿph Fäyolvo Od̄ÿ is derived from the Dwarven language, as Ckÿph Fäyolvo Od̄ÿ was founded by Trorgez Eghehf, who was culturaly Dwarvern.

Climate

Ckÿph Fäyolvo Od̄ÿ has a yearly average temperature of 22°C (71°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 26°C (78°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 18°C (64°F). Ckÿph Fäyolvo Od̄ÿ receives an average of 272 cm/y (107 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Ckÿph Fäyolvo Od̄ÿ covers an area of nearly 530 km2 (212 mi2), and an average elevation of 1342 m (4402 ft) above sea level.

Cultural Notes

Ckÿph Fäyolvo Od̄ÿ's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known for its fluid and florid elaborate style, comprising ornate, asymmetric designs and pastel shades. It is often considered to be a playful, light style, which made exuberant use of curves and emphasized subtle asymmetry in the general shape of its structures. Walls, ceilings and moldings are decorated with numerous interlacing of curves and counter-curves based on the shapes of ‘C’ and ‘S’, along with shell forms and other naturalistic shapes.

Due to the actions of local Kami, autumn is recurring in Ckÿph Fäyolvo Od̄ÿ.

The ﻿Plant-Imbued Ape near Ckÿph Fäyolvo Od̄ÿ are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Ckÿph Fäyolvo Od̄ÿ's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves embarking on a group pilgrimage to channel Chronomancy energies of tier 3 via speaking in tongues.

Points of Interest

Many of Ckÿph Fäyolvo Od̄ÿ’s structures date back to the ancient past and a long-vanished culture. They have unique architectural traits, perhaps being made of some strange substance or with uncanny qualities. The locals find them too useful or too durable to destroy, but the buildings often have unpleasant little surprises in their under-explored corners, and there may be greater structures still buried by long ages beneath Ckÿph Fäyolvo Od̄ÿ’s streets.

Ckÿph Fäyolvo Od̄ÿ's is something of a geological and arcane anomaly, as neither physical nor magical law entirely explains its formation.
