Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird (/kjipt/ /vniˈxlird/ [cannon] [trail]) is a temperate Large City located in the Temryont Principality of the Soheque.

The name Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird is derived from the Sylvin language, as Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird was founded by Tharngwen, who was culturaly Gnoll.

Climate

Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird has a yearly average temperature of 4°C (39°F), with its average temperature during the summer being an icy 19°C (66°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold -11°C (13°F). Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird receives an average of 210 cm/y (82 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird covers an area of nearly 101 km2 (40 mi2), and an average elevation of 4926 m (16161 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird was founded durring the early 13th century in summer of the year 1229, by Tharngwen. The establishment of the new community went well, with no major obsticles durring construction.

Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird was built using the conventions of Gnoll durring the early 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird is buildings are grouped arround an odd layout of restrictive cobblestone streets, which seems to be based on an overlapping squair patern such that there are small squares at the cornor of every bigger square. Sometimes buildings exist in the smaller squaires, other times they are open spaces, or occupied by temporary structures. The city is the proud owner of a properly designed set of renforced walls made from mighty querried stone blocks. Their construction and material choices would make a dwarf weap with joy, for each and every part of the elaborate fortifications are purly functional and robust well byond reason. Even nonexperts can tell the walls are an excelent defencive structure. Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird's exceptionaly well made fortifications has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

A look around Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird shows Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird as a den of corruption. Birbes can be seen changing hands openly, such that it must be customary to do so and must have been for a long time. The locals have no fear, no annoyance at the state of things, it simply is. The city has another layer to it as well. Locals can be overheard having academic discussions, as well as talking about scholarly subjects in general. It’s quite clear Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird places a lot of value on education and being a learned individual, or at least, faking it.

Cultural Notes

Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is which made use of the classical orders and mathematically precise ratios of height and width combined with a desire for symmetry, proportion, and harmony. It used columns, pediments, arches and domes are imaginatively in buildings of all types. Decorative features were seen as largely unnecessary as the sheer beauty of the structure itself was often close to art. However, many buildings with large ceiling spaces had their ceilings decorated with elaborate paintings, simply because the large flat spaces could feel wasted.

Due to the actions of local Kami, summer is short in Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird.

The Wendigo, Ravenous near Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves destroying a prepared ritual vessel to channel Abjuration energies of tier 3 via moments of science.

Points of Interest

Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird's roads were poorly made when first laid. Rather than repairing them correctly, a series of new roads was laid atop the old, leading to the streets of modern Slæ̈rn Kjipt-vnighlird suffering from potholes, cracking, and even sinkholes. The locals often repair the road by putting down wooden decking.
