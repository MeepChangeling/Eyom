Shenê-píêbo Ratê (/ˈʃenɘ/ /ˈp˔ɘbo/ [main] [claw]) is a subtropical Large City located in the Mèbîfege Zone of the Engineer's Guild.

The name Shenê-píêbo Ratê is derived from the Goblin language, as Shenê-píêbo Ratê was founded by Glargum, who was culturaly Iron Elf.

Climate

Shenê-píêbo Ratê has a yearly average temperature of 23°C (73°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 26°C (78°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 21°C (69°F). Shenê-píêbo Ratê receives an average of 120 cm/y (47 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Shenê-píêbo Ratê covers an area of nearly 63 km2 (25 mi2), and an average elevation of 4376 m (14356 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Shenê-píêbo Ratê was founded durring the late 15th century in summer of the year 1394, by Glargum. The establishment of Shenê-píêbo Ratê was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Glargum struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Shenê-píêbo Ratê as a prison colony.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê was built using the conventions of Iron Elf durring the late 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Shenê-píêbo Ratê is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature masoned stone construction which prominantly features pointed arches, pointed ribbed vault cielings, flying buttress', and window tracery all of which share a simmilar gemoetetic patern halfway between organic and inorganic in design formaing a very distinct aesthetically integrated style. BUildings tend to reach for the havens, and more expencive homes are easily identified by their floor count as well as the addition of decorative features intigrated into the building's design such as statues, gargoyals, and embelished joinery.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê is buildings are arranged arround a single broad carved bedrock mainstreet with many smaller streets branching off of it which gives the city a over all rectangular shape, albit one warped and twisted by the nature of the curves of the main road. The city rests behind the absurdity that is a thick, timber braced, wall made of clay bricks. While visualy impressive and certainly an astetic, Shenê-píêbo Ratê's wall provides no actual defence against siege equipment due to the choice of its cosntruction materials. Even nonexperts can tell the town is trying to impress rather than defend with its walls, towers, and gatehouses. Though admittedly, they do look nice... To primitive tribals who have never seen fortifications before. Astonishigly, the political statment focused walls are in pristine condishion, as if they had just been finished before you laied eyes upon them.

A look around Shenê-píêbo Ratê is like a look into a broken heart. There is no planning, no organization. Everyone here clearly goes about their own thing with little thought to anyone around them who isn’t selling something they need. It’s not malicious. Heads are hung. Eyes are empty. Something truly horrible happened here once upon a time and the city never healed.

Civic Infrastructure

Shenê-píêbo Ratê possesses a Aethary Link for its accademic, government, and financial institutions. Public Aethary access is available through one or more of these intitutions.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê has an animal control department which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the control, impoundment, and disposition of animals.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Shenê-píêbo Ratê. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Shenê-píêbo Ratê's parks.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Shenê-píêbo Ratê.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê has a government-funded child care program, overseen by the local Department of Nursemaids, which is responsible for providing childcare to working-class citizens according to local ordinances.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê has a Guild of Nurses, which is tasked with caring for the elderly and infirm in accordance with local ordinances, religious values, and customs.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Shenê-píêbo Ratê's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê has an Arcane Academy which provides higher education in the arcane sciences.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Large City. Shenê-píêbo Ratê's grid is powered by a boiler and turbine based power plant.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê's old civil lighting system was converted to Galvanic Lamps recently, and expanded to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê has a first rate hospital which caters to anyone in need of long term medical care.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê has a library, which keeps a large collection of books, scrolls, and archives all manner of physical items. While not open to the public, the librarians and scholars employed by the library will assist anyone with their research needs, and wealthy individuals can purchase membership to access the library's materials themselves. In spite of being generally closed to the public, the library has a room with several Aether Linked devices available to the public during business hours.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Shenê-píêbo Ratê's natural decorations nor waterways.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Shenê-píêbo Ratê's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is which made use of the classical orders and mathematically precise ratios of height and width combined with a desire for symmetry, proportion, and harmony. It used columns, pediments, arches and domes are imaginatively in buildings of all types. Decorative features were seen as largely unnecessary as the sheer beauty of the structure itself was often close to art. However, many buildings with large ceiling spaces had their ceilings decorated with elaborate paintings, simply because the large flat spaces could feel wasted.

Due to the actions of local Kami, autumn is long in Shenê-píêbo Ratê.

The Lurker in Light near Shenê-píêbo Ratê are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Shenê-píêbo Ratê's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves consuming a local narcotic to channel Abjuration energies of tier 1 via divine sermons.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century, Shenê-píêbo Ratê was attacked by soldiers from another nation, waging a greater campaign. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Shenê-píêbo Ratê lost 298 people, 378 livestock, and 96 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 208, when members of Shenê-píêbo Ratê's militia enacted an operation to destroy or capture a particular enemy resource. The operation was complicated by a significant percentage of the militia choose to go abandon the operation and return home. The conflict ended with pitched battle between both forces, which ended in a crushing defeat for Shenê-píêbo Ratê's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Shenê-píêbo Ratê's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
