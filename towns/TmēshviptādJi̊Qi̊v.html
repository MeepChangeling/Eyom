Tmēshviptād Ji̊ Qi̊v (Tmēsh /ˈviptəˑd/ [lion] [rabbit]) is a subtropical Large City located in the Nijhobbub Region of the Tetburland.

The name Tmēshviptād Ji̊ Qi̊v is derived from the Sylvin language, as Tmēshviptād Ji̊ Qi̊v was founded by Zlkdsí, who was culturaly Tauran.

Climate

Tmēshviptād Ji̊ Qi̊v has a yearly average temperature of 30°C (86°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 33°C (91°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 27°C (80°F). Tmēshviptād Ji̊ Qi̊v receives an average of 296 cm/y (116 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Tmēshviptād Ji̊ Qi̊v covers an area of nearly 115 km2 (46 mi2), and an average elevation of 788 m (2585 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Tmēshviptād Ji̊ Qi̊v was founded durring the early 13th century in spring of the year 1230, by Zlkdsí. The establishment of Tmēshviptād Ji̊ Qi̊v suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Tmēshviptād Ji̊ Qi̊v which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Tmēshviptād Ji̊ Qi̊v was built using the conventions of Tauran durring the early 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Tmēshviptād Ji̊ Qi̊v is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Tmēshviptād Ji̊ Qi̊v is buildings folow an organic layout of restrictive paverstone streets whihch gives the city a shape simmilar to a tree, if one views its streets from above. The city rests behind a thick wall made from clay bricks. The wall has all of the proper fortifications and is well made. Unfortuantly the nature of clay brick leaves it quite vulnerable to siege equipment, though the thickness of the wall lends it simmilar resistnace to a thinner hardrock wall. The city's millitarily questionable fortifications are visibly old, but also obviously maintained semi-regularly. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with ocasional mantance of the citys defences.

Tmēshviptād Ji̊ Qi̊v has a very calm atmosphere. People can be seen relaxing, scocilizing, and going about all manner of business other than the daily grind. Men, women, children, all can be seen enjoying life in a laid-back way in the many parks which line Tmēshviptād Ji̊ Qi̊v’s streets.

Cultural Notes

Tmēshviptād Ji̊ Qi̊v's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is famous for its stately symmetry, classical elements, and grand appearance. Columns and pillars, such as Corinthian columns, are often seen supporting open structures or porticos. Symmetry is an important feature of this style, with each half of a building mirroring the other. Domed ceilings and windows grace these buildings, with everything placed in a mathematical arrangement.

Due to the actions of local Kami, autumn is long in Tmēshviptād Ji̊ Qi̊v.

The ﻿Marble Snake near Tmēshviptād Ji̊ Qi̊v are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Tmēshviptād Ji̊ Qi̊v's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves square dance to channel Elven High Magic energies of tier 3 via mimery.
