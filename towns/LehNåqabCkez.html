Leh Nåqab Ckez (/lɪ̘/ /nɑːˈqab/ [indigo] [runner]) is a subtropical Village located in Nehinuqi County, Halähÿhvwu Kingdom, within the United Kingdom of Undermountain.

The name Leh Nåqab Ckez is derived from the Dwarven language, as Leh Nåqab Ckez was founded by Êrê Hub̄qug, who was culturaly Dwarvern.

Climate

Leh Nåqab Ckez has a yearly average temperature of 31°C (87°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a blistering 35°C (95°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 28°C (82°F). Leh Nåqab Ckez receives an average of 192 cm/y (75 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Leh Nåqab Ckez covers an area of nearly 3 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 19228 m (20134 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Leh Nåqab Ckez was founded durring the early 12th century in winter of the year 1128, by Êrê Hub̄qug. The establishment of Leh Nåqab Ckez suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Leh Nåqab Ckez which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Leh Nåqab Ckez was built using the conventions of Dwarvern durring the early 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Leh Nåqab Ckez is no diffrent. The village's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Leh Nåqab Ckez is buildings folow an organic layout of spacious split-log ties streets whihch gives the village a shape simmilar to a tree, if one views its streets from above. The village posesses a mighty albit amaturly crafted stone wall. It was built using querried stone blocks and posesses all of the standard defencive features, including a few watch towers. While it would last against a siege, the wall's outdated and simplistic construction method percludes the possability of Leh Nåqab Ckez weathering a full scale bombardment durring a siege. The village's budget focused millitary grade defenses are visibly old, but also obviously maintained semi-regularly. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with ocasional mantance of the villages defences.

The people go about their day well enough, but there’s a tention in the air you can cut with a knife. You get the terrible feeling that whatever it was, the wound it left will simply never heal. Civic Infrastructure

Leh Nåqab Ckez has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Leh Nåqab Ckez has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Leh Nåqab Ckez's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Leh Nåqab Ckez has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

Leh Nåqab Ckez's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is which made use of the classical orders and mathematically precise ratios of height and width combined with a desire for symmetry, proportion, and harmony. It used columns, pediments, arches and domes are imaginatively in buildings of all types. Decorative features were seen as largely unnecessary as the sheer beauty of the structure itself was often close to art. However, many buildings with large ceiling spaces had their ceilings decorated with elaborate paintings, simply because the large flat spaces could feel wasted.

In Leh Nåqab Ckez snow is warm to the touch and does not melt within city limits. Also it only happens during summer.

The Mud Elemental, Small near Leh Nåqab Ckez are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Leh Nåqab Ckez's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves embarking on a group pilgrimage to channel Enchantment energies of tier 1 via chanting.
