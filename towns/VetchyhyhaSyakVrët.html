Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët (/ˈvetcha/ /sjak/ [backward] [bead]) is a subtropical Town located in Ed-95wun Zone, Jaz̄omaka District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët was founded by Hëîbê Tununzic, who was culturaly Wood Elven.

Climate

Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët has a yearly average temperature of 22°C (71°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 29°C (84°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 16°C (60°F). Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët receives an average of 76 cm/y (29 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 4190 m (13746 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët was founded durring the early 16th century in spring of the year 1550, by Hëîbê Tununzic. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Hëîbê Tununzic.

Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët was built using the conventions of Wood Elven durring the early 16th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët is buildings are arranged arrounded highly ordered system of crampt paverstone streets which form octogonal paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The town rests behind the absurdity that is a thick, timber braced, wall made of clay bricks. While visualy impressive and certainly an astetic, Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët's wall provides no actual defence against siege equipment due to the choice of its cosntruction materials. Even nonexperts can tell the town is trying to impress rather than defend with its walls, towers, and gatehouses. Though admittedly, they do look nice... To primitive tribals who have never seen fortifications before. The political statment focused walls have not been wellmaintained over the years, and while functional are in dire need of some loving care and perhapse light renovation.

Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët ’s existence was once fed by scholarly pursuits. But the income once drawn in by the works of scribes and academics has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. Everywhere about the town one can see abandoned schools, libraries, and other academic structures. Locals can be overheard having academic discussions, as well as talking about scholarly subjects in general. It’s quite clear the town places a lot of value on education and being a learned individual. Even in these times.

Civic Infrastructure

Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët.

Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët has a first rate hospital which caters to anyone in need of long term medical care.

Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by grandeur of scale, simplicity of geometric forms, dramatic use of columns, and a preference for blank walls. This design ethos extended to re-imagining earlier styles of structure to create them anew, with a similar overall look and feel to one another..

Due to the actions of local Kami, spring is short in Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët.

The Sasquatch near Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves creating small tokens to channel Illusion energies of tier 3 via recitation of scripture.

Points of Interest

A great magical Working has been a critical part of Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët since its creation, but now it's beginning to decay. It may function only intermittently, now, or its effects may have curdled into something double-edged. The locals have no idea how to fix it, and indeed, it may not be possible to repair it with modern science or sorcery.

Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët's roads were poorly made when first laid. Rather than repairing them correctly, a series of new roads was laid atop the old, leading to the streets of modern Vetchyhyha Syak Vrët suffering from potholes, cracking, and even sinkholes. The locals often repair the road by putting down wooden decking.
