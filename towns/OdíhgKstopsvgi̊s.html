Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s (Kstopsv /gi̘s/ [fight] [candle]) is a subtropical Town located in the Altodålph Region of the Tetbur Commune.

The name Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s is derived from the Tauric language, as Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s was founded by Shepîv Griffith, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s has a yearly average temperature of 29°C (84°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 32°C (89°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 26°C (78°F). Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s receives an average of 137 cm/y (53 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 4046 m (13274 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s was founded durring the early 14th century in spring of the year 1346, by Shepîv Griffith. The establishment of Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Shepîv Griffith electing to pay people to resettle in Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s.

Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the early 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s is was constructed arround several broad gravel mainstreets which cross one another at certain axies, with smaller streets branching off of them to premit acess to the many buildings deeper into the road network. The overall fashion is remenessent of a circulatory system, or other organic construct, and is quite effishent in its design. The town rests behind the absurdity that is a thick, timber braced, wall made of clay bricks. While visualy impressive and certainly an astetic, Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s's wall provides no actual defence against siege equipment due to the choice of its cosntruction materials. Even nonexperts can tell the town is trying to impress rather than defend with its walls, towers, and gatehouses. Though admittedly, they do look nice... To primitive tribals who have never seen fortifications before. The town's political statment focused walls are visibly old, but also obviously maintained semi-regularly. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with ocasional mantance of the towns defences.

Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. The people are clearly worried about something, and everyone is either wearing a weapon or has one close at hand. Your gut tells you it’s not mere fate which has caused this town to begin failing...

Cultural Notes

Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is famous for its stately symmetry, classical elements, and grand appearance. Columns and pillars, such as Corinthian columns, are often seen supporting open structures or porticos. Symmetry is an important feature of this style, with each half of a building mirroring the other. Domed ceilings and windows grace these buildings, with everything placed in a mathematical arrangement.

In Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s rainbows form quite often above the Town.

The Ramidreju near Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Odíhg Kstopsvgi̊s's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves consuming a local narcotic to channel Illusion energies of tier 3 via moments of science.
