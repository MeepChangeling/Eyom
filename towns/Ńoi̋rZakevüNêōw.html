Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw (/ɲɔɪ̯̋ɹ/ /zeɪ̯nvuː/ [interior] [Pompoir]) is a subtropical Town located in the Jalisomasi Provence of the Kingdom of Hobben.

The name Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw is derived from the Goblin language, as Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw was founded by False Spiders, who was culturaly Hobgoblin.

Climate

Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw has a yearly average temperature of 19°C (66°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 11°C (51°F). Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw receives an average of 69 cm/y (27 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 5682 m (18641 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw was founded durring the early 15th century in spring of the year 1423, by False Spiders. The establishment of Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to False Spiders electing to pay people to resettle in Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw.

Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw was built using the conventions of Hobgoblin durring the early 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw is buildings are grouped arround an odd layout of broad gravel streets, which seems to be based on an overlapping squair patern such that there are small squares at the cornor of every bigger square. Sometimes buildings exist in the smaller squaires, other times they are open spaces, or occupied by temporary structures. The town rests behind a thick wall made from clay bricks. The wall has all of the proper fortifications and is well made. Unfortuantly the nature of clay brick leaves it quite vulnerable to siege equipment, though the thickness of the wall lends it simmilar resistnace to a thinner hardrock wall. Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw's millitarily questionable fortifications has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. The locals seem to have responded to their slow downfall by recreating Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw as one of the strictest places imaginable. Everyone’s actions are clearly directed by laws they keep in heart and mind at all times. Orderly byond order is a phrase which the town brings to mind.

Cultural Notes

Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known for its fluid and florid elaborate style, comprising ornate, asymmetric designs and pastel shades. It is often considered to be a playful, light style, which made exuberant use of curves and emphasized subtle asymmetry in the general shape of its structures. Walls, ceilings and moldings are decorated with numerous interlacing of curves and counter-curves based on the shapes of ‘C’ and ‘S’, along with shell forms and other naturalistic shapes.

In Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw during thunderstorms, with each flash of lightning, everyone sees different monsters from the corner of their eyes.

The Begedhi, Parasite near Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Ńoi̋r Zakevü Nêōw's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves creating small tokens to channel Chronomancy energies of tier 2 via divine sermons.
