Wăq Mihuv Birěkhka (/wə̝q/ /ˈmihuv/ [cold] [worm]) is a subtropical Town located in the Madraměvì District of the Federation of Alveria.

The name Wăq Mihuv Birěkhka is derived from the Constructi language, as Wăq Mihuv Birěkhka was founded by Èbëkèdo Èdsm, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Wăq Mihuv Birěkhka has a yearly average temperature of 15°C (59°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 22°C (71°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 9°C (48°F). Wăq Mihuv Birěkhka receives an average of 217 cm/y (85 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Wăq Mihuv Birěkhka covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 1156 m (3792 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Wăq Mihuv Birěkhka was founded durring the late 14th century in spring of the year 1287, by Èbëkèdo Èdsm. The establishment of Wăq Mihuv Birěkhka was only bairly constructed. The sheer number of problems with its founding were enough to make several of the backers funding Wăq Mihuv Birěkhka's construction back out of the project. Èbëkèdo Èdsm pushed on reguardles, and Wăq Mihuv Birěkhka was finished, but starts off as a terible place to live.

Wăq Mihuv Birěkhka was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the late 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Wăq Mihuv Birěkhka is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Wăq Mihuv Birěkhka is buildings folow an organic layout of restrictive baked earthen streets whihch gives the town a shape simmilar to a tree, if one views its streets from above. The town is the proud owner of a thick set of fortified walls fashioned from querried stone blocks. While not up to snuff for a fort or castle wall, the town's walls are naturaly much larger than those of forts or castles. Therefore, the construction such a wall is most expencive. Wăq Mihuv Birěkhka's buget focused wall would serve its community well in battle in spite of looking unimpressive compared to castles and fortresses. Wăq Mihuv Birěkhka's top tier civilian fortifications are suffering from significent damage, so much so that examples can be pointed to no matter which section one might have within their line of site, and most of which render sections inoperable at present.

Wăq Mihuv Birěkhka is, in a word, disorder. People seem to be allowed to do as they please with little harmoney to anything. It feels less like a town, and more like a spot people just happened to place their homes. Yet there are small elements here and there which show the underlying structure of the community. It’s just so complex, organic, and flowing one can only understand what is a piece of the puzzle, but not what its neighbors are.
