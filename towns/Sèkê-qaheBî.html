Sèkê-qahe Bî (/ˈsèkɘ/ /ˈqahe/ [keen] [luxury]) is a subtropical Village located in the Skhokraskhobuba Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name Sèkê-qahe Bî is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Sèkê-qahe Bî was founded by Kthelêv Èbmtco, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Sèkê-qahe Bî has a yearly average temperature of 24°C (75°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 21°C (69°F). Sèkê-qahe Bî receives an average of 80 cm/y (31 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Sèkê-qahe Bî covers an area of nearly 3 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 6318 m (20728 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Sèkê-qahe Bî was founded durring the early 15th century in winter of the year 1429, by Kthelêv Èbmtco. The establishment of Sèkê-qahe Bî was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Kthelêv Èbmtco electing to pay people to resettle in Sèkê-qahe Bî.

Sèkê-qahe Bî was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the early 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Sèkê-qahe Bî is no diffrent. The village's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Sèkê-qahe Bî is buildings are built arround a single restrictive flagstone mainstreet which forms a counterclockwise spiral to give the village a over all circular shape. The village lacks any defencive features, though certainly constructing even a simple fence or digging a ditch is at the forefront of the 's mind. At least, one would hope so.

A quick look in any direction shows Sèkê-qahe Bî is filled with vices. There are many taverns, brothels are advertising their services on the streets, cardsharks are plying their illicit trade within public squairs, and every shop has at least three signs advertising various sales on goods. That alone wouldn’t be too noticeable, if it wasn’t just so omnipresent and overwhelmingly how the people of this village live. Or if the general public wasn’t so enthusiastically, openly, and merrily participating in these activities.

Civic Infrastructure

Sèkê-qahe Bî has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Sèkê-qahe Bî has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Sèkê-qahe Bî's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Sèkê-qahe Bî has an Arts Academy which provides higher education in many fields including math, language arts, philosophy, engineering, and other such disciplines.

Sèkê-qahe Bî has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

Sèkê-qahe Bî's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known for its fluid and florid elaborate style, comprising ornate, asymmetric designs and pastel shades. It is often considered to be a playful, light style, which made exuberant use of curves and emphasized subtle asymmetry in the general shape of its structures. Walls, ceilings and moldings are decorated with numerous interlacing of curves and counter-curves based on the shapes of ‘C’ and ‘S’, along with shell forms and other naturalistic shapes.

Due to the actions of local Kami, summer is skipped in Sèkê-qahe Bî.

The Dallo near Sèkê-qahe Bî are known to be quite timid.

Sèkê-qahe Bî's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves drinking to channel Chronomancy energies of tier 1 via throat chanting.
