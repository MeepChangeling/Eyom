Kèr àvdèàbíhbù (/ˈəvdɛ/ /əˈbɪ̞bʊ/ [feast] [confident]) is a subtropical Small Town located in Rayfall Dutchy, Tarnàg Provence, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Kèr àvdèàbíhbù is derived from the Tauric language, as Kèr àvdèàbíhbù was founded by Zeg̈kê Vaugh, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Kèr àvdèàbíhbù has a yearly average temperature of 30°C (86°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 33°C (91°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 27°C (80°F). Kèr àvdèàbíhbù receives an average of 161 cm/y (63 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Kèr àvdèàbíhbù covers an area of nearly 3 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 2214 m (7263 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Kèr àvdèàbíhbù was founded durring the late 15th century in summer of the year 1452, by Zeg̈kê Vaugh. The establishment of Kèr àvdèàbíhbù was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Zeg̈kê Vaugh electing to pay people to resettle in Kèr àvdèàbíhbù.

Kèr àvdèàbíhbù was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the late 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Kèr àvdèàbíhbù is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Kèr àvdèàbíhbù is buildings are arranged arrounded highly ordered system of spacious flagstone streets which form octogonal paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The town is the proud owner of a properly designed set of renforced walls made from mighty querried stone blocks. Their construction and material choices would make a dwarf weap with joy, for each and every part of the elaborate fortifications are purly functional and robust well byond reason. Even nonexperts can tell the walls are an excelent defencive structure. Astonishigly, the exceptionaly well made fortifications are in pristine condishion, as if they had just been finished before you laied eyes upon them.

Kèr àvdèàbíhbù has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Kèr àvdèàbíhbù ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. Likely due to this, Kèr àvdèàbíhbù is, in a word, disorder. People seem to be allowed to do as they please with little harmoney to anything. It feels less like a town, and more like a spot people just happened to have homes. One cannot help but wonder what Kèr àvdèàbíhbù was like during the glorydays.

Cultural Notes

While Kèr àvdèàbíhbù might ostensibly be ruled by some other power, real control lies with the senior members of the local craft and labor guilds. Their decisions have the practical weight of law, and much of their time and effort is spent squeezing out competitors and parceling out economic opportunities in the community. Some guilds might have little or nothing to do with their original trade, and now exist purely as shells for political influence.

Kèr àvdèàbíhbù's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for the combined use of arcuated and trabeated construction, employing arches and constructed with post and lintel. The arch served as the style's chief structural element, with flanking columns serving as buttresses or decorations. While the general shape of their structures could be generously described as simplistic, the use of these arches and columns created a most distinct ascetic which is easily distinguishable on sight to armatures and experts alike.

Due to the actions of local Kami, winter is short in Kèr àvdèàbíhbù.

The Blood Kaktos near Kèr àvdèàbíhbù are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Kèr àvdèàbíhbù's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves reenactments to channel Elven High Magic energies of tier 2 via oratory performances.
