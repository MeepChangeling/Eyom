Áprinf-funo Xluwuf (/ə˞ˈprinf/ /fuˈŋo/ [messy] [pump]) is a subtropical Large City located in the Slïrddáms Principality of the Cochi.

The name Áprinf-funo Xluwuf is derived from the Sylvin language, as Áprinf-funo Xluwuf was founded by Grivguis, who was culturaly Gnoll.

Climate

Áprinf-funo Xluwuf has a yearly average temperature of 5°C (41°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a cold 2°C (35°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 9°C (48°F). Áprinf-funo Xluwuf receives an average of 205 cm/y (80 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the brutaly long winter months. Áprinf-funo Xluwuf covers an area of nearly 90 km2 (36 mi2), and an average elevation of 1766 m (5793 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Áprinf-funo Xluwuf was founded durring the early 13th century in winter of the year 1213, by Grivguis. The establishment of Áprinf-funo Xluwuf suffered from several major issues, resulting in the need to develop many solutions to basic problems. Problems such as a lack of fresh water, logistical support, poor quality tools, and the odd monster or two. Howeaver, these were overcome in time.

Áprinf-funo Xluwuf was built using the conventions of Gnoll durring the early 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Áprinf-funo Xluwuf is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Áprinf-funo Xluwuf is buildings are grouped arround an odd layout of spacious packed earth streets, which seems to be based on an overlapping squair patern such that there are small squares at the cornor of every bigger square. Sometimes buildings exist in the smaller squaires, other times they are open spaces, or occupied by temporary structures. The city is the proud owner of a thick set of fortified walls fashioned from querried stone blocks. While not up to snuff for a fort or castle wall, the city's walls are naturaly much larger than those of forts or castles. Therefore, the construction such a wall is most expencive. Áprinf-funo Xluwuf's buget focused wall would serve its community well in battle in spite of looking unimpressive compared to castles and fortresses. Unfortuantly, these top tier civilian fortifications are in extreem disrepair, so much so that one cannot tell if they are decaying from a lack of mantance or damage incured.

Áprinf-funo Xluwuf has a very calm atmosphere. People can be seen relaxing, scocilizing, and going about all manner of business other than the daily grind. Men, women, children, all can be seen enjoying life in a laid-back way in the many parks which line Áprinf-funo Xluwuf’s streets. Yet, deep down, you know this atmosphere is a carefully created illusion. You can see the ocasional seam in their tapestry. You’re not sure what has them doing their best to act so carefree, but act carefree they do.

Cultural Notes

Áprinf-funo Xluwuf's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known for its fluid and florid elaborate style, comprising ornate, asymmetric designs and pastel shades. It is often considered to be a playful, light style, which made exuberant use of curves and emphasized subtle asymmetry in the general shape of its structures. Walls, ceilings and moldings are decorated with numerous interlacing of curves and counter-curves based on the shapes of ‘C’ and ‘S’, along with shell forms and other naturalistic shapes.

Due to the actions of local Kami, spring is skipped in Áprinf-funo Xluwuf.

The Xtabay near Áprinf-funo Xluwuf are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Áprinf-funo Xluwuf's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves destroying a prepared ritual vessel to channel Transmutation energies of tier 2 via oratory performances.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century Áprinf-funo Xluwuf was struck by a devistating earthquake. The quake brought ruin to Áprinf-funo Xluwuf, which lost 298 people, 275 livestock, and 69 buildings in the earthquake. The day of the quake is remembered by many as Dread's Day.
