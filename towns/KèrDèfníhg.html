Kèr Dèfníhg (/dɛf/ /nɪ̞g/ [dome] [private]) is a subtropical Small Town located in the Utièkíhf Noiē Region of the Tetbur Commune.

The name Kèr Dèfníhg is derived from the Tauric language, as Kèr Dèfníhg was founded by Elennis Merwin, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Kèr Dèfníhg has a yearly average temperature of 17°C (62°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 22°C (71°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 13°C (55°F). Kèr Dèfníhg receives an average of 247 cm/y (97 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Kèr Dèfníhg covers an area of nearly 3 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 744 m (2440 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Kèr Dèfníhg was founded durring the late 16th century in spring of the year 1481, by Elennis Merwin. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Elennis Merwin.

Kèr Dèfníhg was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the late 16th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Kèr Dèfníhg is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Kèr Dèfníhg is buildings have been located at convienant points along the mountain Kèr Dèfníhg was built upon. Navigating the town is therefore a little chalanging as the distance between buildings verris greatly and the premissive packed earth streets flow where they are able to be made rather than folowing the most convienant paths. The town posesses a mighty albit amaturly crafted stone wall. It was built using querried stone blocks and posesses all of the standard defencive features, including a few watch towers. While it would last against a siege, the wall's outdated and simplistic construction method percludes the possability of Kèr Dèfníhg weathering a full scale bombardment durring a siege. The budget focused millitary grade defenses has suffered a visible ammount of structural damage, leaving them effectivly useless. One can't help but wonder why the has not yet effected repairs.

Kèr Dèfníhg has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Kèr Dèfníhg ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. The people are clearly worried about something, and everyone is either wearing a weapon or has one close at hand. Your gut tells you it’s not mere fate which has caused this town to begin failing...

Cultural Notes

Kèr Dèfníhg's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is which made use of the classical orders and mathematically precise ratios of height and width combined with a desire for symmetry, proportion, and harmony. It used columns, pediments, arches and domes are imaginatively in buildings of all types. Decorative features were seen as largely unnecessary as the sheer beauty of the structure itself was often close to art. However, many buildings with large ceiling spaces had their ceilings decorated with elaborate paintings, simply because the large flat spaces could feel wasted.

In Kèr Dèfníhg snow is warm to the touch and does not melt within city limits. Also it only happens during summer.

The Serpentfolk near Kèr Dèfníhg are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Kèr Dèfníhg's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves bloodletting to channel Summoning energies of tier 3 via guttural bellowing.
