Holvaleburg (/hollyvale/ [Translation Unavailable]) is a subtropical Town located in Àfyèmecùt County, Ivbea Kingdom, within the Sylvanian Empire.

The name Holvaleburg is derived from the Sylvin language, as Holvaleburg was founded by Brilêv Loveridge, who was culturaly Sylvin.

Climate

Holvaleburg has a yearly average temperature of 18°C (64°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 26°C (78°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 11°C (51°F). Holvaleburg receives an average of 136 cm/y (53 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Holvaleburg covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 4812 m (15787 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Holvaleburg was founded durring the early 13th century in winter of the year 1210, by Brilêv Loveridge. The establishment of Holvaleburg was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Brilêv Loveridge electing to pay people to resettle in Holvaleburg.

Holvaleburg was built using the conventions of Sylvin durring the early 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Holvaleburg is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Holvaleburg is buildings have been located at convienant points along the gorge Holvaleburg was built upon. Navigating the town is therefore a little chalanging as the distance between buildings verris greatly and the broad packed earth streets flow where they are able to be made rather than folowing the most convienant paths. The town is the proud owner of a thick set of fortified walls fashioned from querried stone blocks. While not up to snuff for a fort or castle wall, the town's walls are naturaly much larger than those of forts or castles. Therefore, the construction such a wall is most expencive. Holvaleburg's buget focused wall would serve its community well in battle in spite of looking unimpressive compared to castles and fortresses. Holvaleburg's top tier civilian fortifications are in an unremarkable state. To some, this is the ideal sate for defences to be in. In need of absoutly nothing, and ready to serve the town as needed.

Holvaleburg has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Holvaleburg ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up.

Civic Infrastructure

Holvaleburg has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Holvaleburg. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Holvaleburg's parks.

Holvaleburg has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Holvaleburg.

Holvaleburg has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Holvaleburg has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Holvaleburg has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Holvaleburg has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Holvaleburg's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Holvaleburg possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Holvaleburg has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Holvaleburg's natural decorations nor waterways.

Holvaleburg has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Holvaleburg has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Holvaleburg's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used makes use of a large oblong hall or building with double colonnades and a semicircular apse and symmetrical central-plan, resulting in buildings with a square central mass and four arms of equal length. Decorative features included domed rooves, arches, soaring spaces, and sumptuous decoration: marble columns and inlay, mosaics on the vaults, inlaid-stone pavements, and sometimes gold coffered ceilings.

In Holvaleburg tiny harmless tornadoes plague the town.

The Marsupial, Kangaroo near Holvaleburg are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Holvaleburg's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves bloodletting to channel Abjuration energies of tier 1 via guttural bellowing.

Points of Interest

Holvaleburg is centered around a major pilgrimage site. This may be a religious location of importance to a major faith, or it may be a more secular institution that draws the traffic, like a famous academy or the remains of some wondrous ancient work. Considerable local tension likely exists over controlling the access to the site and maximizing the profits from foreign visitors.

Holvaleburg has a substantial mill pond located a short distance from town.
