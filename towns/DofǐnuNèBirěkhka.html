Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka (/doˈfɪnu/ /nɤ/ [general; generic] [process; procedure]) is a subtropical Town located in Dijdradasi Zone, Yăvajĭkǐkh District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka is derived from the Constructi language, as Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka was founded by Giant Blister Beetles Brîv, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka has a yearly average temperature of 24°C (75°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 29°C (84°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 20°C (68°F). Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka receives an average of 263 cm/y (103 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 4114 m (13497 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka was founded durring the late 17th century in early spring of the year 1565, by Giant Blister Beetles Brîv. The establishment of Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka suffered from several major issues, resulting in the need to develop many solutions to basic problems. Problems such as a lack of fresh water, logistical support, poor quality tools, and the odd monster or two. Howeaver, these were overcome in time.

Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the late 17th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka is is constructed arround a semi-circular restrictive worn bedrock mainstreet, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The town posesses a fortified albit thin wall of querried stone. This wall posesses most of the features of a castle wall, though it is constructed from cheeper inferior stone. It would pose a minor chalange for an attacking army, though it's clear the wall's true purpose is to crush the hopes of bandits and marauders. The town's failry decent fortifications are visibly old, but also obviously well maintained. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with routine mantance of the town's defences.

A look around Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka is like a look into a broken heart which long ago fell to corruption. There is no planning, no organization. Everyone here clearly goes about their own thing with little thought to anyone around them who isn’t selling something they need… unless of course, they would be an easy target for some robbery. The town has truly fallen to evil.

Civic Infrastructure

Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka's parks.

Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka.

Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Town. Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka's grid is powered by an arcane means.

Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka's natural decorations nor waterways.

Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

There’s more than one leader in Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka, but at least some of them are at each other’s throats. It might be a conflict between formal leadership and informal authorities, or it could be a struggle among civil officials. Their interests might diverge sharply, or it could be a personal grudge that’s boiled over. Outside threats and internal problems are likely being ignored until the power struggle is resolved.

Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for the combined use of arcuated and trabeated construction, employing arches and constructed with post and lintel. The arch served as the style's chief structural element, with flanking columns serving as buttresses or decorations. While the general shape of their structures could be generously described as simplistic, the use of these arches and columns created a most distinct ascetic which is easily distinguishable on sight to armatures and experts alike.

In Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka there is a constant smell of overcooked presumably ethnic food.

The Gremlin near Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka are known to be quite timid.

Dofǐnu Nè Birěkhka's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves creating small tokens to channel Enchantment energies of tier 3 via chanting.
