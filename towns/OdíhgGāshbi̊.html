Odíhg Gāshbi̊ (/gəˑʃ/ /bi̘/ [rubber; tire] [usual]) is a subtropical Small Town located in Fallcastle Dutchy, Ftèwup Provence, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Odíhg Gāshbi̊ is derived from the Tauric language, as Odíhg Gāshbi̊ was founded by Spitting Eurypterids Fluellen, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Odíhg Gāshbi̊ has a yearly average temperature of 26°C (78°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 30°C (86°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 23°C (73°F). Odíhg Gāshbi̊ receives an average of 203 cm/y (79 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Odíhg Gāshbi̊ covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 4842 m (15885 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Odíhg Gāshbi̊ was founded durring the late 11th century, by Spitting Eurypterids Fluellen. The establishment of Odíhg Gāshbi̊ suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Odíhg Gāshbi̊ which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Odíhg Gāshbi̊ was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the late 11th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Odíhg Gāshbi̊ is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Odíhg Gāshbi̊ is buildings have been located at convienant points along the gorge Odíhg Gāshbi̊ was built upon. Navigating the town is therefore a little chalanging as the distance between buildings verris greatly and the narrow packed earth streets flow where they are able to be made rather than folowing the most convienant paths. The town posesses a fortified albit thin wall of querried stone. This wall posesses most of the features of a castle wall, though it is constructed from cheeper inferior stone. It would pose a minor chalange for an attacking army, though it's clear the wall's true purpose is to crush the hopes of bandits and marauders. Astonishigly, the failry decent fortifications are in pristine condishion, as if they had just been finished before you laied eyes upon them.

Right off the bat Odíhg Gāshbi̊ hits you in the face with its success. Everyone, even the peasants, are dressed in well made clothing. Every tool and implement you can see is finely made, and people will boast to you as obvious strangers of the wonders which can be found in their markets. More interestingly is a total lack of beggars, and plenty of new buildings are going up even as you speak. Somehow this town has come into quite a lot of wealth, and recently from the looks of things.

Cultural Notes

Odíhg Gāshbi̊'s locals despise outsiders. For some “outsiders” may be natives of foreign lands, while others might have a grudge against anyone from outside the community. These locals have an active loathing, and the outsiders allowed to trade or interact with them do so at a heavy disadvantage.

Odíhg Gāshbi̊'s chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is which made use of the classical orders and mathematically precise ratios of height and width combined with a desire for symmetry, proportion, and harmony. It used columns, pediments, arches and domes are imaginatively in buildings of all types. Decorative features were seen as largely unnecessary as the sheer beauty of the structure itself was often close to art. However, many buildings with large ceiling spaces had their ceilings decorated with elaborate paintings, simply because the large flat spaces could feel wasted.

In Odíhg Gāshbi̊ all of the cats speak in a dead language.

The Mole near Odíhg Gāshbi̊ are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Odíhg Gāshbi̊'s citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves creating small tokens to channel Charm energies of tier 1 via recitation of scripture.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Odíhg Gāshbi̊ was attacked by soldiers from another nation, waging a greater campaign. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Odíhg Gāshbi̊ lost 118 people, 279 livestock, and 79 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 211, when members of Odíhg Gāshbi̊'s militia enacted an operation to destroy or capture a specific enemy faction leader. The operation was complicated by the officers in charge fight among themselves, weakening the army as a whole. The conflict ended with pitched battle between both forces, which ended in a stalemate for Odíhg Gāshbi̊'s forces. The war is remembered in legend by Odíhg Gāshbi̊'s bards, historians, and legend keepers.
