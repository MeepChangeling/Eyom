Kèr Bègij (/bɛg/ /iʤ/ [century] [pick]) is a subtropical Town located in Sjeríhzùzzùzke County, Fidhi̊si Provence, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Kèr Bègij is derived from the Tauric language, as Kèr Bègij was founded by Vúńó̄ Erméchv 'Bash Dani' Vúńrmē Bé̄nḱ Mä̂f̄y Pritchardd, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Kèr Bègij has a yearly average temperature of 24°C (75°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 22°C (71°F). Kèr Bègij receives an average of 140 cm/y (55 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Kèr Bègij covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 4928 m (16167 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Kèr Bègij was founded durring the early 15th century in fall of the year 1408, by Vúńó̄ Erméchv 'Bash Dani' Vúńrmē Bé̄nḱ Mä̂f̄y Pritchardd. The establishment of Kèr Bègij suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Kèr Bègij which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Kèr Bègij was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the early 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Kèr Bègij is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Kèr Bègij is buildings folow an organic layout of crampt cobblestone streets whihch gives the town a shape simmilar to a tree, if one views its streets from above. The town lacks any defencive features, though certainly constructing even a simple fence or digging a ditch is at the forefront of the 's mind. At least, one would hope so.

A look around Kèr Bègij makes you worry someone might stab you in a dark ally for your boots. It’s not filthy, or dark, but the smiles seem strained, the locals seem to glare daggers in eachothers backs a little too much, and everyone is armed at all times. You may want to keep an eye on your valuables, and make sure you don’t wind up in any position of power.

Cultural Notes

One or more crime bosses have a powerful influence within Kèr Bègij. They may control crime within the community itself, or they may use it simply as a safe haven from which to direct their minions elsewhere. Local law enforcement may know all about them, but lack the strength to confront them and their paid or intimidated henchmen.

Kèr Bègij's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by order, symmetry, formal design, grandiosity, and elaborate ornamentation. Architectural characteristics include balustrades, balconies, columns, cornices, pilasters, and triangular pediments. Stone exteriors are massive and grandiose in their symmetry; interiors are typically polished and lavishly decorated with sculptures, swags, medallions, flowers, and shields. Interiors will often have a grand stairway and opulent ballroom..

In Kèr Bègij snow is warm to the touch and does not melt within city limits. Also it only happens during summer.

The ﻿Mole near Kèr Bègij are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Kèr Bègij's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves gestures to channel Mysticism energies of tier 3 via speaking in tongues.
