Odíhg Ftopùnucukē (Ftopù /ˈnucuc/ [laundry] [possibility]) is a subtropical Town located in the Màvēvāv Region of the Tetbur Commune.

The name Odíhg Ftopùnucukē is derived from the Tauric language, as Odíhg Ftopùnucukē was founded by Erist Naegle, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē has a yearly average temperature of 30°C (86°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 33°C (91°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 27°C (80°F). Odíhg Ftopùnucukē receives an average of 138 cm/y (54 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Odíhg Ftopùnucukē covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 3638 m (11935 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē was founded durring the early 14th century in winter of the year 1334, by Erist Naegle. The establishment of Odíhg Ftopùnucukē was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Erist Naegle electing to pay people to resettle in Odíhg Ftopùnucukē.

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the early 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Odíhg Ftopùnucukē is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē is is constructed arround a series of crampt baked earthen mainstreets which form overlapping circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The town rests behind a thick wall made from clay bricks. The wall has all of the proper fortifications and is well made. Unfortuantly the nature of clay brick leaves it quite vulnerable to siege equipment, though the thickness of the wall lends it simmilar resistnace to a thinner hardrock wall. Astonishigly, the millitarily questionable fortifications are in pristine condishion, as if they had just been finished before you laied eyes upon them.

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Odíhg Ftopùnucukē ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. Even with that as it is, everything is just a little too worn down, a little too dirty, or much of both. The town should be gone, not dying. No one makes eye contact. Kids play quietly, but happily. Occasionally a passerby glances at you out of the corner of their eye, staring just long enough for it to be uncomfortable.

Civic Infrastructure

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Odíhg Ftopùnucukē. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Odíhg Ftopùnucukē's parks.

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Odíhg Ftopùnucukē.

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Odíhg Ftopùnucukē's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Town. Odíhg Ftopùnucukē's grid is powered by hydrogalvanic generators.

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. In spite of the Galvanic Grid, these lights continue to use their old fule sources to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Odíhg Ftopùnucukē's natural decorations nor waterways.

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē is home to a University which provides higher education in a variety of fields, and also serves as a research institute for those same fields.

Cultural Notes

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is based upon new and innovative technologies of construction and the idea that form should follow function. It was an embrace of minimalism and a rejection of ornament. The style became characterized by an emphasis on volume, asymmetrical compositions, and minimal ornamentation..

In Odíhg Ftopùnucukē there are no smells.

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves destroying a prepared ritual vessel to channel Abjuration energies of tier 1 via moments of science.

Odíhg Ftopùnucukē was unknowingly built atop something unstable, and now that substrate is crumbling. History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century, Odíhg Ftopùnucukē was attacked by a group of notorious bandits. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Odíhg Ftopùnucukē lost 123 people, 297 livestock, and 31 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 187, when members of Odíhg Ftopùnucukē's militia enacted an operation to locate a particular solgiers group. The operation was complicated by a trusted officer who turned traitor and defects. The conflict ended with needing to break through the enemy's lines, which ended in a stalemate for Odíhg Ftopùnucukē's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Odíhg Ftopùnucukē's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
