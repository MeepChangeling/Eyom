Tmèvàwanwek Se (Tmèvà /ˈwanwek/ [laundry] [revolver (firearm)]) is a subtropical Town located in the Màvēvāv Region of the Tetbur Commune.

The name Tmèvàwanwek Se is derived from the Tauric language, as Tmèvàwanwek Se was founded by Håch Nash Wynney, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Tmèvàwanwek Se has a yearly average temperature of 30°C (86°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a blistering 34°C (93°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 27°C (80°F). Tmèvàwanwek Se receives an average of 154 cm/y (60 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Tmèvàwanwek Se covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 1930 m (6332 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Tmèvàwanwek Se was founded durring the early 15th century in spring of the year 1442, by Håch Nash Wynney. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Håch Nash Wynney.

Tmèvàwanwek Se was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the early 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Tmèvàwanwek Se is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Tmèvàwanwek Se is buildings have been located at convienant points along the rivers Tmèvàwanwek Se was built upon. Navigating the town is therefore a little chalanging as the distance between buildings verris greatly and the spacious cobblestone streets flow where they are able to be made rather than folowing the most convienant paths. The town sits behind a stone-renforced palisade wall, with stone gatehouses and timber drawbridges for their trench. Astonishigly, the would-be-castle fortifications are in pristine condishion, as if they had just been finished before you laied eyes upon them.

Your first impression of Tmèvàwanwek Se proves to be right on the money. This town is where the rebels chose to settle. Fashion trends are quite simply the opposite of what’s popular in the other nearby communities. THis trend extends everywhere. Things that should be black are white, what would normally be square is organically curved, and what is typically unthinkable for polite society is more than welcome in this public square. Up to the logical limits, of course. There’s no flaying someone alive to eat their skin going on, but the women are dressed a little immodestly, the men are a little more emotional, and the food is all overly complex and terrible.

Civic Infrastructure

Tmèvàwanwek Se has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Tmèvàwanwek Se. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Tmèvàwanwek Se's parks.

Tmèvàwanwek Se has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Tmèvàwanwek Se.

Tmèvàwanwek Se has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Tmèvàwanwek Se has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Tmèvàwanwek Se has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Tmèvàwanwek Se has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Tmèvàwanwek Se has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Tmèvàwanwek Se's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Tmèvàwanwek Se possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Town. Tmèvàwanwek Se's grid is powered by a god's will and kindness.

Tmèvàwanwek Se has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Tmèvàwanwek Se's natural decorations nor waterways.

Tmèvàwanwek Se has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Tmèvàwanwek Se has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Tmèvàwanwek Se's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is based upon new and innovative technologies of construction and the idea that form should follow function. It was an embrace of minimalism and a rejection of ornament. The style became characterized by an emphasis on volume, asymmetrical compositions, and minimal ornamentation..

In Tmèvàwanwek Se yeast remains dormant.

The ﻿Yothga near Tmèvàwanwek Se are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Tmèvàwanwek Se's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves creating small tokens to channel Conjuration energies of tier 2 via oath swearing.
