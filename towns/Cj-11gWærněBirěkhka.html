Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka (/ˈitpre/ /ˈwærnɛ/ [gray] [device; appliance; fixture]) is a subtropical Town located in Yv-4ilakhwik Zone, Pz-339jnup District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka is derived from the Constructi language, as Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka was founded by Nasarodon Brêmesm, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka has a yearly average temperature of 16°C (60°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 9°C (48°F). Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka receives an average of 215 cm/y (84 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 1196 m (3923 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka was founded durring the early 13th century in spring of the year 1243, by Nasarodon Brêmesm. The establishment of Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka suffered from several major issues, resulting in the need to develop many solutions to basic problems. Problems such as a lack of fresh water, logistical support, poor quality tools, and the odd monster or two. Howeaver, these were overcome in time.

Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the early 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka is buildings are speckled and packed arround restrictive packed earth streets with seemingly no patern to them. It appears as if the town's residents simply built streets as they pleased and squeazed buildings in wherever and howeave rpossible, creating an organic, frustrating to navigate, maze of a town. The town has a defencive wall made from large clay bricks. The wall is constructed to the exact specifications of millitary fortifications, but the nature of its clay brick construction leaves it vulnerable to even outdated siege equipment. That said, the town is well defended against anything short of an army. Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka's brittle defences are in an unremarkable state. To some, this is the ideal sate for defences to be in. In need of absoutly nothing, and ready to serve the town as needed.

A look around Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka gives you an uneasy feeling. Everything is just a little too worn down, a little too dirty, or both. No one makes eye contact. Kids play quietly, but happily. Occasionally a passerby glances at you out of the corner of their eye, staring just long enough for it to be uncomfortable. This all rests atop the unmistakable impression the town is one of the strictest places imaginable. Everyone’s actions are clearly directed by laws they keep in heart and mind at all times. Orderly byond order is a phrase which Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka brings to mind.

Civic Infrastructure

Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka has an animal control department which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the control, impoundment, and disposition of animals.

Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka's parks.

Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka.

Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Town. Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka's grid is powered by hydrogalvanic generators.

Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka's old civil lighting system was converted to Galvanic Lamps recently, and expanded to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka's natural decorations nor waterways.

Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for the combined use of arcuated and trabeated construction, employing arches and constructed with post and lintel. The arch served as the style's chief structural element, with flanking columns serving as buttresses or decorations. While the general shape of their structures could be generously described as simplistic, the use of these arches and columns created a most distinct ascetic which is easily distinguishable on sight to armatures and experts alike.

In Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka snow is warm to the touch and does not melt within city limits. Also it only happens during summer.

The Skinstitch near Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves long periods of drunkenness to channel Illusion energies of tier 2 via divine sermons.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century, Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka was attacked by soldiers from another nation, waging a greater campaign. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka lost 157 people, 376 livestock, and 59 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 87, when members of Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka's militia enacted an operation to secure a particular production site to utilize it for a specific task. The operation was complicated by blistering heat, such that many succumbed to heatstroke. The conflict ended with an assault and siege on the production site, which ended in a crushing defeat for Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Cj-11g Wærně Birěkhka's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
