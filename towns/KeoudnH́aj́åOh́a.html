The name Keoudn H́aj́å Oh́a is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as Keoudn H́aj́å Oh́a was founded by Passetura, who was culturaly Rosid.

Overview

Keoudn H́aj́å Oh́a was founded durring the late 13th century in spring of the year 1177, by Passetura. The establishment of Keoudn H́aj́å Oh́a suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Keoudn H́aj́å Oh́a which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Keoudn H́aj́å Oh́a was built using the conventions of Rosid durring the late 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Keoudn H́aj́å Oh́a is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Keoudn H́aj́å Oh́a is buildings are arranged arround a network of restrictive baked earthen streets which form a diagonal shaped grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The valley city sits behind a stone-renforced palisade wall, with stone gatehouses and timber drawbridges for their trench. The city's would-be-castle fortifications are visibly old, but also obviously well maintained. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with routine mantance of the city's defences.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century, Keoudn H́aj́å Oh́a was attacked by savage tribes living nearby Keoudn H́aj́å Oh́a. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Keoudn H́aj́å Oh́a lost 198 people, 361 livestock, and 22 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 216, when members of Keoudn H́aj́å Oh́a's militia enacted an operation to capture a particular enemy powerful weapon intact. The operation was complicated by a natural disaster interrupted the operation, shattering unit cohesion. The conflict ended with a last stand against the enemy until a particular event occurred, which ended in defeat for Keoudn H́aj́å Oh́a's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Keoudn H́aj́å Oh́a's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
