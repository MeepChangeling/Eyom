Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì (/doˈfɪnu/ /ˈnægdɛn kɨ/ [general; generic] [footprint]) is a subtropical Town located in Ib-51vsædgi Zone, Madraměvì District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì is derived from the Constructi language, as Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì was founded by Vq-34z 'Colossus' Êpí, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì has a yearly average temperature of 25°C (77°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 30°C (86°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 21°C (69°F). Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì receives an average of 264 cm/y (103 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 3684 m (12086 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì was founded durring the early 14th century in winter of the year 1348, by Vq-34z 'Colossus' Êpí. The establishment of Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the early 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì is buildings are arranged arround a network of premissive flagstone streets which form a diagonal shaped grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town is protected by a well-crafted cobblestone fence tall enough to provide adiquite cover for defenders to fire from, but no more than that. The frontieer-style defences has suffered a visible ammount of structural damage, leaving them effectivly useless. One can't help but wonder why the has not yet effected repairs.

Right off the bat Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì hits you in the face with its success. Everyone, even the peasants, are dressed in well made clothing. Every tool and implement you can see is finely made, and people will boast to you as obvious strangers of the wonders which can be found in their markets. More interestingly is a total lack of beggars, and plenty of new buildings are going up even as you speak. Somehow this town has come into quite a lot of wealth, and recently from the looks of things. The town is very clearly a joyfull place as well as wealthy. Music can be heard often, as well as laughter. The smell of food and drink permiates the air. You can’t help but smile.

Civic Infrastructure

Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì's parks.

Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì.

Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì's natural decorations nor waterways.

Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known primarily for its use of abstraction and simplicity. Clean lines, right angles, and primary colors characterized this aesthetic and art movement expressed via architecture and paintings. Its design ethos allows only primary colors and non-colors, only squares and rectangles, only straight and horizontal or vertical lines. Vertical and horizontal lines are positioned in layers or planes that do not intersect, thereby allowing each element to exist independently and unobstructed by other elements. These seemingly impossible principals for an architectural style coalesces into structures which most experts find hard to put into words. It is not that their geometry is impossible, but rather the style's attempt at producing works only describable visually was most successful..

In Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì sometimes, near one of the seasonal solstices, the sun appears to split into three and it rains from a clear sky for several hours.

The Assassin Vine near Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves sex to channel Transmutation energies of tier 3 via recitation of poetic epics.

Points of Interest

Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì's roads were poorly made when first laid. Rather than repairing them correctly, a series of new roads was laid atop the old, leading to the streets of modern Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì suffering from potholes, cracking, and even sinkholes. The locals often repair the road by putting down wooden decking.

History

Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì is in secret rebellion against their ostensible liege, having cut deals with his enemies, plotted to betray him for their own gain, or bridled under his tyranny and sought a better lord. The community’s leadership is all in on this plot, and outside viceroys or representatives are being kept carefully ignorant of the reality. The common folk may be oblivious to the truth, though they’ll doubtless have felt the same motivations and promptings that convinced their leaders to turn traitor.

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century, Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì was attacked by members of a peasant revolt. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì lost 161 people, 262 livestock, and 41 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 142, when members of Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì's militia enacted an operation to locate a specific spy. The operation was complicated by a key segment of the operation that deepened entirely on a stealth mission going perfectly. The conflict ended with pitched battle between both forces, which ended in victory for Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Wæm Ue-43m Nægděn Kì's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
