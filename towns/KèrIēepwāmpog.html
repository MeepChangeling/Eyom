Kèr Iēepwāmpog (/jep/ /ˈwəˑmpog/ [iron] [runner]) is a subtropical Large Town located in Undlåldechÿb Hold, Ststèsùp Provence, within the Tauranian United Provinces.

The name Kèr Iēepwāmpog is derived from the Tauric language, as Kèr Iēepwāmpog was founded by Tsåkh́ Gwilym Pughan, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Kèr Iēepwāmpog has a yearly average temperature of 26°C (78°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 30°C (86°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 22°C (71°F). Kèr Iēepwāmpog receives an average of 287 cm/y (112 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Kèr Iēepwāmpog covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 1340 m (4396 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Kèr Iēepwāmpog was founded durring the late 15th century in summer of the year 1373, by Tsåkh́ Gwilym Pughan. The establishment of Kèr Iēepwāmpog was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Tsåkh́ Gwilym Pughan electing to pay people to resettle in Kèr Iēepwāmpog.

Kèr Iēepwāmpog was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the late 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Kèr Iēepwāmpog is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Kèr Iēepwāmpog is buildings are built arround a single narrow cobblestone mainstreet which forms a counterclockwise spiral to give the town a over all circular shape. The town is protected by a renforced stone fence which sits atop earthwork defences, for some of the best inexpencive defences a town of Kèr Iēepwāmpog's size could have. Kèr Iēepwāmpog's well-designed, yet cheep are suffering from significent damage, so much so that examples can be pointed to no matter which section one might have within their line of site, and most of which render sections inoperable at present.

Kèr Iēepwāmpog has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Kèr Iēepwāmpog ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. Likely due to this, Kèr Iēepwāmpog is, in a word, disorder. People seem to be allowed to do as they please with little harmoney to anything. It feels less like a town, and more like a spot people just happened to have homes. One cannot help but wonder what Kèr Iēepwāmpog was like during the glorydays.

Cultural Notes

Kèr Iēepwāmpog's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for its functional shapes, abstract shapes used sparingly for decor, simple color schemes, holistic design, and basic industrial materials. Its simple designs were created to be beautiful, functional, and mass-producible. The style used little to no embellishment or ornamentation, instead drawing attention to the streamlined design, such as flat roofs to create a simple, geometric look. The simplicity masks the style's nearly sinister functionality, as every last feature is designed to guide the people living in the building in how to make the most efficient use of the structure.

In Kèr Iēepwāmpog there is always just enough rain to be annoying.

The Raven Swarm near Kèr Iēepwāmpog are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Kèr Iēepwāmpog's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves sacrificing an animal to channel Charm energies of tier 3 via speaking in tongues.
