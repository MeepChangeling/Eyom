Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ (/iˈjrurrəˑ/ /gi̘/ [border; boundary; verge] [navy blue]) is a subtropical Town located in Maqipulupusu County, Ftèwup Provence, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ is derived from the Tauric language, as Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ was founded by Caw̋chīs Joīń̄ 'Andre Bianca' Erméoī Nīńoī Sénvó Pughan, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ has a yearly average temperature of 26°C (78°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 30°C (86°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 23°C (73°F). Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ receives an average of 208 cm/y (81 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 4652 m (15262 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ was founded durring the early 12th century in spring of the year 1107, by Caw̋chīs Joīń̄ 'Andre Bianca' Erméoī Nīńoī Sénvó Pughan. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Caw̋chīs Joīń̄ 'Andre Bianca' Erméoī Nīńoī Sénvó Pughan.

Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the early 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ is buildings are arranged arround a single premissive cobblestone mainstreet with many smaller streets branching off of it which gives the town a over all rectangular shape, albit one warped and twisted by the nature of the curves of the main road. The town has a defencive wall made from large clay bricks. The wall is constructed to the exact specifications of millitary fortifications, but the nature of its clay brick construction leaves it vulnerable to even outdated siege equipment. That said, the town is well defended against anything short of an army. Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊'s brittle defences are in an unremarkable state. To some, this is the ideal sate for defences to be in. In need of absoutly nothing, and ready to serve the town as needed.

A look around Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ makes it abundantly clear the town suffered something horrible some time ago. It’s a quiet town filled with closed scriptoriums, people quietly yet dejectedly reading in isolated places around town, and the occasional book laying in a heap of garbage.

Civic Infrastructure

Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊'s parks.

Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊.

Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊'s public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊'s natural decorations nor waterways.

Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊'s bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by vertical proportions, pointed arches, external buttressing, and asymmetry in the general shape of its buildings. The decorative features of the style were key, consisting of large arched windows, pointed arches, vaulted ceilings, flying buttresses, and sculptures integrated into the structure itself. Occasionally, for very important buildings, an array of sculptures or one colossal sculpture might replace the entirety of the entrance to said building.

Due to the actions of local Kami, autumn is recurring in Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊.

The Armadillo near Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊ are known to be quite timid.

Odíhg Iiērurrāgi̊'s citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves square dance to channel Truename Magic energies of tier 3 via speaking in tongues.
