Caistle (/caistle / [Translation Unavailable]) is a subtropical Large Town located in Barrowwolf Dutchy, Ziirnea County, within the Kingdom of Helsteria.

The name Caistle is derived from the Sylvin language, as Caistle was founded by Snuthosh Tsmtho Clapton, who was culturaly Sylvin.

Climate

Caistle has a yearly average temperature of 19°C (66°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 23°C (73°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 16°C (60°F). Caistle receives an average of 203 cm/y (79 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Caistle covers an area of nearly 7 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 3482 m (11423 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Caistle was founded durring the late 10th century, by Snuthosh Tsmtho Clapton. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Snuthosh Tsmtho Clapton.

Caistle was built using the conventions of Sylvin durring the late 10th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Caistle is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Caistle is buildings are built arround a single premissive packed earth mainstreet which forms a counterclockwise spiral to give the town a over all circular shape. The town is the proud owner of a thick set of fortified walls fashioned from querried stone blocks. While not up to snuff for a fort or castle wall, the town's walls are naturaly much larger than those of forts or castles. Therefore, the construction such a wall is most expencive. Caistle 's buget focused wall would serve its community well in battle in spite of looking unimpressive compared to castles and fortresses. Caistle 's top tier civilian fortifications has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

A look around Caistle makes it abundantly clear the town suffered something horrible some time ago. It’s a quiet town filled with closed scriptoriums, people quietly yet dejectedly reading in isolated places around town, and the occasional book laying in a heap of garbage.

Civic Infrastructure

Caistle has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Caistle . They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Caistle 's parks.

Caistle has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Caistle .

Caistle has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Caistle has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Caistle has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Caistle 's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Caistle has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Caistle 's natural decorations nor waterways.

Caistle has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Caistle has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Caistle has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Caistle 's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by their massive, monolithic and 'blocky' appearance with a rigid geometric style. It was best known for its rough, unfinished surfaces, unusual shapes, heavy-looking materials, straight lines, and small windows. Modular elements were often used to form masses representing specific functional zones, grouped into a unified whole.

Due to the actions of local Kami, autumn is skipped in Caistle .

The Spider, Scarlet (Tiny) near Caistle are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Caistle 's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves line dance to channel Wild Magic energies of tier 2 via recitation of poetic epics.
