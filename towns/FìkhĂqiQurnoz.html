Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz (/ˈə̝qi/ /ˈqurnoz/ [comfortable] [militia]) is a subtropical Town located in Z̄ububulora Parish, Kosenilu District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz is derived from the Constructi language, as Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz was founded by Gilëd Tshëmeshe, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz has a yearly average temperature of 26°C (78°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 30°C (86°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 23°C (73°F). Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz receives an average of 150 cm/y (59 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 5322 m (17460 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz was founded durring the late 12th century in fall of the year 1085, by Gilëd Tshëmeshe. The establishment of Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz was only bairly constructed. The sheer number of problems with its founding were enough to make several of the backers funding Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz's construction back out of the project. Gilëd Tshëmeshe pushed on reguardles, and Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz was finished, but starts off as a terible place to live.

Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the late 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz is buildings are arranged within a network of crampt worn bedrock streets which form a rectangular grid, where each block verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller block has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town rests behind a thick wall made from clay bricks. The wall has all of the proper fortifications and is well made. Unfortuantly the nature of clay brick leaves it quite vulnerable to siege equipment, though the thickness of the wall lends it simmilar resistnace to a thinner hardrock wall. The town's millitarily questionable fortifications are visibly old, but also obviously well maintained. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with routine mantance of the town's defences.

A look around Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz has something terribly wrong with it. It’s impossible to put one’s finger on, but something is horribly wrong. Maybe it’s the way fog blankets the ground, but only in the connors of places. Maybe it’s the vermin scuttling between shadows in the corner of your eyes. Perhaps it’s the overcast sky which seemed to creep out of nowhere, or the distant howling of wolves. Maybe it’s all of those things together, or perhaps it's the way these elements combine which makes you worry someone might stab you in a dark ally for your boots. It’s not filthy, or dark, but the smiles seem strained, the locals seem to glare daggers in eachothers backs a little too much, and everyone is armed at all times. You may want to keep an eye on your valuables, and make sure you don’t wind up in any position of power. Regardless, you do not feel it would be wise to remain in Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz long.

Cultural Notes

Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by order, symmetry, formal design, grandiosity, and elaborate ornamentation. Architectural characteristics include balustrades, balconies, columns, cornices, pilasters, and triangular pediments. Stone exteriors are massive and grandiose in their symmetry; interiors are typically polished and lavishly decorated with sculptures, swags, medallions, flowers, and shields. Interiors will often have a grand stairway and opulent ballroom..

Due to the actions of local Kami, summer is long in Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz.

The Flea, Giant near Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz are known to be quite timid.

Fìkh Ăqi Qurnoz's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves consuming a local toxin to channel Augury energies of tier 3 via recitation of scripture.

The community’s leadership is all in on this plot, and outside viceroys or representatives are being kept carefully ignorant of the reality.
