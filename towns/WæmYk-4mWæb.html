Wæm Yk-4m Wæb (/ˈjɪslo/ /wæb/ [serious; sincere; severe; earnest] [knee]) is a subtropical Town located in Babagehiz̄i Zone, Sktkrmmepo District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Wæm Yk-4m Wæb is derived from the Constructi language, as Wæm Yk-4m Wæb was founded by Ga̋sh Yä̌shv 'Lena Bullet' Bërmér Séńó̄ Nīkida̋s Brëgfê, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Wæm Yk-4m Wæb has a yearly average temperature of 20°C (68°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 15°C (59°F). Wæm Yk-4m Wæb receives an average of 212 cm/y (83 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Wæm Yk-4m Wæb covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 8258 m (-15856 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Wæm Yk-4m Wæb was founded in time immemorial, reportedly some time durring the early 8th century, by Ga̋sh Yä̌shv 'Lena Bullet' Bërmér Séńó̄ Nīkida̋s Brëgfê. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Ga̋sh Yä̌shv 'Lena Bullet' Bërmér Séńó̄ Nīkida̋s Brëgfê.

Wæm Yk-4m Wæb was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the early 8th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Wæm Yk-4m Wæb is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

The town is protected by a renforced stone fence which sits atop earthwork defences, for some of the best inexpencive defences a town of Wæm Yk-4m Wæb's size could have. Wæm Yk-4m Wæb has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Wæm Yk-4m Wæb ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. Likely due to this, Wæm Yk-4m Wæb is, in a word, disorder. Cultural Notes

Wæm Yk-4m Wæb's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known for its buildings being predominantly formed through the combination of basic geometric shapes. However, it shown in the detailing given to the structures such as its characteristic tall columns, intricate detail, symmetry, harmony, and balance in their designs to an astonishing degree of precision. Decorative elements for the buildings tended to be built into the structure itself, making great use of fluting, frescoes, inlays, and embossing.

In Wæm Yk-4m Wæb yeast remains dormant.

The ﻿Fly, Giant near Wæm Yk-4m Wæb are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Wæm Yk-4m Wæb's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves line dance to channel Chronomancy energies of tier 1 via divine sermons.

Points of Interest

Wæm Yk-4m Wæb is remarkably well-fortified for a site of its size and role. Tall, stout walls, strong points inside the community, concentric defenses, a strategic terrain location, or a large body of standing troops might be present. Some threat is thought to exist that makes maintaining this fortification worthwhile, though it may come at a dear cost to the locals. The community’s suzerain may be uncomfortable with these defenses, as they could just as easily be used to defy the ruler.

Due to a magical anomaly, Wæm Yk-4m Wæb is directly accessible from a nearby river, despite the lack of a physical connection between the town's pond and the river.
