Kad Vjänf-znä (/vjɑnf/ /znɑ/ [opposite] [worm]) is a temperate Large City located in the Vēkæ̈piijuwitlu Principality of the Shayeli.

The name Kad Vjänf-znä is derived from the Goblin language, as Kad Vjänf-znä was founded by Zlèbêv, who was culturaly Gnoll.

Climate

Kad Vjänf-znä has a yearly average temperature of 5°C (41°F), with its average temperature during the summer being an icy 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing -14°C (7°F). Kad Vjänf-znä receives an average of 157 cm/y (61 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the short winter months. Kad Vjänf-znä covers an area of nearly 102 km2 (40 mi2), and an average elevation of 4916 m (16128 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Kad Vjänf-znä was founded durring the early 13th century in winter of the year 1249, by Zlèbêv. The establishment of Kad Vjänf-znä suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Kad Vjänf-znä which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Kad Vjänf-znä was built using the conventions of Gnoll durring the early 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Kad Vjänf-znä is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Kad Vjänf-znä is buildings are arranged arrounded highly ordered system of narrow cobblestone streets which form octogonal paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The city has a fortified albit thin brick wall. The wall has most of the feeatures of a typical castle wall, just on a much smaller scale and and budget. Notably brick isn't a particuarly soild choice for resisting siege weapons. Kad Vjänf-znä's wall wouldn't hinder a proper army, but it is more than sufishent for bandits and other small marauding groups. Kad Vjänf-znä's monster and outlaw focused fortifications has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

A look around Kad Vjänf-znä gives the unmistakable impression the town is one of the strictest places imaginable. Everyone’s actions are clearly directed by laws they keep in heart and mind at all times. Orderly byond order is a phrase which Kad Vjänf-znä brings to mind.

Civic Infrastructure

Kad Vjänf-znä possesses a Aethary Link for its accademic, government, and financial institutions. Public Aethary access is available through one or more of these intitutions.

Kad Vjänf-znä has an animal control department which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the control, impoundment, and disposition of animals.

Kad Vjänf-znä has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Kad Vjänf-znä. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Kad Vjänf-znä's parks.

Kad Vjänf-znä has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Kad Vjänf-znä.

Kad Vjänf-znä has a government-funded child care program, overseen by the local Department of Nursemaids, which is responsible for providing childcare to working-class citizens according to local ordinances.

Kad Vjänf-znä has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Kad Vjänf-znä has a Guild of Nurses, which is tasked with caring for the elderly and infirm in accordance with local ordinances, religious values, and customs.

Kad Vjänf-znä has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Kad Vjänf-znä has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Kad Vjänf-znä has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Kad Vjänf-znä has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Kad Vjänf-znä's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Kad Vjänf-znä has an Millitary Academy which trains military officers and specilists.

Kad Vjänf-znä possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Large City. Kad Vjänf-znä's grid is powered by a direct leyline tap.

Kad Vjänf-znä's old civil lighting system was converted to Galvanic Lamps recently, and expanded to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Kad Vjänf-znä has a first rate hospital which caters to anyone in need of long term medical care.

Kad Vjänf-znä has a library, which keeps a large collection of books, scrolls, and archives all manner of physical items. The library is open to the public, including the Aether Link.

Kad Vjänf-znä has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Kad Vjänf-znä's natural decorations nor waterways.

Kad Vjänf-znä has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Kad Vjänf-znä has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Kad Vjänf-znä has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Kad Vjänf-znä has a long tradition of martial expertise. This may be a crisply-organized history of skilled native levies, or it may be a natural belligerence in the people that leaves them familiar with bloodshed. While their neighbors and liege doubtless respect their talents, this very aptitude might make them more willing to turn to steel than prudence would advise.

Kad Vjänf-znä's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by order, symmetry, formal design, grandiosity, and elaborate ornamentation. Architectural characteristics include balustrades, balconies, columns, cornices, pilasters, and triangular pediments. Stone exteriors are massive and grandiose in their symmetry; interiors are typically polished and lavishly decorated with sculptures, swags, medallions, flowers, and shields. Interiors will often have a grand stairway and opulent ballroom..

In Kad Vjänf-znä sometimes, near one of the seasonal solstices, the sun appears to split into three and it rains from a clear sky for several hours.

The Soulsliver near Kad Vjänf-znä are known to be quite timid.

Kad Vjänf-znä's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves consuming a local toxin to channel Chronomancy energies of tier 3 via oath swearing.
