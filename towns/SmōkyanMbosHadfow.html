Smōkyan Mbos Hadfow (/ˈsmoːkjan/ /mbos/ [smooth; sleek] [code]) is a subtropical Large City located in the Ko̠ktugttö Moot of the Rosid.

The name Smōkyan Mbos Hadfow is derived from the Sylvin language, as Smōkyan Mbos Hadfow was founded by Jayden, who was culturaly Wood Elf.

Climate

Smōkyan Mbos Hadfow has a yearly average temperature of 13°C (55°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 26°C (78°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 1°C (33°F). Smōkyan Mbos Hadfow receives an average of 147 cm/y (57 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Smōkyan Mbos Hadfow covers an area of nearly 202 km2 (80 mi2), and an average elevation of 4430 m (14534 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Smōkyan Mbos Hadfow was founded durring the early 11th century, by Jayden. The establishment of the new community went well, with no major obsticles durring construction.

Smōkyan Mbos Hadfow was built using the conventions of Wood Elf durring the early 11th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Smōkyan Mbos Hadfow is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Smōkyan Mbos Hadfow is buildings are arranged arround a single broad cobblestone mainstreet with many smaller streets branching off of it which gives the city a over all rectangular shape, albit one warped and twisted by the nature of the curves of the main road. The city has a defencive wall made from large clay bricks. The wall is constructed to the exact specifications of millitary fortifications, but the nature of its clay brick construction leaves it vulnerable to even outdated siege equipment. That said, the city is well defended against anything short of an army. Smōkyan Mbos Hadfow's brittle defences are in an unremarkable state. To some, this is the ideal sate for defences to be in. In need of absoutly nothing, and ready to serve the city as needed.

A look around Smōkyan Mbos Hadfow has something terribly wrong with it. It’s impossible to put one’s finger on, but something is horribly wrong. Maybe it’s the way fog blankets the ground, but only in the connors of places. Maybe it’s the vermin scuttling between shadows in the corner of your eyes. Perhaps it’s the overcast sky which seemed to creep out of nowhere, or the distant howling of wolves. Maybe it’s all of those things together, or perhaps it's the way these elements combine with the simple fact that it is abundantly clear Smōkyan Mbos Hadfow suffered something horrible some time ago. It's as if the town itself is depressed. Smiles are few, cheer is nowhere to be had. Everyone quietly goes about their daily business not looking anyone in the eye. Regardless, you do not feel it would be wise to remain in Smōkyan Mbos Hadfow long.

Cultural Notes

Smōkyan Mbos Hadfow's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by order, symmetry, formal design, grandiosity, and elaborate ornamentation. Architectural characteristics include balustrades, balconies, columns, cornices, pilasters, and triangular pediments. Stone exteriors are massive and grandiose in their symmetry; interiors are typically polished and lavishly decorated with sculptures, swags, medallions, flowers, and shields. Interiors will often have a grand stairway and opulent ballroom..

In Smōkyan Mbos Hadfow during thunderstorms, with each flash of lightning, everyone sees different monsters from the corner of their eyes.

The Mud Elemental, Small near Smōkyan Mbos Hadfow are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Smōkyan Mbos Hadfow's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves embarking on a group pilgrimage to channel Invocation energies of tier 2 via recitation of scripture.
