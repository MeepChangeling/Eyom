Odíhg Gāwkùhij̼ (/gəˑw/ /ˈkʊhij̼/ [husband] [delicious]) is a subtropical Small Town located in Nuri̊m County, Krièvlùji̊ La Provence, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Odíhg Gāwkùhij̼ is derived from the Tauric language, as Odíhg Gāwkùhij̼ was founded by Nendes Castell Iwan, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Odíhg Gāwkùhij̼ has a yearly average temperature of 30°C (86°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a blistering 34°C (93°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 27°C (80°F). Odíhg Gāwkùhij̼ receives an average of 297 cm/y (116 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Odíhg Gāwkùhij̼ covers an area of nearly 3 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 1992 m (6535 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Odíhg Gāwkùhij̼ was founded durring the early 13th century in fall of the year 1245, by Nendes Castell Iwan. The establishment of Odíhg Gāwkùhij̼ suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Odíhg Gāwkùhij̼ which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Odíhg Gāwkùhij̼ was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the early 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Odíhg Gāwkùhij̼ is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature masoned stone construction which prominantly features pointed arches, pointed ribbed vault cielings, flying buttress', and window tracery all of which share a simmilar gemoetetic patern halfway between organic and inorganic in design formaing a very distinct aesthetically integrated style. BUildings tend to reach for the havens, and more expencive homes are easily identified by their floor count as well as the addition of decorative features intigrated into the building's design such as statues, gargoyals, and embelished joinery.

Odíhg Gāwkùhij̼ is buildings folow an organic layout of restrictive baked earthen streets whihch gives the town a shape simmilar to a tree, if one views its streets from above. The town has a defencive wall made from large clay bricks. The wall is constructed to the exact specifications of millitary fortifications, but the nature of its clay brick construction leaves it vulnerable to even outdated siege equipment. That said, the town is well defended against anything short of an army. Unfortuantly, these brittle defences are in extreem disrepair, so much so that one cannot tell if they are decaying from a lack of mantance or damage incured.

Odíhg Gāwkùhij̼ has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Odíhg Gāwkùhij̼ ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up.

Cultural Notes

Odíhg Gāwkùhij̼'s mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by their massive, monolithic and 'blocky' appearance with a rigid geometric style. It was best known for its rough, unfinished surfaces, unusual shapes, heavy-looking materials, straight lines, and small windows. Modular elements were often used to form masses representing specific functional zones, grouped into a unified whole.

In Odíhg Gāwkùhij̼ most nights are accompanied by colorful ribbons of light in the sky.

The Fox, Fading near Odíhg Gāwkùhij̼ are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.
