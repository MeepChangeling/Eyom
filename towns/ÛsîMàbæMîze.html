Ûsî Màbæ Mîze (/ˈʊsɪː/ /ˈməbæ/ [yellow; orange; brown] [rank (military); rank (position)]) is a temperate Town located in Sumipunabu Parish, Mî Kûrà Empire, within the Warrenese Shogunate.

The name Ûsî Màbæ Mîze is derived from the Wareneese language, as Ûsî Màbæ Mîze was founded by Dyëm Dyegare, who was culturaly Wareneese.

Climate

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze has a yearly average temperature of 8°C (46°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing -9°C (16°F). Ûsî Màbæ Mîze receives an average of 165 cm/y (64 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Ûsî Màbæ Mîze covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 4230 m (13877 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze was founded durring the late 9th century, by Dyëm Dyegare. The establishment of Ûsî Màbæ Mîze was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Dyëm Dyegare struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Ûsî Màbæ Mîze as a prison colony.

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze was built using the conventions of Wareneese durring the late 9th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Ûsî Màbæ Mîze is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze is buildings are located arround a single crampt paverstone mainstreet which forms a clockwise spiral to give the town a over all circular shape. The town is protected by a well-crafted cobblestone fence tall enough to provide adiquite cover for defenders to fire from, but no more than that. Ûsî Màbæ Mîze's frontieer-style defences has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

Right off the bat Ûsî Màbæ Mîze hits you in the face with its success. Everyone, even the peasants, are dressed in well made clothing. Every tool and implement you can see is finely made, and people will boast to you as obvious strangers of the wonders which can be found in their markets. More interestingly is a total lack of beggars, and plenty of new buildings are going up even as you speak. Somehow this town has come into quite a lot of wealth, and recently from the looks of things. The new wealth has created some worrying attitudes in many passers by, such that it’s clear new laws have recently been enacted and enforced with such extreme scrutiny the locals seem to be going about their day as if by clockwork.

Civic Infrastructure

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Ûsî Màbæ Mîze. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Ûsî Màbæ Mîze's parks.

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Ûsî Màbæ Mîze.

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Ûsî Màbæ Mîze's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Town. Ûsî Màbæ Mîze's grid is powered by mana accumulators.

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze's old civil lighting system was converted to Galvanic Lamps recently, and expanded to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Ûsî Màbæ Mîze's natural decorations nor waterways.

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by order, symmetry, formal design, grandiosity, and elaborate ornamentation. Architectural characteristics include balustrades, balconies, columns, cornices, pilasters, and triangular pediments. Stone exteriors are massive and grandiose in their symmetry; interiors are typically polished and lavishly decorated with sculptures, swags, medallions, flowers, and shields. Interiors will often have a grand stairway and opulent ballroom..

In Ûsî Màbæ Mîze the milk never sours.

The Venomroach near Ûsî Màbæ Mîze are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves destroying a prepared ritual vessel to channel Wild Magic energies of tier 3 via mimery.

History

Ûsî Màbæ Mîze's residents established a new industry (21294 % 6)+1 years ago, and it’s making them a great deal of profit. Old patterns of authority and wealth are being disrupted, and the old gentry are unlikely to be pleased about it. They may be trying to take over the industry, or they may have been the ones to enable it in the first place and are using it to crush the life out of any rival power bases. Outsiders might be playing a major role as well, and it could be they plot to siphon off the profits.

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century, Ûsî Màbæ Mîze was attacked by soldiers from another nation, waging a greater campaign. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Ûsî Màbæ Mîze lost 246 people, 305 livestock, and 64 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 67, when members of Ûsî Màbæ Mîze's militia enacted an operation to train a group of civilians for a special clandestine operation. The operation was complicated by a trusted officer who turned traitor and defects. The conflict ended with the defense of the mine against a siege, which ended in a crushing defeat for Ûsî Màbæ Mîze's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Ûsî Màbæ Mîze's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
