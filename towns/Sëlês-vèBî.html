Sëlês-vè Bî (/ˈkɪθa/ /vè/ [casual] [printing press]) is a subtropical Town located in the Nëpimtrêsë Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name Sëlês-vè Bî is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Sëlês-vè Bî was founded by Tsåk Treg̈kê, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Sëlês-vè Bî has a yearly average temperature of 27°C (80°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 30°C (86°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 24°C (75°F). Sëlês-vè Bî receives an average of 252 cm/y (99 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Sëlês-vè Bî covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 2510 m (8234 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Sëlês-vè Bî was founded durring the late 14th century in summer of the year 1266, by Tsåk Treg̈kê. The establishment of Sëlês-vè Bî suffered from several major issues, resulting in the need to develop many solutions to basic problems. Problems such as a lack of fresh water, logistical support, poor quality tools, and the odd monster or two. Howeaver, these were overcome in time.

Sëlês-vè Bî was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the late 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Sëlês-vè Bî is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Sëlês-vè Bî is buildings are located arround a single premissive carved bedrock mainstreet which forms a clockwise spiral to give the town a over all circular shape. The town has a fortified albit thin brick wall. The wall has most of the feeatures of a typical castle wall, just on a much smaller scale and and budget. Notably brick isn't a particuarly soild choice for resisting siege weapons. Sëlês-vè Bî's wall wouldn't hinder a proper army, but it is more than sufishent for bandits and other small marauding groups. Sëlês-vè Bî's monster and outlaw focused fortifications are suffering from significent damage, so much so that examples can be pointed to no matter which section one might have within their line of site, and most of which render sections inoperable at present.

Sëlês-vè Bî is not quite well. Something happened here, perhapse recently, perhapse long ago. Whatever it was, it settled into the very soul of the town like a festering wound. The people go about their day well enough, but there’s a tention in the air you can cut with a knife.

Civic Infrastructure

Sëlês-vè Bî has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Sëlês-vè Bî.

Sëlês-vè Bî has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Sëlês-vè Bî has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Sëlês-vè Bî has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Sëlês-vè Bî's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Sëlês-vè Bî has an Millitary Academy which trains military officers and specilists.

Sëlês-vè Bî has a first rate hospital which caters to anyone in need of long term medical care.

Sëlês-vè Bî has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

Sëlês-vè Bî has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Sëlês-vè Bî has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Sëlês-vè Bî's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is admittedly strange and non-linear style rooted in defiance of symmetrical shapes. It championed the creation of buildings with a unique visual appearance. the structural norms of classic buildings and deforms or moves away from elementary architectural principles. By including non-linear designs processed into its buildings and favoring fragmentation, this style expressed a form of controlled chaos. Its buildings appear out-of-the-ordinary, draw the eye in immediately and sometimes create a feeling of strangeness. These distorted shapes and structure are not reserved to the building’s outer facade, they destabilize interior elements too, favoring minimalism and play on people’s perceptions by injecting a futuristic touch.

In Sëlês-vè Bî there is no wind.

The ﻿Dallo near Sëlês-vè Bî are known to be quite timid.

Sëlês-vè Bî's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves consuming a local toxin to channel Enchantment energies of tier 1 via divine sermons.

Points of Interest

A great magical Working has been a critical part of Sëlês-vè Bî since its creation, but now it's beginning to decay. It may function only intermittently, now, or its effects may have curdled into something double-edged. The locals have no idea how to fix it, and indeed, it may not be possible to repair it with modern science or sorcery.

Sëlês-vè Bî is known for its odd use of round-a-bouts, small ring roads used in place of intersections.
