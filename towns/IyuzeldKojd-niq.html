Iyuzeld Kojd-niq (/kojd/ /niq/ [comic] [ship]) is a temperate Town located in the Sluptprond Principality of the Koiapian City-state.

The name Iyuzeld Kojd-niq is derived from the Gnollish language, as Iyuzeld Kojd-niq was founded by Tsialme vyim Roxlendcu, who was culturaly Gnollish.

Climate

Iyuzeld Kojd-niq has a yearly average temperature of 6°C (42°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a cold 8°C (46°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 4°C (39°F). Iyuzeld Kojd-niq receives an average of 236 cm/y (92 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Iyuzeld Kojd-niq covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 6442 m (21135 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Iyuzeld Kojd-niq was founded durring the late 15th century in fall of the year 1458, by Tsialme vyim Roxlendcu. The establishment of Iyuzeld Kojd-niq suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Iyuzeld Kojd-niq which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Iyuzeld Kojd-niq was built using the conventions of Gnollish durring the late 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Iyuzeld Kojd-niq is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Iyuzeld Kojd-niq is buildings are arranged arround a network of restrictive cobblestone streets which form a grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town is the proud owner of a thick set of fortified walls fashioned from querried stone blocks. While not up to snuff for a fort or castle wall, the town's walls are naturaly much larger than those of forts or castles. Therefore, the construction such a wall is most expencive. Iyuzeld Kojd-niq's buget focused wall would serve its community well in battle in spite of looking unimpressive compared to castles and fortresses. The top tier civilian fortifications has suffered a visible ammount of structural damage, leaving them effectivly useless. One can't help but wonder why the has not yet effected repairs.

A look around Iyuzeld Kojd-niq gives you an uneasy feeling. Everything is just a little too worn down, a little too dirty, or both. No one makes eye contact. Kids play quietly, but happily. Occasionally a passerby glances at you out of the corner of their eye, staring just long enough for it to be uncomfortable. This all rests atop the unmistakable impression the town is one of the strictest places imaginable. Everyone’s actions are clearly directed by laws they keep in heart and mind at all times. Orderly byond order is a phrase which Iyuzeld Kojd-niq brings to mind.

Civic Infrastructure

Iyuzeld Kojd-niq has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Iyuzeld Kojd-niq. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Iyuzeld Kojd-niq's parks.

Iyuzeld Kojd-niq has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Iyuzeld Kojd-niq.

Iyuzeld Kojd-niq has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Iyuzeld Kojd-niq has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Iyuzeld Kojd-niq has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Iyuzeld Kojd-niq has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Iyuzeld Kojd-niq has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Iyuzeld Kojd-niq's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Iyuzeld Kojd-niq possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Iyuzeld Kojd-niq has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Iyuzeld Kojd-niq's natural decorations nor waterways.

Iyuzeld Kojd-niq has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Iyuzeld Kojd-niq has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Iyuzeld Kojd-niq's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by order, symmetry, formal design, grandiosity, and elaborate ornamentation. Architectural characteristics include balustrades, balconies, columns, cornices, pilasters, and triangular pediments. Stone exteriors are massive and grandiose in their symmetry; interiors are typically polished and lavishly decorated with sculptures, swags, medallions, flowers, and shields. Interiors will often have a grand stairway and opulent ballroom..

In Iyuzeld Kojd-niq sometimes, near one of the seasonal solstices, the sun appears to split into three and it rains from a clear sky for several hours.

The Vukodlak near Iyuzeld Kojd-niq are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Iyuzeld Kojd-niq's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves line dance to channel Abjuration energies of tier 1 via throat singing.

Points of Interest

Many of Iyuzeld Kojd-niq’s structures date back to the ancient past and a long-vanished culture. They have unique architectural traits, perhaps being made of some strange substance or with uncanny qualities. The locals find them too useful or too durable to destroy, but the buildings often have unpleasant little surprises in their under-explored corners, and there may be greater structures still buried by long ages beneath Iyuzeld Kojd-niq’s streets.

Iyuzeld Kojd-niq is known for its odd use of round-a-bouts, small ring roads used in place of intersections.
