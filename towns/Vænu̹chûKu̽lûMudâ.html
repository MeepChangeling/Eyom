Vænu̹chû Ku̽lû Mudâ (/væˈnu̹ʧʊ/ /ˈku̽lʊ/ [due] [will]) is a subtropical Large City located in Qàcha Qûs̺ilæ County, Napesia Earldom, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Vænu̹chû Ku̽lû Mudâ is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as Vænu̹chû Ku̽lû Mudâ was founded by Sam, who was culturaly Warrenese.

Climate

Vænu̹chû Ku̽lû Mudâ has a yearly average temperature of 23°C (73°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 28°C (82°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 18°C (64°F). Vænu̹chû Ku̽lû Mudâ receives an average of 255 cm/y (100 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Vænu̹chû Ku̽lû Mudâ covers an area of nearly 270 km2 (108 mi2), and an average elevation of 1512 m (4960 ft) above sea level.

Cultural Notes

Vænu̹chû Ku̽lû Mudâ's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for its functional shapes, abstract shapes used sparingly for decor, simple color schemes, holistic design, and basic industrial materials. Its simple designs were created to be beautiful, functional, and mass-producible. The style used little to no embellishment or ornamentation, instead drawing attention to the streamlined design, such as flat roofs to create a simple, geometric look. The simplicity masks the style's nearly sinister functionality, as every last feature is designed to guide the people living in the building in how to make the most efficient use of the structure.

In Vænu̹chû Ku̽lû Mudâ during thunderstorms, with each flash of lightning, everyone sees different monsters from the corner of their eyes.

The Amoeba, Giant near Vænu̹chû Ku̽lû Mudâ are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Vænu̹chû Ku̽lû Mudâ's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves consuming a local narcotic to channel Charm energies of tier 2 via mimery.

History

Vænu̹chû Ku̽lû Mudâ used to be more prosperous, but something happened relatively long ago that left it a shrunken shadow of its former self. If the settlement is prosperous, the locals often lament how much more they could have had. If the settlement is not prosperous, the locals blame their ill fortunes on that event. Reminders of this better time can be found in many places within Vænu̹chû Ku̽lû Mudâ.

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century the Kami spared the town a natural disaster. One of Vænu̹chû Ku̽lû Mudâ's local festivals commemorates this miracle.
