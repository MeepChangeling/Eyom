Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v (Stsdiyà /kɪ̞ʃ/ [wall; barrier; hurdle] [result; outcome]) is a subtropical Large City located in the Nijhobbub Region of the Tetburland.

The name Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v is derived from the Sylvin language, as Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v was founded by Treshêbe, who was culturaly Tauran.

Climate

Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v has a yearly average temperature of 21°C (69°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 17°C (62°F). Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v receives an average of 215 cm/y (84 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v covers an area of nearly 226 km2 (90 mi2), and an average elevation of 852 m (2795 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v was founded durring the early 11th century, by Treshêbe. The establishment of Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v was only bairly constructed. The sheer number of problems with its founding were enough to make several of the backers funding Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v's construction back out of the project. Treshêbe pushed on reguardles, and Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v was finished, but starts off as a terible place to live.

Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v was built using the conventions of Tauran durring the early 11th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature masoned stone construction which prominantly features pointed arches, pointed ribbed vault cielings, flying buttress', and window tracery all of which share a simmilar gemoetetic patern halfway between organic and inorganic in design formaing a very distinct aesthetically integrated style. BUildings tend to reach for the havens, and more expencive homes are easily identified by their floor count as well as the addition of decorative features intigrated into the building's design such as statues, gargoyals, and embelished joinery.

Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v is buildings are arranged arrounded highly ordered system of restrictive paverstone streets which form hexical paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The city resides behind a palisade wall complete with battlments, a moat, and timber gatehouses with drawbridges. Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v's robustly designed timber walls are in an unremarkable state. To some, this is the ideal sate for defences to be in. In need of absoutly nothing, and ready to serve the city as needed.

Looking around Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v you immediately realise that the locals are looking back at you. All of them. Everywhere you look somone is staring back at you analytically, looking over every inch of you, your gear, and your companions. Cultural Notes

Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used has a sleek, linear appearance with stylized, often geometric ornamentation. The primary facade of its buildings often featured a series of set backs that create a stepped outline. Low-relief decorative panels can be found at entrances, around windows, along roof edges or as string courses. It was best known for its use of smooth finish building materials such as stucco, concrete block, glazed brick or mosaic tile. Decorative details can incorporate various artistic or exotic motifs to suit the building's function or the architect's whim. Chevrons, zigzags, and other geometrical motifs are common forms of ornament.

In Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v the utterance of expletives is impossible within city limits. This has changed as the limits have changed, but has not kept up with slang, or swearing in foreign languages.

The Yothga near Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v are known to be quite timid.

Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves sex to channel Elven High Magic energies of tier 2 via divine sermons.

Points of Interest

While private tutelage of worthy apprentices can be had even in most remote villages, Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v is home to a proper school dedicated to teaching magic. Such schools are usually small, with no more than a few dozen pupils, most of whom will fail for lack of talent or discipline. The instructors are rarely first-rate, usually serving only for the pay and status, but sometimes a genius sorcerer will find a reason to observe likely apprentices here. Given the unfortunate accident potential of the school, it’s probably isolated or well-fortified.

Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v's roads were poorly made when first laid. Rather than repairing them correctly, a series of new roads was laid atop the old, leading to the streets of modern Stsdiyàkíhsh Ji̊ Qi̊v suffering from potholes, cracking, and even sinkholes. The locals often repair the road by putting down wooden decking.
