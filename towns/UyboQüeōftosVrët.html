Uybo Qüeōftos Vrët (/ˈujbo/ /ˈq˔oːftos/ [compact] [trouble; problem]) is a subtropical Town located in Trunjetmole Zone, Nto̠ftǟmǟw District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Uybo Qüeōftos Vrët is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as Uybo Qüeōftos Vrët was founded by Zlelêv Ezaenzo, who was culturaly Wood Elven.

Climate

Uybo Qüeōftos Vrët has a yearly average temperature of 16°C (60°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 9°C (48°F). Uybo Qüeōftos Vrët receives an average of 255 cm/y (100 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Uybo Qüeōftos Vrët covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 9134 m (-12982 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Uybo Qüeōftos Vrët was founded durring the early 9th century, by Zlelêv Ezaenzo. The establishment of the new community went well, with no major obsticles durring construction.

Uybo Qüeōftos Vrët was built using the conventions of Wood Elven durring the early 9th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Uybo Qüeōftos Vrët is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Uybo Qüeōftos Vrët is buildings are arranged arround a network of broad packed earth streets which form a diagonal shaped grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town emploies a series of defencive earthworks and fences to provide minimal protection against wild beasts and smaller groups of intelegent foes. Uybo Qüeōftos Vrët's somewhat suffishent are suffering from significent damage, so much so that examples can be pointed to no matter which section one might have within their line of site, and most of which render sections inoperable at present.

Right off the bat Uybo Qüeōftos Vrët hits you in the face with its success. Everyone, even the peasants, are dressed in well made clothing. Every tool and implement you can see is finely made, and people will boast to you as obvious strangers of the wonders which can be found in their markets. More interestingly is a total lack of beggars, and plenty of new buildings are going up even as you speak. Somehow this town has come into quite a lot of wealth, and recently from the looks of things.

Cultural Notes

Uybo Qüeōftos Vrët's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is which made use of the classical orders and mathematically precise ratios of height and width combined with a desire for symmetry, proportion, and harmony. It used columns, pediments, arches and domes are imaginatively in buildings of all types. Decorative features were seen as largely unnecessary as the sheer beauty of the structure itself was often close to art. However, many buildings with large ceiling spaces had their ceilings decorated with elaborate paintings, simply because the large flat spaces could feel wasted.

In Uybo Qüeōftos Vrët most nights are accompanied by colorful ribbons of light in the sky.

The Herd Animal, Sheep near Uybo Qüeōftos Vrët are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Uybo Qüeōftos Vrët's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves gestures to channel Wild Magic energies of tier 1 via recitation of poetic epics.
