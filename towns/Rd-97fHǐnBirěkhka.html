Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka (/ˈmɪtʤɛ/ /hɪn/ [exact; precise] [ceiling]) is a subtropical Town located in Vnöfof Parish, Liwbărǐm District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka is derived from the Constructi language, as Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka was founded by Syntoma Tsîgesêg̈ Trgë, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka has a yearly average temperature of 30°C (86°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a blistering 34°C (93°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 27°C (80°F). Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka receives an average of 219 cm/y (86 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 2162 m (7093 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka was founded durring the late 14th century in winter of the year 1296, by Syntoma Tsîgesêg̈ Trgë. The establishment of Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Syntoma Tsîgesêg̈ Trgë struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka as a prison colony.

Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the late 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka is buildings are arranged arround a single broad cobblestone mainstreet with many smaller streets branching off of it which gives the town a over all rectangular shape, albit one warped and twisted by the nature of the curves of the main road. The town rests behind the absurdity that is a thick, timber braced, wall made of clay bricks. While visualy impressive and certainly an astetic, Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka's wall provides no actual defence against siege equipment due to the choice of its cosntruction materials. Even nonexperts can tell the town is trying to impress rather than defend with its walls, towers, and gatehouses. Though admittedly, they do look nice... To primitive tribals who have never seen fortifications before. The town's political statment focused walls have recently undergone extensive repairs and renovations, such that the repairwork is imeadiently apparent and can be spotted due to the diffring ages of materials. One can't help but wonder what brought the need for those repairs to the town.

Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. The locals seem to have responded to their slow downfall by recreating Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka as one of the strictest places imaginable. Everyone’s actions are clearly directed by laws they keep in heart and mind at all times. Orderly byond order is a phrase which the town brings to mind.

Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Town. Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka's grid is powered by a direct leyline tap.

Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka's old civil lighting system was converted to Galvanic Lamps recently, and expanded to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka's natural decorations nor waterways.

Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by order, symmetry, formal design, grandiosity, and elaborate ornamentation. Architectural characteristics include balustrades, balconies, columns, cornices, pilasters, and triangular pediments. Stone exteriors are massive and grandiose in their symmetry; interiors are typically polished and lavishly decorated with sculptures, swags, medallions, flowers, and shields. Interiors will often have a grand stairway and opulent ballroom..

Due to the actions of local Kami, spring is short in Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka.

The Giant hissing cockroach near Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka are known to be quite timid.

Rd-97f Hǐn Birěkhka's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves sacrificing an animal to channel Transmutation energies of tier 3 via divine sermons.
