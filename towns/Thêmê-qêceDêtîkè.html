Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè (/ˈʃɘmɘ/ /ˈqɘce/ [ironic] [spectrum]) is a temperate Town located in the Qíîsîrêyê Sêpíêto Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè was founded by Vris Glkëmêpí, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè has a yearly average temperature of 27°C (80°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 30°C (86°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 25°C (77°F). Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè receives an average of 222 cm/y (87 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 1954 m (6410 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè was founded durring the early 13th century in summer of the year 1203, by Vris Glkëmêpí. The establishment of the new community went well, with no major obsticles durring construction.

Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the early 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè is buildings are arranged arround a single spacious cobblestone mainstreet with many smaller streets branching off of it which gives the town a over all rectangular shape, albit one warped and twisted by the nature of the curves of the main road. The town is protected by a renforced stone fence which sits atop earthwork defences, for some of the best inexpencive defences a town of Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè's size could have. Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè's well-designed, yet cheep are suffering from significent damage, so much so that examples can be pointed to no matter which section one might have within their line of site, and most of which render sections inoperable at present.

Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè has a very calm atmosphere. People can be seen relaxing, scocilizing, and going about all manner of business other than the daily grind. Men, women, children, all can be seen enjoying life in a laid-back way in the many parks which line Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè’s streets. The town is very clearly a joyfull place as well as relaxing. Civic Infrastructure

Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè's parks.

Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè.

Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Town. Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè's grid is powered by hydrogalvanic generators.

Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè's natural decorations nor waterways.

Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known for its fluid and florid elaborate style, comprising ornate, asymmetric designs and pastel shades. It is often considered to be a playful, light style, which made exuberant use of curves and emphasized subtle asymmetry in the general shape of its structures. Walls, ceilings and moldings are decorated with numerous interlacing of curves and counter-curves based on the shapes of ‘C’ and ‘S’, along with shell forms and other naturalistic shapes.

Due to the actions of local Kami, summer is short in Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè.

The ﻿Eel Hound near Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Thêmê-qêce Dêtîkè's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves consuming a local narcotic to channel Conjuration energies of tier 1 via throat singing.
