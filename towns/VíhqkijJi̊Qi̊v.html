Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v (/vɪ̞q/ /kij/ [load] [previous]) is a subtropical Large City located in Sjerfíhùzyíh County, Tafogin Provence, within the Daland.

The name Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v is derived from the Goblin language, as Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v was founded by Glkeshonkdo, who was culturaly Tauran.

Climate

Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v has a yearly average temperature of 22°C (71°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 20°C (68°F). Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v receives an average of 136 cm/y (53 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v covers an area of nearly 100 km2 (40 mi2), and an average elevation of 6550 m (-21460 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v was founded durring the early 13th century in fall of the year 1221, by Glkeshonkdo. The establishment of Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Glkeshonkdo struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v as a prison colony.

Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v was built using the conventions of Tauran durring the early 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v is buildings are arranged arround a single spacious cobblestone mainstreet with many smaller streets branching off of it which gives the city a over all rectangular shape, albit one warped and twisted by the nature of the curves of the main road. The city is defended by arcane means. It's hard to spot at first, but there's a tell tell shimmer in the air arround Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v, and you can spot the ocasional warding glyph carved into a rock or tree all arround town. These mystical defences are ancient, unknowable, and unassailable by current means... Assuming everything is in working order. Otherwise, the wards are little more than a deathtrap. Astonishigly, the Relic of the World That Was are in pristine condishion, as if they had just been finished before you laied eyes upon them.

Right off the bat Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v hits you in the face with its success. Everyone, even the peasants, are dressed in well made clothing. Every tool and implement you can see is finely made, and people will boast to you as obvious strangers of the wonders which can be found in their markets. More interestingly is a total lack of beggars, and plenty of new buildings are going up even as you speak. Somehow this city has come into quite a lot of wealth, and recently from the looks of things. The city is very clearly a joyfull place as well as wealthy. Music can be heard often, as well as laughter. The smell of food and drink permiates the air. You can’t help but smile.

Cultural Notes

While Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v has a public leader, the real authority is hidden from outsiders. This ruler may draw their authority from rationales unacceptable to outsiders, they may have cowed the public authority into obedience, or they may have a mutually beneficial private arrangement with the official ruler.

Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known primarily for its use of abstraction and simplicity. Clean lines, right angles, and primary colors characterized this aesthetic and art movement expressed via architecture and paintings. Its design ethos allows only primary colors and non-colors, only squares and rectangles, only straight and horizontal or vertical lines. Vertical and horizontal lines are positioned in layers or planes that do not intersect, thereby allowing each element to exist independently and unobstructed by other elements. These seemingly impossible principals for an architectural style coalesces into structures which most experts find hard to put into words. It is not that their geometry is impossible, but rather the style's attempt at producing works only describable visually was most successful..

In Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v yeast remains dormant.

The Ramidreju near Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Víhqkij Ji̊ Qi̊v's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves gestures to channel Invocation energies of tier 1 via speaking in tongues.
