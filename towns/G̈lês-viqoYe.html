G̈lês-viqo Ye (/ˈfèzɪ/ /ˈviqo/ [stupid; dumb; silly] [screen; curtain]) is a subtropical Large City located in the Aselumeman County of the Union of Engineers.

The name G̈lês-viqo Ye is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as G̈lês-viqo Ye was founded by Glêtesho, who was culturaly Iron Elvish.

Climate

G̈lês-viqo Ye has a yearly average temperature of 22°C (71°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 21°C (69°F). G̈lês-viqo Ye receives an average of 213 cm/y (83 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. G̈lês-viqo Ye covers an area of nearly 270 km2 (108 mi2), and an average elevation of 5352 m (17559 ft) above sea level.

Overview

G̈lês-viqo Ye was founded durring the late 14th century in spring of the year 1351, by Glêtesho. The establishment of G̈lês-viqo Ye was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Glêtesho electing to pay people to resettle in G̈lês-viqo Ye.

G̈lês-viqo Ye was built using the conventions of Iron Elvish durring the late 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and G̈lês-viqo Ye is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

G̈lês-viqo Ye is is constructed arround a semi-circular broad baked earthen mainstreet, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The riverside city posesses a mighty albit amaturly crafted stone wall. It was built using querried stone blocks and posesses all of the standard defencive features, including a few watch towers. While it would last against a siege, the wall's outdated and simplistic construction method percludes the possability of G̈lês-viqo Ye weathering a full scale bombardment durring a siege. G̈lês-viqo Ye's budget focused millitary grade defenses are in an unremarkable state. To some, this is the ideal sate for defences to be in. In need of absoutly nothing, and ready to serve the city as needed.

Right off the bat G̈lês-viqo Ye hits you in the face with its success. Everyone, even the peasants, are dressed in well made clothing. Every tool and implement you can see is finely made, and people will boast to you as obvious strangers of the wonders which can be found in their markets. More interestingly is a total lack of beggars, and plenty of new buildings are going up even as you speak. Many of those buildings are schools, scriptoriums, and even one college. One can only wonder what knowledge the town has come into.

G̈lês-viqo Ye is home to a University which provides higher education in a variety of fields, and also serves as a research institute for those same fields.

Cultural Notes

G̈lês-viqo Ye is home to a distinct subculture, who are either the majority here or have the dominant positions in the community. Architecture, local laws, and social customs are all tuned to suit them, and they may not be particularly forgiving or friendly to the major culture of the region. Communities that are not outright independent usually make an arm's-length submission to a local lord.

G̈lês-viqo Ye's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for the combined use of arcuated and trabeated construction, employing arches and constructed with post and lintel. The arch served as the style's chief structural element, with flanking columns serving as buttresses or decorations. While the general shape of their structures could be generously described as simplistic, the use of these arches and columns created a most distinct ascetic which is easily distinguishable on sight to armatures and experts alike.

Due to the actions of local Kami, autumn is recurring in G̈lês-viqo Ye.

The Vodyanoi near G̈lês-viqo Ye are known to be quite timid.

G̈lês-viqo Ye's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves line dance to channel Invocation energies of tier 3 via chanting.
