Sdo Drark-gháp ([root words not on file] [Translation Unavailable]) is a temperate Metropolis located in the Brö Qöv Meadows of the meadow.

The name Sdo Drark-gháp is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as Sdo Drark-gháp was founded by Vur Tununzic Tibe, who was culturaly Wood Elven.

Climate

Sdo Drark-gháp has a yearly average temperature of 30°C (86°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a blistering 34°C (93°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 27°C (80°F). Sdo Drark-gháp receives an average of 297 cm/y (116 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Sdo Drark-gháp covers an area of nearly 658 km2 (263 mi2), and an average elevation of 1920 m (6299 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Sdo Drark-gháp was founded durring the early 9th century, by Vur Tununzic Tibe. The establishment of Sdo Drark-gháp was only bairly constructed. The sheer number of problems with its founding were enough to make several of the backers funding Sdo Drark-gháp's construction back out of the project. Vur Tununzic Tibe pushed on reguardles, and Sdo Drark-gháp was finished, but starts off as a terible place to live.

Sdo Drark-gháp was built using the conventions of Wood Elven durring the early 9th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Sdo Drark-gháp is no diffrent. The metropolis's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Sdo Drark-gháp is is constructed arround a series of spacious worn bedrock mainstreets which form overlapping circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The byou metropolis rests behind a thick wall made from clay bricks. The wall has all of the proper fortifications and is well made. Unfortuantly the nature of clay brick leaves it quite vulnerable to siege equipment, though the thickness of the wall lends it simmilar resistnace to a thinner hardrock wall. The metropolis's millitarily questionable fortifications are visibly old, but also obviously maintained semi-regularly. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with ocasional mantance of the metropoliss defences.

A look around Sdo Drark-gháp gives the unmistakable impression the town is one of the strictest places imaginable. Everyone’s actions are clearly directed by laws they keep in heart and mind at all times. Orderly byond order is a phrase which Sdo Drark-gháp brings to mind.

Civic Infrastructure

Sdo Drark-gháp possesses a Aethary Link for its accademic, government, and financial institutions. Public Aethary access is available through one or more of these intitutions.

Sdo Drark-gháp has an animal control department which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the control, impoundment, and disposition of animals.

Sdo Drark-gháp has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Sdo Drark-gháp. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Sdo Drark-gháp's parks.

Sdo Drark-gháp has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Sdo Drark-gháp.

Sdo Drark-gháp has a government-funded child care program, overseen by the local Department of Nursemaids, which is responsible for providing childcare to working-class citizens according to local ordinances.

Sdo Drark-gháp has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Sdo Drark-gháp has a Guild of Nurses, which is tasked with caring for the elderly and infirm in accordance with local ordinances, religious values, and customs.

Sdo Drark-gháp has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Sdo Drark-gháp has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Sdo Drark-gháp has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Sdo Drark-gháp has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Sdo Drark-gháp's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Sdo Drark-gháp has an Arcane Academy which provides higher education in the arcane sciences.

Sdo Drark-gháp possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Metropolis. Sdo Drark-gháp's grid is powered by a god's will and kindness.

Sdo Drark-gháp's old civil lighting system was converted to Galvanic Lamps recently, and expanded to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Sdo Drark-gháp has a first rate hospital which caters to anyone in need of long term medical care.

Sdo Drark-gháp has a library, which keeps a large collection of books, scrolls, and archives all manner of physical items. While not open to the public, the librarians and scholars employed by the library will assist anyone with their research needs, and wealthy individuals can purchase membership to access the library's materials themselves. In spite of being generally closed to the public, the library has a room with several Aether Linked devices available to the public during business hours.

Sdo Drark-gháp has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Sdo Drark-gháp's natural decorations nor waterways.

Sdo Drark-gháp has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Sdo Drark-gháp has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Sdo Drark-gháp has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Sdo Drark-gháp is home to a University which provides higher education in a variety of fields, and also serves as a research institute for those same fields.

Cultural Notes

Sdo Drark-gháp is led by one or more incompetents. While they must have been very good at something to have acquired the position, they are fundamentally incapable of leading. Uncontrolled passions or lusts, commitment to a hopelessly impractical ideal, pigheaded obstinacy in the face of failure, a total lack of charisma or interpersonal skills, or profound laziness might all unfit them for their post.

Sdo Drark-gháp's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known for its buildings being predominantly formed through the combination of basic geometric shapes. However, it shown in the detailing given to the structures such as its characteristic tall columns, intricate detail, symmetry, harmony, and balance in their designs to an astonishing degree of precision. Decorative elements for the buildings tended to be built into the structure itself, making great use of fluting, frescoes, inlays, and embossing.

In Sdo Drark-gháp sometimes, near one of the seasonal solstices, the sun appears to split into three and it rains from a clear sky for several hours.
