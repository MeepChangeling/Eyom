Vè-êyî Fêqê (/vè/ /ˈɘjɪ/ [printing press] [game; sport]) is a subtropical Large Town located in Mijlejeji Dutchy, Mèhabicê Provence, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Vè-êyî Fêqê is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Vè-êyî Fêqê was founded by Fyegfêyèj Tresëg̈, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Vè-êyî Fêqê has a yearly average temperature of 21°C (69°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 19°C (66°F). Vè-êyî Fêqê receives an average of 152 cm/y (59 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Vè-êyî Fêqê covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 5356 m (17572 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Vè-êyî Fêqê was founded durring the late 16th century in summer of the year 1474, by Fyegfêyèj Tresëg̈. The establishment of Vè-êyî Fêqê was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Fyegfêyèj Tresëg̈ struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Vè-êyî Fêqê as a prison colony.

Vè-êyî Fêqê was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the late 16th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Vè-êyî Fêqê is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Vè-êyî Fêqê is buildings have been located at convienant points along the rivers Vè-êyî Fêqê was built upon. Navigating the town is therefore a little chalanging as the distance between buildings verris greatly and the premissive flagstone streets flow where they are able to be made rather than folowing the most convienant paths. The town is the proud owner of a properly designed set of renforced walls made from mighty querried stone blocks. Their construction and material choices would make a dwarf weap with joy, for each and every part of the elaborate fortifications are purly functional and robust well byond reason. Even nonexperts can tell the walls are an excelent defencive structure. Vè-êyî Fêqê's exceptionaly well made fortifications has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

The town shows nothing out of the ordinary, at first glance. Then you start to notice all the small things. Each window has seven iron nails pounded into the sill. Every door has a sprig of holly overhead. Every well is branded with purity seals and runes to ward the contents. Everyone is carrying a small charm for safety or fortune, be it a rabbit’s foot, a horseshoe, or other little totems.

Civic Infrastructure

Vè-êyî Fêqê has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Vè-êyî Fêqê. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Vè-êyî Fêqê's parks.

Vè-êyî Fêqê has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Vè-êyî Fêqê.

Vè-êyî Fêqê has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Vè-êyî Fêqê has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Vè-êyî Fêqê has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Vè-êyî Fêqê's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Vè-êyî Fêqê possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Vè-êyî Fêqê has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Vè-êyî Fêqê's natural decorations nor waterways.

Vè-êyî Fêqê has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Vè-êyî Fêqê has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Vè-êyî Fêqê's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is which made use of the classical orders and mathematically precise ratios of height and width combined with a desire for symmetry, proportion, and harmony. It used columns, pediments, arches and domes are imaginatively in buildings of all types. Decorative features were seen as largely unnecessary as the sheer beauty of the structure itself was often close to art. However, many buildings with large ceiling spaces had their ceilings decorated with elaborate paintings, simply because the large flat spaces could feel wasted.

In Vè-êyî Fêqê leaves and flower petals never touch the ground.

The Spider, Assassin (Giant) near Vè-êyî Fêqê are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Vè-êyî Fêqê's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves destroying a prepared ritual vessel to channel Necromancy energies of tier 3 via recitation of scripture.

Vè-êyî Fêqê used to be more prosperous, but something happened relatively long ago that left it a shrunken shadow of its former self. If the settlement is prosperous, the locals often lament how much more they could have had. If the settlement is not prosperous, the locals blame their ill fortunes on that event.
