Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ (/ˈbʊbu/ /ˈmuni/ [black; dark] [pan; saucepan; pot]) is a temperate Large City located in Triqtppipasu Kingdom, Mijgake Empire, within the Warren.

The name Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ is derived from the Sylvin language, as Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ was founded by Tarad, who was culturaly Rattu.

Climate

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has a yearly average temperature of 16°C (60°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 8°C (46°F). Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ receives an average of 73 cm/y (28 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ covers an area of nearly 102 km2 (40 mi2), and an average elevation of 6378 m (20925 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ was founded durring the early 15th century in spring of the year 1421, by Tarad. The establishment of Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ was only bairly constructed. The sheer number of problems with its founding were enough to make several of the backers funding Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ's construction back out of the project. Tarad pushed on reguardles, and Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ was finished, but starts off as a terible place to live.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ was built using the conventions of Rattu durring the early 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ is is constructed arround a semi-circular premissive cobblestone mainstreet, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The city is the proud owner of a thick set of fortified walls fashioned from querried stone blocks. While not up to snuff for a fort or castle wall, the city's walls are naturaly much larger than those of forts or castles. Therefore, the construction such a wall is most expencive. Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ's buget focused wall would serve its community well in battle in spite of looking unimpressive compared to castles and fortresses. The city's top tier civilian fortifications are visibly old, but also obviously well maintained. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with routine mantance of the city's defences.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has the unmistakable air of a city on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ ’s existence has dried up and the city is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. Even with that as it is, everything is just a little too worn down, a little too dirty, or much of both. The town should be gone, not dying. No one makes eye contact. Kids play quietly, but happily. Occasionally a passerby glances at you out of the corner of their eye, staring just long enough for it to be uncomfortable.

Civic Infrastructure

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ possesses a Aethary Link for its accademic, government, and financial institutions. Public Aethary access is available through one or more of these intitutions.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has an animal control department which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the control, impoundment, and disposition of animals.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ's parks.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has a government-funded child care program, overseen by the local Department of Nursemaids, which is responsible for providing childcare to working-class citizens according to local ordinances.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has a Guild of Nurses, which is tasked with caring for the elderly and infirm in accordance with local ordinances, religious values, and customs.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has an Arcane Academy which provides higher education in the arcane sciences.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Large City. Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ's grid is powered by a boiler and turbine based power plant.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. In spite of the Galvanic Grid, these lights continue to use their old fule sources to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has a first rate hospital which caters to anyone in need of long term medical care.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has a library, which keeps a large collection of books, scrolls, and archives all manner of physical items. While not open to the public, the librarians and scholars employed by the library will assist anyone with their research needs, and wealthy individuals can purchase membership to access the library's materials themselves. In spite of being generally closed to the public, the library has a room with several Aether Linked devices available to the public during business hours.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ's natural decorations nor waterways.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is inspired by the natural world, characterized by sinuous, sculptural, organic shapes, arches, curving lines, and sensual ornamentation. Common motifs included stylized versions of leaves, flowers, vines, insects, animals, and other natural elements. Decorative elements found on the inside and outside of buildings include intricate mosaic work, curved windows, and decorative trim work. .

In Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ sometimes, near one of the seasonal solstices, the sun appears to split into three and it rains from a clear sky for several hours.

The Shredskin near Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Bûbu Muni Kâchyâ's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves line dance to channel Conjuration energies of tier 1 via guttural bellowing.
