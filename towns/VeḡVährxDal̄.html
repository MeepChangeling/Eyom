Veḡ Vährx Dal̄ (/liːz/ /dall/ [Propername] [fan (enthusiast)]) is a temperate Large City located in Ckÿchäckewck Hold, Balar Province, within the United Kingdom of Undermountain.

The name Veḡ Vährx Dal̄ is derived from the Dwarven language, as Veḡ Vährx Dal̄ was founded by Anguipiones Nuldzafral, who was culturaly Dwarvern.

Climate

Veḡ Vährx Dal̄ has a yearly average temperature of 21°C (69°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 17°C (62°F). Veḡ Vährx Dal̄ receives an average of 250 cm/y (98 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Veḡ Vährx Dal̄ covers an area of nearly 536 km2 (214 mi2), and an average elevation of 2022 m (6633 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Veḡ Vährx Dal̄ was founded durring the late 16th century in winter of the year 1498, by Anguipiones Nuldzafral. The establishment of Veḡ Vährx Dal̄ was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Anguipiones Nuldzafral electing to pay people to resettle in Veḡ Vährx Dal̄.

Veḡ Vährx Dal̄ was built using the conventions of Dwarvern durring the late 16th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Veḡ Vährx Dal̄ is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Veḡ Vährx Dal̄ is buildings are grouped arround an odd layout of spacious cobblestone streets, which seems to be based on an overlapping squair patern such that there are small squares at the cornor of every bigger square. Sometimes buildings exist in the smaller squaires, other times they are open spaces, or occupied by temporary structures. The riverside city has a fortified albit thin brick wall. The wall has most of the feeatures of a typical castle wall, just on a much smaller scale and and budget. Notably brick isn't a particuarly soild choice for resisting siege weapons. Veḡ Vährx Dal̄'s wall wouldn't hinder a proper army, but it is more than sufishent for bandits and other small marauding groups. The monster and outlaw focused fortifications have not been wellmaintained over the years, and while functional are in dire need of some loving care and perhapse light renovation.

Veḡ Vährx Dal̄ has a very calm atmosphere. People can be seen relaxing, scocilizing, and going about all manner of business other than the daily grind. Men, women, children, all can be seen enjoying life in a laid-back way in the many parks which line Veḡ Vährx Dal̄’s streets.

Cultural Notes

Veḡ Vährx Dal̄'s chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is famous for its stately symmetry, classical elements, and grand appearance. Columns and pillars, such as Corinthian columns, are often seen supporting open structures or porticos. Symmetry is an important feature of this style, with each half of a building mirroring the other. Domed ceilings and windows grace these buildings, with everything placed in a mathematical arrangement.

Due to the actions of local Kami, spring is long in Veḡ Vährx Dal̄.

The Mercane near Veḡ Vährx Dal̄ are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Veḡ Vährx Dal̄'s citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves orgies to channel Wild Magic energies of tier 3 via chanting.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century, Veḡ Vährx Dal̄ was attacked by soldiers from another nation, waging a greater campaign. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Veḡ Vährx Dal̄ lost 170 people, 376 livestock, and 92 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 106, when members of Veḡ Vährx Dal̄'s militia enacted an operation to fortify a particular production site. The operation was complicated by a trusted officer who turned traitor and defects. The conflict ended with an assault and siege on the production site, which ended in defeat for Veḡ Vährx Dal̄'s forces. The war is remembered in legend by Veḡ Vährx Dal̄'s bards, historians, and legend keepers.
