Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê (/ˈsɘbɘ/ /ˈqɘg̃ɘ/ [stubborn] [verse]) is a temperate Village located in the Skhokraskhobuba Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê was founded by Naykhie Brtheg̈, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê has a yearly average temperature of 22°C (71°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 20°C (68°F). Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê receives an average of 72 cm/y (28 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê covers an area of nearly 3 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 6830 m (-20541 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê was founded durring the early 9th century, by Naykhie Brtheg̈. The establishment of Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Naykhie Brtheg̈ electing to pay people to resettle in Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê.

Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the early 9th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê is no diffrent. The village's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê is buildings are arranged arrounded a highly ordered system of crampt paverstone streets which form triangular paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The village lacks any defencive features, though certainly constructing even a simple fence or digging a ditch is at the forefront of the 's mind. At least, one would hope so.

A look around Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê seems to be home to a quite vibrant and boisterous community. Everywhere one looks they can see people going out their daily business with a smile and a spring in their step. Children play loudly in the streets, causing untold havoc as youth are want and allowed to do.

Civic Infrastructure

Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is admittedly strange and non-linear style rooted in defiance of symmetrical shapes. It championed the creation of buildings with a unique visual appearance. the structural norms of classic buildings and deforms or moves away from elementary architectural principles. By including non-linear designs processed into its buildings and favoring fragmentation, this style expressed a form of controlled chaos. Its buildings appear out-of-the-ordinary, draw the eye in immediately and sometimes create a feeling of strangeness. These distorted shapes and structure are not reserved to the building’s outer facade, they destabilize interior elements too, favoring minimalism and play on people’s perceptions by injecting a futuristic touch.

In Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê there are unidentifiable people in the fog, but it seems to be okay.

The Serpentfolk near Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves destroying a prepared ritual vessel to channel Summoning energies of tier 2 via recitation of scripture.

History

Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê used to be much richer, but something happened in the last (31314 % 6)+1 years to crush its source of prosperity. Different factions of the community might be trying to grasp at the remaining dregs of wealth, others might try to restart the failed industry, and some might look for a new livelihood. Any group or entity thought responsible for the collapse is likely to be treated very harshly, and some locals might find profit in shifting the blame to their enemies.

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century Lake Vihar began to boil, and released a thick toxic cloud from beneath its waters which was isolated to the area around Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê, which was swallowed by the fumes for several days. Sêbê-qêg̈ê Fêqê lost 161 people, 194 livestock, and 83 buildings in the disaster, though it is of note the local plant life flourished after the disaster. The disaster is referred to as the Loss Wind.
