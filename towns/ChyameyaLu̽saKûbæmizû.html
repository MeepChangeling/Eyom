Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû (/ruˈkəbə/ /ˌkʊbæˈmizʊ/ [attractive] [record]) is a temperate Large City located in the Temmī̄f Principality of the Lallalo.

The name Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû is derived from the Sylvin language, as Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû was founded by Dyênhê, who was culturaly Rattu.

Climate

Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû has a yearly average temperature of 5°C (41°F), with its average temperature during the summer being an icy 22°C (71°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing -12°C (11°F). Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû receives an average of 73 cm/y (28 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû covers an area of nearly 195 km2 (78 mi2), and an average elevation of 5552 m (18215 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû was founded durring the early 13th century in fall of the year 1207, by Dyênhê. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Dyênhê.

Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû was built using the conventions of Rattu durring the early 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû is was constructed arround several spacious baked earthen mainstreets which cross one another at certain axies, with smaller streets branching off of them to premit acess to the many buildings deeper into the road network. The overall fashion is remenessent of a circulatory system, or other organic construct, and is quite effishent in its design. The city is the proud owner of a thick set of fortified walls fashioned from querried stone blocks. While not up to snuff for a fort or castle wall, the city's walls are naturaly much larger than those of forts or castles. Therefore, the construction such a wall is most expencive. Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû's buget focused wall would serve its community well in battle in spite of looking unimpressive compared to castles and fortresses. Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû's top tier civilian fortifications are in an unremarkable state. To some, this is the ideal sate for defences to be in. In need of absoutly nothing, and ready to serve the city as needed.

A look around Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû gives you an uneasy feeling. Everything is just a little too worn down, a little too dirty, or both. No one makes eye contact. Kids play quietly, but happily. Occasionally a passerby glances at you out of the corner of their eye, staring just long enough for it to be uncomfortable. This all rests atop the unmistakable impression the town is one of the strictest places imaginable. Everyone’s actions are clearly directed by laws they keep in heart and mind at all times. Orderly byond order is a phrase which Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû brings to mind.

They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû's natural decorations nor waterways.

Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû is home to a University which provides higher education in a variety of fields, and also serves as a research institute for those same fields.

Cultural Notes

Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû's locals are divided into several castes. They may be organized by social role, by imputed nobility of birth, by ethnic origins, or any other dividing principle, but they cannot imagine any other way of organizing themselves. A hierarchy of castes is not inevitable, but there will be social and legal limits applied to ensure that each caste remains fixed in its function. The outside world may or may not respect these distinctions when dealing with the locals.

Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by grandeur of scale, simplicity of geometric forms, dramatic use of columns, and a preference for blank walls. This design ethos extended to re-imagining earlier styles of structure to create them anew, with a similar overall look and feel to one another..

In Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû yeast remains dormant.

The Giant hissing cockroach near Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves performance art to channel Invocation energies of tier 1 via mimery.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century Loch Urpad began to boil, and released a thick toxic cloud from beneath its waters which was isolated to the area around Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû, which was swallowed by the fumes for several days. Chyameya Lu̽sa Kûbæmizû lost 218 people, 198 livestock, and 29 buildings in the disaster, though it is of note the local plant life flourished after the disaster. The disaster is referred to as the Anguish Wind.
