Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ (/ˈvisa/ /ˈdas̺æ/ [middle] [gun; rifle; pistol]) is a subtropical Large City located in Gokâzû Kîmu Kingdom, Gæchà Dîrîdî Empire, within the Warren.

The name Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ is derived from the Sylvin language, as Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ was founded by Nīkida̋s Ha̋mërmē 'Frivolous Bullet' Da̋chīy Vúóchīy Cä̌sh, who was culturaly Rattu.

Climate

Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ has a yearly average temperature of 12°C (53°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing -3°C (27°F). Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ receives an average of 307 cm/y (120 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ covers an area of nearly 59 km2 (23 mi2), and an average elevation of 4080 m (13385 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ was founded durring the early 10th century, by Nīkida̋s Ha̋mërmē 'Frivolous Bullet' Da̋chīy Vúóchīy Cä̌sh. The establishment of Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Nīkida̋s Ha̋mërmē 'Frivolous Bullet' Da̋chīy Vúóchīy Cä̌sh electing to pay people to resettle in Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ.

Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ was built using the conventions of Rattu durring the early 10th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature masoned stone construction which prominantly features pointed arches, pointed ribbed vault cielings, flying buttress', and window tracery all of which share a simmilar gemoetetic patern halfway between organic and inorganic in design formaing a very distinct aesthetically integrated style. BUildings tend to reach for the havens, and more expencive homes are easily identified by their floor count as well as the addition of decorative features intigrated into the building's design such as statues, gargoyals, and embelished joinery.

Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ is is constructed arround a series of premissive worn bedrock mainstreets which form concentric circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to each other at varrious points. The city rests behind the absurdity that is a thick, timber braced, wall made of clay bricks. While visualy impressive and certainly an astetic, Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ's wall provides no actual defence against siege equipment due to the choice of its cosntruction materials. Even nonexperts can tell the town is trying to impress rather than defend with its walls, towers, and gatehouses. Though admittedly, they do look nice... To primitive tribals who have never seen fortifications before. The city's political statment focused walls have recently undergone extensive repairs and renovations, such that the repairwork is imeadiently apparent and can be spotted due to the diffring ages of materials. One can't help but wonder what brought the need for those repairs to the city.

A look around Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ makes it abundantly clear the city suffered something horrible some time ago. It’s a quiet city filled with closed scriptoriums, people quietly yet dejectedly reading in isolated places around town, and the occasional book laying in a heap of garbage.

Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ is home to a University which provides higher education in a variety of fields, and also serves as a research institute for those same fields.

Cultural Notes

Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for its striking sculptural forms and often dazzling ornamental detail that characterizes the buildings general shape. The radiant colors, rich patterns, and symmetrical silhouettes employed by this style were backed up by rich decorative features including gardens, courtyards, extruded arches, domes, pointed domes, vaulted ceilings, elaborate painted and inlaid designs, and decorative sculptures.

Due to the actions of local Kami, summer is recurring in Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ.

The Calathgar near Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Lûhyû Das̺æ Kâchyâ's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves consuming a local narcotic to channel Augury energies of tier 3 via speaking in tongues.
