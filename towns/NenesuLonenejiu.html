Nenesu Lonenejiu (/ˈlone/ /ˈneʤu/ [task; chore] [physical]) is a subtropical Large Town located in the Ůch Ḿåwa Moot of the Viceroyalty of Rosid.

The name Nenesu Lonenejiu is derived from the Faeish language, as Nenesu Lonenejiu was founded by Belnyl Qoůh́aynts, who was culturaly Fae.

Climate

Nenesu Lonenejiu has a yearly average temperature of 8°C (46°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing -7°C (20°F). Nenesu Lonenejiu receives an average of 148 cm/y (58 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Nenesu Lonenejiu covers an area of nearly 7 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 5714 m (18746 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Nenesu Lonenejiu was founded durring the early 11th century, by Belnyl Qoůh́aynts. The establishment of the new community went well, with no major obsticles durring construction.

Nenesu Lonenejiu was built using the conventions of Fae durring the early 11th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Nenesu Lonenejiu is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Nenesu Lonenejiu is buildings are arranged arrounded highly ordered system of crampt canals streets which form hexical paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The town posesses a mighty albit amaturly crafted stone wall. It was built using querried stone blocks and posesses all of the standard defencive features, including a few watch towers. While it would last against a siege, the wall's outdated and simplistic construction method percludes the possability of Nenesu Lonenejiu weathering a full scale bombardment durring a siege. The budget focused millitary grade defenses have not been wellmaintained over the years, and while functional are in dire need of some loving care and perhapse light renovation.

Nenesu Lonenejiu is a bustling hive of activity. Everyone moves at nothing short of a jog, each convocation is a mile a minute, and there’s dedicated lanes for riding through town in the center of each street. The locals all appear to be not merely occupied, but in a true hurry for everything from drinking a pint of ale to their daily work.

Civic Infrastructure

Nenesu Lonenejiu has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Nenesu Lonenejiu. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Nenesu Lonenejiu's parks.

Nenesu Lonenejiu has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Nenesu Lonenejiu.

Nenesu Lonenejiu has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Nenesu Lonenejiu has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Nenesu Lonenejiu has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Nenesu Lonenejiu's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Nenesu Lonenejiu possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Nenesu Lonenejiu has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Nenesu Lonenejiu's natural decorations nor waterways.

Nenesu Lonenejiu has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Nenesu Lonenejiu has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Nenesu Lonenejiu has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Nenesu Lonenejiu's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by grandeur of scale, simplicity of geometric forms, dramatic use of columns, and a preference for blank walls. This design ethos extended to re-imagining earlier styles of structure to create them anew, with a similar overall look and feel to one another..

In Nenesu Lonenejiu tiny harmless tornadoes plague the town.

The Marble Snake near Nenesu Lonenejiu are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Nenesu Lonenejiu's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves square dance to channel Transmutation energies of tier 2 via singing.

Points of Interest

While private tutelage of worthy apprentices can be had even in most remote villages, Nenesu Lonenejiu is home to a proper school dedicated to teaching magic. Such schools are usually small, with no more than a few dozen pupils, most of whom will fail for lack of talent or discipline. The instructors are rarely first-rate, usually serving only for the pay and status, but sometimes a genius sorcerer will find a reason to observe likely apprentices here. Given the unfortunate accident potential of the school, it’s probably isolated or well-fortified.
