Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè (/ʃiˈgëɪ/ /ˈdɘcɘ/ [random] [chance; odds]) is a subtropical Large Town located in Nepimakho County, Pifîkèli Provence, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè was founded by Shnih Shonhê, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè has a yearly average temperature of 31°C (87°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a blistering 35°C (95°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 27°C (80°F). Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè receives an average of 142 cm/y (55 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 2944 m (9658 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè was founded durring the early 12th century in spring of the year 1106, by Shnih Shonhê. The establishment of Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Shnih Shonhê electing to pay people to resettle in Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè.

Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the early 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè is buildings are located arround a single premissive cobblestone mainstreet which forms a clockwise spiral to give the town a over all circular shape. The town is the proud owner of a properly designed set of renforced walls made from mighty querried stone blocks. Their construction and material choices would make a dwarf weap with joy, for each and every part of the elaborate fortifications are purly functional and robust well byond reason. Even nonexperts can tell the walls are an excelent defencive structure. Unfortuantly, these exceptionaly well made fortifications are in extreem disrepair, so much so that one cannot tell if they are decaying from a lack of mantance or damage incured.

Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. On top of this is an unmistakable feeling that Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè is in this condition because there is something terribly wrong with the town. Maybe it’s the way fog blankets the ground, but only in the connors of places. Maybe it’s the vermin scuttling between shadows in the corner of your eyes. Perhaps it’s the overcast sky which seemed to creep out of nowhere, or the distant howling of wolves. Maybe it’s all of those things together, or perhaps it's the way these elements combine which makes you worry someone might stab you in a dark ally for your boots. It’s not filthy, or dark, but the smiles seem strained, the locals seem to glare daggers in eachothers backs a little too much, and everyone is armed at all times. You may want to keep an eye on your valuables, and make sure you don’t wind up in any position of power. Regardless, you do not feel it would be wise to remain in Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè long.

Civic Infrastructure

Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè's parks.

Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè.

Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè has an Arcane Academy which provides higher education in the arcane sciences.

Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Large Town. Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè's grid is powered by an arcane means.

Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè's natural decorations nor waterways.

Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

There’s a relatively new religion in Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè which is rapidly gaining power. It might be a sectarian offshoot of a major faith, the unique product of a new prophet, or an outside faith backed by wealthy and powerful foreign supporters. Depending on the demands made on believers, the new faith may be a matter of concern only to the existing clergy, or it might be a major flashpoint for conflict in the community.

Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by their massive, monolithic and 'blocky' appearance with a rigid geometric style. It was best known for its rough, unfinished surfaces, unusual shapes, heavy-looking materials, straight lines, and small windows. Modular elements were often used to form masses representing specific functional zones, grouped into a unified whole.

Due to the actions of local Kami, spring is long in Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè.

The Howler near Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè are known to be quite timid.

Shigëî-dêcê Dêtîkè's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves sex to channel Charm energies of tier 2 via throat singing.
