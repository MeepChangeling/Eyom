Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy (/mbɛ́æ/ /kaʊ̯̌ɜː/ [corn] [ornament; accessory; fixture]) is a subtropical Town located in the Hatupujuqe Provence of the Kingdom of Hobben.

The name Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy is derived from the Goblin language, as Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy was founded by Brêmepî Èdsho Glkë, who was culturaly Hobgoblin.

Climate

Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy has a yearly average temperature of 23°C (73°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 28°C (82°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 19°C (66°F). Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy receives an average of 125 cm/y (49 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 5826 m (19114 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy was founded durring the early 12th century in fall of the year 1125, by Brêmepî Èdsho Glkë. The establishment of Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Brêmepî Èdsho Glkë struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy as a prison colony.

Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy was built using the conventions of Hobgoblin durring the early 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy is is constructed arround a series of spacious gravel mainstreets which form concentric circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to each other at varrious points. The town emploies a series of defencive earthworks and fences to provide minimal protection against wild beasts and smaller groups of intelegent foes. Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy's somewhat suffishent has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

Before you’ve even set foot into the heart of Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy, you can smell it. The incense. It hangs about the town like a cloud. Monks, priests, and clerics are everywhere, all dedicated to the same god, all performing the same rituals to bless and anoint buildings, streets, people, animals, you name it they are or have blessed it. The same holy symbol is everywhere too. Its on buildings, on people, and even branded into livestock. This certainly loves its god. More than it loves wealth. The town is very clearly poor. Buildings are run down in ways that are not imeadiatly obvious. The people are a bit too thin. The market is very eager to sell to newcomers, but not so eager to buy from them. There’s also a general lack of the hum and buzz of healthy industry in Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy.

Civic Infrastructure

Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy.

Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy has a long tradition of martial expertise. This may be a crisply-organized history of skilled native levies, or it may be a natural belligerence in the people that leaves them familiar with bloodshed. While their neighbors and liege doubtless respect their talents, this very aptitude might make them more willing to turn to steel than prudence would advise.

Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known primarily for its use of abstraction and simplicity. Clean lines, right angles, and primary colors characterized this aesthetic and art movement expressed via architecture and paintings. Its design ethos allows only primary colors and non-colors, only squares and rectangles, only straight and horizontal or vertical lines. Vertical and horizontal lines are positioned in layers or planes that do not intersect, thereby allowing each element to exist independently and unobstructed by other elements. These seemingly impossible principals for an architectural style coalesces into structures which most experts find hard to put into words. It is not that their geometry is impossible, but rather the style's attempt at producing works only describable visually was most successful..

Due to the actions of local Kami, summer is short in Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy.

The Blood Hawk near Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves destroying a prepared ritual vessel to channel Illusion energies of tier 3 via moments of science.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy was rocked by a string of powerful winter storms. The worst of the disaster struck The storm coated everything in a thick layer of ice and freezing countless animals, plants, and people to death. Mbá́ē Coǔr Vǖy lost 184 people, and 203 livestock in the disaster.. The disaster is remembered as the Horror Screams, after the sounds of the winds.
