Muv Vreqüeiz Ner (/muv/ /ˈvreq˔iz/ [polite] [reservation]) is a subtropical Large Town located in Ănaniw Zone, Olaöt Diocese, within the Covenent of Irus.

The name Muv Vreqüeiz Ner is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as Muv Vreqüeiz Ner was founded by Hèshi Eenzin, who was culturaly Wood Elven.

Climate

Muv Vreqüeiz Ner has a yearly average temperature of 17°C (62°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 26°C (78°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 8°C (46°F). Muv Vreqüeiz Ner receives an average of 143 cm/y (56 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Muv Vreqüeiz Ner covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 5936 m (19475 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Muv Vreqüeiz Ner was founded durring the early 16th century in summer of the year 1550, by Hèshi Eenzin. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Hèshi Eenzin.

Muv Vreqüeiz Ner was built using the conventions of Wood Elven durring the early 16th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Muv Vreqüeiz Ner is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Muv Vreqüeiz Ner is was constructed arround several narrow cobblestone mainstreets which cross one another at certain axies, with smaller streets branching off of them to premit acess to the many buildings deeper into the road network. The overall fashion is remenessent of a circulatory system, or other organic construct, and is quite effishent in its design. The town rests behind a thick wall made from clay bricks. The wall has all of the proper fortifications and is well made. Unfortuantly the nature of clay brick leaves it quite vulnerable to siege equipment, though the thickness of the wall lends it simmilar resistnace to a thinner hardrock wall. The millitarily questionable fortifications have not been wellmaintained over the years, and while functional are in dire need of some loving care and perhapse light renovation.

Muv Vreqüeiz Ner has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Muv Vreqüeiz Ner ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. The locals seem to have responded to their slow downfall by recreating Muv Vreqüeiz Ner as one of the strictest places imaginable. Everyone’s actions are clearly directed by laws they keep in heart and mind at all times. Orderly byond order is a phrase which the town brings to mind.

Civic Infrastructure

Muv Vreqüeiz Ner has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Muv Vreqüeiz Ner. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Muv Vreqüeiz Ner's parks.

Muv Vreqüeiz Ner has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Muv Vreqüeiz Ner.

Muv Vreqüeiz Ner has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Muv Vreqüeiz Ner has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Muv Vreqüeiz Ner has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Muv Vreqüeiz Ner has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Muv Vreqüeiz Ner has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Muv Vreqüeiz Ner's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Muv Vreqüeiz Ner has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Muv Vreqüeiz Ner's natural decorations nor waterways.

Muv Vreqüeiz Ner has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Muv Vreqüeiz Ner has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Muv Vreqüeiz Ner's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is inspired by the natural world, characterized by sinuous, sculptural, organic shapes, arches, curving lines, and sensual ornamentation. Common motifs included stylized versions of leaves, flowers, vines, insects, animals, and other natural elements. Decorative elements found on the inside and outside of buildings include intricate mosaic work, curved windows, and decorative trim work. .

In Muv Vreqüeiz Ner the utterance of expletives is impossible within city limits. This has changed as the limits have changed, but has not kept up with slang, or swearing in foreign languages.

The Baccae near Muv Vreqüeiz Ner are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Muv Vreqüeiz Ner's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves ritual combat to channel Truename Magic energies of tier 1 via moments of science.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century Mount Death, an iconic vista located neare Muv Vreqüeiz Ner, proved to be volcanic when the mountain erupted. The eruption was isolated to the area around Muv Vreqüeiz Ner, which was swallowed in ash, lava flows, and pyroclastic gasses. Muv Vreqüeiz Ner lost 118 people, 287 livestock, and 38 buildings in the disaster. The event is forever remembered as the Day of Death's Wrath.
