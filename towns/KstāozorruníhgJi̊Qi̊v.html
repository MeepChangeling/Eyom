Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v (Kstāo /zoˈrrunɪ̞g/ [counter (flat; elevated surface)] [farmer]) is a subtropical City located in the Màvēvāv Region of the Tetburland.

The name Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v is derived from the Sylvin language, as Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v was founded by Belegiel, who was culturaly Tauran.

Climate

Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v has a yearly average temperature of 29°C (84°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 32°C (89°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 26°C (78°F). Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v receives an average of 203 cm/y (79 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v covers an area of nearly 25 km2 (10 mi2), and an average elevation of 2874 m (9429 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v was founded durring the late 13th century in summer of the year 1180, by Belegiel. The establishment of Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Belegiel electing to pay people to resettle in Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v.

Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v was built using the conventions of Tauran durring the late 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v is buildings are arranged within a network of premissive flagstone streets which form a rectangular grid, where each block verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller block has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The city has a set of well fortified walls, with gatehouses, watch towers, battlments, and even a moat, which are fashioned from stone and timber. Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v's walls are, howeaver, fashioned from stone and timber. While unorthadox, the design looks to be functional to a reasonable degree. With luck, the untested design will remain untested for years to come. The city's unusual though effective defenses are visibly old, but also obviously maintained semi-regularly. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with ocasional mantance of the citys defences.

A look around Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v is like a look into a broken heart. There is no planning, no organization. Everyone here clearly goes about their own thing with little thought to anyone around them who isn’t selling something they need. It’s not malicious. Heads are hung. Eyes are empty. Something truly horrible happened here once upon a time and the city never healed.

Cultural Notes

Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v's locals despise outsiders. For some “outsiders” may be natives of foreign lands, while others might have a grudge against anyone from outside the community. These locals have an active loathing, and the outsiders allowed to trade or interact with them do so at a heavy disadvantage.

Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by grandeur of scale, simplicity of geometric forms, dramatic use of columns, and a preference for blank walls. This design ethos extended to re-imagining earlier styles of structure to create them anew, with a similar overall look and feel to one another..

Due to the actions of local Kami, summer is skipped in Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v.

The Skinwraith near Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Kstāozorruníhg Ji̊ Qi̊v's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves reenactments to channel Mysticism energies of tier 3 via throat chanting.
