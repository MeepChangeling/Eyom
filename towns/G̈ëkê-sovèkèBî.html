G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî (/ˈg̃ëkɘ/ /soˈvèkè/ [reluctant] [excitement]) is a subtropical Town located in the Fêlqêfè Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî was founded by Alyar Trgfe, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî has a yearly average temperature of 24°C (75°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 28°C (82°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 20°C (68°F). G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî receives an average of 74 cm/y (29 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 7222 m (-19255 ft) above sea level.

Overview

G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî was founded durring the early 13th century in spring of the year 1202, by Alyar Trgfe. The establishment of G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Alyar Trgfe struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî as a prison colony.

G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the early 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî is buildings are arranged arrounded highly ordered system of spacious paverstone streets which form octogonal paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The town lacks any defencive features, though certainly constructing even a simple fence or digging a ditch is at the forefront of the 's mind. At least, one would hope so.

Even the most brief look arround G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî has a good chance of putting a church or shrine into the center of your view. The town is most certainly a god fearing community. It’s also definitely a god loving community. THe streetcornors are occupied by preachers, with the occasional intersection playing host to an actually friendly debate relating to the merits of various gods. Even the less popular as well as the less politically loved gods seem to have a space in G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî, there’s even shrines to gods known to the region only through hearsay.

Civic Infrastructure

G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî's parks.

G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî.

G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Town. G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî's grid is powered by hydrogalvanic generators.

G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî's natural decorations nor waterways.

G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

A substantial minority of the locals are descended from foreigners alien to their local neighbors. They may have been religious exiles, economic migrants, indigenous locals surrounded by the existing polity, or a foreign settlement conquered within the relatively recent past. The locals may not be enthusiastic about being ruled by others not of their kind, and their neighbors may look askance at the way foreign customs or even laws may be maintained.

G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used embraces individualism and experimentation. It emerged as a movement against traditional, classical styles and sought to make buildings dynamic and fun while breaking the rules. The style incorporated elements of previous architectural styles in exaggerated and whimsical ways. Traditional, conservative leanings were void in this era, with most scholars of architecture agreeing it was a time of “anything goes.”.

In G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî birds speak prophesy.

The Gravebound near G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî are known to be more aggressive than normal.

G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves destroying a prepared ritual vessel to channel Transmutation energies of tier 1 via guttural bellowing.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century a most peculiar disaster struck G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî, causing great chunks of ice to fall from the sky. G̈ëkê-sovèkè Bî lost 263 people, 267 livestock, and 54 buildings in the disaster.. The ice rain is generally remembered as the Woe Rain.
