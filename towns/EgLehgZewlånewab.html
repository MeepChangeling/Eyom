Eg Lehg Zewlånewab (/s̼eˈnu/ /zuːˌlɑːnuːˈab/ [controversy] [farm laborer]) is a subtropical Town located in the Evwåschyothäb Provence of the Kingdom of Hobben.

The name Eg Lehg Zewlånewab is derived from the Dwarven language, as Eg Lehg Zewlånewab was founded by Dyêbê Zÿhbafral, who was culturaly Dwarvern.

Climate

Eg Lehg Zewlånewab has a yearly average temperature of 26°C (78°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 31°C (87°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 22°C (71°F). Eg Lehg Zewlånewab receives an average of 268 cm/y (105 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Eg Lehg Zewlånewab covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 3228 m (10590 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Eg Lehg Zewlånewab was founded durring the early 12th century in winter of the year 1112, by Dyêbê Zÿhbafral. The establishment of Eg Lehg Zewlånewab was only bairly constructed. The sheer number of problems with its founding were enough to make several of the backers funding Eg Lehg Zewlånewab's construction back out of the project. Dyêbê Zÿhbafral pushed on reguardles, and Eg Lehg Zewlånewab was finished, but starts off as a terible place to live.

Eg Lehg Zewlånewab was built using the conventions of Dwarvern durring the early 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Eg Lehg Zewlånewab is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Eg Lehg Zewlånewab is buildings are located arround a single premissive gravel mainstreet which forms a clockwise spiral to give the town a over all circular shape. The town posesses a mighty albit amaturly crafted stone wall. It was built using querried stone blocks and posesses all of the standard defencive features, including a few watch towers. While it would last against a siege, the wall's outdated and simplistic construction method percludes the possability of Eg Lehg Zewlånewab weathering a full scale bombardment durring a siege. Eg Lehg Zewlånewab's budget focused millitary grade defenses are in an unremarkable state. To some, this is the ideal sate for defences to be in. In need of absoutly nothing, and ready to serve the town as needed.

Eg Lehg Zewlånewab seems to be abandoned at first. There are people present, working their trades and going about their business like any community, it’s just they are absurdly quiet. Just as you start to think there might be trouble, you see most of them are smiling, quietly exchanging words with a friend, or simply enjoying the day as they work. It reminds you of an evening at home as everyone dined, having run out of things to talk about and embraced the savory meal before them.

Cultural Notes

Eg Lehg Zewlånewab's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known primarily for its use of abstraction and simplicity. Clean lines, right angles, and primary colors characterized this aesthetic and art movement expressed via architecture and paintings. Its design ethos allows only primary colors and non-colors, only squares and rectangles, only straight and horizontal or vertical lines. Vertical and horizontal lines are positioned in layers or planes that do not intersect, thereby allowing each element to exist independently and unobstructed by other elements. These seemingly impossible principals for an architectural style coalesces into structures which most experts find hard to put into words. It is not that their geometry is impossible, but rather the style's attempt at producing works only describable visually was most successful..

In Eg Lehg Zewlånewab most nights are accompanied by colorful ribbons of light in the sky.

The Ramidreju near Eg Lehg Zewlånewab are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Eg Lehg Zewlånewab's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves destroying a prepared ritual vessel to channel Augury energies of tier 3 via oratory performances.

Eg Lehg Zewlånewab is still bloodied by a recent violent conflict.
