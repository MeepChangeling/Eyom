Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê (/qo/ /foˈp˔aqɘ/ [thin (slender); slim] [grandmother]) is a temperate Town located in the Rëthîvîcêli Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê was founded by Joīsh Ga̋ń̄r 'Bug Dee' Vú̄kīy Goidaw̋ch Sēsh Glkare, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê has a yearly average temperature of 27°C (80°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 29°C (84°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 25°C (77°F). Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê receives an average of 212 cm/y (83 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 4124 m (13530 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê was founded durring the early 13th century in fall of the year 1229, by Joīsh Ga̋ń̄r 'Bug Dee' Vú̄kīy Goidaw̋ch Sēsh Glkare. The establishment of Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Joīsh Ga̋ń̄r 'Bug Dee' Vú̄kīy Goidaw̋ch Sēsh Glkare struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê as a prison colony.

Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the early 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê is buildings have been located at convienant points along the swamp Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê was built upon. Navigating the town is therefore a little chalanging as the distance between buildings verris greatly and the spacious paverstone streets flow where they are able to be made rather than folowing the most convienant paths. The town is the proud owner of a properly designed set of renforced walls made from mighty querried stone blocks. Their construction and material choices would make a dwarf weap with joy, for each and every part of the elaborate fortifications are purly functional and robust well byond reason. Even nonexperts can tell the walls are an excelent defencive structure. Unfortuantly, these exceptionaly well made fortifications are in extreem disrepair, so much so that one cannot tell if they are decaying from a lack of mantance or damage incured.

Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. Likely due to this, Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê is, in a word, disorder. People seem to be allowed to do as they please with little harmoney to anything. It feels less like a town, and more like a spot people just happened to have homes. One cannot help but wonder what Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê was like during the glorydays.

Civic Infrastructure

Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê's parks.

Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê.

Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê has an Scientific Academy which provides higher education in the natural sciences.

Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê's natural decorations nor waterways.

Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Religious leaders are influential in almost any community, but in Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê they make up the final authorities. It may be an explicit theocracy, with rule by the clerics of a particular faith, or a temple might be so important and powerful that the official leaders are helpless to resist its will. The locals can be expected to be loyal adherents to the faith, or else the less pious majority is deeply intimidated by the religion’s believers.

Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is based upon new and innovative technologies of construction and the idea that form should follow function. It was an embrace of minimalism and a rejection of ornament. The style became characterized by an emphasis on volume, asymmetrical compositions, and minimal ornamentation..

The Skin Stitcher near Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Qo-fopíaqê Fêqê's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves orgies to channel Truename Magic energies of tier 2 via recitation of scripture.
