Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè (/ˈiqɪ/ /p˔ɘˈkanɘ/ [fun] [shooting star]) is a subtropical Town located in the Fêlqêfè Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè was founded by Nrêl Trêgepí, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè has a yearly average temperature of 25°C (77°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 29°C (84°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 22°C (71°F). Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè receives an average of 72 cm/y (28 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 6248 m (20498 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè was founded durring the late 13th century in spring of the year 1174, by Nrêl Trêgepí. The establishment of Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Nrêl Trêgepí struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè as a prison colony.

Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the late 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè is buildings are built arround a single narrow paverstone mainstreet which forms a counterclockwise spiral to give the town a over all circular shape. The town has a defencive wall made from querried stone. The wall is equipped with a full set of battlments but the nature of its construction methodology leaves it somewhat vulnerable to siege equipment. That said, the town is well defended against anything short of an army. Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè's cost-cutting-focused defences has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

A look around Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè shows Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè as a den of corruption. Birbes can be seen changing hands openly, such that it must be customary to do so and must have been for a long time. The locals have no fear, no annoyance at the state of things, it simply is. The town has another layer to it as well. Locals can be overheard having academic discussions, as well as talking about scholarly subjects in general. It’s quite clear Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè places a lot of value on education and being a learned individual, or at least, faking it.

Civic Infrastructure

Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè.

Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè is home to a University which provides higher education in a variety of fields, and also serves as a research institute for those same fields.

Cultural Notes

Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by grandeur of scale, simplicity of geometric forms, dramatic use of columns, and a preference for blank walls. This design ethos extended to re-imagining earlier styles of structure to create them anew, with a similar overall look and feel to one another..

In Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè grains of dust blow into perfectly neat rows.

The Tangtal (Dupli-Cat) near Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Iqî-píêkanê Dêtîkè's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves performance art to channel Invocation energies of tier 3 via divine sermons.
