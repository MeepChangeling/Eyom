G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê (/g̃oˈlɪvɪ/ /ˈvèbɪ/ [sad] [volcano]) is a subtropical Small Town located in the Mèbîfege Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê was founded by Lowri Èbê, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê has a yearly average temperature of 24°C (75°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 22°C (71°F). G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê receives an average of 96 cm/y (37 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 5578 m (18300 ft) above sea level.

Overview

G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê was founded durring the late 16th century in fall of the year 1491, by Lowri Èbê. The establishment of the new community went well, with no major obsticles durring construction.

G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the late 16th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature masoned stone construction which prominantly features pointed arches, pointed ribbed vault cielings, flying buttress', and window tracery all of which share a simmilar gemoetetic patern halfway between organic and inorganic in design formaing a very distinct aesthetically integrated style. BUildings tend to reach for the havens, and more expencive homes are easily identified by their floor count as well as the addition of decorative features intigrated into the building's design such as statues, gargoyals, and embelished joinery.

G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê is is constructed arround a series of premissive canals mainstreets which form overlapping circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The town has a defencive wall made from querried stone. The wall is equipped with a full set of battlments but the nature of its construction methodology leaves it somewhat vulnerable to siege equipment. That said, the town is well defended against anything short of an army. G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê's cost-cutting-focused defences has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

A look around G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê makes you worry someone might stab you in a dark ally for your boots. It’s not filthy, or dark, but the smiles seem strained, the locals seem to glare daggers in eachothers backs a little too much, and everyone is armed at all times. You may want to keep an eye on your valuables, and make sure you don’t wind up in any position of power.

Civic Infrastructure

G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê.

G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for the combined use of arcuated and trabeated construction, employing arches and constructed with post and lintel. The arch served as the style's chief structural element, with flanking columns serving as buttresses or decorations. While the general shape of their structures could be generously described as simplistic, the use of these arches and columns created a most distinct ascetic which is easily distinguishable on sight to armatures and experts alike.

Due to the actions of local Kami, autumn is skipped in G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê.

The Achaierai near G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves consuming a local narcotic to channel Wild Magic energies of tier 1 via proclamations.

Points of Interest

Many of G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê’s structures date back to the ancient past and a long-vanished culture. They have unique architectural traits, perhaps being made of some strange substance or with uncanny qualities. The locals find them too useful or too durable to destroy, but the buildings often have unpleasant little surprises in their under-explored corners, and there may be greater structures still buried by long ages beneath G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê’s streets.

G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê makes use of canals for some of its streets. Locals often fish in the canals.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century an unusualy harsh winter spawned a deadly blizard to G̈olîvî-vèbî Fêqê. The ice, snow, and wind killed 259 people, 184 livestock, and destroyed 96. The event is remembered as Calamity's Breath.
