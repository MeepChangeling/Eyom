Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè (/ˈvɘp˔ɘ/ /ˈteθɘ/ [external] [bench]) is a temperate Town located in the G̈mthêldèthêkè Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè was founded by Titanic Whip Spiders Trèbëgë, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè has a yearly average temperature of 28°C (82°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 32°C (89°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 25°C (77°F). Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè receives an average of 89 cm/y (35 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 9564 m (-11571 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè was founded durring the early 16th century in winter of the year 1525, by Titanic Whip Spiders Trèbëgë. The establishment of Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè suffered from several major issues, resulting in the need to develop many solutions to basic problems. Problems such as a lack of fresh water, logistical support, poor quality tools, and the odd monster or two. Howeaver, these were overcome in time.

Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the early 16th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè is is constructed arround a series of premissive cobblestone mainstreets which form overlapping circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The town sits behind an impressive looking clay brick wall. The wall is notable for querried stone blocks being used to renforce the clay bricks in the expected manner and locations. While the wall was built to the exact specifications of modern fortifications, one has to wonder why the money invested into creating such a well fortified wall wasnt spent on a less impressive looking wall crated from sturdier materials. Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè's walls would endure a brief bombardment, but only from light siege weapons. It seems more likly the town's walls were not designed with defence in mind but rather lending the town a certain air with visitors. Unfortuantly, these boondoggle-of-a-fortified wall are in extreem disrepair, so much so that one cannot tell if they are decaying from a lack of mantance or damage incured.

A look around Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè seems to be home to a quite vibrant and boisterous community. Everywhere one looks they can see people going out their daily business with a smile and a spring in their step. Children play loudly in the streets, causing untold havoc as youth are want and allowed to do.

Civic Infrastructure

Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè.

Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known primarily for its use of abstraction and simplicity. Clean lines, right angles, and primary colors characterized this aesthetic and art movement expressed via architecture and paintings. Its design ethos allows only primary colors and non-colors, only squares and rectangles, only straight and horizontal or vertical lines. Vertical and horizontal lines are positioned in layers or planes that do not intersect, thereby allowing each element to exist independently and unobstructed by other elements. These seemingly impossible principals for an architectural style coalesces into structures which most experts find hard to put into words. It is not that their geometry is impossible, but rather the style's attempt at producing works only describable visually was most successful..

In Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè all of the cats speak the local language with prefect diction.

The Ifrit near Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves long periods of drunkenness to channel Elven High Magic energies of tier 3 via mimery.

History

Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè is still bloodied by a recent violent conflict. A crushing bandit raid, a lost siege, getting caught at the periphery of a major battle, or some other calamity has inflicted severe damage on the place. Some communities may suffer a longer-term version of this, their youths lost in a grinding, endless battle against some perpetual threat.

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè was attacked by savage sprigganss living nearby. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè lost 121 people, 104 livestock, and 73 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 205, when members of Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè's militia enacted an operation to destroy or capture a particular strategic location. The operation was complicated by a key segment of the operation that deepened entirely on a stealth mission going perfectly. The conflict ended with pitched battle between both forces, which ended in a crushing defeat for Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Vêpíê-tethê Dêtîkè's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
