History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Nopo Matakelabotu was attacked by a renegade group of the army. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Nopo Matakelabotu lost 196 people, 397 livestock, and 32 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 160, when members of Nopo Matakelabotu's militia enacted an operation to destroy a particular enemy warchest. The operation was complicated by a significant percentage of the militia choose to go abandon the operation and return home. The conflict ended with a last stand against the enemy until a particular event occurred, which ended in defeat for Nopo Matakelabotu's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Nopo Matakelabotu's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
