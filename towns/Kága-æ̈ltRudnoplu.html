Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu (/iˈkæ̝/ /æ̝lt/ [technological] [whiskey; liquor]) is a subtropical Small Town located in Êniyeqêmè County, Jáijotirīirv Kingdom, within the United Kingdom of Undermountain.

The name Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu is derived from the Gnollish language, as Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu was founded by Mri vyim Rolɑrtɑcu, who was culturaly Gnollish.

Climate

Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu has a yearly average temperature of 14°C (57°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 28°C (82°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing 0°C (32°F). Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu receives an average of 197 cm/y (77 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu covers an area of nearly 3 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 4004 m (13136 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu was founded durring the early 13th century in fall of the year 1226, by Mri vyim Rolɑrtɑcu. The establishment of Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu was built using the conventions of Gnollish durring the early 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu is buildings are arranged arround a network of crampt worn bedrock streets which form a grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town emploies a series of defencive earthworks, spikes, and fences to provide some protection against wild beasts and smaller groups of intelegent foes. The minimaly adiquite have not been wellmaintained over the years, and while functional are in dire need of some loving care and perhapse light renovation.

A quick look in any direction shows Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu is filled with vices. There are many taverns, brothels are advertising their services on the streets, cardsharks are plying their illicit trade within public squairs, and every shop has at least three signs advertising various sales on goods. That alone wouldn’t be too noticeable, if it wasn’t just so omnipresent and overwhelmingly how the people of this town live.

Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for its functional shapes, abstract shapes used sparingly for decor, simple color schemes, holistic design, and basic industrial materials. Its simple designs were created to be beautiful, functional, and mass-producible. The style used little to no embellishment or ornamentation, instead drawing attention to the streamlined design, such as flat roofs to create a simple, geometric look. The simplicity masks the style's nearly sinister functionality, as every last feature is designed to guide the people living in the building in how to make the most efficient use of the structure.

In Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu the milk never sours.

The Yothga near Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Kága-æ̈lt Rudnoplu's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves drinking to channel Chronomancy energies of tier 1 via oath swearing.
