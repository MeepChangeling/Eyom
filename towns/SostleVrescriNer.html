Sostle Vrescri Ner (/ˈsostle/ /ˈvrescri/ [FALSE] [behavior]) is a subtropical Town located in Höwnow Parish, Zōzqut Diocese, within the Covenent of Irus.

The name Sostle Vrescri Ner is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as Sostle Vrescri Ner was founded by Onshi Zulvarash, who was culturaly Wood Elven.

Climate

Sostle Vrescri Ner has a yearly average temperature of 25°C (77°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 30°C (86°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 20°C (68°F). Sostle Vrescri Ner receives an average of 152 cm/y (59 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Sostle Vrescri Ner covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 5220 m (17125 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Sostle Vrescri Ner was founded durring the late 14th century in fall of the year 1270, by Onshi Zulvarash. The establishment of Sostle Vrescri Ner was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Onshi Zulvarash electing to pay people to resettle in Sostle Vrescri Ner.

Sostle Vrescri Ner was built using the conventions of Wood Elven durring the late 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Sostle Vrescri Ner is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Sostle Vrescri Ner is is constructed arround a semi-circular narrow flagstone mainstreet, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The town rests behind a thick wall made from clay bricks. The wall has all of the proper fortifications and is well made. Unfortuantly the nature of clay brick leaves it quite vulnerable to siege equipment, though the thickness of the wall lends it simmilar resistnace to a thinner hardrock wall. The millitarily questionable fortifications have not been wellmaintained over the years, and while functional are in dire need of some loving care and perhapse light renovation.

Your first impression of Sostle Vrescri Ner proves to be right on the money. This town is where the rebels chose to settle. Fashion trends are quite simply the opposite of what’s popular in the other nearby communities. THis trend extends everywhere. Things that should be black are white, what would normally be square is organically curved, and what is typically unthinkable for polite society is more than welcome in this public square. Up to the logical limits, of course. There’s no flaying someone alive to eat their skin going on, but the women are dressed a little immodestly, the men are a little more emotional, and the food is all overly complex and terrible.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Sostle Vrescri Ner was attacked by savage tribes living nearby Sostle Vrescri Ner. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Sostle Vrescri Ner lost 275 people, 322 livestock, and 47 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 156, when members of Sostle Vrescri Ner's militia enacted an operation to escort a particular solgiers group to a safe location. The operation was complicated by at least some of the intelligence related to the operation was incorrect. The conflict ended with pitched battle between both forces, which ended in defeat for Sostle Vrescri Ner's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Sostle Vrescri Ner's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
