Re̜tlmaskvö Vët Med (/re̜ˈtlmaskvo˞/ /vɛt/ [coherent] [level (degree; stage)]) is a subtropical Large City located in the Menertahasha County of the Viceroyalty of Rosid.

The name Re̜tlmaskvö Vët Med is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as Re̜tlmaskvö Vët Med was founded by Mā̌ Rä̌s 'Valentina Lily' Cās Haw̄b Ca̋nvé̄y Tibe, who was culturaly Wood Elven.

Climate

Re̜tlmaskvö Vët Med has a yearly average temperature of 16°C (60°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 7°C (44°F). Re̜tlmaskvö Vët Med receives an average of 150 cm/y (59 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Re̜tlmaskvö Vët Med covers an area of nearly 276 km2 (110 mi2), and an average elevation of 4730 m (15518 ft) above sea level.

Re̜tlmaskvö Vët Med has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Re̜tlmaskvö Vët Med is home to a University which provides higher education in a variety of fields, and also serves as a research institute for those same fields.

Cultural Notes

Re̜tlmaskvö Vët Med's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for the combined use of arcuated and trabeated construction, employing arches and constructed with post and lintel. The arch served as the style's chief structural element, with flanking columns serving as buttresses or decorations. While the general shape of their structures could be generously described as simplistic, the use of these arches and columns created a most distinct ascetic which is easily distinguishable on sight to armatures and experts alike.

In Re̜tlmaskvö Vët Med sometimes, near one of the seasonal solstices, the sun appears to split into three and it rains from a clear sky for several hours.

Re̜tlmaskvö Vët Med's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves long periods of drunkenness to channel Illusion energies of tier 1 via throat chanting.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century a great windstorm struck Re̜tlmaskvö Vët Med. Its winds were so great they picked up unsecured objects and flung them with enough force to smash brick and skull alike. Buildings collapsed under the gale, burying countless people alive. The storm's devistation was focused on the area arround Re̜tlmaskvö Vët Med, which lost 155 people, 320, and 36 livestock in the disaster.. History remembers the strom as The Storm of Calamity.
