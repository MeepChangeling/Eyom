Tshizijrè Ji̊ Qi̊v (Tshi /ˈzijrɛ/ [glass] [content]) is a subtropical Large City located in the Tmíhiy̼gelfàf Region of the Tetburland.

The name Tshizijrè Ji̊ Qi̊v is derived from the Sylvin language, as Tshizijrè Ji̊ Qi̊v was founded by Shonhëg̈, who was culturaly Tauran.

Climate

Tshizijrè Ji̊ Qi̊v has a yearly average temperature of 29°C (84°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a blistering 35°C (95°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 24°C (75°F). Tshizijrè Ji̊ Qi̊v receives an average of 176 cm/y (69 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Tshizijrè Ji̊ Qi̊v covers an area of nearly 197 km2 (78 mi2), and an average elevation of 1536 m (5039 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Tshizijrè Ji̊ Qi̊v was founded durring the early 13th century in summer of the year 1236, by Shonhëg̈. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Shonhëg̈.

Tshizijrè Ji̊ Qi̊v was built using the conventions of Tauran durring the early 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Tshizijrè Ji̊ Qi̊v is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature masoned stone construction which prominantly features pointed arches, pointed ribbed vault cielings, flying buttress', and window tracery all of which share a simmilar gemoetetic patern halfway between organic and inorganic in design formaing a very distinct aesthetically integrated style. BUildings tend to reach for the havens, and more expencive homes are easily identified by their floor count as well as the addition of decorative features intigrated into the building's design such as statues, gargoyals, and embelished joinery.

Tshizijrè Ji̊ Qi̊v is buildings are arranged arrounded a highly ordered system of premissive cobblestone streets which form triangular paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The city is the proud owner of a thick set of fortified walls fashioned from querried stone blocks. While not up to snuff for a fort or castle wall, the city's walls are naturaly much larger than those of forts or castles. Therefore, the construction such a wall is most expencive. Tshizijrè Ji̊ Qi̊v's buget focused wall would serve its community well in battle in spite of looking unimpressive compared to castles and fortresses. Tshizijrè Ji̊ Qi̊v's top tier civilian fortifications has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

Tshizijrè Ji̊ Qi̊v has a very calm atmosphere. People can be seen relaxing, scocilizing, and going about all manner of business other than the daily grind. Men, women, children, all can be seen enjoying life in a laid-back way in the many parks which line Tshizijrè Ji̊ Qi̊v’s streets. Yet, deep down, you know this atmosphere is a carefully created illusion. You can see the ocasional seam in their tapestry. You’re not sure what has them doing their best to act so carefree, but act carefree they do.

Cultural Notes

Tshizijrè Ji̊ Qi̊v's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for the combined use of arcuated and trabeated construction, employing arches and constructed with post and lintel. The arch served as the style's chief structural element, with flanking columns serving as buttresses or decorations. While the general shape of their structures could be generously described as simplistic, the use of these arches and columns created a most distinct ascetic which is easily distinguishable on sight to armatures and experts alike.

Due to the actions of local Kami, spring is long in Tshizijrè Ji̊ Qi̊v.

The Jupiter Bloodsucker near Tshizijrè Ji̊ Qi̊v are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Tshizijrè Ji̊ Qi̊v's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves square dance to channel Conjuration energies of tier 1 via oratory performances.
