Luchâ Ûvû Mîze (/ˈluʧɑ/ /ˈʊvʊ/ [mature; ripe] [trouble; problem]) is a subtropical Small Town located in Chyûlàlà âyi Kingdom, Chyûyu̽ Lu̹sû Empire, within the Warrenese Shogunate.

The name Luchâ Ûvû Mîze is derived from the Wareneese language, as Luchâ Ûvû Mîze was founded by Mb̪f̄nvé Jō̋g 'Dixon Quinn' Ha̋nvéch Ha̋nvér Vúchvór Glëg̈ Garepí Fyesmë, who was culturaly Wareneese.

Climate

Luchâ Ûvû Mîze has a yearly average temperature of 11°C (51°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing -2°C (29°F). Luchâ Ûvû Mîze receives an average of 298 cm/y (117 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Luchâ Ûvû Mîze covers an area of nearly 3 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 4346 m (14258 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Luchâ Ûvû Mîze was founded durring the early 16th century in winter of the year 1525, by Mb̪f̄nvé Jō̋g 'Dixon Quinn' Ha̋nvéch Ha̋nvér Vúchvór Glëg̈ Garepí Fyesmë. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Mb̪f̄nvé Jō̋g 'Dixon Quinn' Ha̋nvéch Ha̋nvér Vúchvór Glëg̈ Garepí Fyesmë.

Luchâ Ûvû Mîze was built using the conventions of Wareneese durring the early 16th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Luchâ Ûvû Mîze is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature masoned stone construction which prominantly features pointed arches, pointed ribbed vault cielings, flying buttress', and window tracery all of which share a simmilar gemoetetic patern halfway between organic and inorganic in design formaing a very distinct aesthetically integrated style. BUildings tend to reach for the havens, and more expencive homes are easily identified by their floor count as well as the addition of decorative features intigrated into the building's design such as statues, gargoyals, and embelished joinery.

Luchâ Ûvû Mîze is buildings are arranged within a network of restrictive paverstone streets which form a rectangular grid, where each block verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller block has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town lacks any defencive features, though certainly constructing even a simple fence or digging a ditch is at the forefront of the 's mind. At least, one would hope so.

Luchâ Ûvû Mîze is not quite well. Something happened here, perhapse recently, perhapse long ago. Whatever it was, it settled into the very soul of the town like a festering wound. The people go about their day well enough, but there’s a tention in the air you can cut with a knife.

Cultural Notes

There’s more than one leader in Luchâ Ûvû Mîze, but at least some of them are at each other’s throats. It might be a conflict between formal leadership and informal authorities, or it could be a struggle among civil officials. Their interests might diverge sharply, or it could be a personal grudge that’s boiled over. Outside threats and internal problems are likely being ignored until the power struggle is resolved.

Luchâ Ûvû Mîze's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is which made use of the classical orders and mathematically precise ratios of height and width combined with a desire for symmetry, proportion, and harmony. It used columns, pediments, arches and domes are imaginatively in buildings of all types. Decorative features were seen as largely unnecessary as the sheer beauty of the structure itself was often close to art. However, many buildings with large ceiling spaces had their ceilings decorated with elaborate paintings, simply because the large flat spaces could feel wasted.

The Lurker in Light near Luchâ Ûvû Mîze are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Luchâ Ûvû Mîze's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves creating small tokens to channel Augury energies of tier 2 via oath swearing.
