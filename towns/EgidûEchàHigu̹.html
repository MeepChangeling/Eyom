Egidû Echà Higu̹ (/eˈgidʊ/ /ˈeʧə/ [backward] [constitution]) is a subtropical Large City located in S̺àû Chyide Kingdom, Siyalefia County, within the Warrenese Shogunate.

The name Egidû Echà Higu̹ is derived from the Sylvin language, as Egidû Echà Higu̹ was founded by Shovnoh, who was culturaly Warrenese.

Climate

Egidû Echà Higu̹ has a yearly average temperature of 13°C (55°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 23°C (73°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 4°C (39°F). Egidû Echà Higu̹ receives an average of 209 cm/y (82 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Egidû Echà Higu̹ covers an area of nearly 267 km2 (106 mi2), and an average elevation of 7050 m (-19819 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Egidû Echà Higu̹ was founded durring the late 13th century in fall of the year 1180, by Shovnoh. The establishment of the new community went well, with no major obsticles durring construction.

Egidû Echà Higu̹ was built using the conventions of Warrenese durring the late 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Egidû Echà Higu̹ is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Egidû Echà Higu̹ is buildings have been located at convienant points along the rivers Egidû Echà Higu̹ was built upon. Navigating the town is therefore a little chalanging as the distance between buildings verris greatly and the restrictive paverstone streets flow where they are able to be made rather than folowing the most convienant paths. The riverside city has a set of well fortified walls, with gatehouses, watch towers, battlments, and even a moat, which are fashioned from stone and timber. Egidû Echà Higu̹'s walls are, howeaver, fashioned from stone and timber. While unorthadox, the design looks to be functional to a reasonable degree. With luck, the untested design will remain untested for years to come. Egidû Echà Higu̹'s unusual though effective defenses are suffering from significent damage, so much so that examples can be pointed to no matter which section one might have within their line of site, and most of which render sections inoperable at present.

Egidû Echà Higu̹ seems to be abandoned at first. There are people present, working their trades and going about their business like any community, it’s just they are absurdly quiet. Just as you start to think there might be trouble, you see most of them are reading. The entire city feels like a library, with those not reading remaining quite to not disturb those who have their nose in a book. The longer you remain in Egidû Echà Higu̹ the more you start to wonder if you’ve entered some strange open-concept University rather than a city, but you can’t quite say such with certainty.

Cultural Notes

Egidû Echà Higu̹'s garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known for its fluid and florid elaborate style, comprising ornate, asymmetric designs and pastel shades. It is often considered to be a playful, light style, which made exuberant use of curves and emphasized subtle asymmetry in the general shape of its structures. Walls, ceilings and moldings are decorated with numerous interlacing of curves and counter-curves based on the shapes of ‘C’ and ‘S’, along with shell forms and other naturalistic shapes.

In Egidû Echà Higu̹ rain occurs in reverse with water pooling up from the earth then falling into the skies to from clouds, this unsettles every visitor that sees it.

The Giant hissing cockroach near Egidû Echà Higu̹ are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Egidû Echà Higu̹'s citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves creating small tokens to channel Truename Magic energies of tier 1 via moments of science.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century, Egidû Echà Higu̹ was attacked by a group of nobles with their private armies. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Egidû Echà Higu̹ lost 293 people, 313 livestock, and 51 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 215, when members of Egidû Echà Higu̹'s militia enacted an operation to capture a specific enemy 's family member alive. The operation was complicated by a significant percentage of the militia choose to go abandon the operation and return home. The conflict ended with pitched battle between both forces, which ended in a stalemate for Egidû Echà Higu̹'s forces. The war is remembered in legend by Egidû Echà Higu̹'s bards, historians, and legend keepers.
