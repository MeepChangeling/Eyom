Nozbu Lud Vrët (/ˈnozbu/ /lud/ [comfortable] [bargain; discount]) is a subtropical Town located in the Iastrokbruk Moot of the Viceroyalty of Rosid.

The name Nozbu Lud Vrët is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as Nozbu Lud Vrët was founded by Syntoma Bigiu Krazron, who was culturaly Wood Elven.

Climate

Nozbu Lud Vrët has a yearly average temperature of 18°C (64°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 10°C (50°F). Nozbu Lud Vrët receives an average of 152 cm/y (59 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Nozbu Lud Vrët covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 2922 m (9586 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Nozbu Lud Vrët was founded durring the early 16th century in fall of the year 1507, by Syntoma Bigiu Krazron. The establishment of Nozbu Lud Vrët was only bairly constructed. The sheer number of problems with its founding were enough to make several of the backers funding Nozbu Lud Vrët's construction back out of the project. Syntoma Bigiu Krazron pushed on reguardles, and Nozbu Lud Vrët was finished, but starts off as a terible place to live.

Nozbu Lud Vrët was built using the conventions of Wood Elven durring the early 16th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Nozbu Lud Vrët is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature masoned stone construction which prominantly features pointed arches, pointed ribbed vault cielings, flying buttress', and window tracery all of which share a simmilar gemoetetic patern halfway between organic and inorganic in design formaing a very distinct aesthetically integrated style. BUildings tend to reach for the havens, and more expencive homes are easily identified by their floor count as well as the addition of decorative features intigrated into the building's design such as statues, gargoyals, and embelished joinery.

Nozbu Lud Vrët is buildings are arranged arrounded highly ordered system of premissive paverstone streets which form octogonal paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The town is protected by a humble stone fence which is tall enough to provide adiquite cover for defenders to fire from, but no more than that. The peasent-grade have not been wellmaintained over the years, and while functional are in dire need of some loving care and perhapse light renovation.

A look around Nozbu Lud Vrët gives you an uneasy feeling. Everything is just a little too worn down, a little too dirty, or both. No one makes eye contact. Kids play quietly, but happily. Occasionally a passerby glances at you out of the corner of their eye, staring just long enough for it to be uncomfortable. This all rests atop the unmistakable impression the town is one of the strictest places imaginable. Everyone’s actions are clearly directed by laws they keep in heart and mind at all times. Orderly byond order is a phrase which Nozbu Lud Vrët brings to mind.

Civic Infrastructure

Nozbu Lud Vrët has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Nozbu Lud Vrët.

Nozbu Lud Vrët has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Nozbu Lud Vrët has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Nozbu Lud Vrët has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Nozbu Lud Vrët's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Nozbu Lud Vrët has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

Nozbu Lud Vrët has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

Two or more groups of citizens within Nozbu Lud Vrët hate each other. Their neighbors or the local law have kept things from too-overt violence, but members of the groups will constantly interfere with their rivals and cause whatever misery they can get away with. This hate may spring from recent events, or it may be an inherited spite from old wrongs.

Nozbu Lud Vrët's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for the combined use of arcuated and trabeated construction, employing arches and constructed with post and lintel. The arch served as the style's chief structural element, with flanking columns serving as buttresses or decorations. While the general shape of their structures could be generously described as simplistic, the use of these arches and columns created a most distinct ascetic which is easily distinguishable on sight to armatures and experts alike.

In Nozbu Lud Vrët rainbows form quite often above the Town.

The Ice Elemental, Medium near Nozbu Lud Vrët are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Nozbu Lud Vrët's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves orgies to channel Augury energies of tier 3 via speaking in tongues.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century Nozbu Lud Vrët was struck by a devistating earthquake. The quake brought ruin to Nozbu Lud Vrët, which lost 288 people, 223 livestock, and 97 buildings in the earthquake. The day of the quake is remembered by many as Loss's Day.
