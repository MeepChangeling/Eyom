Odíhg Lawi̊samwā (/ˈlawi̘/ /ˈsamwəˑ/ [wire] [arrogant]) is a subtropical Town located in Qodivob County, Tafogin Provence, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Odíhg Lawi̊samwā is derived from the Tauric language, as Odíhg Lawi̊samwā was founded by Shadda Shankland, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā has a yearly average temperature of 29°C (84°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 33°C (91°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 25°C (77°F). Odíhg Lawi̊samwā receives an average of 155 cm/y (61 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Odíhg Lawi̊samwā covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 2700 m (8858 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā was founded durring the early 18th century in late summer of the year 1711, by Shadda Shankland. The establishment of Odíhg Lawi̊samwā was only bairly constructed. The sheer number of problems with its founding were enough to make several of the backers funding Odíhg Lawi̊samwā's construction back out of the project. Shadda Shankland pushed on reguardles, and Odíhg Lawi̊samwā was finished, but starts off as a terible place to live.

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the early 18th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Odíhg Lawi̊samwā is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā is buildings are grouped arround an odd layout of premissive canals streets, which seems to be based on an overlapping squair patern such that there are small squares at the cornor of every bigger square. Sometimes buildings exist in the smaller squaires, other times they are open spaces, or occupied by temporary structures. The town sits behind a stone-renforced palisade wall, with stone gatehouses and timber drawbridges for their trench. The town's would-be-castle fortifications have recently undergone extensive repairs and renovations, such that the repairwork is imeadiently apparent and can be spotted due to the diffring ages of materials. One can't help but wonder what brought the need for those repairs to the town.

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Odíhg Lawi̊samwā ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. On top of this is an unmistakable feeling that Odíhg Lawi̊samwā is in this condition because there is something terribly wrong with the town. Maybe it’s the way fog blankets the ground, but only in the connors of places. Maybe it’s the vermin scuttling between shadows in the corner of your eyes. Perhaps it’s the overcast sky which seemed to creep out of nowhere, or the distant howling of wolves. Maybe it’s all of those things together, or perhaps it's the way these elements combine which makes you worry someone might stab you in a dark ally for your boots. It’s not filthy, or dark, but the smiles seem strained, the locals seem to glare daggers in eachothers backs a little too much, and everyone is armed at all times. You may want to keep an eye on your valuables, and make sure you don’t wind up in any position of power. Regardless, you do not feel it would be wise to remain in Odíhg Lawi̊samwā long.

Civic Infrastructure

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā possesses a city-wide Aethary Link which provides Aethary access anywhere within its metropolitan. This allows citizens who can afford the relevant devices access in their places of work, and rarely homes.

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Odíhg Lawi̊samwā. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Odíhg Lawi̊samwā's parks.

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Odíhg Lawi̊samwā.

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā has a government-funded child care program, overseen by the local Department of Nursemaids, which is responsible for providing childcare to working-class citizens according to local ordinances.

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Odíhg Lawi̊samwā's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Town. Odíhg Lawi̊samwā's grid is powered by a boiler and turbine based power plant.

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Odíhg Lawi̊samwā's natural decorations nor waterways.

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used which employed abundant symbolic geometry, using pure forms such as the circle and square, and plans are based on often symmetrical layouts featuring rectangular courtyards and halls. These structures were is decorated with carved stone or stucco reliefs and made use of colorful stone mosaics..

Due to the actions of local Kami, spring is short in Odíhg Lawi̊samwā.

The Ifrit near Odíhg Lawi̊samwā are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves ritual combat to channel Illusion energies of tier 3 via throat singing.

Points of Interest

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā is cursed with recurrent spells of some troublesome disease. The affliction isn’t so fatal as to make living there impossible, but it adds suffering and expense to local lives. The plague might be the product of an ancient curse, the results of long lost toxic remains, or an unavoidable byproduct of whatever industry or purpose justifies the city. It’s probably not overly contagious, but visitors may be in some peril all the same.

Odíhg Lawi̊samwā is accessed from a nearby river via an intricate series of locks.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Odíhg Lawi̊samwā was attacked by soldiers from another nation, waging a greater campaign. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Odíhg Lawi̊samwā lost 115 people, 193 livestock, and 73 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 105, when members of Odíhg Lawi̊samwā's militia enacted an operation to extract assets from a port under siege by the enemy. The operation was complicated by the officers in charge fight among themselves, weakening the army as a whole. The conflict ended with the defense of the port against a siege, which ended in defeat for Odíhg Lawi̊samwā's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Odíhg Lawi̊samwā's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
