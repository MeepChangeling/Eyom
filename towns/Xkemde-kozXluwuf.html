Xkemde-koz Xluwuf (/xcemˈde/ /koz/ [ceiling] [fish]) is a subtropical Large City located in the Mïpirudnoplu Principality of the Tano.

The name Xkemde-koz Xluwuf is derived from the Sylvin language, as Xkemde-koz Xluwuf was founded by Hurtrerh, who was culturaly Gnoll.

Climate

Xkemde-koz Xluwuf has a yearly average temperature of 7°C (44°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a cool 20°C (68°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing -6°C (22°F). Xkemde-koz Xluwuf receives an average of 226 cm/y (88 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Xkemde-koz Xluwuf covers an area of nearly 126 km2 (50 mi2), and an average elevation of 1474 m (4835 ft) above sea level.

Xkemde-koz Xluwuf was founded durring the early 13th century in summer of the year 1213, by Hurtrerh. The establishment of Xkemde-koz Xluwuf was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Hurtrerh struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Xkemde-koz Xluwuf as a prison colony.

Cultural Notes

Xkemde-koz Xluwuf's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by vertical proportions, pointed arches, external buttressing, and asymmetry in the general shape of its buildings. The decorative features of the style were key, consisting of large arched windows, pointed arches, vaulted ceilings, flying buttresses, and sculptures integrated into the structure itself. Occasionally, for very important buildings, an array of sculptures or one colossal sculpture might replace the entirety of the entrance to said building.

Due to the actions of local Kami, spring is short in Xkemde-koz Xluwuf.

The Blood Hawk near Xkemde-koz Xluwuf are known to be quite timid.

Xkemde-koz Xluwuf's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves reenactments to channel Enchantment energies of tier 2 via speaking in tongues.
