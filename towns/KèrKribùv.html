Kèr Kribùv (Kri /bʊv/ [bay; cove] [news]) is a subtropical Large Town located in Loktktsuprmmaku County, Tarnàg Provence, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Kèr Kribùv is derived from the Tauric language, as Kèr Kribùv was founded by Tsîlêd Beddoes, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Kèr Kribùv has a yearly average temperature of 29°C (84°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 32°C (89°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 26°C (78°F). Kèr Kribùv receives an average of 140 cm/y (55 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Kèr Kribùv covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 2808 m (9212 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Kèr Kribùv was founded durring the early 12th century in fall of the year 1106, by Tsîlêd Beddoes. The establishment of Kèr Kribùv suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Kèr Kribùv which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Kèr Kribùv was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the early 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Kèr Kribùv is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Kèr Kribùv is buildings are arranged within a network of crampt paverstone streets which form a rectangular grid, where each block verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller block has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town sits behind a stone-renforced palisade wall, with stone gatehouses and timber drawbridges for their trench. The town's would-be-castle fortifications are visibly old, but also obviously well maintained. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with routine mantance of the town's defences.

The first thing you notice about Kèr Kribùv is the large sign at the entrance welcoming you to their humble town. Children come up to you and say hi, some asking if you’ve fought any big monsters, and others just zipping away after their hello. The adults are much the same. By the time you’ve made it a significant way into the town you’ve been invited to a tavern for a pint to drink in exchange for news of the world. You’ve also had no less than three older women give you a sweetroll and direct your attention to their still unmarried children. It would be creepy if it wasn't so sincere.

Civic Infrastructure

Kèr Kribùv has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Kèr Kribùv. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Kèr Kribùv's parks.

Kèr Kribùv has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Kèr Kribùv.

Kèr Kribùv has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Kèr Kribùv has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Kèr Kribùv has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Kèr Kribùv's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Kèr Kribùv possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Kèr Kribùv has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Kèr Kribùv's natural decorations nor waterways.

Kèr Kribùv has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Kèr Kribùv has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Kèr Kribùv's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used which employed abundant symbolic geometry, using pure forms such as the circle and square, and plans are based on often symmetrical layouts featuring rectangular courtyards and halls. These structures were is decorated with carved stone or stucco reliefs and made use of colorful stone mosaics..

In Kèr Kribùv tiny harmless tornadoes plague the town.

The Mud Elemental, Small near Kèr Kribùv are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Kèr Kribùv's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves drinking to channel Augury energies of tier 1 via speaking in tongues.
