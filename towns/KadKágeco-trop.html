Kad Kágeco-trop (/sdæ̝dˈnumnulˌni/ /trop/ [boring] [rationale]) is a subtropical Large City located in the Sluptprond Principality of the Koiapi.

The name Kad Kágeco-trop is derived from the Goblin language, as Kad Kágeco-trop was founded by Ubleth, who was culturaly Gnoll.

Climate

Kad Kágeco-trop has a yearly average temperature of 3°C (37°F), with its average temperature during the summer being an icy 18°C (64°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool -12°C (11°F). Kad Kágeco-trop receives an average of 213 cm/y (83 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Kad Kágeco-trop covers an area of nearly 50 km2 (20 mi2), and an average elevation of 4836 m (15866 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Kad Kágeco-trop was founded durring the late 14th century in summer of the year 1262, by Ubleth. The establishment of Kad Kágeco-trop suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Kad Kágeco-trop which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Kad Kágeco-trop was built using the conventions of Gnoll durring the late 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Kad Kágeco-trop is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Kad Kágeco-trop is is constructed arround a series of crampt baked earthen mainstreets which form concentric circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to each other at varrious points. The city has a set of well fortified walls, with gatehouses, watch towers, battlments, and even a moat, which are fashioned from stone and timber. Kad Kágeco-trop's walls are, howeaver, fashioned from stone and timber. While unorthadox, the design looks to be functional to a reasonable degree. With luck, the untested design will remain untested for years to come. Kad Kágeco-trop's unusual though effective defenses are suffering from significent damage, so much so that examples can be pointed to no matter which section one might have within their line of site, and most of which render sections inoperable at present.

Before you’ve even set foot into the heart of Kad Kágeco-trop, you can smell it. The incense. It hangs about the town like a cloud. Monks, priests, and clerics are everywhere, all dedicated to the same god, all preforming the same rituals to bless and anoint building,s streets, people, animals, you name it they are or have blessed it. The same holysymbols is everywhere too. Its on buildings, on people, and even branded into livestock. This city certainly loves its god.

They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Kad Kágeco-trop's parks.

Kad Kágeco-trop has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Kad Kágeco-trop.

Kad Kágeco-trop has a government-funded child care program, overseen by the local Department of Nursemaids, which is responsible for providing childcare to working-class citizens according to local ordinances.

Kad Kágeco-trop has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Kad Kágeco-trop has a Guild of Nurses, which is tasked with caring for the elderly and infirm in accordance with local ordinances, religious values, and customs.

Kad Kágeco-trop has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Kad Kágeco-trop has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Kad Kágeco-trop has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Kad Kágeco-trop has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Kad Kágeco-trop's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Kad Kágeco-trop has an Millitary Academy which trains military officers and specilists.

Kad Kágeco-trop possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Large City. Kad Kágeco-trop's grid is powered by mana accumulators.

Kad Kágeco-trop possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. In spite of the Galvanic Grid, these lights continue to use their old fule sources to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Kad Kágeco-trop has a library, which keeps a large collection of books, scrolls, and archives all manner of physical items. While not open to the public, the librarians and scholars employed by the library will assist anyone with their research needs, and wealthy individuals can purchase membership to access the library's materials themselves. In spite of being generally closed to the public, the library has a room with several Aether Linked devices available to the public during business hours.

Kad Kágeco-trop has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Kad Kágeco-trop's natural decorations nor waterways.

Kad Kágeco-trop has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Kad Kágeco-trop has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Kad Kágeco-trop has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

The locals are convinced that there is some terrible threat against them working from within their society. It may be a matter of dark sorcerers, foreign spies, traitorous neighbors, shape shifting monsters, or some other hidden evil. This evil may be a recent fear, or it may be an inherited peril they’ve always had to guard against. The danger itself may or may not exist, or if it exists it may not justify the steps being taken.

Kad Kágeco-trop's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for its functional shapes, abstract shapes used sparingly for decor, simple color schemes, holistic design, and basic industrial materials. Its simple designs were created to be beautiful, functional, and mass-producible. The style used little to no embellishment or ornamentation, instead drawing attention to the streamlined design, such as flat roofs to create a simple, geometric look. The simplicity masks the style's nearly sinister functionality, as every last feature is designed to guide the people living in the building in how to make the most efficient use of the structure.

Due to the actions of local Kami, winter is recurring in Kad Kágeco-trop.

The Sandman near Kad Kágeco-trop are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Kad Kágeco-trop's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves sacrificing an animal to channel Conjuration energies of tier 2 via recitation of poetic epics.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century, Kad Kágeco-trop was attacked by soldiers from another nation, waging a greater campaign. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Kad Kágeco-trop lost 129 people, 299 livestock, and 79 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 123, when members of Kad Kágeco-trop's militia enacted an operation to transport a specific resource to a particular port unharmed. The operation was complicated by blistering heat, such that many succumbed to heatstroke. The conflict ended with an assault and siege on the port, which ended in defeat for Kad Kágeco-trop's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Kad Kágeco-trop's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
