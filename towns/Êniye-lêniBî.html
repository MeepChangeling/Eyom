Êniye-lêni Bî (/ɘˈnije/ /ˈlɘni/ [intimate] [space (empty area); room (space)]) is a temperate Town located in the Z̄upaguv Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name Êniye-lêni Bî is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Êniye-lêni Bî was founded by Deborah Zèlkë, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Êniye-lêni Bî has a yearly average temperature of 26°C (78°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 29°C (84°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 23°C (73°F). Êniye-lêni Bî receives an average of 247 cm/y (97 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Êniye-lêni Bî covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 6956 m (-20128 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Êniye-lêni Bî was founded durring the late 14th century in summer of the year 1356, by Deborah Zèlkë. The establishment of Êniye-lêni Bî suffered from several major issues, resulting in the need to develop many solutions to basic problems. Problems such as a lack of fresh water, logistical support, poor quality tools, and the odd monster or two. Howeaver, these were overcome in time.

Êniye-lêni Bî was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the late 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Êniye-lêni Bî is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Êniye-lêni Bî is buildings are arranged arrounded highly ordered system of premissive cobblestone streets which form octogonal paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The town lacks any defencive features, though certainly constructing even a simple fence or digging a ditch is at the forefront of the 's mind. At least, one would hope so.

The town shows nothing out of the ordinary, at first glance. Then you start to notice all the small things. Each window has seven iron nails pounded into the sill. Every door has a sprig of holly overhead. Every well is branded with purity seals and runes to ward the contents. Everyone is carrying a small charm for safety or fortune, be it a rabbit’s foot, a horseshoe, or other little totems.

Civic Infrastructure

Êniye-lêni Bî has an animal control department which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the control, impoundment, and disposition of animals.

Êniye-lêni Bî has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Êniye-lêni Bî. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Êniye-lêni Bî's parks.

Êniye-lêni Bî has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Êniye-lêni Bî.

Êniye-lêni Bî has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Êniye-lêni Bî has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Êniye-lêni Bî has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Êniye-lêni Bî has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Êniye-lêni Bî's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Êniye-lêni Bî possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Town. Êniye-lêni Bî's grid is powered by an arcane means.

Êniye-lêni Bî has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Êniye-lêni Bî's natural decorations nor waterways.

Êniye-lêni Bî has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Êniye-lêni Bî has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Êniye-lêni Bî's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used makes use of a large oblong hall or building with double colonnades and a semicircular apse and symmetrical central-plan, resulting in buildings with a square central mass and four arms of equal length. Decorative features included domed rooves, arches, soaring spaces, and sumptuous decoration: marble columns and inlay, mosaics on the vaults, inlaid-stone pavements, and sometimes gold coffered ceilings.

In Êniye-lêni Bî vermin leave small tokens in payment for food taken.

The Badger near Êniye-lêni Bî are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Êniye-lêni Bî's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves consuming a local narcotic to channel Abjuration energies of tier 1 via oratory performances.

Points of Interest

Êniye-lêni Bî has a conflict with a neighboring community. This usually isn't part of a larger war, but is instead a personal animosity between them. It may be the community has suffered at their enemy's hands, or they may have been the ones applying the suffering. Constant low-level skirmishes and trouble making go on between the two.

Êniye-lêni Bî is known for its odd use of round-a-bouts, small ring roads used in place of intersections.
