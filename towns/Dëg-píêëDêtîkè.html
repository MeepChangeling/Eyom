Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè (/ˈtoʤɘ/ /ˈp˔ɘë/ [cool (fashionable); casual] [rebel; guerrilla]) is a temperate Town located in the Ineg̈èsho Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè was founded by Ruzhohr Glkega, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè has a yearly average temperature of 22°C (71°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 29°C (84°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 16°C (60°F). Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè receives an average of 280 cm/y (110 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 1442 m (4730 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè was founded durring the late 12th century in winter of the year 1074, by Ruzhohr Glkega. The establishment of Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the late 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè is is constructed arround a semi-circular crampt cobblestone mainstreet, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The town sits behind a stone-renforced palisade wall, with stone gatehouses and timber drawbridges for their trench. Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè's would-be-castle fortifications are in an unremarkable state. To some, this is the ideal sate for defences to be in. In need of absoutly nothing, and ready to serve the town as needed.

Looking around Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè you immediately realise that the locals are looking back at you. All of them. Everywhere you look somone is staring back at you analytically, looking over every inch of you, your gear, and your companions. As you get close to people, their hands move closer to their belt knife, or dagger. You may want to watch where you go and what you say...

Civic Infrastructure

Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè.

Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè has a long tradition of martial expertise. This may be a crisply-organized history of skilled native levies, or it may be a natural belligerence in the people that leaves them familiar with bloodshed. While their neighbors and liege doubtless respect their talents, this very aptitude might make them more willing to turn to steel than prudence would advise.

Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is based upon new and innovative technologies of construction and the idea that form should follow function. It was an embrace of minimalism and a rejection of ornament. The style became characterized by an emphasis on volume, asymmetrical compositions, and minimal ornamentation..

Due to the actions of local Kami, autumn is skipped in Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè.

The ﻿Ghoul near Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Dëg-píêë Dêtîkè's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves reenactments to channel Truename Magic energies of tier 1 via chanting.
