Fomba Fton Köz Med (/ˈfomba/ /fton ko˞z/ [coral] [earthquake]) is a temperate Large City located in the Wetharvhei Barony of the Viceroyalty of Rosid.

The name Fomba Fton Köz Med is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as Fomba Fton Köz Med was founded by Inzilo Shuzoreth, who was culturaly Wood Elven.

Climate

Fomba Fton Köz Med has a yearly average temperature of 12°C (53°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing 0°C (32°F). Fomba Fton Köz Med receives an average of 258 cm/y (101 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Fomba Fton Köz Med covers an area of nearly 267 km2 (106 mi2), and an average elevation of 1614 m (5295 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Fomba Fton Köz Med was founded durring the early 17th century in late spring of the year 1617, by Inzilo Shuzoreth. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Inzilo Shuzoreth.

Fomba Fton Köz Med was built using the conventions of Wood Elven durring the early 17th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Fomba Fton Köz Med is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Fomba Fton Köz Med is buildings are arranged arround a network of restrictive split-log ties streets which form a diagonal shaped grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The city rests behind the absurdity that is a thick, timber braced, wall made of clay bricks. While visualy impressive and certainly an astetic, Fomba Fton Köz Med's wall provides no actual defence against siege equipment due to the choice of its cosntruction materials. Even nonexperts can tell the town is trying to impress rather than defend with its walls, towers, and gatehouses. Though admittedly, they do look nice... To primitive tribals who have never seen fortifications before. The city's political statment focused walls have recently undergone extensive repairs and renovations, such that the repairwork is imeadiently apparent and can be spotted due to the diffring ages of materials. One can't help but wonder what brought the need for those repairs to the city.

Even the most brief look arround Fomba Fton Köz Med has a good chance of putting a church or shrine into the center of your view. The city is most certainly a god fearing community. It’s also definitely a god loving community. THe streetcornors are occupied by preachers, with the occasional intersection playing host to an actually friendly debate relating to the merits of various gods. Even the less popular as well as the less politically loved gods seem to have a space in Fomba Fton Köz Med, there’s even shrines to gods known to the region only through hearsay.

They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Fomba Fton Köz Med's parks.

Fomba Fton Köz Med has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Fomba Fton Köz Med.

Fomba Fton Köz Med has a government-funded child care program, overseen by the local Department of Nursemaids, which is responsible for providing childcare to working-class citizens according to local ordinances.

Fomba Fton Köz Med has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Fomba Fton Köz Med has a Guild of Nurses, which is tasked with caring for the elderly and infirm in accordance with local ordinances, religious values, and customs.

Fomba Fton Köz Med has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Fomba Fton Köz Med has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Fomba Fton Köz Med has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Fomba Fton Köz Med has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Fomba Fton Köz Med's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Fomba Fton Köz Med has an Theological Academy which trains clergy in various arcane and theological topics required for their occupations.

Fomba Fton Köz Med possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Large City. Fomba Fton Köz Med's grid is powered by a boiler and turbine based power plant.

Fomba Fton Köz Med's old civil lighting system was converted to Galvanic Lamps recently, and expanded to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Fomba Fton Köz Med has a first rate hospital which caters to anyone in need of long term medical care.

Fomba Fton Köz Med has a library, which keeps a large collection of books, scrolls, and archives all manner of physical items. While not open to the public, the librarians and scholars employed by the library will assist anyone with their research needs, and wealthy individuals can purchase membership to access the library's materials themselves. In spite of being generally closed to the public, the library has a room with several Aether Linked devices available to the public during business hours.

Fomba Fton Köz Med has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Fomba Fton Köz Med's natural decorations nor waterways.

Fomba Fton Köz Med has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Fomba Fton Köz Med has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Fomba Fton Köz Med has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Fomba Fton Köz Med is home to a University which provides higher education in a variety of fields, and also serves as a research institute for those same fields.

Cultural Notes

Fomba Fton Köz Med's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is based upon new and innovative technologies of construction and the idea that form should follow function. It was an embrace of minimalism and a rejection of ornament. The style became characterized by an emphasis on volume, asymmetrical compositions, and minimal ornamentation..

In Fomba Fton Köz Med every night at precisely midnight every structure in town is engulfed by sailors fire until the end of the witching hour.

The Anhana near Fomba Fton Köz Med are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Fomba Fton Köz Med's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves embarking on a group pilgrimage to channel Invocation energies of tier 1 via chanting.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Fomba Fton Köz Med was attacked by savage selkiess living nearby. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Fomba Fton Köz Med lost 119 people, 248 livestock, and 41 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 182, when members of Fomba Fton Köz Med's militia enacted an operation to destroy or capture a particular temple. The operation was complicated by extremely hostile wildlife which took many out of action. The conflict ended with pitched battle between both forces, which ended in victory for Fomba Fton Köz Med's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Fomba Fton Köz Med's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
