Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya (/ˈlu̽dɪː/ /ˈhigu̹/ [earnest] [capital (city)]) is a temperate Large City located in S̺u̹zû Màbæ Kingdom, Vu̹chi Bu̽su Empire, within the Warren.

The name Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya is derived from the Sylvin language, as Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya was founded by Iposha, who was culturaly Rattu.

Climate

Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya has a yearly average temperature of 16°C (60°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 24°C (75°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 9°C (48°F). Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya receives an average of 164 cm/y (64 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya covers an area of nearly 63 km2 (25 mi2), and an average elevation of 1990 m (6528 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya was founded durring the late 14th century in fall of the year 1274, by Iposha. The establishment of Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Iposha electing to pay people to resettle in Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya.

Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya was built using the conventions of Rattu durring the late 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya is buildings are arranged arround a single broad carved bedrock mainstreet with many smaller streets branching off of it which gives the city a over all rectangular shape, albit one warped and twisted by the nature of the curves of the main road. The city is the proud owner of a thick set of fortified walls fashioned from querried stone blocks. While not up to snuff for a fort or castle wall, the city's walls are naturaly much larger than those of forts or castles. Therefore, the construction such a wall is most expencive. Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya's buget focused wall would serve its community well in battle in spite of looking unimpressive compared to castles and fortresses. The top tier civilian fortifications has suffered a visible ammount of structural damage, leaving them effectivly useless. One can't help but wonder why the has not yet effected repairs.

Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya is a bustling hive of activity. Everyone moves at nothing short of a jog, each convocation is a mile a minute, and there’s dedicated lanes for riding through town in the center of each street. The locals all appear to be not merely occupied, but in a true hurry for everything from drinking a pint of ale to their daily work.

Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya has an Theological Academy which trains clergy in various arcane and theological topics required for their occupations.

Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Large City. Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya's grid is powered by a boiler and turbine based power plant.

Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. In spite of the Galvanic Grid, these lights continue to use their old fule sources to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya has a first rate hospital which caters to anyone in need of long term medical care.

Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya has a library, which keeps a large collection of books, scrolls, and archives all manner of physical items. The library is open to the public, including the Aether Link.

Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya's natural decorations nor waterways.

Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

A substantial minority of the locals are descended from foreigners alien to their local neighbors. They may have been religious exiles, economic migrants, indigenous locals surrounded by the existing polity, or a foreign settlement conquered within the relatively recent past. The locals may not be enthusiastic about being ruled by others not of their kind, and their neighbors may look askance at the way foreign customs or even laws may be maintained.

Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used which employed abundant symbolic geometry, using pure forms such as the circle and square, and plans are based on often symmetrical layouts featuring rectangular courtyards and halls. These structures were is decorated with carved stone or stucco reliefs and made use of colorful stone mosaics..

In Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya there is always just enough rain to be annoying.

The Serpentfolk near Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya are known to be quite timid.

Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves bloodletting to channel Invocation energies of tier 3 via divine sermons.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya was attacked by a group of guerrilla fighters. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya lost 174 people, 325 livestock, and 68 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 134, when members of Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya's militia enacted an operation to deliver supplies to a particular solgiers group. The operation was complicated by major logistical problems. The conflict ended with a last stand against the enemy until a particular event occurred, which ended in defeat for Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Lu̽dî Higu̹ Gûchya's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
