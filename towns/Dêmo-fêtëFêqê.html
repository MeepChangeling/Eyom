Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê (/ˈdɘmo/ /ˈfɘtë/ [leg] [custom]) is a temperate Town located in the Yêmëcê Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê was founded by Dhan Trêye, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê has a yearly average temperature of 26°C (78°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 32°C (89°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 21°C (69°F). Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê receives an average of 254 cm/y (99 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 1654 m (5426 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê was founded durring the early 12th century in fall of the year 1133, by Dhan Trêye. The establishment of Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the early 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê is is constructed arround a series of narrow packed earth mainstreets which form concentric circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to each other at varrious points. The town resides behind a palisade wall complete with battlments, a moat, and timber gatehouses with drawbridges. Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê's robustly designed timber walls are suffering from significent damage, so much so that examples can be pointed to no matter which section one might have within their line of site, and most of which render sections inoperable at present.

Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê is not quite well. Something happened here, perhapse recently, perhapse long ago. Whatever it was, it settled into the very soul of the town like a festering wound. The people go about their day well enough, but there’s a tention in the air you can cut with a knife.

Cultural Notes

Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by vertical proportions, pointed arches, external buttressing, and asymmetry in the general shape of its buildings. The decorative features of the style were key, consisting of large arched windows, pointed arches, vaulted ceilings, flying buttresses, and sculptures integrated into the structure itself. Occasionally, for very important buildings, an array of sculptures or one colossal sculpture might replace the entirety of the entrance to said building.

Due to the actions of local Kami, summer is short in Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê.

The Shredskin near Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves consuming a local narcotic to channel Invocation energies of tier 3 via throat singing.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century several years of heavy rains culminated in a peculiar disaster. A great hole opened up beneath Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê, swallowing large sections of the community. Dêmo-fêtë Fêqê lost 227 people, 173 livestock, and 44 buildings in the disaster. This disaster is often refereed to as The Heartbreak Swallowing.
