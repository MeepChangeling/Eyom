Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze (/ˈvuvə/ /ˈcʊʧæ/ [stem] [compost; fertilizer]) is a subtropical Town located in Kijohisau County, Mî Kûrà Empire, within the Warrenese Shogunate.

The name Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze is derived from the Wareneese language, as Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze was founded by Tshê Tresêv Tshë, who was culturaly Wareneese.

Climate

Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze has a yearly average temperature of 13°C (55°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 23°C (73°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 3°C (37°F). Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze receives an average of 145 cm/y (57 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 4972 m (16312 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze was founded durring the early 14th century in fall of the year 1325, by Tshê Tresêv Tshë. The establishment of Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Tshê Tresêv Tshë electing to pay people to resettle in Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze.

Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze was built using the conventions of Wareneese durring the early 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze is buildings are arranged arround a single spacious flagstone mainstreet with many smaller streets branching off of it which gives the town a over all rectangular shape, albit one warped and twisted by the nature of the curves of the main road. The town is protected by a humble stone fence which is tall enough to provide adiquite cover for defenders to fire from, but no more than that. Unfortuantly, these peasent-grade are in extreem disrepair, so much so that one cannot tell if they are decaying from a lack of mantance or damage incured.

A quick look in any direction shows Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze is filled with vices. There are many taverns, brothels are advertising their services on the streets, cardsharks are plying their illicit trade within public squairs, and every shop has at least three signs advertising various sales on goods. That alone wouldn’t be too noticeable, if it wasn’t just so omnipresent and overwhelmingly how the people of this town live.

Cultural Notes

Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is inspired by the natural world, characterized by sinuous, sculptural, organic shapes, arches, curving lines, and sensual ornamentation. Common motifs included stylized versions of leaves, flowers, vines, insects, animals, and other natural elements. Decorative elements found on the inside and outside of buildings include intricate mosaic work, curved windows, and decorative trim work. .

In Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze tiny harmless tornadoes plague the town.

The Manticore near Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves bloodletting to channel Summoning energies of tier 1 via divine sermons.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze was attacked by soldiers from another nation, waging a greater campaign. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze lost 281 people, 268 livestock, and 95 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 198, when members of Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze's militia enacted an operation to patrol a specific area to dissuade the enemy from their current activities. The operation was complicated by a key segment of the operation that deepened entirely on a stealth mission going perfectly. The conflict ended with a last stand against the enemy until a particular event occurred, which ended in a stalemate for Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Chyûha Chyûchæ Mîze's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
