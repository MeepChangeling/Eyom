Chyameya Viho Habe (/ˈviho/ /ˈhabe/ [old; former] [irony]) is a subtropical Small City located in the Lakâ Kâzi Principality of the Kakoray.

The name Chyameya Viho Habe is derived from the Sylvin language, as Chyameya Viho Habe was founded by Trëka, who was culturaly Rattu.

Climate

Chyameya Viho Habe has a yearly average temperature of 14°C (57°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 2°C (35°F). Chyameya Viho Habe receives an average of 263 cm/y (103 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Chyameya Viho Habe covers an area of nearly 10 km2 (4 mi2), and an average elevation of 2662 m (8733 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Chyameya Viho Habe was founded durring the late 17th century in early summer of the year 1588, by Trëka. The establishment of Chyameya Viho Habe was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Trëka electing to pay people to resettle in Chyameya Viho Habe.

Chyameya Viho Habe was built using the conventions of Rattu durring the late 17th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Chyameya Viho Habe is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Chyameya Viho Habe is buildings are arranged arrounded highly ordered system of premissive paverstone streets which form octogonal paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The city is protected by a renforced stone fence which sits atop earthwork defences, for some of the best inexpencive defences a city of Chyameya Viho Habe's size could have. Chyameya Viho Habe's well-designed, yet cheep has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

Maybe it’s the way fog blankets the ground, but only in the connors of places. Maybe it’s the vermin scuttling between shadows in the corner of your eyes. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Chyameya Viho Habe's parks.

Chyameya Viho Habe has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Chyameya Viho Habe.

Chyameya Viho Habe has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Chyameya Viho Habe has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Chyameya Viho Habe has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Chyameya Viho Habe's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Chyameya Viho Habe has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Chyameya Viho Habe's natural decorations nor waterways.

Chyameya Viho Habe has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Chyameya Viho Habe has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Chyameya Viho Habe has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Chyameya Viho Habe has a long tradition of martial expertise. This may be a crisply-organized history of skilled native levies, or it may be a natural belligerence in the people that leaves them familiar with bloodshed. While their neighbors and liege doubtless respect their talents, this very aptitude might make them more willing to turn to steel than prudence would advise.

Chyameya Viho Habe's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known for its fluid and florid elaborate style, comprising ornate, asymmetric designs and pastel shades. It is often considered to be a playful, light style, which made exuberant use of curves and emphasized subtle asymmetry in the general shape of its structures. Walls, ceilings and moldings are decorated with numerous interlacing of curves and counter-curves based on the shapes of ‘C’ and ‘S’, along with shell forms and other naturalistic shapes.

Due to the actions of local Kami, summer is long in Chyameya Viho Habe.

The Megafauna, Embolotherium near Chyameya Viho Habe are known to be quite timid.

Chyameya Viho Habe's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves long periods of drunkenness to channel Augury energies of tier 1 via moments of science.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Chyameya Viho Habe was attacked by soldiers from another nation, waging a greater campaign. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Chyameya Viho Habe lost 123 people, 136 livestock, and 25 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 100, when members of Chyameya Viho Habe's militia enacted an operation to assassinate an enemyinfluential figure. The operation was complicated by a significant percentage of the militia choose to go abandon the operation and return home. The conflict ended with pitched battle between both forces, which ended in defeat for Chyameya Viho Habe's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Chyameya Viho Habe's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
