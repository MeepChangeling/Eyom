Kad Slist-yäk (/slist/ /jɑk/ [medium] [leisure; recreation]) is a subtropical Large City located in the Sluptprond Principality of the Koiapi.

The name Kad Slist-yäk is derived from the Goblin language, as Kad Slist-yäk was founded by Grraegrurth, who was culturaly Gnoll.

Climate

Kad Slist-yäk has a yearly average temperature of 6°C (42°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a cold 2°C (35°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 10°C (50°F). Kad Slist-yäk receives an average of 250 cm/y (98 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Kad Slist-yäk covers an area of nearly 103 km2 (41 mi2), and an average elevation of 4542 m (14901 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Kad Slist-yäk was founded durring the late 14th century in summer of the year 1256, by Grraegrurth. The establishment of Kad Slist-yäk was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Grraegrurth struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Kad Slist-yäk as a prison colony.

Kad Slist-yäk was built using the conventions of Gnoll durring the late 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Kad Slist-yäk is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Kad Slist-yäk is is constructed arround a semi-circular crampt flagstone mainstreet, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The city posesses a fortified albit thin wall of querried stone. This wall posesses most of the features of a castle wall, though it is constructed from cheeper inferior stone. It would pose a minor chalange for an attacking army, though it's clear the wall's true purpose is to crush the hopes of bandits and marauders. Unfortuantly, these failry decent fortifications are in extreem disrepair, so much so that one cannot tell if they are decaying from a lack of mantance or damage incured.

A look around Kad Slist-yäk gives you a feeling of distrust and paranoia. Also greed. The locals seem to be very much into scholastic pursuits, based on the hushed, murmured conversations you hear at least. Everyone in Kad Slist-yäk acts like they have discovered something wondrous, and are worried they will endure some horrible fate so another can take what they’ve discovered for themselves.

Civic Infrastructure

Kad Slist-yäk possesses a city-wide Aethary Link which provides Aethary access anywhere within its metropolitan. This allows citizens who can afford the relevant devices access in their places of work, and rarely homes.

Kad Slist-yäk has an animal control department which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the control, impoundment, and disposition of animals.

Kad Slist-yäk has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Kad Slist-yäk. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Kad Slist-yäk's parks.

Kad Slist-yäk has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Kad Slist-yäk.

Kad Slist-yäk has a government-funded child care program, overseen by the local Department of Nursemaids, which is responsible for providing childcare to working-class citizens according to local ordinances.

Kad Slist-yäk has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Kad Slist-yäk has a Guild of Nurses, which is tasked with caring for the elderly and infirm in accordance with local ordinances, religious values, and customs.

Kad Slist-yäk has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Kad Slist-yäk has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Kad Slist-yäk has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Kad Slist-yäk has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Kad Slist-yäk's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Kad Slist-yäk has an Arcane Academy which provides higher education in the arcane sciences.

Kad Slist-yäk possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Large City. Kad Slist-yäk's grid is powered by a direct leyline tap.

Kad Slist-yäk possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. In spite of the Galvanic Grid, these lights continue to use their old fule sources to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Kad Slist-yäk has a first rate hospital which caters to anyone in need of long term medical care.

Kad Slist-yäk has a library, which keeps a large collection of books, scrolls, and archives all manner of physical items. While not open to the public, the librarians and scholars employed by the library will assist anyone with their research needs, and wealthy individuals can purchase membership to access the library's materials themselves. In spite of being generally closed to the public, the library has a room with several Aether Linked devices available to the public during business hours.

Kad Slist-yäk has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Kad Slist-yäk's natural decorations nor waterways.

Kad Slist-yäk has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Kad Slist-yäk has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Kad Slist-yäk has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Kad Slist-yäk's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used which employed abundant symbolic geometry, using pure forms such as the circle and square, and plans are based on often symmetrical layouts featuring rectangular courtyards and halls. These structures were is decorated with carved stone or stucco reliefs and made use of colorful stone mosaics..

In Kad Slist-yäk sometimes, near one of the seasonal solstices, the sun appears to split into three and it rains from a clear sky for several hours.

The Kapre near Kad Slist-yäk are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Kad Slist-yäk's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves line dance to channel Enchantment energies of tier 2 via proclamations.

History

Kad Slist-yäk is in secret rebellion against their ostensible liege, having cut deals with his enemies, plotted to betray him for their own gain, or bridled under his tyranny and sought a better lord. The community’s leadership is all in on this plot, and outside viceroys or representatives are being kept carefully ignorant of the reality. The common folk may be oblivious to the truth, though they’ll doubtless have felt the same motivations and promptings that convinced their leaders to turn traitor.

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century was ravaged by a deadly sickness. was the epicenter of the disaster, but Kad Slist-yäk suffered the most. Kad Slist-yäk lost 230 people, 322 livestock, and 56 buildings in the disaster.. The disaster is spoken of in hushed whispers as the Wrath of Grieving.
