Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê (/ˈfèvi/ /ˈfɘgo/ [fake] [paint]) is a subtropical Small Town located in the Fîthdekî Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê was founded by Shtin Dyêtco, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê has a yearly average temperature of 26°C (78°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 28°C (82°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 25°C (77°F). Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê receives an average of 266 cm/y (104 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê covers an area of nearly 3 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 2678 m (8786 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê was founded durring the late 12th century in fall of the year 1051, by Shtin Dyêtco. The establishment of Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Shtin Dyêtco struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê as a prison colony.

Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the late 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê is is constructed arround a series of narrow packed earth mainstreets which form overlapping circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The town lacks any defencive features, though certainly constructing even a simple fence or digging a ditch is at the forefront of the 's mind. At least, one would hope so.

A look around Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê gives you a feeling of distrust and paranoia. Also greed. The locals seem to be very much into scholastic pursuits, based on the hushed, murmured conversations you hear at least. Everyone in Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê acts like they have discovered something wondrous, and are worried they will endure some horrible fate so another can take what they’ve discovered for themselves.

Cultural Notes

There’s a relatively new religion in Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê which is rapidly gaining power. It might be a sectarian offshoot of a major faith, the unique product of a new prophet, or an outside faith backed by wealthy and powerful foreign supporters. Depending on the demands made on believers, the new faith may be a matter of concern only to the existing clergy, or it might be a major flashpoint for conflict in the community.

Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is which made use of the classical orders and mathematically precise ratios of height and width combined with a desire for symmetry, proportion, and harmony. It used columns, pediments, arches and domes are imaginatively in buildings of all types. Decorative features were seen as largely unnecessary as the sheer beauty of the structure itself was often close to art. However, many buildings with large ceiling spaces had their ceilings decorated with elaborate paintings, simply because the large flat spaces could feel wasted.

In Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê leaves and flower petals never touch the ground.

The Howler near Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Fèvi-fêgo Fêqê's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves performance art to channel Transmutation energies of tier 2 via recitation of scripture.
