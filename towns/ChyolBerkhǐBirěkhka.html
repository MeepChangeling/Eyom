Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka (/col/ /ˈberxɪ/ [gorgeous; handsome] [hobby]) is a subtropical Town located in Ci-51qdibri Zone, Khǐnèzhog District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka is derived from the Constructi language, as Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka was founded by In-22l 'Tom Servo' Tsëm, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka has a yearly average temperature of 20°C (68°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 29°C (84°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 12°C (53°F). Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka receives an average of 229 cm/y (90 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 1796 m (5892 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka was founded durring the late 11th century, by In-22l 'Tom Servo' Tsëm. The establishment of Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to In-22l 'Tom Servo' Tsëm electing to pay people to resettle in Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka.

Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the late 11th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka is buildings have been located at convienant points along the valley Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka was built upon. Navigating the town is therefore a little chalanging as the distance between buildings verris greatly and the premissive flagstone streets flow where they are able to be made rather than folowing the most convienant paths. The town is protected by a series of wooden fences ringing the town's parimiter, which are likly intended to keep varrious beasts out of town rather than protect it from attack by any intelegent agents. Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka's bare minimum defences are in an unremarkable state. To some, this is the ideal sate for defences to be in. In need of absoutly nothing, and ready to serve the town as needed.

A quick look in any direction shows Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka is filled with vices. There are many taverns, brothels are advertising their services on the streets, cardsharks are plying their illicit trade within public squairs, and every shop has at least three signs advertising various sales on goods. That alone wouldn’t be too noticeable, if it wasn’t just so omnipresent and overwhelmingly how the people of this town live.

Cultural Notes

Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is which made use of the classical orders and mathematically precise ratios of height and width combined with a desire for symmetry, proportion, and harmony. It used columns, pediments, arches and domes are imaginatively in buildings of all types. Decorative features were seen as largely unnecessary as the sheer beauty of the structure itself was often close to art. However, many buildings with large ceiling spaces had their ceilings decorated with elaborate paintings, simply because the large flat spaces could feel wasted.

In Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka grains of dust blow into perfectly neat rows.

The Thoqqua near Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Chyol Berkhǐ Birěkhka's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves consuming a local narcotic to channel Summoning energies of tier 2 via throat singing.
