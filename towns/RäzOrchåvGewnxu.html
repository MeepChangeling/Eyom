Räz Orchåv Gewnxu (/jijˈvo/ /guːnˈxu/ [poor] [sling]) is a subtropical Small City located in Genephfurn Hold, Gÿtgesheyd Kingdom, within the Undermountain.

The name Räz Orchåv Gewnxu is derived from the Sylvin language, as Räz Orchåv Gewnxu was founded by Shêbêv, who was culturaly Undermountain.

Climate

Räz Orchåv Gewnxu has a yearly average temperature of 24°C (75°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 29°C (84°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 20°C (68°F). Räz Orchåv Gewnxu receives an average of 198 cm/y (77 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Räz Orchåv Gewnxu covers an area of nearly 13 km2 (5 mi2), and an average elevation of 4564 m (14973 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Räz Orchåv Gewnxu was founded durring the early 12th century in spring of the year 1139, by Shêbêv. The establishment of Räz Orchåv Gewnxu suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Räz Orchåv Gewnxu which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Räz Orchåv Gewnxu was built using the conventions of Undermountain durring the early 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Räz Orchåv Gewnxu is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Räz Orchåv Gewnxu is buildings folow an organic layout of broad flagstone streets whihch gives the city a shape simmilar to a tree, if one views its streets from above. The city rests behind the absurdity that is a thick, timber braced, wall made of clay bricks. While visualy impressive and certainly an astetic, Räz Orchåv Gewnxu's wall provides no actual defence against siege equipment due to the choice of its cosntruction materials. Even nonexperts can tell the town is trying to impress rather than defend with its walls, towers, and gatehouses. Though admittedly, they do look nice... To primitive tribals who have never seen fortifications before. Unfortuantly, these political statment focused walls are in extreem disrepair, so much so that one cannot tell if they are decaying from a lack of mantance or damage incured.

Räz Orchåv Gewnxu has the unmistakable air of a city on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Räz Orchåv Gewnxu ’s existence has dried up and the city is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. Likely due to this, Räz Orchåv Gewnxu is, in a word, disorder. People seem to be allowed to do as they please with little harmoney to anything. It feels less like a city, and more like a spot people just happened to have homes. One cannot help but wonder what Räz Orchåv Gewnxu was like during the glorydays.

They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Räz Orchåv Gewnxu's parks.

Räz Orchåv Gewnxu has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Räz Orchåv Gewnxu.

Räz Orchåv Gewnxu has a government-funded child care program, overseen by the local Department of Nursemaids, which is responsible for providing childcare to working-class citizens according to local ordinances.

Räz Orchåv Gewnxu has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Räz Orchåv Gewnxu has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Räz Orchåv Gewnxu has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Räz Orchåv Gewnxu's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Räz Orchåv Gewnxu possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Small City. Räz Orchåv Gewnxu's grid is powered by a boiler and turbine based power plant.

Räz Orchåv Gewnxu has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Räz Orchåv Gewnxu's natural decorations nor waterways.

Räz Orchåv Gewnxu has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Räz Orchåv Gewnxu has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Räz Orchåv Gewnxu has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Räz Orchåv Gewnxu's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by dynamic designs and complex architectural plan forms; intended to heighten feelings of motion and sensuality, and frequently based on the oval. It made extensive and extreme use of: Grandeur, Contrast, Curves and twists, Rich surface treatments, Gilded statuary, Bright colors, Vividly painted ceilings, Fragmented or deliberately incomplete elements, Large-scale frescoes, Dramatic central projections on an external facade, the use of plaster, stucco, or marble finishing, Illusory effects such as trompe l’oeil, and pear-shaped domes. While beloved by the nobility, the common folk tended to despise the style due to the massive consumption of resources required for even a small building constructed in this style.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century, Räz Orchåv Gewnxu was attacked by soldiers from another nation, waging a greater campaign. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Räz Orchåv Gewnxu lost 133 people, 325 livestock, and 80 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 93, when members of Räz Orchåv Gewnxu's militia enacted an operation to bring aid to another fighting force. The operation was complicated by a key segment of the operation that deepened entirely on a stealth mission going perfectly. The conflict ended with needing to break through the enemy's lines, which ended in a stalemate for Räz Orchåv Gewnxu's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Räz Orchåv Gewnxu's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
