El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka (/wə̝x/ /ˈkæxgi/ [loose; easy] [show (performance); exhibit]) is a subtropical Town located in Sagomeli Parish, Pz-339jnup District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka is derived from the Constructi language, as El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka was founded by Brêrêtco Gadshêyê, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka has a yearly average temperature of 24°C (75°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 29°C (84°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 19°C (66°F). El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka receives an average of 224 cm/y (88 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 1932 m (6338 ft) above sea level.

Overview

El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka was founded durring the early 14th century in fall of the year 1319, by Brêrêtco Gadshêyê. The establishment of El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the early 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka is buildings are speckled and packed arround premissive cobblestone streets with seemingly no patern to them. It appears as if the town's residents simply built streets as they pleased and squeazed buildings in wherever and howeave rpossible, creating an organic, frustrating to navigate, maze of a town. The town has a defencive wall made from querried stone. The wall is equipped with a full set of battlments but the nature of its construction methodology leaves it somewhat vulnerable to siege equipment. That said, the town is well defended against anything short of an army. The town's cost-cutting-focused defences are visibly old, but also obviously well maintained. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with routine mantance of the town's defences.

El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up.

Cultural Notes

El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka is led by one or more incompetents. While they must have been very good at something to have acquired the position, they are fundamentally incapable of leading. Uncontrolled passions or lusts, commitment to a hopelessly impractical ideal, pigheaded obstinacy in the face of failure, a total lack of charisma or interpersonal skills, or profound laziness might all unfit them for their post.

El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is known for its fluid and florid elaborate style, comprising ornate, asymmetric designs and pastel shades. It is often considered to be a playful, light style, which made exuberant use of curves and emphasized subtle asymmetry in the general shape of its structures. Walls, ceilings and moldings are decorated with numerous interlacing of curves and counter-curves based on the shapes of ‘C’ and ‘S’, along with shell forms and other naturalistic shapes.

In El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka tiny harmless tornadoes plague the town.

The Carrion Moth near El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

El-48u Kækhgi Birěkhka's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves consuming a local narcotic to channel Wild Magic energies of tier 3 via singing.
