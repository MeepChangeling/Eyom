Fèvi-diyono Fêqê (/ˈfèvi/ /diˈjono/ [fake] [poet]) is a subtropical Town located in the Z̄upaguv Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name Fèvi-diyono Fêqê is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Fèvi-diyono Fêqê was founded by Moctîj Shêpîtco, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Fèvi-diyono Fêqê has a yearly average temperature of 26°C (78°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 30°C (86°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 23°C (73°F). Fèvi-diyono Fêqê receives an average of 69 cm/y (27 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Fèvi-diyono Fêqê covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 8646 m (-14583 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Fèvi-diyono Fêqê was founded durring the late 11th century, by Moctîj Shêpîtco. The establishment of Fèvi-diyono Fêqê suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Fèvi-diyono Fêqê which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Fèvi-diyono Fêqê was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the late 11th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Fèvi-diyono Fêqê is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Fèvi-diyono Fêqê is is constructed arround a semi-circular spacious flagstone mainstreet, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The town is protected by a series of wooden fences ringing the town's parimiter, which are likly intended to keep varrious beasts out of town rather than protect it from attack by any intelegent agents. The bare minimum defences has suffered a visible ammount of structural damage, leaving them effectivly useless. One can't help but wonder why the has not yet effected repairs.

A look around Fèvi-diyono Fêqê makes it abundantly clear the town suffered something horrible some time ago. It's as if the town itself is depressed. Smiles are few, cheer is nowhere to be had. Everyone quietly goes about their daily business not looking anyone in the eye.

Cultural Notes

Fèvi-diyono Fêqê's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for the combined use of arcuated and trabeated construction, employing arches and constructed with post and lintel. The arch served as the style's chief structural element, with flanking columns serving as buttresses or decorations. While the general shape of their structures could be generously described as simplistic, the use of these arches and columns created a most distinct ascetic which is easily distinguishable on sight to armatures and experts alike.

Due to the actions of local Kami, spring is recurring in Fèvi-diyono Fêqê.

The Stag, Emperor near Fèvi-diyono Fêqê are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Fèvi-diyono Fêqê's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves embarking on a group pilgrimage to channel Necromancy energies of tier 3 via guttural bellowing.
