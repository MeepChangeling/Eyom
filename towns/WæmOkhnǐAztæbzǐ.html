Wæm Okhnǐ Aztæbzǐ (/ˈoxnɪ/ /azˈtæbzɪ/ [formal] [peach]) is a subtropical Town located in Ikhoha Parish, Wækwìilbu District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Wæm Okhnǐ Aztæbzǐ is derived from the Constructi language, as Wæm Okhnǐ Aztæbzǐ was founded by Funabus Trêrí Dyesí, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Wæm Okhnǐ Aztæbzǐ has a yearly average temperature of 28°C (82°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 33°C (91°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 24°C (75°F). Wæm Okhnǐ Aztæbzǐ receives an average of 214 cm/y (84 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Wæm Okhnǐ Aztæbzǐ covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 8282 m (-15777 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Wæm Okhnǐ Aztæbzǐ was founded durring the early 10th century, by Funabus Trêrí Dyesí. The establishment of Wæm Okhnǐ Aztæbzǐ was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Funabus Trêrí Dyesí electing to pay people to resettle in Wæm Okhnǐ Aztæbzǐ.

Wæm Okhnǐ Aztæbzǐ was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the early 10th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Wæm Okhnǐ Aztæbzǐ is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Wæm Okhnǐ Aztæbzǐ is is constructed arround a series of crampt cobblestone mainstreets which form overlapping circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The town is protected by a well-crafted cobblestone fence tall enough to provide adiquite cover for defenders to fire from, but no more than that. Unfortuantly, these frontieer-style defences are in extreem disrepair, so much so that one cannot tell if they are decaying from a lack of mantance or damage incured.

Wæm Okhnǐ Aztæbzǐ has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Wæm Okhnǐ Aztæbzǐ ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. The locals seem to have responded to their slow downfall by recreating Wæm Okhnǐ Aztæbzǐ as one of the strictest places imaginable. Everyone’s actions are clearly directed by laws they keep in heart and mind at all times. Orderly byond order is a phrase which the town brings to mind.
