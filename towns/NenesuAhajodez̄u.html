Nenesu Ahajodez̄u (/ˈaha/ /joˈdez̚u/ [dusk; twilight] [careful]) is a subtropical Town located in the expanse.

The name Nenesu Ahajodez̄u is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Nenesu Ahajodez̄u was founded by Bé̄kīsh Māshīy 'Buxom Ophelia' Caw̄sh Joīnvé̄ Vúō̄s Èdsèbmë hu Senneferi, who was culturaly Ancient.

Climate

Nenesu Ahajodez̄u has a yearly average temperature of 10°C (50°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a cold 10°C (50°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 11°C (51°F). Nenesu Ahajodez̄u receives an average of 203 cm/y (79 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Nenesu Ahajodez̄u covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 246 m (807 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Nenesu Ahajodez̄u was founded durring the early 11th century, by Bé̄kīsh Māshīy 'Buxom Ophelia' Caw̄sh Joīnvé̄ Vúō̄s Èdsèbmë hu Senneferi. The establishment of Nenesu Ahajodez̄u was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Bé̄kīsh Māshīy 'Buxom Ophelia' Caw̄sh Joīnvé̄ Vúō̄s Èdsèbmë hu Senneferi electing to pay people to resettle in Nenesu Ahajodez̄u.

Nenesu Ahajodez̄u was built using the conventions of Ancient durring the early 11th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Nenesu Ahajodez̄u is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Nenesu Ahajodez̄u is buildings are arranged within a network of premissive baked earthen streets which form a rectangular grid, where each block verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller block has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town has a defencive wall made from large clay bricks. The wall is constructed to the exact specifications of millitary fortifications, but the nature of its clay brick construction leaves it vulnerable to even outdated siege equipment. That said, the town is well defended against anything short of an army. Nenesu Ahajodez̄u's brittle defences are in an unremarkable state. To some, this is the ideal sate for defences to be in. In need of absoutly nothing, and ready to serve the town as needed.

A look arround Nenesu Ahajodez̄u reveals an abundance of schools, libraries, and other academic structures. Locals can be overheard having academic discussions, as well as talking about scholarly subjects in general. It’s quite clear the town places a lot of value on education and being a learned individual.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Nenesu Ahajodez̄u was attacked by soldiers from another nation, waging a greater campaign. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Nenesu Ahajodez̄u lost 235 people, 269 livestock, and 62 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 131, when members of Nenesu Ahajodez̄u's militia enacted an operation to recruit a specific influential figure. The operation was complicated by a key segment of the operation that deepened entirely on a stealth mission going perfectly. The conflict ended with an assault and siege on the bridge, which ended in defeat for Nenesu Ahajodez̄u's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Nenesu Ahajodez̄u's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
