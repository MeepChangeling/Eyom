Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè (/ˈqovë/ /ˈmivɪ/ [purple] [tribunal]) is a subtropical Town located in the Skhokraskhobuba Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè was founded by Trthèbe Zèdonm, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè has a yearly average temperature of 25°C (77°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 29°C (84°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 22°C (71°F). Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè receives an average of 72 cm/y (28 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 8508 m (-15036 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè was founded durring the late 13th century in summer of the year 1187, by Trthèbe Zèdonm. The establishment of Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè was only bairly constructed. The sheer number of problems with its founding were enough to make several of the backers funding Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè's construction back out of the project. Trthèbe Zèdonm pushed on reguardles, and Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè was finished, but starts off as a terible place to live.

Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the late 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè is buildings are grouped arround an odd layout of crampt cobblestone streets, which seems to be based on an overlapping squair patern such that there are small squares at the cornor of every bigger square. Sometimes buildings exist in the smaller squaires, other times they are open spaces, or occupied by temporary structures. The town is protected by a well-crafted cobblestone fence tall enough to provide adiquite cover for defenders to fire from, but no more than that. The frontieer-style defences have not been wellmaintained over the years, and while functional are in dire need of some loving care and perhapse light renovation.

A look around Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè is like a look into a broken heart. There is no planning, no organization. Everyone here clearly goes about their own thing with little thought to anyone around them who isn’t selling something they need. It’s not malicious. Heads are hung. Eyes are empty. Something truly horrible happened here once upon a time and the town never healed.

Civic Infrastructure

Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè.

Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè possesses an older civil lighting system consisting of street lamps. These lights provide nighttime illumination to most city streets.

Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by order, symmetry, formal design, grandiosity, and elaborate ornamentation. Architectural characteristics include balustrades, balconies, columns, cornices, pilasters, and triangular pediments. Stone exteriors are massive and grandiose in their symmetry; interiors are typically polished and lavishly decorated with sculptures, swags, medallions, flowers, and shields. Interiors will often have a grand stairway and opulent ballroom..

Due to the actions of local Kami, winter is long in Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè.

The Ahlinni near Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves sacrificing an animal to channel Charm energies of tier 3 via oath swearing.

Points of Interest

Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè has been cursed with some blight that makes life difficult, albeit not impossible. An offended sorcerer's vengeful Working, an outraged god's wrath, a local distortion of the Legacy, or a simple history of bad feng shui in the area may have brought the curse about. I(devise not only the curse, but the reason why the locals haven't left for better lands).

Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè's roads were poorly made when first laid. Rather than repairing them correctly, a series of new roads was laid atop the old, leading to the streets of modern Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè suffering from potholes, cracking, and even sinkholes. The locals often repair the road by putting down wooden decking.

POI

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century the Kami spared the town from the rampage of a legendary monster. One of Qovë-mivî Dêtîkè's local festivals commemorates this miracle.
