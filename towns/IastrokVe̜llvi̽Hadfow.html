Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow (/ˈjastrok/ /ˈve̜llvi̽/ [angry; mad] [temple; church]) is a subtropical Large City located in the Ko̠ktugttö Moot of the Rosid.

The name Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow is derived from the Goblin language, as Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow was founded by Zoliod, who was culturaly Wood Elf.

Climate

Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow has a yearly average temperature of 13°C (55°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 26°C (78°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 1°C (33°F). Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow receives an average of 148 cm/y (58 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow covers an area of nearly 97 km2 (38 mi2), and an average elevation of 4048 m (13280 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow was founded durring the early 12th century in summer of the year 1138, by Zoliod. The establishment of Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Zoliod struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow as a prison colony.

Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow was built using the conventions of Wood Elf durring the early 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow is buildings are arranged arround a network of broad cobblestone streets which form a diagonal shaped grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The city rests behind a set of well fortified walls, with gatehouses, watch towers, and battlments. Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow's walls are, howeaver, fashioned from stone and timber. While unorthadox, the design looks to be functional to a reasonable degree. With luck, the untested design will remain untested for years to come. Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow's unusual yet seemingly effective fortifications has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

A look around Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow gives you a feeling of distrust and paranoia. Also greed. The locals seem to be very much into scholastic pursuits, based on the hushed, murmured conversations you hear at least. Everyone in Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow acts like they have discovered something wondrous, and are worried they will endure some horrible fate so another can take what they’ve discovered for themselves.

Cultural Notes

Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for the combined use of arcuated and trabeated construction, employing arches and constructed with post and lintel. The arch served as the style's chief structural element, with flanking columns serving as buttresses or decorations. While the general shape of their structures could be generously described as simplistic, the use of these arches and columns created a most distinct ascetic which is easily distinguishable on sight to armatures and experts alike.

In Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow the water is caffeinated.

The Venomroach near Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow are known to be quite timid.

Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves sacrificing an animal to channel Enchantment energies of tier 1 via divine sermons.

Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow is surrounded by dangerous terrain: miasmatic swamps, perilous crevasses, radioactive badlands, a pocket of or some other harmful topography. Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow might prefer the defensive potential of the terrain here, or have found a precious resource worth the danger. The terrain might have formed at some time since the founding, with the citizens struggling to make terms with the new danger.

Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow's roads were poorly made when first laid. Rather than repairing them correctly, a series of new roads was laid atop the old, leading to the streets of modern Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow suffering from potholes, cracking, and even sinkholes. The locals often repair the road by putting down wooden decking.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow was attacked by savage ogress living nearby. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow lost 180 people, 289 livestock, and 68 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 122, when members of Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow's militia enacted an operation to construct defenses in a particular settlment. The operation was complicated by a trusted officer who turned traitor and defects. The conflict ended with the defense of the settlment against a siege, which ended in victory for Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Iastrok Ve̜llvi̽ Hadfow's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
