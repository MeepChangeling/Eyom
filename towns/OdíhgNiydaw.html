Odíhg Niydaw (/nij/ /daw/ [option] [obese]) is a subtropical Town located in Vi̋tḩoi̋ër Dutchy, Ftèwup Provence, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Odíhg Niydaw is derived from the Tauric language, as Odíhg Niydaw was founded by Dyeshe Wales Beynon Naegle Medwin Iwan Rheon Gakēè, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Odíhg Niydaw has a yearly average temperature of 24°C (75°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 21°C (69°F). Odíhg Niydaw receives an average of 276 cm/y (108 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Odíhg Niydaw covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 6440 m (21128 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Odíhg Niydaw was founded durring the late 9th century, by Dyeshe Wales Beynon Naegle Medwin Iwan Rheon Gakēè. The establishment of Odíhg Niydaw was somewhat plagued by a lack of willing colonists, leading to Dyeshe Wales Beynon Naegle Medwin Iwan Rheon Gakēè electing to pay people to resettle in Odíhg Niydaw.

Odíhg Niydaw was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the late 9th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Odíhg Niydaw is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature masoned stone construction which prominantly features pointed arches, pointed ribbed vault cielings, flying buttress', and window tracery all of which share a simmilar gemoetetic patern halfway between organic and inorganic in design formaing a very distinct aesthetically integrated style. BUildings tend to reach for the havens, and more expencive homes are easily identified by their floor count as well as the addition of decorative features intigrated into the building's design such as statues, gargoyals, and embelished joinery.

Odíhg Niydaw is was constructed arround several premissive packed earth mainstreets which cross one another at certain axies, with smaller streets branching off of them to premit acess to the many buildings deeper into the road network. The overall fashion is remenessent of a circulatory system, or other organic construct, and is quite effishent in its design. The town has a defencive wall made from large clay bricks. The wall is constructed to the exact specifications of millitary fortifications, but the nature of its clay brick construction leaves it vulnerable to even outdated siege equipment. That said, the town is well defended against anything short of an army. Astonishigly, the brittle defences are in pristine condishion, as if they had just been finished before you laied eyes upon them.

A look around Odíhg Niydaw makes it abundantly clear the town suffered something horrible some time ago. It's as if the town itself is depressed. Smiles are few, cheer is nowhere to be had. Everyone quietly goes about their daily business not looking anyone in the eye.

Civic Infrastructure

Odíhg Niydaw has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Odíhg Niydaw. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Odíhg Niydaw's parks.

Odíhg Niydaw has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Odíhg Niydaw.

Odíhg Niydaw has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Odíhg Niydaw has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Odíhg Niydaw has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Odíhg Niydaw's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Odíhg Niydaw has an Scientific Academy which provides higher education in the natural sciences.

Odíhg Niydaw has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Odíhg Niydaw's natural decorations nor waterways.

Odíhg Niydaw has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Odíhg Niydaw has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Odíhg Niydaw's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by order, symmetry, formal design, grandiosity, and elaborate ornamentation. Architectural characteristics include balustrades, balconies, columns, cornices, pilasters, and triangular pediments. Stone exteriors are massive and grandiose in their symmetry; interiors are typically polished and lavishly decorated with sculptures, swags, medallions, flowers, and shields. Interiors will often have a grand stairway and opulent ballroom..
