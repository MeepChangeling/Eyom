Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya (/ˈləsæ/ /ˈcəzɪː/ [arrogant] [priority]) is a subtropical City located in the Lakâ Kâzi Principality of the Kakoray.

The name Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya is derived from the Goblin language, as Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya was founded by Cleleus, who was culturaly Rattu.

Climate

Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya has a yearly average temperature of 11°C (51°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing -5°C (23°F). Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya receives an average of 245 cm/y (96 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya covers an area of nearly 37 km2 (14 mi2), and an average elevation of 4582 m (15032 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya was founded durring the early 13th century in fall of the year 1242, by Cleleus. The establishment of Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Cleleus struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya as a prison colony.

Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya was built using the conventions of Rattu durring the early 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya is buildings are arranged arround a network of crampt packed earth streets which form a diagonal shaped grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The city is protected by a renforced stone fence which sits atop earthwork defences, for some of the best inexpencive defences a city of Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya's size could have. Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya's well-designed, yet cheep are in an unremarkable state. To some, this is the ideal sate for defences to be in. In need of absoutly nothing, and ready to serve the city as needed.

A look around Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya gives you an uneasy feeling. Everything is just a little too worn down, a little too dirty, or both. No one makes eye contact. Kids play quietly, but happily. Occasionally a passerby glances at you out of the corner of their eye, staring just long enough for it to be uncomfortable. This all rests atop the unmistakable impression the town is one of the strictest places imaginable. Everyone’s actions are clearly directed by laws they keep in heart and mind at all times. Orderly byond order is a phrase which Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya brings to mind.

Cultural Notes

Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya has a long tradition of martial expertise. This may be a crisply-organized history of skilled native levies, or it may be a natural belligerence in the people that leaves them familiar with bloodshed. While their neighbors and liege doubtless respect their talents, this very aptitude might make them more willing to turn to steel than prudence would advise.

Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used embraces individualism and experimentation. It emerged as a movement against traditional, classical styles and sought to make buildings dynamic and fun while breaking the rules. The style incorporated elements of previous architectural styles in exaggerated and whimsical ways. Traditional, conservative leanings were void in this era, with most scholars of architecture agreeing it was a time of “anything goes.”.

Due to the actions of local Kami, summer is skipped in Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya.

The Lemming near Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Mærame Chyàzî Gûchya's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in autumn and involves drinking to channel Abjuration energies of tier 2 via recitation of scripture.
