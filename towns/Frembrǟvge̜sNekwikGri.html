Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri (/fremˈbraːvge̜s/ /ˈnekwik/ [serious; sincere; earnest] [capsule; tablet]) is a subtropical Large City located in Kiz̄ukhiqanu Zone, Mijlequni District, within the Alveria.

The name Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri is derived from the Sylvin language, as Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri was founded by Liklepa, who was culturaly Wood Elf.

Climate

Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri has a yearly average temperature of 21°C (69°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 15°C (59°F). Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri receives an average of 130 cm/y (51 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri covers an area of nearly 166 km2 (66 mi2), and an average elevation of 5126 m (16817 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri was founded durring the early 13th century in winter of the year 1212, by Liklepa. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Liklepa.

Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri was built using the conventions of Wood Elf durring the early 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri is buildings are arranged arrounded a highly ordered system of broad cobblestone streets which form triangular paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The city is the proud owner of a proper castle-style stone wall complete with all of the trimmings. It has towers, a moat, gatehouses, drawbridges, and even merticulationsshortsizeleadershipname.. Astonishigly, the perhapse unnessisarily well built defences are in pristine condishion, as if they had just been finished before you laied eyes upon them.

Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri has a very calm atmosphere. People can be seen relaxing, scocilizing, and going about all manner of business other than the daily grind. Men, women, children, all can be seen enjoying life in a laid-back way in the many parks which line Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri’s streets. In spite of this, quite a few people can be seen reading and the city has an abundance of libraries. It’s quite clear the community values education.

Cultural Notes

While Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri might ostensibly be ruled by some other power, real control lies with the senior members of the local craft and labor guilds. Their decisions have the practical weight of law, and much of their time and effort is spent squeezing out competitors and parceling out economic opportunities in the community. Some guilds might have little or nothing to do with their original trade, and now exist purely as shells for political influence.

Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for its functional shapes, abstract shapes used sparingly for decor, simple color schemes, holistic design, and basic industrial materials. Its simple designs were created to be beautiful, functional, and mass-producible. The style used little to no embellishment or ornamentation, instead drawing attention to the streamlined design, such as flat roofs to create a simple, geometric look. The simplicity masks the style's nearly sinister functionality, as every last feature is designed to guide the people living in the building in how to make the most efficient use of the structure.

In Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri the water is caffeinated.

The ﻿Sylph near Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in spring and involves performance art to channel Necromancy energies of tier 2 via recitation of scripture.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri was attacked by soldiers from another nation, waging a greater campaign. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri lost 274 people, 115 livestock, and 99 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 207, when members of Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri's militia enacted an operation to recruit a particular solgiers group. The operation was complicated by the enemy forces had far more numbers than estimated. The conflict ended with pitched battle between both forces, which ended in defeat for Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Frembrǟvge̜s Nekwik Gri's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
