Tmèhāclíh Se (Tmè /ˈhəˑclɪ̞/ [option] [grave]) is a subtropical Town located in Hakhpiz̄o County, Ftèwup Provence, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Tmèhāclíh Se is derived from the Tauric language, as Tmèhāclíh Se was founded by Ýåts Colliams Llywellen, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Tmèhāclíh Se has a yearly average temperature of 22°C (71°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a pleasant 25°C (77°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 19°C (66°F). Tmèhāclíh Se receives an average of 233 cm/y (91 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Tmèhāclíh Se covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 9308 m (-12411 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Tmèhāclíh Se was founded durring the late 14th century in spring of the year 1296, by Ýåts Colliams Llywellen. The establishment of Tmèhāclíh Se suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Tmèhāclíh Se which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Tmèhāclíh Se was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the late 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Tmèhāclíh Se is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Tmèhāclíh Se is is constructed arround a semi-circular premissive packed earth mainstreet, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The town sits behind an impressive looking clay brick wall. The wall is notable for querried stone blocks being used to renforce the clay bricks in the expected manner and locations. While the wall was built to the exact specifications of modern fortifications, one has to wonder why the money invested into creating such a well fortified wall wasnt spent on a less impressive looking wall crated from sturdier materials. Tmèhāclíh Se's walls would endure a brief bombardment, but only from light siege weapons. It seems more likly the town's walls were not designed with defence in mind but rather lending the town a certain air with visitors. Tmèhāclíh Se's boondoggle-of-a-fortified wall has sufferd soem light damage, reducing its function a little in some spots, but could almsot certainly preform as expected... Though some of the worse spots could lead to the loss of defenders lives if attackers identified the weaknesses ahead of time.

Before you’ve even set foot into the heart of Tmèhāclíh Se, you can smell it. The incense. It hangs about the town like a cloud. Monks, priests, and clerics are everywhere, all dedicated to the same god, all performing the same rituals to bless and anoint buildings, streets, people, animals, you name it they are or have blessed it. The same holy symbol is everywhere too. Its on buildings, on people, and even branded into livestock. This certainly loves its god. More than it loves wealth. The town is very clearly poor. Buildings are run down in ways that are not imeadiatly obvious. The people are a bit too thin. The market is very eager to sell to newcomers, but not so eager to buy from them. There’s also a general lack of the hum and buzz of healthy industry in Tmèhāclíh Se.

Civic Infrastructure

Tmèhāclíh Se has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Tmèhāclíh Se. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Tmèhāclíh Se's parks.

Tmèhāclíh Se has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Tmèhāclíh Se.

Tmèhāclíh Se has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Tmèhāclíh Se has a Guild of Nurses, which is tasked with caring for the elderly and infirm in accordance with local ordinances, religious values, and customs.

Tmèhāclíh Se has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Tmèhāclíh Se has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Tmèhāclíh Se has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Tmèhāclíh Se has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Tmèhāclíh Se's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Tmèhāclíh Se possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Town. Tmèhāclíh Se's grid is powered by a god's will and kindness.

Tmèhāclíh Se has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Tmèhāclíh Se's natural decorations nor waterways.

Tmèhāclíh Se has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Tmèhāclíh Se has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Tmèhāclíh Se's garrison was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is inspired by the natural world, characterized by sinuous, sculptural, organic shapes, arches, curving lines, and sensual ornamentation. History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century several years of heavy rains culminated in a peculiar disaster. A great hole opened up beneath Tmèhāclíh Se, swallowing large sections of the community. Tmèhāclíh Se lost 106 people, 268 livestock, and 49 buildings in the disaster. This disaster is often refereed to as The Horror Swallowing.
