Vekhu Waqip Birěkhka (/ˈvexu/ /ˈwaqip/ [arrogant] [widow]) is a subtropical Town located in the Wozqoěs District of the Federation of Alveria.

The name Vekhu Waqip Birěkhka is derived from the Constructi language, as Vekhu Waqip Birěkhka was founded by Byog̈i Dyèdsm Bresèbrê, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Vekhu Waqip Birěkhka has a yearly average temperature of 19°C (66°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 28°C (82°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 10°C (50°F). Vekhu Waqip Birěkhka receives an average of 169 cm/y (66 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Vekhu Waqip Birěkhka covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 3000 m (9842 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Vekhu Waqip Birěkhka was founded durring the late 12th century in fall of the year 1074, by Byog̈i Dyèdsm Bresèbrê. The establishment of Vekhu Waqip Birěkhka suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Vekhu Waqip Birěkhka which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Vekhu Waqip Birěkhka was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the late 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Vekhu Waqip Birěkhka is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature stone brick construction with most buildings posessing towers, turrets, dormers, and wide wrap-around porches. An extreem emphasis on decorative features and emblishments can be seen even on the poorest of homes and makes even the most humble of structures seem a bit regal.

Vekhu Waqip Birěkhka is buildings are speckled and packed arround crampt baked earthen streets with seemingly no patern to them. It appears as if the town's residents simply built streets as they pleased and squeazed buildings in wherever and howeave rpossible, creating an organic, frustrating to navigate, maze of a town. The town resides behind a palisade wall complete with battlments, a moat, and timber gatehouses with drawbridges. Unfortuantly, these robustly designed timber walls are in extreem disrepair, so much so that one cannot tell if they are decaying from a lack of mantance or damage incured.

Vekhu Waqip Birěkhka is, in a word, disorder. People seem to be allowed to do as they please with little harmoney to anything. It feels less like a town, and more like a spot people just happened to place their homes. Yet there are small elements here and there which show the underlying structure of the community. It’s just so complex, organic, and flowing one can only understand what is a piece of the puzzle, but not what its neighbors are.
