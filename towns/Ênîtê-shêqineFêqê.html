Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê (/ɘˈnɪtɘ/ /ʃɘˈqine/ [clean; clear] [trunk (large box)]) is a temperate Town located in the Dlzironedu Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê was founded by Shelëi Zlkêpî, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê has a yearly average temperature of 22°C (71°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 18°C (64°F). Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê receives an average of 73 cm/y (28 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 5636 m (18490 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê was founded durring the early 16th century in winter of the year 1538, by Shelëi Zlkêpî. The establishment of Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê suffered from several major issues, resulting in the need to develop many solutions to basic problems. Problems such as a lack of fresh water, logistical support, poor quality tools, and the odd monster or two. Howeaver, these were overcome in time.

Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the early 16th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê is buildings are arranged arround a network of restrictive packed earth streets which form a diagonal shaped grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town is protected by a humble stone fence which is tall enough to provide adiquite cover for defenders to fire from, but no more than that. The peasent-grade has suffered a visible ammount of structural damage, leaving them effectivly useless. One can't help but wonder why the has not yet effected repairs.

Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê has the unmistakable air of a town on its last legs. Everything is a bit slipshod and ramshackle. Everyone is at work, or drinking. No one has anything in their eyes other than fear and despair. Whatever industry once fueled Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê ’s existence has dried up and the town is drifting down the stream of history as it dries up. Even with that as it is, everything is just a little too worn down, a little too dirty, or much of both. The town should be gone, not dying. No one makes eye contact. Kids play quietly, but happily. Occasionally a passerby glances at you out of the corner of their eye, staring just long enough for it to be uncomfortable.

Civic Infrastructure

Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê's parks.

Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê.

Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Town. Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê's grid is powered by a direct leyline tap.

Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê's natural decorations nor waterways.

Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used has a sleek, linear appearance with stylized, often geometric ornamentation. The primary facade of its buildings often featured a series of set backs that create a stepped outline. Low-relief decorative panels can be found at entrances, around windows, along roof edges or as string courses. It was best known for its use of smooth finish building materials such as stucco, concrete block, glazed brick or mosaic tile. Decorative details can incorporate various artistic or exotic motifs to suit the building's function or the architect's whim. Chevrons, zigzags, and other geometrical motifs are common forms of ornament.

In Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê rainbows form quite often above the Town.

The Leucrotta near Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves creating small tokens to channel Chronomancy energies of tier 1 via recitation of scripture.

History

Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê used to be much richer, but something happened in the last (25258 % 6)+1 years to crush its source of prosperity. Different factions of the community might be trying to grasp at the remaining dregs of wealth, others might try to restart the failed industry, and some might look for a new livelihood. Any group or entity thought responsible for the collapse is likely to be treated very harshly, and some locals might find profit in shifting the blame to their enemies.

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century the Kami ended a famine plaguing Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê. One of Ênîtê-shêqine Fêqê's local festivals commemorates this miracle.
