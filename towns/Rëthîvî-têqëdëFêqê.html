Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê (/rëˈʃɪvɪ/ /tɘˈqëdë/ [risky] [trophy]) is a subtropical Town located in the Jĭǐkhěn District of the Federation of Alveria.

The name Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê was founded by Zëmê Glêbrê, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê has a yearly average temperature of 26°C (78°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 29°C (84°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 23°C (73°F). Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê receives an average of 155 cm/y (61 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 2140 m (7020 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê was founded durring the early 15th century in summer of the year 1435, by Zëmê Glêbrê. The establishment of Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê suffered from several major issues, resulting in the need to develop many solutions to basic problems. Problems such as a lack of fresh water, logistical support, poor quality tools, and the odd monster or two. Howeaver, these were overcome in time.

Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the early 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê is buildings are arranged arround a network of restrictive packed earth streets which form a diagonal shaped grid, where each square verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller square has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town is protected by a renforced stone fence which sits atop earthwork defences, for some of the best inexpencive defences a town of Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê's size could have. Unfortuantly, these well-designed, yet cheep are in extreem disrepair, so much so that one cannot tell if they are decaying from a lack of mantance or damage incured.

A look around Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê gives you an uneasy feeling. Everything is just a little too worn down, a little too dirty, or both. No one makes eye contact. Kids play quietly, but happily. Occasionally a passerby glances at you out of the corner of their eye, staring jsut long enough for it to be uncomfortable. Beneath this surface, the people of Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê can be overheard having academic discussions, as well as talking about scholarly subjects in general. It’s quite clear Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê places a lot of value on education and being a learned individual.

Civic Infrastructure

Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê has an animal control department which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the control, impoundment, and disposition of animals.

Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê.

Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê's mayor's house was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for its functional shapes, abstract shapes used sparingly for decor, simple color schemes, holistic design, and basic industrial materials. Its simple designs were created to be beautiful, functional, and mass-producible. The style used little to no embellishment or ornamentation, instead drawing attention to the streamlined design, such as flat roofs to create a simple, geometric look. The simplicity masks the style's nearly sinister functionality, as every last feature is designed to guide the people living in the building in how to make the most efficient use of the structure.

In Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê all of the cats speak in a dead language.

The Barracuda, Swamp near Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê are known to be a mutant strain of the creature.

Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves line dance to channel Invocation energies of tier 3 via throat chanting.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century, Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê was attacked by soldiers from another nation, waging a greater campaign. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê lost 282 people, 154 livestock, and 79 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 87, when members of Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê's militia enacted an operation to train a group of civilians for a special clandestine operation. The operation was complicated by one of the key objectives in the operation requiring sticking to a very strict and short time table. The conflict ended with an assault and siege on the bridge, which ended in victory for Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Rëthîvî-têqëdë Fêqê's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
