Eg Quyla Ÿhchpheh (/quˈjla/ /ɪ̘kˈfɪ̘/ [extinct] [citadel]) is a subtropical Town located in Fùshdùjnā County, Gandlacckÿrf Kingdom, within the United Kingdom of Undermountain.

The name Eg Quyla Ÿhchpheh is derived from the Dwarven language, as Eg Quyla Ÿhchpheh was founded by Acharnith Rådschå, who was culturaly Dwarvern.

Climate

Eg Quyla Ÿhchpheh has a yearly average temperature of 28°C (82°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 31°C (87°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 25°C (77°F). Eg Quyla Ÿhchpheh receives an average of 174 cm/y (68 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Eg Quyla Ÿhchpheh covers an area of nearly 4 km2 (1 mi2), and an average elevation of 4032 m (13228 ft) above sea level.

This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Acharnith Rådschå.

Eg Quyla Ÿhchpheh was built using the conventions of Dwarvern durring the early 10th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Eg Quyla Ÿhchpheh is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Eg Quyla Ÿhchpheh is was constructed arround several spacious baked earthen mainstreets which cross one another at certain axies, with smaller streets branching off of them to premit acess to the many buildings deeper into the road network. The overall fashion is remenessent of a circulatory system, or other organic construct, and is quite effishent in its design. The town lacks any defencive features, though certainly constructing even a simple fence or digging a ditch is at the forefront of the 's mind. At least, one would hope so.

Maybe it’s the way fog blankets the ground, but only in the connors of places. Maybe it’s the vermin scutteling between shadows in the corner of your eyes. History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century Mount Horror, an iconic vista located neare Eg Quyla Ÿhchpheh, proved to be volcanic when the mountain erupted. The eruption was isolated to the area around Eg Quyla Ÿhchpheh, which was swallowed in ash, lava flows, and pyroclastic gasses. Eg Quyla Ÿhchpheh lost 280 people, 320 livestock, and 31 buildings in the disaster. The event is forever remembered as the Day of Horror's Wrath.
