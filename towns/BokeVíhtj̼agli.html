Boke Víhtj̼agli (/vɪ̞t/ /ˈj̼agli/ [slope; slant] [gorgeous]) is a temperate Large City located in the Ftiyāobāt Region of the Tetburland.

The name Boke Víhtj̼agli is derived from the Sylvin language, as Boke Víhtj̼agli was founded by Nrëd, who was culturaly Tauran.

Climate

Boke Víhtj̼agli has a yearly average temperature of 28°C (82°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a hot 32°C (89°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 25°C (77°F). Boke Víhtj̼agli receives an average of 252 cm/y (99 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Boke Víhtj̼agli covers an area of nearly 122 km2 (48 mi2), and an average elevation of 4230 m (13877 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Boke Víhtj̼agli was founded durring the late 14th century in fall of the year 1256, by Nrëd. The establishment of Boke Víhtj̼agli was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Nrëd struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Boke Víhtj̼agli as a prison colony.

Boke Víhtj̼agli was built using the conventions of Tauran durring the late 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Boke Víhtj̼agli is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Boke Víhtj̼agli is buildings are arranged arrounded a highly ordered system of spacious packed earth streets which form triangular paterns, allowing the incides of the octagons to be common grounds for the buildings on the edges, be it for parkland, yardspace, plazas, or markets. The city rests behind the absurdity that is a thick, timber braced, wall made of clay bricks. While visualy impressive and certainly an astetic, Boke Víhtj̼agli's wall provides no actual defence against siege equipment due to the choice of its cosntruction materials. Even nonexperts can tell the town is trying to impress rather than defend with its walls, towers, and gatehouses. Though admittedly, they do look nice... To primitive tribals who have never seen fortifications before. The political statment focused walls have not been wellmaintained over the years, and while functional are in dire need of some loving care and perhapse light renovation.

A look around Boke Víhtj̼agli is like a look into a broken heart. There is no planning, no organization. Everyone here clearly goes about their own thing with little thought to anyone around them who isn’t selling something they need. It’s not malicious. Heads are hung. Eyes are empty. Something truly horrible happened here once upon a time and the city never healed.

Civic Infrastructure

Boke Víhtj̼agli possesses a city-wide Aethary Link which provides Aethary access anywhere within its metropolitan. This allows citizens who can afford the relevant devices access in their places of work, and rarely homes.

Boke Víhtj̼agli has an animal control department which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the control, impoundment, and disposition of animals.

Boke Víhtj̼agli has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Boke Víhtj̼agli. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Boke Víhtj̼agli's parks.

Boke Víhtj̼agli has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Boke Víhtj̼agli.

Boke Víhtj̼agli has a government-funded child care program, overseen by the local Department of Nursemaids, which is responsible for providing childcare to working-class citizens according to local ordinances.

Boke Víhtj̼agli has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Boke Víhtj̼agli has a Guild of Nurses, which is tasked with caring for the elderly and infirm in accordance with local ordinances, religious values, and customs.

Boke Víhtj̼agli has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Boke Víhtj̼agli has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Boke Víhtj̼agli has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Boke Víhtj̼agli has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Boke Víhtj̼agli's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Boke Víhtj̼agli has an Millitary Academy which trains military officers and specilists.

Boke Víhtj̼agli possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Large City. Boke Víhtj̼agli's grid is powered by a god's will and kindness.

Boke Víhtj̼agli's old civil lighting system was converted to Galvanic Lamps recently, and expanded to provide nighttime illumination to all city streets.

Boke Víhtj̼agli has a first rate hospital which caters to anyone in need of long term medical care.

Boke Víhtj̼agli has a library, which keeps a large collection of books, scrolls, and archives all manner of physical items. While not open to the public, the librarians and scholars employed by the library will assist anyone with their research needs, and wealthy individuals can purchase membership to access the library's materials themselves. In spite of being generally closed to the public, the library has a room with several Aether Linked devices available to the public during business hours.

Boke Víhtj̼agli has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Boke Víhtj̼agli's natural decorations nor waterways.

Boke Víhtj̼agli has a Guild of Roadworkers, who are responsible for maintaining the roadways and public paths within town. They also have the duty of enforcing all civil laws relating to the roadways.

Boke Víhtj̼agli has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Boke Víhtj̼agli has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Boke Víhtj̼agli's chapel was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is admittedly strange and non-linear style rooted in defiance of symmetrical shapes. It championed the creation of buildings with a unique visual appearance. the structural norms of classic buildings and deforms or moves away from elementary architectural principles. By including non-linear designs processed into its buildings and favoring fragmentation, this style expressed a form of controlled chaos. Its buildings appear out-of-the-ordinary, draw the eye in immediately and sometimes create a feeling of strangeness. These distorted shapes and structure are not reserved to the building’s outer facade, they destabilize interior elements too, favoring minimalism and play on people’s perceptions by injecting a futuristic touch.

Due to the actions of local Kami, autumn is short in Boke Víhtj̼agli.

The Urdefhan near Boke Víhtj̼agli are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Boke Víhtj̼agli's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves creating small tokens to channel Mysticism energies of tier 3 via mimery.

History

Boke Víhtj̼agli's residents established a new industry 2 years ago, and it’s making them a great deal of profit. Old patterns of authority and wealth are being disrupted, and the old gentry are unlikely to be pleased about it. They may be trying to take over the industry, or they may have been the ones to enable it in the first place and are using it to crush the life out of any rival power bases. Outsiders might be playing a major role as well, and it could be they plot to siphon off the profits.

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century the Kami spared the town from the rampage of a legendary monster. One of Boke Víhtj̼agli's local festivals commemorates this miracle.
