Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka (/ˈqufɨ/ /ˈwiwtɛv/ [deaf] [cement]) is a subtropical Large Town located in Glilikthez̄u Parish, Wv-0săbb District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka is derived from the Constructi language, as Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka was founded by Alphaen Brtheg̈, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka has a yearly average temperature of 30°C (86°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a blistering 34°C (93°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a warm 27°C (80°F). Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka receives an average of 204 cm/y (80 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka covers an area of nearly 6 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 8646 m (-14583 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka was founded durring the early 11th century, by Alphaen Brtheg̈. The establishment of Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka suffered from several major issues, resulting in the need to develop many solutions to basic problems. Problems such as a lack of fresh water, logistical support, poor quality tools, and the odd monster or two. Howeaver, these were overcome in time.

Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the early 11th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka is buildings are arranged within a network of crampt worn bedrock streets which form a rectangular grid, where each block verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller block has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The town rests behind the absurdity that is a thick, timber braced, wall made of clay bricks. While visualy impressive and certainly an astetic, Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka's wall provides no actual defence against siege equipment due to the choice of its cosntruction materials. Even nonexperts can tell the town is trying to impress rather than defend with its walls, towers, and gatehouses. Though admittedly, they do look nice... To primitive tribals who have never seen fortifications before. Unfortuantly, these political statment focused walls are in extreem disrepair, so much so that one cannot tell if they are decaying from a lack of mantance or damage incured.

Right off the bat Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka hits you in the face with its success. Everyone, even the peasants, are dressed in well made clothing. Every tool and implement you can see is finely made, and people will boast to you as obvious strangers of the wonders which can be found in their markets. More interestingly is a total lack of beggars, and plenty of new buildings are going up even as you speak. Somehow this town has come into quite a lot of wealth, and recently from the looks of things. The town is very clearly a joyfull place as well as wealthy. Music can be heard often, as well as laughter. The smell of food and drink permiates the air. You can’t help but smile.

Civic Infrastructure

Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka has an Office of Civil Groundskeeping, which works to enforce local ordinances relating to the construction and upkeep up of all plant life, water features, and other natural decorations within Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka. They are also responsible for the maintenance of these features. Notably, the OCG is not responsible for Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka's parks.

Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka.

Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka has a Hall of Slayers, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands. They are not to be confused with the Office of Civil Groundskeeping as they do not hold authority over nor responsibility for Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka's natural decorations nor waterways.

Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

There’s more than one leader in Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka, but at least some of them are at each other’s throats. It might be a conflict between formal leadership and informal authorities, or it could be a struggle among civil officials. Their interests might diverge sharply, or it could be a personal grudge that’s boiled over. Outside threats and internal problems are likely being ignored until the power struggle is resolved.

Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by vertical proportions, pointed arches, external buttressing, and asymmetry in the general shape of its buildings. The decorative features of the style were key, consisting of large arched windows, pointed arches, vaulted ceilings, flying buttresses, and sculptures integrated into the structure itself. Occasionally, for very important buildings, an array of sculptures or one colossal sculpture might replace the entirety of the entrance to said building.

Due to the actions of local Kami, autumn is recurring in Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka.

The Snake, Constrictor near Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Qufì Wiwtěv Birěkhka's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves reenactments to channel Summoning energies of tier 3 via mimery.
