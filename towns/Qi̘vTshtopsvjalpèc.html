Qi̘v Tshtopsvjalpèc (Tshtopsv /ˈʤalpɛc/ [security] [apology]) is a subtropical Large City located in Bipsíhr County, Sidbury Landgrave, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Qi̘v Tshtopsvjalpèc is derived from the Tauric language, as Qi̘v Tshtopsvjalpèc was founded by Brynmor Wynner Williams, who was culturaly Tauric.

Climate

Qi̘v Tshtopsvjalpèc has a yearly average temperature of 23°C (73°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 26°C (78°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 21°C (69°F). Qi̘v Tshtopsvjalpèc receives an average of 137 cm/y (53 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the spring. Qi̘v Tshtopsvjalpèc covers an area of nearly 269 km2 (107 mi2), and an average elevation of 4696 m (15406 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Qi̘v Tshtopsvjalpèc was founded durring the late 15th century in summer of the year 1389, by Brynmor Wynner Williams. The establishment of Qi̘v Tshtopsvjalpèc was plagued by a lack of willing colonists. After attempts to pay people to resettle failed Brynmor Wynner Williams struck deals with nearby nations and communities to establish Qi̘v Tshtopsvjalpèc as a prison colony.

Qi̘v Tshtopsvjalpèc was built using the conventions of Tauric durring the late 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Qi̘v Tshtopsvjalpèc is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature plaster covered brickwork used to form structures with an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, geometry and the regularity of parts. Orderly arrangements of columns, pilasters and lintels, as well as the use of semicircular arches, hemispherical domes, niches and aediculae can be found everywhere such that only size of building and yard can be used to measure the general prosparity of a given building's owners due to a general wealthy feeling the style gives off.

Qi̘v Tshtopsvjalpèc is was constructed arround several spacious baked earthen mainstreets which cross one another at certain axies, with smaller streets branching off of them to premit acess to the many buildings deeper into the road network. The overall fashion is remenessent of a circulatory system, or other organic construct, and is quite effishent in its design. The cliffside city is defended by arcane means. It's hard to spot at first, but there's a tell tell shimmer in the air arround Qi̘v Tshtopsvjalpèc, and you can spot the ocasional warding glyph carved into a rock or tree all arround town. These mystical defences are ancient, unknowable, and unassailable by current means... Assuming everything is in working order. Otherwise, the wards are little more than a deathtrap. The Relic of the World That Was have not been wellmaintained over the years, and while functional are in dire need of some loving care and perhapse light renovation.

Right off the bat Qi̘v Tshtopsvjalpèc hits you in the face with its success. Everyone, even the peasants, are dressed in well made clothing. Every tool and implement you can see is finely made, and people will boast to you as obvious strangers of the wonders which can be found in their markets. More interestingly is a total lack of beggars, and plenty of new buildings are going up even as you speak. Many of those buildings are schools, scriptoriums, and even one college. One can only wonder what knowledge the town has come into.
