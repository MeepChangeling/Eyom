Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka (/rib/ /ˈfɪnvɛ/ [former] [destiny; fate]) is a temperate Town located in Skrōvnil Zone, Madraměvì District, within the Federation of Alveria.

The name Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka is derived from the Constructi language, as Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka was founded by Snocthè Zêyêye, who was culturaly Constructi.

Climate

Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka has a yearly average temperature of 16°C (60°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a cool 20°C (68°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 12°C (53°F). Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka receives an average of 235 cm/y (92 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the summer. Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 1632 m (5354 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka was founded durring the early 12th century in winter of the year 1102, by Snocthè Zêyêye. The establishment of Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka was built using the conventions of Constructi durring the early 12th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka is buildings are grouped arround an odd layout of crampt cobblestone streets, which seems to be based on an overlapping squair patern such that there are small squares at the cornor of every bigger square. Sometimes buildings exist in the smaller squaires, other times they are open spaces, or occupied by temporary structures. The town resides behind a palisade wall complete with battlments, a moat, and timber gatehouses with drawbridges. The robustly designed timber walls has suffered a visible ammount of structural damage, leaving them effectivly useless. One can't help but wonder why the has not yet effected repairs.

A look around Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka is like a look into a broken heart. There is no planning, no organization. Everyone here clearly goes about their own thing with little thought to anyone around them who isn’t selling something they need. It’s not malicious. Heads are hung. Eyes are empty. Something truly horrible happened here once upon a time and the town never healed.

Cultural Notes

Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by grandeur of scale, simplicity of geometric forms, dramatic use of columns, and a preference for blank walls. This design ethos extended to re-imagining earlier styles of structure to create them anew, with a similar overall look and feel to one another..

In Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka hail is always enormous, yet harmlessly plinks off people, creatures, and structures.

The Forgotten One near Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka are known to be quite timid.

Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves square dance to channel Abjuration energies of tier 2 via speaking in tongues.

History

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the early 2nd century, Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka was attacked by savage tribes living nearby Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka. The details of the conflict are hazy at best due to many conflicting accounts. What is known is Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka lost 152 people, 196 livestock, and 74 buildings. The conflict ended after roughly 79, when members of Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka's militia enacted an operation to escort a particular special forces group to a safe location. The operation was complicated by one of the key objectives in the operation requiring sticking to a very strict and short time table. The conflict ended with an assault and siege on the prison, which ended in defeat for Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka's forces. The war is remembered in legend by Rib Fǐnvě Birěkhka's bards, historians, and legend keepers.
