Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya (/ˌbæmaˈlolə/ /ˌhʊcæˈbæga/ [sick] [sadness]) is a subtropical Small City located in Gà S̺ûku̽chû Kingdom, Àsu̹ Ûyu̹ Empire, within the Warren.

The name Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya is derived from the Goblin language, as Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya was founded by Theggvong, who was culturaly Rattu.

Climate

Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya has a yearly average temperature of 6°C (42°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a cool 17°C (62°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing -5°C (23°F). Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya receives an average of 247 cm/y (97 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya covers an area of nearly 18 km2 (7 mi2), and an average elevation of 1444 m (4737 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya was founded durring the late 17th century in late spring of the year 1565, by Theggvong. The establishment of Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya was only bairly constructed. The sheer number of problems with its founding were enough to make several of the backers funding Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya's construction back out of the project. Theggvong pushed on reguardles, and Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya was finished, but starts off as a terible place to live.

Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya was built using the conventions of Rattu durring the late 17th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya is buildings folow an organic layout of crampt packed earth streets whihch gives the city a shape simmilar to a tree, if one views its streets from above. The city is protected by a well-crafted cobblestone fence tall enough to provide adiquite cover for defenders to fire from, but no more than that. The city's frontieer-style defences are visibly old, but also obviously well maintained. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with routine mantance of the city's defences.

Looking around Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya you immediately realise that the locals are looking back at you. All of them. Everywhere you look somone is staring back at you analytically, looking over every inch of you, your gear, and your companions. As you get close to people, their hands move closer to their belt knife, or dagger. You may want to watch where you go and what you say...

Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya has a public septic system, which allows its citizens to have indoor bathrooms. The septic system is overseen by the League of Sewerkeepers, who posses the legal authority to enforce all laws relating to the septic system, and are also tasked with its maintenance and upkeep.

Cultural Notes

Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya's bank was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by vertical proportions, pointed arches, external buttressing, and asymmetry in the general shape of its buildings. The decorative features of the style were key, consisting of large arched windows, pointed arches, vaulted ceilings, flying buttresses, and sculptures integrated into the structure itself. Occasionally, for very important buildings, an array of sculptures or one colossal sculpture might replace the entirety of the entrance to said building.

Due to the actions of local Kami, autumn is skipped in Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya.

The Skin Stitcher near Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya are known to be almost tame, such that they can be put to domestic use.

Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves drinking to channel Charm energies of tier 3 via guttural bellowing.

History

A vast influx of newcomers over the last 6 years has greatly spiked Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya's population. They may have been drawn by economic opportunities, or fled some pursuing peril, or been forcibly moved there by a ruler who wanted to dilute the existing native cohesion. The natives may not have the resources or opportunities to integrate these newcomers, and it may be that the new population has no desire to stay longer than is necessary.

In time immemorial, reportedly some time during the late 2nd century the Kami ended a drought plaguing Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya. One of Vûhi Hûchyæbæga Gûchya's local festivals commemorates this miracle.
