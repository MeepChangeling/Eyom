Vêdè-atè Fêqê (/ˈvɘdè/ /ˈatè/ [tropical] [midriff]) is a subtropical Town located in Roseston Dutchy, Èjila Provence, within the Dalandic Empire.

The name Vêdè-atè Fêqê is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Vêdè-atè Fêqê was founded by Dyèdshêm Zèqth, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Vêdè-atè Fêqê has a yearly average temperature of 24°C (75°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 26°C (78°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a pleasant 22°C (71°F). Vêdè-atè Fêqê receives an average of 212 cm/y (83 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Vêdè-atè Fêqê covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 5206 m (17080 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Vêdè-atè Fêqê was founded durring the early 13th century in fall of the year 1249, by Dyèdshêm Zèqth. The establishment of the new community went well, though many minor issues had to be solved as time went on. This was enough to delay construction and push back the formal opening ceramony, leading to some embarisment for Dyèdshêm Zèqth.

Vêdè-atè Fêqê was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the early 13th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Vêdè-atè Fêqê is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Vêdè-atè Fêqê is is constructed arround a series of broad cobblestone mainstreets which form overlapping circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The town emploies a series of defencive earthworks, spikes, and fences to provide some protection against wild beasts and smaller groups of intelegent foes. The town's minimaly adiquite are visibly old, but also obviously well maintained. Its likly the local malishia or garrison are tasked with routine mantance of the town's defences.

A look around Vêdè-atè Fêqê gives you an uneasy feeling. Everything is just a little too worn down, a little too dirty, or both. No one makes eye contact. Kids play quietly, but happily. Occasionally a passerby glances at you out of the corner of their eye, staring jsut long enough for it to be uncomfortable.

Civic Infrastructure

Vêdè-atè Fêqê has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Vêdè-atè Fêqê.

Vêdè-atè Fêqê has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Vêdè-atè Fêqê has a Department of Firefighters, which is responsible for organizing fire fighting efforts during a fire and enforcing local ordinances relating to fire safety.

Vêdè-atè Fêqê has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Vêdè-atè Fêqê has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Vêdè-atè Fêqê's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Vêdè-atè Fêqê has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

Vêdè-atè Fêqê has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.

Cultural Notes

Vêdè-atè Fêqê's town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by grandeur of scale, simplicity of geometric forms, dramatic use of columns, and a preference for blank walls. This design ethos extended to re-imagining earlier styles of structure to create them anew, with a similar overall look and feel to one another..

Due to the actions of local Kami, autumn is long in Vêdè-atè Fêqê.

The Gravebound near Vêdè-atè Fêqê are known to be more aggressive than normal.

Vêdè-atè Fêqê's citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in winter and involves line dance to channel Augury energies of tier 3 via oratory performances.
