Nobun Sa Qo̠ (/ˈnobun/ /sa/ [friendly] [profit]) is a subtropical Large City located in Vrotflom Kingdom, Ghrondor County, within the Warrenese Shogunate.

The name Nobun Sa Qo̠ is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as Nobun Sa Qo̠ was founded by Trëg̈g̈ Phrubegra, who was culturaly Wood Elven.

Climate

Nobun Sa Qo̠ has a yearly average temperature of 14°C (57°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 26°C (78°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cold 2°C (35°F). Nobun Sa Qo̠ receives an average of 148 cm/y (58 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Nobun Sa Qo̠ covers an area of nearly 276 km2 (110 mi2), and an average elevation of 3870 m (12696 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Nobun Sa Qo̠ was founded durring the early 14th century in fall of the year 1300, by Trëg̈g̈ Phrubegra. The establishment of the new community went well, with no major obsticles durring construction.

Nobun Sa Qo̠ was built using the conventions of Wood Elven durring the early 14th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Nobun Sa Qo̠ is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Nobun Sa Qo̠ is buildings are arranged arround a network of spacious cobblestone streets which form a diamond shaped grid, where each diamond verries in size given the proximity of the paralell streets forming each section. The ocasional smaller diamond has been used to construct a park, plaza, and other communal structures. The riverside city resides behind a palisade wall complete with battlments, a moat, and timber gatehouses with drawbridges. The robustly designed timber walls have not been wellmaintained over the years, and while functional are in dire need of some loving care and perhapse light renovation.

A look arround Nobun Sa Qo̠ reveals an abundance of schools, libraries, and other academic structures. Locals can be overheard having academic discussions, as well as talking about scholarly subjects in general. It’s quite clear the city places a lot of value on education and being a learned individual.

Cultural Notes

Nobun Sa Qo̠'s town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is best known for its striking sculptural forms and often dazzling ornamental detail that characterizes the buildings general shape. The radiant colors, rich patterns, and symmetrical silhouettes employed by this style were backed up by rich decorative features including gardens, courtyards, extruded arches, domes, pointed domes, vaulted ceilings, elaborate painted and inlaid designs, and decorative sculptures.

Nobun Sa Qo̠ is in secret rebellion against their ostensible liege, having cut deals with his enemies, plotted to betray him for their own gain, or bridled under his tyranny and sought a better lord. The community’s leadership is all in on this plot, and outside viceroys or representatives are being kept carefully ignorant of the reality.
