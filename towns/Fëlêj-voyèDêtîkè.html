Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè (/ˈɘje/ /ˈvojè/ [last (final); last (previous)] [shirt; blouse]) is a temperate Town located in the G̈mthêldèthêkè Zone of the Union of Engineers.

The name Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè is derived from the Iron Elvish language, as Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè was founded by Phaeta Tshêthèdo Shêyepí, who was culturaly Iron Elven.

Climate

Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè has a yearly average temperature of 23°C (73°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a cool 20°C (68°F). Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè receives an average of 191 cm/y (75 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of rain during the fall. Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè covers an area of nearly 5 km2 (2 mi2), and an average elevation of 9394 m (-12129 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè was founded durring the early 15th century in summer of the year 1438, by Phaeta Tshêthèdo Shêyepí. The establishment of Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè was only bairly constructed. The sheer number of problems with its founding were enough to make several of the backers funding Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè's construction back out of the project. Phaeta Tshêthèdo Shêyepí pushed on reguardles, and Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè was finished, but starts off as a terible place to live.

Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè was built using the conventions of Iron Elven durring the early 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè is no diffrent. The town's buildings feature waddle and daub construction with good timber frames and a stone foundation protected by thatched or shingled rooves. Most buildings with second floors are built in such a way as to overhang into the streets on the upper floors for more space, as building size seems to be the primary indicator of wealth within the community. Most buildings are not decorated with any integral features, but instead use ivy, flowers, and other natural elements in planters of on trellices to breathe life into the structure they grow upon.

Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè is was constructed arround several spacious paverstone mainstreets which cross one another at certain axies, with smaller streets branching off of them to premit acess to the many buildings deeper into the road network. The overall fashion is remenessent of a circulatory system, or other organic construct, and is quite effishent in its design. The town is the proud owner of a properly designed set of renforced walls made from mighty querried stone blocks. Their construction and material choices would make a dwarf weap with joy, for each and every part of the elaborate fortifications are purly functional and robust well byond reason. Even nonexperts can tell the walls are an excelent defencive structure. The exceptionaly well made fortifications have not been wellmaintained over the years, and while functional are in dire need of some loving care and perhapse light renovation.

Right off the bat Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè hits you in the face with its success. Everyone, even the peasants, are dressed in well made clothing. Every tool and implement you can see is finely made, and people will boast to you as obvious strangers of the wonders which can be found in their markets. More interestingly is a total lack of beggars, and plenty of new buildings are going up even as you speak. Many of those buildings are schools, scriptoriums, and even one college. One can only wonder what knowledge the town has come into.

Civic Infrastructure

Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè has an Office of Civil Vicary, which is responsible for providing a livelyhood for all officialy recognised religious figures within Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè.

Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè has a Gravedigger's Guild, which is responsible for collecting the dead and laying them to rest according to all applicable laws and religious customs.

Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè has a Highwayman's Guild, which is tasked with maintaining the roads and highways leading into town as well as keeping them safe for travelers.

Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè has a monistary of an order of Civil Monks, who provide divine-related services to the general public and maintain Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè's public wards, blessings, and other arcane systems.

Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè possesses a Galvanic Power Grid, which brings galvanic current to most if not all buildings in town, and permits a great many consumer goods to function within the Town. Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè's grid is powered by an arcane means.

Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè has a Parks and Recreation Department, which is responsible for the construction, management, and usage rights for all of its parks and parklands.

Fëlêj-voyè Dêtîkè has a public schooling program overseen by the Hall of Sages who has the responsibility of ensuring access to affordable high-quality education in all basic classes (Reading, Writing, Mathmatics, General Sciences, General Arcana, and Social Education) is made available to all citizens.
