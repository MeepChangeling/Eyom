Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠ (/stro˞/ /ˈco˞wto˞k/ [private] [edge; verge]) is a subtropical Large City located in the Meneghelmofe Earldom of the Viceroyalty of Rosid.

The name Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠ is derived from the Wood Elvish language, as Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠ was founded by Důh́å Ghuldrash, who was culturaly Wood Elven.

Climate

Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠ has a yearly average temperature of 10°C (50°F), with its average temperature during the summer being a warm 27°C (80°F) and its average temperature during the winter being a freezing -7°C (20°F). Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠ receives an average of 272 cm/y (107 in/y) of precipitation, most of which comes in the form of snow during the pleasantly short winter months. Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠ covers an area of nearly 531 km2 (212 mi2), and an average elevation of 1878 m (6161 ft) above sea level.

Overview

Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠ was founded durring the early 15th century in summer of the year 1418, by Důh́å Ghuldrash. The establishment of Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠ suffered from many setbacks, delays, and obsticles, most notably a group of Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠ which required millitary assistance exterminate before the community could finish being built.

Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠ was built using the conventions of Wood Elven durring the early 15th century. Naturaly, all settlmentss have their own look to them, and Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠ is no diffrent. The city's buildings feature timber framed wooden shiethed or brick construction, which gives form to a very formalized, rational, expence effishent arcatectural style based on strictly symmetrical designs which universaly feature pitched roofs, shutters, and the occasional column or pilaster for a decorative touch.

Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠ is is constructed arround a series of restrictive gravel mainstreets which form overlapping circles, with smaller strait roads linking the circiles to eachother at varrious points. The riverside city has a set of well fortified walls, with gatehouses, watch towers, battlments, and even a moat, which are fashioned from stone and timber. Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠'s walls are, howeaver, fashioned from stone and timber. While unorthadox, the design looks to be functional to a reasonable degree. With luck, the untested design will remain untested for years to come. Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠'s unusual though effective defenses are suffering from significent damage, so much so that examples can be pointed to no matter which section one might have within their line of site, and most of which render sections inoperable at present.

Right off the bat Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠ hits you in the face with its success. Everyone, even the peasants, are dressed in well made clothing. Every tool and implement you can see is finely made, and people will boast to you as obvious strangers of the wonders which can be found in their markets. More interestingly is a total lack of beggars, and plenty of new buildings are going up even as you speak. Somehow this city has come into quite a lot of wealth, and recently from the looks of things.

Cultural Notes

Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠'s town hall was built using a different architectural style from the rest of the town. The style used is characterized by vertical proportions, pointed arches, external buttressing, and asymmetry in the general shape of its buildings. The decorative features of the style were key, consisting of large arched windows, pointed arches, vaulted ceilings, flying buttresses, and sculptures integrated into the structure itself. Occasionally, for very important buildings, an array of sculptures or one colossal sculpture might replace the entirety of the entrance to said building.

Due to the actions of local Kami, spring is short in Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠.

The Hungry Flesh near Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠ are known to be quite timid.

Strö Chyhyöwtök Qo̠'s citizens partake in a curious ritual relating to their local kami. It takes place in summer and involves orgies to channel Truename Magic energies of tier 3 via speaking in tongues.
