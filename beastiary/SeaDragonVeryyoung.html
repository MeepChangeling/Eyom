Sea Dragon, Very Young

The long, undulating body of this serpentine dragon rolls and bends with scales the color of the ocean’s waves.

Very Young Sea Dragon CR 6

XP 2,400
CG Medium dragon (water)
Init +5; Senses dragon senses; Perception +5

AC 19, touch 11, flat-footed 18 (+1 Dex, +8 natural)
hp 59 (7d12+14)
Fort +9, Ref +6, Will +8
Immune electricity, paralysis, sleep

Speed 40 ft., fly 150 ft. (average), swim 60 ft.
Melee bite +10 (1d8+4), 2 claws +10 (1d6+3), gore +10 (1d6+4)
Space 5 ft.; Reach 5 ft. (10 ft. with bite and gore)
Special Attacks breath weapon (30-ft. cone, 4d6 fire damage, DC 15), torrent breath

Str 17, Dex 12, Con 15, Int 16, Wis 17, Cha 16
Base Atk +7; CMB +10; CMD 21 (25 vs. trip)
Feats Alertness, Great Fortitude, Improved Initiative, Lunge
Skills Diplomacy +13, Fly +11, Intimidate +13, Knowledge (arcana, nature) +13, Perception +5, Sense Motive +15, Stealth +11, Survival +13, Swim +21
Languages Aquan, Common, Draconic
SQ water breathing

Torrent Breath (Su)

Instead of a cone of super-heated steam, a very young or older sea dragon can breathe a line of pressurized water twice the length of the sea dragon’s cone breath weapon. This line deals bludgeoning damage.

Environment any water
Organization solitary
Treasure triple

Infused with the power of waves and storms, sea dragons or jiaolungs, as they are known in many lands are draconic protectors of oceans and their creatures. Possessing tempestuous natures, sea dragons wander widely, sometimes claiming thousands of miles of ocean and coastlines as their protectorates.
