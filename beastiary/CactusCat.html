Intimidating Yowl

As a swift action, the cactus cat can emit a terrifying yowl 4 times per encounter that strikes fear into the hearts of its opponents. Victims that fail the DC 16 intimidate check are shaken for 1d4 rounds. A shaken player is considered flat-footed.

Environment temperate and hot plains
Organization solitary, pair, or pack (known as a cacophony) (3-12)
Treasure catsap

The cactus cat is actually a mobile cactus which resembles a feline roughly the size of a bobcat with a spiky coat and a branched tail.

Cactus cats photosynthesize like all plants, but they also obtain nutrients by using their razor sharp claws to cut into local cacti and drink the sap. They will return to the cactus many times as the sap ferments and when they overindulge, enjoy a drunken caterwaul long into the night. Upon occasion, cats from multiple territories will congregate when drunk and conduct a group yowl.

The catsap a cactus cat produces after drinking from normal cacti is very potent and the resulting hangover is entirely resistant to magical cures. While it does not harm the cat to harvest the catsap, they will likely resist the process of extracting it.

In late summer, the cats will produce a flower on the head or neck, which can give them the appearance of wearing a hat or jewelry.

Components

The flowers of a cactus cat can be ground up and added to any potion, increasing the caster level of the spell by +1. The sap can be added to any potion increasing the effective level by +3. However, ingesting the sap causes the person to become immediately inebriated for 1 hour. After which, they will be nauseated for another 2 hours as the hangover sets in.

Familiar

A cactus cat can become a familiar to any 7th level spellcaster with the Improved Familiar feat.
